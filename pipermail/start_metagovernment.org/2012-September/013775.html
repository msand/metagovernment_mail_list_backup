  My questions: 

  Can any member post an issue?
  Who sets the life-time limit for an issues?
  What’s the difference between an issue and a proposition?
  Can a user create a new unit by itself?
  Does voting in Liquid Feedback lead to results that are binding for the Pirates, or is it just a precursor to the actual voting?

  I can only tell something about the situation here in Belgium. The pirates can decides for themselves locally how they will work. All citizens can vote, only members can vote, results are binding for the party and so on. Here in Brasschaat, a suburb of Antwerp,  http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Antwerp we are planning to launch local “democratic experiments” whereby the citizens can decide about some issues (demoex system). This can be on street level, neighbourhood level or communal level. Not informative but decisive. That way we will give the citizens the opportunity to get acquaintance with direct democracy and the fact that they have decisive power if they want to have it. Of course the vote can only bind the pirate party at this moment but this is a first step and the only one we have at our disposal for now.    

  Can I add links and pictures to an issue in Liquid Feedback?
  Supporters and Delegations are not anonymous. Why is this so?

  I think this is because we need to know how the delegate intend to vote. That way you are always able to vote yourself or appoint another delegate.

  What is the difference between Liquid Feedback and Adhocracy?

    Hello Michael,

    "Does this answer your larger question?" Unfortunately, not at all. What I see (and I have seen a lot) is that there a lot of smart people that spend a lot of time and energy on working brilliant concepts. At the same time there a lot of people anticipating a change to happen in our political world. 

    But progress is painstakingly slow, if there is any at all. It seems to me, that we think our concepts are so brilliant that the world is just waiting for us to deliver so they can use it, but that is, at least according to my experience, not the case. 

    Just as Per Norbäck has described in his Blog http://pernor.wordpress.com/2011/01/18/the-fight-for-new-democratic-forms/ things are not so easy. For Per things are so bad that he titles his blog "A failure or success?".

    James Watt, the inventor of the steam machine and his partner spent more than ten years being on the knocker until his invention was finally accepted and turned into a success. Finally, his invention kick off on of the greatest revolutions in history: the industrial revolution.

    If we leave our inventions to themselves without promoting and lobbying I am afraid they will go down without ever being known to the world.

    That is why I am concerned about how all these concepts fit into big picture, because, if they don't, I do not see a chance for them to ever come to life.
