Hello Thomas,

I also think that the move to online-supported politics is unstoppable. 
But the question is: what will it look like? The worst case scenario for 
me is where the traditional parties have implemented half-hearted (and 
badly made) offers for participation via Internet, just enough to stay 
in power (just like the FDP recently did), instead of truly taking 
advantage of the new possibilities, for a better democracy.

As we know, due the demographical development in Germany the 
Baby-Boomers (born around 1950-1970), i.e. the non-digital-natives, will 
be in the majority all the way into the 2030s. So, if we leave it to the 
digital natives, it will take another 20 years to see a change 
happening. I will be 70 by then.

At the same time, the kids are waiting for something to come around. For 
instance, Nina Pauer stated in an interview "I am political, but my 
political energies don't find a channel." 
www.spiegel.de/spiegel/unispiegel/d-82971132.html 
<http://www.spiegel.de/spiegel/unispiegel/d-82971132.html>.

And, sorry to say, but so far I find the Pirate Party's approach not so 
appealing. It's solution is targeted at the already political active 
person. It still leaves the common man, who does not want to spent too 
much time and effort on political work, but never the less wants his 
will to be represented, out.

This is why I came up with the idea of the "Opinion Forming" 
(Meinungsbildung) process, Post-Voting etc. in my concept (which is not 
competing with the PP approach, but rather extending it). But more on 
that in my mail on the translation of my concept to English.

Mit freundlichen Grüßen
