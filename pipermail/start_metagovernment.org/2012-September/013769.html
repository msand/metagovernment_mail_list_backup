> James Watt, the inventor of the steam machine and his partner spent 
> more than ten years being on the knocker until his invention was 
> finally accepted and turned into a success.

I believe with e-democracy as a whole we are far beyond the point of it 
being stoppable. The big German Pirate-Party using it is just one proof 
for that. And from here the process seems clear to me: the software will 
get better and better over time, with the best concept succeding. And 
the best concept imo is the one, where all the different e-dem-tools are 
interconnected and the users are free to choose their favorite.

We are of an older age and didn't grow up with the internet. But the new 
generation of "digital natives" will soon find a democracy without the 
"e-" completely unacceptable. Don't you agree?

Greetings,
Thomas
