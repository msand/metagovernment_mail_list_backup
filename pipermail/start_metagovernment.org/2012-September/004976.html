> I do not claim do understand Votorola good enough to able to
> criticize the concept as such. My thoughts are more on the
> environment, in which Votorola is to take place.

And:
> These question all circle around the question "how do we get

The environment we anticipate is one in which individuals are free to
choose the toolset and practices that best meet their personal needs
and abilities, or, failing that, to develop new toolsets of their own.
A new toolset with a single user is equally viable vs. one with a
million users in this roll-your-own environment, and individuals
cannot be herded into a one-size-fits-all, canned solution except
where they choose to be.

We learned here in Metagov that no other environment is possible,
neither in principle nor in practice.  So we're working to build it.
We know that Votorola will always be just one technical choice among
many.  We aren't competing on a winner-take-all field.  Does this
answer your larger question?
