This is interesting, though I find it overly restrictive. My critique:

1. It is limited by a rigid hierarchy of categorization. Who gets to  
decide what is a duplicate argument? Who decides what is a distinct  
issue? These appear to be left to an authority instead of the  
community. By contrast, I very much like Manuel's and Pietro's idea (I  
think), of using ground-up categorization through tagging. This lets  
ideas be classified in a more organic and flexible way.

2. It relies on moderators; again an imposed authority. Who moderates  
the moderators?

3. It restricts arguments to pro and con, which overly validates those  
specific issues as self-sustaining. That is, something which has a pro  
and a con must be valid as something to be answered yes or no. This  
discourages synthesis.

I'll write to the author and invite him to join the list.
