I have been looking into the requirements for migrating the current  
site to the TuxFamily host, and to do so, I have to resolve where the  
wiki should reside. It comes to a question of the below:

On May 25, 2011, at 3:40 AM, Michael Allan wrote:

> Ed Pastore wrote:
>> Michael Allan wrote:
>>> I meant that Metagov would probably be restricted to a single area
>>> in the pollwiki: http://u.zelea.com/w/Site:List_of_areas
>>>
>>> Metagov content then would be stored as subpages of the area page.
>>> Assuming area name 'M', the pages would be named M/this and M/that.
>>> See for example how Toronto's pages are named:
>>> http://u.zelea.com/w/Special:PrefixIndex/Tor
>>>
>>> The only pages sitting at top level in a multi-area wiki are the  
>>> area
>>> pages themselves: http://u.zelea.com/w/Category:Area
>>
>> But there also needs to be "meta"content which is outside the scope  
>> of
>> the issue-related content: something that helps orient people to the
>> site when they first visit it. Could you flip the idea, and have all
>> of the area pages inside a directory and all related content in sub-
>> directories of that?
>
> Unfortunately no.  A multi-area pollwiki is a logical stand-in for
> multiple wikis.  If we allowed meta-content, then it would seem to
> take priority over area content.  If an area admin disagreed with the
> meta-content, then he'd be tempted to decamp and set up a pollwiki of
> his own.  We can't support that yet and don't want to spend time on it
> till it's crucial, so we came up with the idea of a strictly neutral,
> multi-area pollwiki, just as a stopgap.  More here:
> http://zelea.com/project/outcast/dep.xht

After reading this a through several times and thinking on it, I begin  
to see the wisdom in it. Metagov is just another project, which itself  
is in need of governance, so it makes sense for it to be an "area."

But, Michael, Thomas, and Conseo, I have to admit, I have lost  
perspective of the difference between Metagov, Votorola, and  
Crossforum as they exist in your mind. Is a pollwiki strictly a  
function of Votorola or of the crossforum? Or do you see it as simply  
a discussion tool that can be annexed into any voting system (i.e.,  
free-range drafting)?

(Please note, I'm not trying to draw "lines in the sand" here. I just  
can't tell what is what, and I think it is useful for developers to  
occasionally step back and give a little documentation of what they're  
thinking of. I am having a tough time following a lot of the recent  
threads because I'm not sure what object they are really about, and  
how that object fits in with the others being discussed. I have to  
imagine that a lot of other list members feel the same. You could have  
more participation in this process if you can help clarify these ideas  
in non-developer-speak, and let people know precisely where help is  
needed. Please feel free to spin off a different thread called  
"status" or something, if appropriate.)

Separately, if Metagov were to be an Area within a pollwiki, would you  
see it as still appropriate to at least have a shell of meta content,  
such as the simplistic draft discussed in the thread I just posted on?
http://metagovernment.org/wiki/User:Ed_Pastore/redesign/draft2
Something to act as a gateway for people who don't know what they are  
looking at.


>> Neither that nor your solution sounds very elegant. Are you
>> envisioning merging the two wikis? Couldn't they exist side-by-side?
>
> Definitely.  A pollwiki is a user-editable database of polling data
> and related content.  It may contain non-polling content too, but only
> if that makes sense and is convenient.  The best arrangement for
> Metagov is probably:
>
>  a) Ordinary content in ordinary wiki.
>
>  b) Polling related data/content in an area of the common pollwiki.
>
> Later Metagov can go independent if necessary by upgrading the
> ordinary wiki into a pollwiki proper (semantic extensions etc.) and
> migrating the polling data/content across.  You'd have to deploy your
> own cluster of vote-servers too, so this is no small operation.  See:
> http://zelea.com/project/outcast/dep.xht#mul

If instead we go the parallel route, then would it make logistical  
sense to have each be a subdomain of the (Tux-hosted) Metagov site?  
That would mean something like:
polls.metagovernment.org - pollwiki (a concept I am still unclear on,  
as noted above)
project.metagovernment.org - the current metagov wiki
www.metagovernment.org - a small shell, with a simplistic home page,  
and links to the other two.
...perhaps. Does that seem right?

In detailing all of the above, I'm wondering if a live meeting  
wouldn't be a better venue for working some of this out. We haven't  
had one of those in a while anyway...
