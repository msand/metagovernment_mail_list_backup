Hi all,
while I was reading the reply about alpha users in this mailing list, I was 
thinking that maybe we should think about reaching for a larger community around 
Votorola.
I think the community already exist, but involves mainly technical people, 
not the usual people.
I think it is good to have a inner community of people working on the 
project, but it would be even better to have a larger community of people 
supporting (even if not directly involved  in) Votorola and the Metagovernment 
project as externals as this would help making more people knowing about the 
project (people speaking to other people by word of mouth / Facebook)
So, I propose the following: 
1)    build a Twitter account to Twitter the last updates about the Votorola project
2)    build a Facebook page for Votorola and/or for the whole Metagovernment 
community
3)    Make a Flash site for Votorola with a graphic presentation, to 
attract non-technical guys
4)    The whole Votorola site can be embedded in/be part of the new flash site 
5)    Have a banner exchange for Votorola with other sites, so that people 
can know about Votorola from other sites
 
In addition of course of having a forum site (in discussion in a separate 
thread).
 
I think a Twitter and Facebook page can be opened quite easily, I can also 
help in case we may want to build a good presentation site.
The only thing is that maybe it can be too early for this effort in reach a 
larger community, as we may want to have a beta version or a final version of 
Votorola available.
So we may decide to postpone this effort somewhere in the future.
 
What do you think?Also, shall the above be done for Votorola only or for the whole Metagovernment project? 
Matteo
