Alexander Praetorius wrote:
> +1000

If alpha users gave us the thumbs up in regard to a beta promotion,
then I'd say "Go for it!"  But we had 4 alpha users and none of them
advised us that we're ready for beta.  We only just completed the
design of the alpha prototype this month.  We can do about 3-4 months
of coding without any users at all.  It's actually faster that way and
will bring us a step closer to beta.  The main drawback is that
development will no longer be open to non-techs - Thomas excepted,
since he already owns pieces of the design - which exposes us to
design flaws on our blind side.

Experience teaches me (though I sometimes forget the lesson) that any
attempt to communicate technology via ideas, designs or other
generalities is a waste of time.  You said as much in one of your
earlier posts Alex, and I thought, "Wow!  This guy understands
engineering."  The only way to connect with non-technologists is via
hands on practice.
