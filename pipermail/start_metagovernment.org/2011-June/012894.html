> one of my main concerns, and one of the major design aims of the system. If
> that wasn't clear, I will need to do some more rewriting, and I could use
> your help in guiding me regarding what to write.)
>
> Regarding "decisions come from alliances," certainly in public voting
> systems, legislatures, bureaucracies, and corporations many decisions do
> involve alliances, and tribes. However, the same history that supports that
> view also supports the view that non humans and future generations and other
> entities that cannot directly join the alliances generally suffer when
> decisions come from alliances of tribes. Alliances of tribes tend to put
> their own interests ahead of the interests of those outside of the
> alliances. A "nobody objects" consensus system (such as the appraisal
> system) can much more reliably support the interests of non humans (through
> even only a few attuned humans). In such a system, decisions do not come
> from alliances, and protection of non humans does not depend in each
> particular decision on "the generosity of strangers."
>
> Regarding building tribes who share the value of a more harmonious and just
> relationship between humans and non-humans, yes! And also, I would hope and
> expect that such tribes will see the wisdom of having a social decision
> making system that can be trusted to uphold that value in every decision.
