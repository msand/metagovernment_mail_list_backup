Anthony,

 thank you for your email.

As for metagovernment.org, the wiki is waiting for some member to update the wiki software. The problem is not as much technical as it may seem. I would simply say that there is too little will to resurect the old wiki.

Imho the main problem is the policy of the wiki. Basically, it was easy to create new wiki page and list it on different wiki pages. On the other hand, editing an existing site was quite difficult, because consent was required. So the wiki was bloating with pages that have just a few editors.

As for the consent, there was one interesting remark, that decision-making in metagovernment should have differnt decision-making method from what we see as optimal in politics.

In other words metagovernment is different from politics in some important aspects 

- it is entirely online, but politics do not need to be (see deliberative democracy) 

- human needs are mostly absent in metagovernment (see J. W. Burton or Ian Shapiro)

- size / scale of groups is different

- metagovernment is full of educated people, nobody will tell you here: take my vote, use it as you like

- and more



So here we are. I will better start another email.

Michal
