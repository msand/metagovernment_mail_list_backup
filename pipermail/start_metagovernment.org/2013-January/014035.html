Hi, Zohar, and welcome.

Perhaps you can have a little flexibility here? ;) Many of the projects here are very committed to flexibility, but they are also being realistic in their approach: sometimes you need to develop something, and then worry about building it out.

With that said... Michael has not yet mentioned the Crossforum which is built to allow the ultimate flexibility. It allows users to participate (vote, discuss, etc.) where they want, when they want, and it aggregates that participation. You can get an introduction here:
http://metagovernment.org/wiki/Crossforum
and note that the black bar at the top of our site is a spinoff of the corssforum: allowing you to migrate between different systems (or it will: at the moment, only Votorola is enabled). Click the tiny triangle in the very upper-left of any page on the site.

As for anonymity, that has been a hotly-debated topic on this forum. There are many reasons for it and many against it. Here's part of a summary:
http://metagovernment.org/wiki/Anonymous_interactions_within_the_metagovernment

It is my understanding that several of the projects participating here allow anonymity as an option, or at least as something that an admin can hack on/off. (P.S. We've even looked into flexibility of administration itself, though it's a tricky idea: http://metagovernment.org/wiki/Distributed_Administration_Network .)
