> In my opinion, it's the responsibility of the system (or let's say
> challange), to figure out, whenever some kind of "micro consensus" is found
> by a "group of people > 1 person" and show it TO THEM, whenever they
> discuss, so that they "can grow their consensus".
>

> While I'm talking (I'm doing it right now), i discuss stuff and others
> agree, disagree or are indifferent towards what I say.
> There needs to be a method (e.g. select text + click agree/disagree) that
> everyone can use whenever he likes/dislikes a specific statement a lot. The
> system now gathers that around the current "branch of discussion" (however
> that is tracked)
> ... maybe through a "title" or maybe by me, selecting/quoting ANY
> STATEMENT OF YOU GUYS, and starting a discussion around it.
>
>
> This could even be combined with EMAIL, by having a "static screenshot" of
> the discussion from a web app that fulfills all requirements and when i
> click a button "reply", shown on that screenshot email, it opens the
> browser, i'm automatically logged in based on "get params" of that link in
> that email, and i can start discussing in that web app.
>
> Everything works via email or mobile notifications so I'm always reminded
> of what interests me.

>>>>> Hi All. I discussed the idea of a conference with Alessandro before.
>>>>> And just wanted to share with everybody how I think it is a great idea.
>>>>> Somehow we need a middle ground between general offline conferences that
>>>>> cost a lot of money (both to host and to attend) and mailing lists that
>>>>> risks being attended by few people.
>>>>>
>>>>> Something where each person is given half an hour of video
>>>>> presentation on google hangout. WHich thus has the possibility for few
>>>>> people to be present in hangout asking questions, and everybody else to be
>>>>> present on youtube. And the presentation is then stored for posterity.
>>>>> Something people prepare, but not so much like conferences.  Conferences
>>>>> those days are a bit a medium too slow for a world that progresses every
>>>>> day so rapidly. While we simply do not have the time to follow every
>>>>> conversation on a topic. So an online conference that happens anything
>>>>> between once every 2 months and once every 6 months sounds optimal. With
>>>>> the number of presentations to inversely proportional to how often the
>>>>> conference is (once every 6 months would make longer conferences).
>>>>>
>>>>> Personally I have results to present both in terms of Vilfredo, and in
>>>>> terms of my personal research. Although I will chose what to present to
