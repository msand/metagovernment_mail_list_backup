On Mon, Mar 2, 2015 at 3:08 PM, Reid Millerd <mrpdublin at gmail.com> wrote:

> It's a pretty straight forward process, but it's hard to put into a complete
> example because it moves in stages.

That seems to me to make it even more essential that you have a
demo/tutorial/walkthrough.

> Anyone can post a proposal for a change in the way the world works. Then
> anyone else can contribute to the idea with their own posts or by up voting
> any post they agree with.

No rating or flagging?  How is this any different from what Loomio provides?

> The original proposer can then adopt any post into
> their proposal by check marking it, indicating that the post be included in
> the final version of the proposal. Once significant input has been
> contributed the proposal can move to a voting stage where someone like a
> president will take all the adopted posts and highly up voted criticism
> posts and make a simplified version of what the whole idea will look like in
> an easily read paragraph or two for the general voter.

Seems like you've just introduced a SDAP at a key point in the
process.  What's to stop them from tweaking things to preserve their
power or the status quo?  Why isn't the original proposer responsible
for the revisions?

> I appreciate the irony don't worry. I think this is the most direct route
> though. If I'm elected I'll be able to release all the information myself to
> the site allowing people to run themselves. My task afterwards would just be
> as an active participant on the site, helping individuals legitimize their
> proposals. I'd also be redrafting proposals for the final vote initially but
> we'll be able to establish a more public option later. The opposition can
> say what they want but if I'm president ultimately what I say goes and I
> would be subject to the people. Initiating our direct democracy without
> opposition.

Right, the benevolent dictator model of government ;-)  What happens
when Stalin succeeds you?
