Greetings. 

This was sent to <contact at metagovernment.org>, which simply goes to me. I can not be said to be a “representative” of Metagovernment, since we are a diverse group. In fact, our function is pretty much the same as that of the new group you are thinking of forming. Couldn’t you just use the Metagov list as your point of communication? Doesn’t this sound similar to what you describe?
http://metagovernment.org/wiki/Main_Page <http://metagovernment.org/wiki/Main_Page>

The only difference I see between Metagov and what you describe is that sometimes we attempt to achieve consensus within our community. We often do not; but we do it largely as self-experimentation; using tools of member projects.

There are no “requirements” for joining our community, though you probably should not be pro-authoritarian, and I’d urge (though other members here would disagree) that you not be pro-representation.

Here’s how you join our list (and I urge you to do so whether or not you are forming another):
http://metagovernment.org/mailman/listinfo/start_metagovernment.org <http://metagovernment.org/mailman/listinfo/start_metagovernment.org>
