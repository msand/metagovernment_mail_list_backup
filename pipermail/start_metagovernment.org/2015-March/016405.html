It's a pretty straight forward process, but it's hard to put into a
complete example because it moves in stages.
Anyone can post a proposal for a change in the way the world works. Then
anyone else can contribute to the idea with their own posts or by up voting
any post they agree with. The original proposer can then adopt any post
into their proposal by check marking it, indicating that the post be
included in the final version of the proposal. Once significant input has
been contributed the proposal can move to a voting stage where someone like
a president will take all the adopted posts and highly up voted criticism
posts and make a simplified version of what the whole idea will look like
in an easily read paragraph or two for the general voter. Then all users
will be able to get a recap of the lengthy conversation broken down to be
able to effectively make an educated decision on which direction they
intend to vote. Proposals are then categorized as rejected or passed after
a period of voting.

I appreciate the irony don't worry. I think this is the most direct route
though. If I'm elected I'll be able to release all the information myself
to the site allowing people to run themselves. My task afterwards would
just be as an active participant on the site, helping individuals
legitimize their proposals. I'd also be redrafting proposals for the final
vote initially but we'll be able to establish a more public option later.
The opposition can say what they want but if I'm president ultimately what
I say goes and I would be subject to the people. Initiating our direct
democracy without opposition.
