True enough, I should work on one.

> No rating or flagging?  How is this any different from what Loomio
provides?

Rating is done on every post, the amount of up arrows a post gets (I call
it a prop) the quicker it rises on the feed and the higher the posters
overall rating is. A new post always starts on the top of the feed and then
drops down unless fueled by props. There is no down vote but there is a
report/flag button that can result in a suspension if a post receives many
reports/flags. There's also a search engine to look for any past posts.

> Seems like you've just introduced a SDAP at a key point in the
> process.  What's to stop them from tweaking things to preserve their
> power or the status quo?  Why isn't the original proposer responsible
>for the revisions?

Every ideas man isn't an writer. The original proposer has swayed to the
public opinion as best as they can, adding comments and tweaking their idea
anywhere that there was public support for, this is all done in an attempt
to compromise to best suit public opinion. I argue you then need a trusted
source to summarize all the points at the end of the discussion because an
original proposer would just summarize whatever would get them votes, they
wouldn't include the damning facts of the proposal. This is why you need an
outsider to break it down truthfully, initially I would do it, but in time
we would establish trusted individuals who always reported the stories with
accuracy, they would become like our trusted reporters and be compensated
for their work.

> Right, the benevolent dictator model of government ;-)  What happens
> when Stalin succeeds you?

The site is not at it's most capable yet, when all is done there will be no
need for a successor. It will all be run through an open source program
that regulates itself. Wired in to every interaction and transaction, it
would all be transparent. Improvements to the code would have to be checked
and approved by a super majority, otherwise they should never change. No
one need be in charge of the whole thing. We're all watching it equally.
