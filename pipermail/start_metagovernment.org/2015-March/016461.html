Thanks for the replies Scott.

> It's certainly less competitive and less potentially offensive than
> rating. Probably also less subject to trolls mucking things up by
> giving 5 star ratings to crap just to gum up the works (trolls will
> get flagged, not rated, in my system). Those are all good things if
> your FB or Slashdot and are more interested in collecting eyeballs
> than in collecting good comments. Our priorities are the opposite. Our
> goal must be to allow people to do what they need to do spending as
> *little* time on the system as possible, not try to suck them in and
> keep them as long as possible.

I feel 5 star rating is too subjective. A comment that people don't agree
with will be overwhelmingly rated a 1 and thus much less likely to be read.
Sometimes the unpopular opinion needs to be said though and I don't think
should be labeled as bad just because people don't agree with it. In my
system the controversial posts will get equal exposure as the highly rated
posts (the ones with most props). Each time a comment gets a reply it's
bumped to the top of the feed, however the comment falls down the feed if
it's not supported by props, This gives us a mixture of both types, and
helps highlight the best debates which will fuel conversation. The final
version of a proposal should be brief but the discussion beforehand should
be engaging. Keeping comment limited to just props won't rank the comments
in any order of goodness, it instead helps them all fluctuate which adds
variety. Only at the end will the highest rated comments be ranked, at this
time it will be proven that this comment speaks to a lot of people and
should be taken into consideration.

> Again, I think the cure is worse than the disease here: Any time you
> introduce some "elite group" directly into the path of the legislative
> process, to me that says "SDAP control". We certainly don't want to
> require everyone to read and rate every proposal or comment (my
> proposal is to use sampling for the latter), but if I've written a
> proposal that will likely be approved and will be effective (because I
> know something about social engineering or because someone who does
> has reviewed and endorsed it), the *last* thing I want is some SDAP
> "professional" dicking around with it just prior to the voting phase.

But if this "professional" was approved time and time again by the people
then it wouldn't be an SDAP. Each proposal gets redrafted by a bunch of
"professionals" and the most accurate one gets chosen by the participants
of the discussion. As long as the re-drafter included the key points the
participants would give that individual a good rating and a trust system
would be established.
