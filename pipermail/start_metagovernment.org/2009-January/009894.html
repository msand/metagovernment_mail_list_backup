> It's only his first day in office, but President Obama has already signaled
> a serious commitment to transparency and accountability in government. The
> President ordered federal agencies in a memorandum released today to
> approach the Freedom of Information Act (FOIA) "with a clear presumption: in
> the face of doubt, openness prevails."
>
> This message is in line with advice EFF and other nonprofits gave the Obama
> Transition Team on transparency issues shortly after the election.
>
> According to Obama's memo:
>
> All agencies should adopt a presumption in favor of disclosure, in order to
> renew their commitment to the principles embodied in FOIA, and to usher in a
> new era of open Government. The presumption of disclosure should be applied
> to all decisions involving FOIA.
