On Jul 28, 2008, at 2:46 PM, Manuel Barkhau wrote:

> Every change to some piece will have a revision number, by virtue of
> being in a repository. If in a discussion you want to talk about a
> change made to some legislation, you can use a revision tag. If
> however, you want to refer to a piece of legislation rather than any
> particular revision, you would use the legislation tag.

So imagine someone makes a resolution which proposes that a bridge  
should be repaired. Then someone else proposes that the bridge be torn  
down, and someone else proposes that it be replaced. Would the latter  
two just be revisions of the first?

In other words, at what point do you draw the line between revision  
and separate legislation?
