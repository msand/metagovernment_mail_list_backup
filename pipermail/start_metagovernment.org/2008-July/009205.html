On Jul 23, 2008, at 4:10 PM, Paul Codd MacDonald wrote:

> The owner of the algorithm is in a very powerful position indeed  
> (see Google) and we should be conscious to make it a community  
> controlled feature.
> IMO we need to strongly differentiate between merit accumulated by  
> content and merit accumulated by contributors. The maths and method  
> of accumulation could be very different for each. Some communities  
> may decide to only accumulate merit on one and not the other.
>
> As a general point I think there is no way we can know the right  
> answers at this stage. We need to initiate experiments we can learn  
> from. These experiments don't have to work, just enlighten us a  
> little further. I don't think we should rule anything out at this  
> early stage

I very much agree with all three of the points you are making here.  
Scoring factors should be:
- variable by community
- controlled by the community
- optional

We don't know what is really going to happen when the code encounters  
the general public, but I think it is prudent to at least allow for  
scoring as much as possible, so that communities can experiment with  
it and see what ends up working best.
