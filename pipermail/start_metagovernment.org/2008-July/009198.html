On Jul 23, 2008, at 11:44 AM, Aur Saraf wrote:

> On Wed, Jul 23, 2008 at 6:37 PM, Ed Pastore <epastore at metagovernment.org 
> > wrote:
>> This is why I had proposed a *recursive* scoring system, where
>> voluminous posting might barely increment your score, but where a
>> small amount of *good* posting could substantially increase your  
>> score.
>
>
> On Jul 23, 2008, at 6:33 AM, Aur Saraf wrote:
> The only way to eliminate populist advantage is to eliminate user
> scoring, don't you agree?

Not with recursive scoring, no. Getting scored by a huge number of  
people with average scores does very little to one's own score.  
Getting scored by a small number of people with high scores  
significantly effects one's score (positively or negatively).

That's not populism at all. But I would suggest that it is also not  
elitism: because of the community structure. If each user has a unique  
score within each community, then a high score in any particular  
community isn't saying "I am better than you and deserve more power  
than you;" instead it is saying "I have done more to contribute to  
this community, and other people in this community respect those  
efforts and give me more credence in regard to future issues."
