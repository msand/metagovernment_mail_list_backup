Hi Marc, (again -- fourth response to same email)

For Reference:

    * (CV) = the abstract "Count Votes" decision rule
    * (NUO) = the abstract "No Unresolved Objections" decision rule

------------------------------------------------------------------------

    Marc> From my point of view there are two serious differences; a
    qualitative and a quantitative one:

        * (1) While (NUO) focuses on equity, (CV) strives for majority.

    Ned> I think I may agree that "(NUO) focuses on equity", but I would
    need to know more about what you mean by "equity" to know for sure.

    Marc> Bingo! IMHO this is one of the elementary questions! What is
    "equity"? To be honest; I can't tell you! But, I believe that we all
    together as a society need to define what we would like the answer
    should be...

        ... for me this includes ethical and morally guidelines of how
        to reach a *wise decision* that respects (nearly) all facets of
        the domain where the decision is to be made.

Ned> OK. Now I am closer to understanding what you mean by "equity", but 
not closer to understanding why you use that term.

Would it be fair for me to restate what you said above thusly?

    * (NUO) focuses on making wise decisions.
    * (CV) focuses on making popular decisions.

If this restatement is not a fair restatement of your meaning, I would 
be interested to learn why not. (That might help me understand why you 
use the term "equity".)

And also, I heartily agree with the distinction as I have stated it:

    * (NUO) more reliably produces wise decisions, that are sometimes
      popular.
    * (CV) always produces popular decisions, that are sometimes (I
      would say seldom) wise.

Since I am interested in having a global democratic decision system that 
reliably produces wise decisions, and don't really care whether the 
decisions are vote-count popular or not, I much prefer (NUO) to (CV).
