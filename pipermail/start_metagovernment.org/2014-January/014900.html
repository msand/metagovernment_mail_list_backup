The trouble we had back then is that you've "no facility to express
agreement at the level of whole issues, such as texts (macro-voting)."
https://service.piratenpartei.de/pipermail/ag-meinungsfindungstool/2013-April/002220.html

So if Votorola were to cooperate with AG-MFT, we'd have to design and
implement a macro-voting plug-in framework by ourselves.  This would
be risky, because there might never be another facility to "plug in"
to that framework.

Last we spoke, you were going to look at reducing the risk by finding
a partner for us, somebody to cooperate with; perhaps a LiquidFeedback
developer (though LiquidFeedback is more for decision making than
discussion purposes).

But that's a difficult job.  Never before have two voting projects
cooperated in order to give users a fair choice between their tools.
We've been trying to find a willing project for years.  We're still
trying, but AFAIK only Votorola is willing.  (It gives us big heads,
thinking we're the only good guys in the world of voting technology.
Or thinking others are just scared of competing against us. ;^)
