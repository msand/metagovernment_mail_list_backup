Hi Max,

As you know, when philosophers such as Rawls and Habermas use the term 
"rational discourse", they actually mean something special by it.

    Rawls> Our exercise of political power is fully proper only when it
    is exercised in accordance with a constitution, the essentials of
    which all citizens as free and equal may be reasonably expected to
    endorse in the light of principles and ideals acceptable to their
    common human reason -- Political Liberalism, p.137

After Mike turned me on to Habermas a while ago, I started finding the 
term popping up in my own writing.

But then last summer at a certain point, Stephen gently took me aside 
and apprised me of his observation that "rational discourse sounds like 
something a bunch of old-white-male-philoshphy professors do for fun on 
a Friday night while sipping Sherry and smoking cigars." Since then, I 
have been attempting to discretely distance myself from the term. 
(Nothing personal, just business -- some of my best friends are rational 
...).

So anyway, should I take your remarks to be actionable in some way?

    7. The two stones I wish to place in the mosaic matrix is a
