> Hi Marc,
>
> You are indeed asking a right question. In this thread, I cast "the
> question" as that of choosing between two abstract decision rules. I did
> that to try to clarify that there really are two distinct abstract
> rules, that really are fundamentally different, and that we really are
> choosing between them when we choose a particular decision system. (And,
> I do indeed think that, abstractly, (NUO) is "better" than (CV).)
>
> But the truth is, both God and the Devil are always in the details. It
> is possible to make really bad instantiations of either abstract rule.
> So, in responding to your query about my actual aims, I will abandon
> characterizing the choice as being a "pure" choice between abstract
> rules, and instead say this:
>
>      
> ------------------------------------------------------------------------
>    * There are lots of public matters that are global and
>      multi-generational in scope: that affect humanity, nature, and
>      future generations. (All of the decisions over decades and across
>      continents that led to the Fukushima disaster are a case in point.)
>      
> ------------------------------------------------------------------------
>    * I suggest that this reality entails that we need a single global
>      grassroot democratic decision system. (Not a collection of
>      independent systems that cannot work together as one system to
>      produce decisions dealing with public matters that affect
>      humanity, nature, and future generations.)
>      
> ------------------------------------------------------------------------
>    * I suggest that we do not have the resources to independently
>      develop, demonstrate, and deploy multiple possible global decision
>      systems that cannot work together as one system. We need to
>      collaborate, and focus our efforts, if we wish to succeed ("save
>      the world") in a timely fashion.
>      
> ------------------------------------------------------------------------
>    * In the "consensus through synthesis" design phase (which is mostly
>      where we are now), I think that we should consider all available
>      designs and design ideas and decision rules and such, and compare
>      and contrast and combine and improve them as best we can to
>      create/select the single best design that we can come up with, to
>      serve as starting point.
>      
> ------------------------------------------------------------------------
>    * I think that we should then collaborate and focus on developing,
>      demonstrating, and deploying that single best global-scale
>      democratic decision system design (which will of course undergo
>      further changes all along the way).
>      
> ------------------------------------------------------------------------
>    * My initial contribution to the design phase effort is Blinap.
>      Blinap does use (NUO), and does not use (CV), but that is only one
>      of many factors that (in my view) make it "better" than other
>      designs that are currently floating around.
>
> ------------------------------------------------------------------------
>
