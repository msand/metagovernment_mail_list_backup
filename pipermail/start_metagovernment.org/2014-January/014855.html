> People,
>
> I decided to implement yet another test decision attempt.
>
> Someone mentioned that the mailing list member list has about 200 names.
> This then suggests an upper bound on the size of MG as a community.  Since
> typically being a member of a mailing list does not imply receiving mail,
> reading mail, or responding to mail, the actual size of the MG community,
> depending on definitions, is likely to be smaller.
>
> Polls and surveys are an important part of the political process around
> me.  If I sent a survey out to the names on the mailing list, simply the
> number of responses would be interesting.
>
> It appears that survey monkey will help do surveys for free as long as the
> number of responses is limited to 100.  The survey can have up to ten
> questions.  The survey monkey site says they are good people with respect
> to confidentiality. ?
>
> So here is the decision process.
>
> The admin of the shared resource, the mailing list, says he will cooperate
> under some sort of protocol.
>
> I say that I am enthusiastic and will spend the rest of this month making
> this work.
>
> Five or more MG members submit one or more questions in a format and
> content and size that I approve. My decisions here will be transparent.  My
> inclinations are to short likker approval questions.
>
> No one says I will do whatever I can to stop this survey.
>
> I would think it too obvious to say but notice there was a statement of
> leadership, resource controller approval, participation commitment, and no
> one is willing to commit to war as a decision criteria.
>
> The anticipated consequence is we know how many responses we get.  We may
> have useful reports on responses to up to ten questions. The maybe
> unanticipated consequences might be privacy violations and offensive
> stupidity on my or your part.
>
> So continuing,
>
> We have a leadership commitment.  Who might want this survey to happen?
