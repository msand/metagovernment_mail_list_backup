Hi Ed, Mark, and All, (Happy New Year!)

At this point we (various members of the group) do not share a vision of 
what collaborative governance would best look like. I see that as 
potentially a tremendous opportunity. We have the opportunity to 
collaborate in clarifying and working through our differences, with the 
objective to arrive at a synthesis design that we can all wholeheartedly 
support.

Variety of views at the outset is good because it can enrich and improve 
the eventual synthesis solution. Variety of views is not good as an end 
(in our situation), because it scatters our meager resources across more 
projects than those meager resources can support, with the result that 
none of our projects thrive. If we indeed think that collaboration is 
good and synthesis is good, then it behooves us to collaborate and 
synthesize.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

When you (Ed) speak of "coming up with a somewhat more formal internal 
governance structure" for the group, it is not clear to me that the 
group needs an internal governance structure. At this point, we each are 
bringing our own personal resources to the party, and each personally 
control and direct those personal resources. I do not quite see what 
there is to "govern" that would need a governance "structure".

Instead what I see is that we very much need a way that we all support 
to make group decisions, one by one. (I would not even say "binding" 
group decisions -- we are all in fact utterly autonomous with respect to 
all group matters, which I take to be a good thing.)

The decision system would have a structure, and the decisions made might 
sometimes have to do with governance of the group in some sense, but the 
decision system itself would just produce decisions, not govern. The 
term "governance structure" for me harks back to the bad old days when 
there used to be a distinction between governors and governed.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

The blog model suffers from the problems that are discussed in the most 
excellent TruthMapping video:
http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=T8XgPDs_pHc&feature=youtu.be

My thought is that we would be better off focusing our efforts on 
proposing and upon making decisions with respect to those proposals, 
with all discussion serving those ends. The world (in my view) has 
enough "thought for the day" blogs. It needs more good proposals, and 
more good decisions.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

At this point, I would see "speaking on behalf of the group" as 
problematic. We share an intense interest in "Metagovernment" issues. 
But our views about those issues are all over the map.

Which brings me back to the suggestion that we focus our efforts on 
proposing and upon making decisions with respect to those proposals. As 
our portfolio of decisions-made grew, there would be more and more to 
speak about "on behalf of the group".

------------------------------------------------------------------------
