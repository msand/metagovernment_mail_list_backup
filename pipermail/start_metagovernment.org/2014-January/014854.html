Sounds like fun -- I am looking forward to seeing the results. Here are 
my ten candidate survey items. (Do with them as you will -- they belong 
to you now.)

      ------------------------------------------------------------------------
   1. Definition: Proposal Filter

      A "proposal filter" is a conception of some sort that proposals
      must successfully "pass through" in order to be eligible to be
      implemented. (Examples to follow ...)

      Once successfully proposed to a particular public decision system,
      a proposal filter can function as the basis for objecting to
      subsequent particular proposals made to that decision system.

      Is this definition/explanation clear enough?

      (WPP) "If an action or policy has a suspected risk of causing harm
      to the public or to the environment, in the absence of scientific
      consensus that the action or policy is harmful, the burden of
      proof that it is NOT harmful falls on those proposing the policy
      or action."

      Would you be willing to accept the Precautionary Principle as a

      original proposal has not successfully passed."

      Would you be willing to accept (BCF) as a valid proposal filter?
