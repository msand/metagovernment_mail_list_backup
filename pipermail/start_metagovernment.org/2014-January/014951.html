Reid,

There were three threads on my intent:

Free and Open Source Licensing (FOSS)

Security of communications.  Complex subject but anything vaguely
effective  is always under effective restriction in the US.  For the formal
statutory issues, there was enough there for you to start some research.

Unattended install.  Again Google stuff like pxe and openboot.

Here is how it might look to you.

Stick in  a usb stick into a particular kind of readily available
computer.  Do a special boot from your friendly and trusted DHCP server.
Eventually, without touching your hard drive, a relatively secure web
browser comes up defaulting to some site. After a period of inactivity, the
computer shuts down and you pocket the stick. Next time you boot from the
stick.

This as described is effectively useless for casual consumer use because
typical commercial Web sites will not tolerate a somewhat secure browser.
