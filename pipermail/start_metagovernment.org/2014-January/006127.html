So, (unless/until you suggest otherwise), my plan is to start with the

    Gustavo> Not sure I will be helpful here, but I think Ned is really
    trying to figure out how to not solve problems through voting and
    hierarchical structures. And that his main problem is with issues
    related to tyranny of the majority.

Ned> When I first started working on the project that has led to 
Isonomea and Blinap, my thinking was pretty much entirely "in the box". 
At the start, I viewed the project as being about writing planks for a 
new political party. In fact, the original name for the project was "The 
Meek Party Platform".

During the first three years of the project, I assumed without question 
that voting could be made to "work", and that the problem was just a 
"tuneup" problem -- a matter of finding just the right tweaks.

But what I discovered in the process of trying to tweak is that there 
are problems intrinsic to all "Count Votes" systems that no amount of 
tweaking can resolve, or even mitigate. I discovered what Alexander 
Tyler among many others had discovered long before me: "Count Votes" 
systems cannot be used to produce sustainable democracy. This was not 
what I had expected to find.

However, once I had "grokked in fullness" that "Count Votes" could not 
serve as the basis for sustainable democracy, I then turned to the task 
of trying to find/formulate a method of public-matter decision making 
that WOULD be able to serve as the basis for sustainable democracy, at 
the global scale.
