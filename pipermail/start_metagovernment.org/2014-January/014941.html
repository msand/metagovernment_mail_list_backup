Hi Ed,

I used to take your own participation for granted.  But that's a
mistake, of course.  So let me ask, what would be your own answer?
What would send you packing if you didn't find it here?

> ... How about if we start trying out other systems for set periods
> of time [?] ...

Speaking for myself, the first thing I'd need to know is the purpose
of these try-outs.  It sounds to me like we'd be pushing toward a goal
(which is good).  So my first question would be, What goal precisely?

Maybe it's a goal I share.  Then I'd want to know who else shares it;
how clearly we articulate it; and generally whether we (together) are
capable of making progress toward it.  Then I'd decide whether to
participate, bearing in mind that I'd be expected to shoulder a bit of
the work even when my own interests weren't directly at stake.
Finally, I'd lend a hand in the critique of your try-out proposal, so
together we could judge whether or not it would help the goal.

Here I reiterate my own answer to the title question at some length,
not because I think my personal participation is especially important
to the group, but only to shed light on the meaning of the question.
The question again is:

> > What would it take for you to participate in Metagov even if it
> > did *not* have a mailing list attached?  What would you require in
> > order to participate even when it was not directly in your own
> > interest, but rather for the sake of the group?

Mike
