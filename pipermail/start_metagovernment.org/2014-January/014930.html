What happens next is that you (the only participant of the community agreeing on your proposal) can go ahead and start the nonprofit.

That will change one we have a charter; but asked what happens before there is a charter. Once there is a charter, then if, for example, we use a decision-rule similar to the one used for the Metagov wiki, then it will require a minimum of three people to make any binding decision, and the agreement among all voting parties must hold for at least a week (or more). If a fourth person jumps in and disagrees, then we start over.

Also I should note that the nonprofit I hope to build has no intention of affecting legislation. I don't care what current governments do. They are the antiquated systems we intend to push out of the way as we present better, more effective, more democratic community institutions.
