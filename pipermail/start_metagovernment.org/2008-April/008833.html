On 13/04/2008, Jacki Buros <jburos at gmail.com> wrote:
>  Particularly, here:
>        "The version control system gives the project a way to undo the
>  effects of bad or hasty judgement."
>
>  We should discuss how such a system might 'undo' the results of bad or
>  damaging legislation, since having continuous modification of many
>  bills (taxes, fiscal budgets, unlawful actions, etc) could create
>  havoc.

I'm not quite sure what Karl is referring to here is necessarily
relevant to us. "bad or hasty judgement" refers to a patch that
was sent to the central repository, by a trusted member of the
community, only it turned out he had a bad day. If we set this up
correctly, we won't have this problem, since bad changes wont ever
arrive in the repository. Only changes which are obviously accepted
will ever make it into the repository. Granted, existing legislation
will want to be revised, but that isn't something you can simply
undo in the way Karl is referring to. You have to offer an
alternative in most cases, in the form of a patch to the legislation.

By patch I mean a file that will be applied to the repository and modify
its contents. Not a minor edit, to existing legislation. A patch could
also take a form like this.

# Replacement of absurdly complicated archaic tax code.
@@ 0-100 @@
+ new simple legislation
@@ 0-5000 @@
- 100 pages of bad legislation
