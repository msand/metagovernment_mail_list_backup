Huh? This was an eye opener for me Manuel - thanks.
I suggest that we go down a database agnostic route as other projects such as Drupal.org have done. They created a database abstraction layer which means that drupal runs mysql and postgreSQL out of the box, and theoretically it is easy to build/tweak abstraction layers to cope with any RDBMS. This agnostic option would give us greater flexibility, no reliance on one project, and possibly greater reach and take-up.
It would mean a little bit of extra work getting started tho. What does everyone think?
