On Sat, Apr 12, 2008 at 7:40 PM, Ed Pastore <epastore at metagovernment.org>
wrote:

> Project governance
> - How to manage this group and other initial projects before Metascore.
> - How projects will be administered in the future (we will always need
> SourceForge administrators, I.T. administrators, etc.). Can
> bureaucratic tasks such as I.T. administration be run in the context
> of Metascore, or will we still need individuals to be in charge? If
> the latter, how do we control for corruption in those individuals?
>
> Non-Profit Foundations
> - Which formal organizations need to be formed? This will be relevant
> when we want to take monetary donations. Should Metagovernment and
> Metascore have separate organizations...?
> - What nation to form in? Looking for the best host for our
> organizations. I am based in the United States and doubt that it is
> our best choice.
> - What kind of entity to form as (depends on the above).
>
> Software
> - Basic form of the software - What is the interface and basic
> presentation?
> - Develop the math - Must provide balance, and must control for
> negatives (one major negative remains unresolved: how to control the
> formation of cliques, where a group of people all collaborate to give
> each other good scores, thus gaming the system)
> - Codebase - Can we adapt existing software (perhaps Vanilla BB)? If
> not, what language/database (etc.) to use?
> - Failsafes - How to deal with hackers, malicious ISPs, and other
> technological threats to the underlying systems?
> - Project plan - Develop timeline and give assignments.
>


Building the software on top of a distributed version control system, I
think, would solve many of those questions, like the IT governance and the
failsafes.

I'm against starting with a framework that gives an interface and
implementing the guts on top of it (vanilla). I'd rather start from a
framework that would give powerful guts (a DVCS system?) and build an
interface - the easier part - on top of it.
