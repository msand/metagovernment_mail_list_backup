I think you're on to something here. Let's use a rather literal  
definition and say a constitution is simply the definition of what  
*constitutes* a community. So the constitution for a hospital might  
consist of the people who work at that hospital.

Since anyone in the world can contribute to any government, then a  
constitution would also provide a score multiplier to anyone it  
defines as being directly a member of its community. In other words,  
anyone could participate in a hospital's government, but staff would  
have much more say in that government.

If the community so desires, their constitution can also give  
different multipliers to different groups of people. A hospital may  
wish to give doctors an extra large multiplier, and it may also wish  
to grant a small multiplier to any patient; since even though they are  
not staff, patients are a part of the community.

As for the software providing for the creating of new governments, it  
might be as simple as tagging. Think of all communities as existing on  
one combined platform, and individual constitutions, laws,  
resolutions, and discussions are assigned to a particular community by  
a key-tag.
