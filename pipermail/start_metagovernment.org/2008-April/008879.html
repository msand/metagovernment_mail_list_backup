Another proposal I would make is related to the problem of access -- 
most people have to pay for internet access, which if this project 
succeeds would unnecessarily limit citizen access to the government. I 
would propose that a component of the non-profit funds or community 
funds should go to support public-access portals like those available in 
public libraries and coffee shops throughout communities to provide free 
access to the metagovernment site. They could be restricted to provide 
access *only* to the metagovernment site (to limit cost), or perhaps we 
could negotiate with current ISPs such as verizon or t-mobile to not 
charge for access to our site.

This is a cheaper alternative to the typical representative system where 
enormous amounts of time and money are spent on campaigning, etc though 
the most important issues are rarely engaged.

Thoughts?
