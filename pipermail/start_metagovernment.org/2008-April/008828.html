On Apr 13, 2008, at 2:40 PM, Jacki Buros wrote:
> Question: If we're not 'voting' -- ie, if there is no tally of whether
> a person agrees or dissents, how do we know when consensus is reached?

These were the ideas behind implementing a complex scoring system, as  
roughly defined at http://www.metagovernment.org/#How_it_works and
http://www.metagovernment.org/#Scoring_system

The idea of a Consensus Score is to have a mathematical equation that  
would account for the size of the community, the amount of activity in  
the community, and the scores of the people supporting a particular  
resolution. It's tough math, but I think it may be possible.

> Does one have to propose an alternative in order to dissent?
>
> And, if only 1 person of 2 million proposes an alternative, and all
> others agree with the first proposal, is consensus reached?

This is where the synthesis scores come in. The purpose there is to  
enable a new idea to flourish if people believe that it synthesizes  
other proposals into a better solution.
