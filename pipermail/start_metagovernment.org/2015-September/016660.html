>> 1) Should abortion be legal?
> => everyone decides for himself, because it's affecting nobody else

The standard libertarian answer. To which I respond:
OK, so if a woman decides she wants to abort a perfectly healthy 9
month old fetus because she's decided she would rather party than take
care of a child, we're supposed to be OK with that? So what if she
considers that fetus to be something less than a person, it doesn't
affect you, right? Unfortunately surveys show that very few of us are
OK with this, and the few that say it's still OK are actually just
fighting against some mythical slippery slope that any restriction on
abortion will just lead to more restrictions.

What if that women considers black people, or Germans, or some other
class of people to be something less than a person? Does she get to
own slaves? Commit genocide?

>> 2) Should same-sex marriages be sanctioned by The State?
>
> => everyone decides for himself, because it's affecting nobody else

So if they want to raise pit-bulls and fight them, that's no one's
business too, right?  Dogs are not people, so we shouldn't be able to
infringe on anyone's rights to do whatever they want with them (hey,
fight them, marry them, whatever they want!)

This kind of superficial understanding of what morals are and why we
humans have a moral systems in the first place is unfortunately
characteristic of libertarianism, a philosophy I really want to like,
but simply can't because it's so underdeveloped. You might benefit
from actually reading matchism.org. Even if you don't accept all of it
(and I've actually yet to receive any substantive criticism about any
flaws in it), at least it will prepare you to debate people who
actually have thought about the issue rather than just having adopted
an ideology without ever having examined the ramifications.
