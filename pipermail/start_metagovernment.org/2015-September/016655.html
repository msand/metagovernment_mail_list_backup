i agree with "minimum hierarchy".

For now, all "discussing & voting, etc..." tools and ideas have failed to
convince me.
There needs to be actions every single day and they shape reality while
others are busy discussing and voting around.
The FORCE that shapes reality every day is what people do every day, which
is WORK
WORK is directed by who pays and even though the government is spending
some "tax money", the vast majority of money comes from
economic programs "sponsored" by loans given by private banks.

Spending money is VOTING on what people do every day and thus how they
shape REALITY.
This "thing" we call ECONOMY and it's already "GRASS ROOTS", because
everyone is involved on a daily basis.

And YES, you can argue the economy and the way money works is not fair and
not everyone has the same voting power and a lot more can be said about
issues with that system, BUT,
despite that, it works already on a daily basis and it's grass roots in the
sense that everyone can participate all the time.

So if everyone was literate and would know programming, we could all
happiely discuss how we shape our money systems (using custom block chain
technology for replacing what we currently have - by building superior
systems and advertising their usage to people - which will vote with THEIR
MONEY and buy into a better alternative)

...but we don't.
I lost all hope discussing some fairytale stories of how i can delegate
votes or give some points on some proposals that might go to arbitrary
processes to reach some consensus and then nothing happens. Who will
implement? will we advertise those new methods to existing government,
which will then let go of their power and implement the new things... and
maybe in some decades or generations we vote maybe twice a year and on some
issues directly... which is still far from the "GRASS ROOTS EVERY DAY
THING" that we ALREADY have when looking at the real democracy, which is
the economy and "voting with money". (For example - I'm from germany and we
don't even have the right to vote on issues on a country wide level - so
when greece had a "referendum" to vote for or against a certain policy -
this doesnt exist in germany. But check your calendar, it's 2015 already -
and still nothing)

*I'm sick of this waiting process. Power belongs to the people and it has
to be taken. You don't beg for something that is already yours.*
The way to go is to build superior alternatives that make the current
system obsolete by proofing that there is more efficiency and more wealth
for everyone.
If this is shown, people will join you happily and the ones under pressure
now will be the first ones to go with a superior alternative.


I guess my point of view is to radical for most people, but that's fine -
what can i do...
