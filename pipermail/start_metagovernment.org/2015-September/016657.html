> 4) Can my neighbor build a skyscraper on his lot and blot out the sun
> that falls on mine?
>

=> Interesting question - I have some thoughts about it, but I think it
doesnt make sense to tell them now.
Instead:
How is this currently being solved?
I live in germany and I experienced a lot of times in my life, how some
"public projects" created new buildings, that made
the situation for a lot of people really really shitty. The ones i
personally experienced was:
=> expanding the airport in "Frankfurt am Main" which made the southern
regions so loud, many people got into serious health problems and the value
of their living decreased a lot.

This is the reality everywhere - in germany strong laws exist that make
those things hard to happen, but they still do.
In the vast majority of countries, governments don't give a shit about it
and follow the money anyway.



*Imagine the following:*1. Government or Major (corrupted or not) decides
to allow a certain skyskraper/nuclear powerplant/airport/... to be build in
the immediate neighbourhood of several thousand people.
Once that becomes "law", the police will use force to make it happen.
*OR*
2. Someone buys the land and wants to build something that would affect
thousands of people living there which can then pool all their resources to
make life hell for the person who wants to build.
- Maybe people in general are allowed to buy shares of anything surrounding
them all the time and can block certain decisions if they want to have
certain guarantees
- Maybe people just re-arrange all the "supply routes" to make it
economically un-feasible to do something
- Maybe they don't care and just move
- Maybe some people even like a certain project

What is the right strategy depends a lot on "contract law", which for
example would be TRANSPARENT to everyone, because it would be "BLOCKCHAIN
BASED" (see projects like https://www.ethereum.org/ )

Sure, sometimes things can go wrong, but they already do - in what I
propose, people can learn and immediately learn and check all "contracts"
that exist on the "Blockchain" which would affect some decision you take
(e.g. buying a house in a certain location and move in with your family).
You can check and follow the feed of "contracts" and "contract updates"
surrounding "your life" :-) And discuss with the community surrounding your
life and create contracts with them to prevent certain scenarios to happen
that you would not like to see.
