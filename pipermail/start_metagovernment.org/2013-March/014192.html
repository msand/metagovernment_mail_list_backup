Well, it was no loss.  As a dress rehearsal, I think it was a success.
Thanks to everyone who helped, we now have:

  1. Metagov plus 4 technical providers signed on to the idea:
     http://metagovernment.org/wiki/Knight_News_Challenge/Free-range_voting#Who_are_you.3F
     The latest providers are InterMix (thanks Roger) and Agreedis
     (thanks Steven).

     We had zero before, so this is an improvement.

  2. Exposure and potentially useful contacts.  We discovered that
     EveryVote (Mitch Downey and Vincent Schramer) are concerned with
     the same kind of monopoly problem.  Again they're uncommitted to
     the particular solution we're proposing, but not unfriendly.
     http://www.everyvote.org/about/goals/ (see also their cartoon
     video with Lincoln and Washington)

     One of the projects that made the cut (the only primary voting
     project) is Civic IQ.  Robert Girling (founder and designer)
     replies positively to the idea, though he's uncommitted.
     https://www.newschallenge.org/open/open-government/submission/civic-iq-a-digital-platform-for-creative-collaboration-and-civic-engagement-#c-ef3716c13735f797e27d9d1bb3f2bfdd

     Other promising contacts, too.

  3. A list of 43 providers who are more-or-less eligible to sign on:
     http://zelea.com/w/Stuff:List_of_primary_voting_projects

     So now we know who we are, and how many.

  4. The text of the submission.
      http://metagovernment.org/wiki/Knight_News_Challenge/Free-range_voting

     So next time we won't have to start from scratch.

We don't necessarily need funding to get started.  Any money that's
required will materialize once we have a basic inter-network running.
We know that each of the 43 projects on the list (if it survives) will
eventually be joining us; we can't see how a project could possibly
survive outside an inter-network.  And we know that multiple
inter-networks cannot be isolated from each other (effectively they
are one, which is the whole idea).  So although we don't have Knight
on board yet, we have time on our side. :-)
