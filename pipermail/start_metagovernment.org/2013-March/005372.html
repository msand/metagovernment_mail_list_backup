Dear News Challenge Applicant,

Thanks so much for submitting your idea to the [1]Knight News Challenge (and
thanks to those of you who had to suffer through our server issues - we
appreciate your enthusiasm & patience).

We wanted to share some information on what happens next.

Feedback, through March 29.
We’re asking anyone interested in this topic to comment on and ask questions
about the submissions. Check out the feedback and, if you want, edit your
submission. You’ll get an e-mail notification any time someone comments on your
entry or replies to a comment of yours. (Side note: neither ‘applause’ nor view
count is a factor we use in reviewing submissions.) Next week, we’ll huddle with
[2]the eight readers who are helping us by reading submissions and engaging on
the site. By the end of that week, we’ll have a set of semifinalists who will
enter the Refinement stage.

Refinement, March 29 - April 5.
On March 29, we’ll announce which applications will move on to the Refinement
stage as semifinalists. We expect there to be about 50 projects in this group.
During refinement, we’ll ask semifinalists for more detailed information, for a
budget, and to describe their projects in a 30-second video.

Evaluation, April 5 - June 10.
On April 16, we’re gathering a group of advisers to help us narrow the 50
semifinalists to a smaller group of finalists. This is a process we’ve done
since the start of the News Challenge, and following it we’ll interview the
finalists and recommend to our trustees a group of winners. This part of the
process will not be public.

Announcement, June 24.
We will announce the winners at the MIT Knight Civic Media conference.

You can reach us anytime at [3]cksopher at knightfoundation.org or on Twitter
[4]@cksopher or [5]@heychrisbarr.

Cheers, Chris Barr & Chris Sopher
