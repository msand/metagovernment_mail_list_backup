True, that wouldn't be primary voting.  The definition is here:
http://zelea.com/w/Stuff:List_of_primary_voting_projects

It's just the petitions I was looking at: http://theciviccommons.com/

I guess Civic Commons is trying to put healthy discussion in front of
the petitioning process.  Petitions alone aren't conducive to healthy
discussion, of course; and without that they haven't much legitimacy.
(Nor have the results of any vote.)  So here the voting system needs
the support of the discussion system.

We don't often think how the reverse might be true.  But imagine the
voting system one day said "Thank you ma'am!" and turned around to
support to the discussion system.  The two are going to be married
anyway, so why shouldn't they get along?

That's why I keep this list of primary voting projects.  I think
something's going to happen here in the not-too-distant future.
Although we don't have all the moves down yet, we're trying hard to
reach out to you D&D folks.  When we succeed, I think you'll see that
you need us just as badly as we need you.
