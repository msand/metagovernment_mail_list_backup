If government is "that which guides", or "that which controls", then I
agree 100% that The Government ought not to govern.  The citizens
ought to govern.  The Government is a big, dumb and powerful machine
that needs guiding and controlling by the citizens.  So here I agree.

But don't we still need the machine for action?  Even in tiny Metagov,
we still need administrators for the mailing list, and the wiki.

I don't think of adminstration as being "of, by, and for the people".
I really do picture the Government (the ultimate in adminstration) as
a big, dumb and powerful machine (a steam shovel to be precise).  I
can't reconcile that picture with something that is people oriented.
I hardly think of it as human at all.  I certainly don't want to get
close and comfortable with it.  Only the people *as such* are "of, by,
and for the people", but they lose that quality immediately when they
start shoveling with any kind of power.

I don't know if I could do much with the text except on these terms.
And the March 31 deadline (again I didn't realize) doesn't leave much
time for anything, least of all discussion.  But let me know what you
think.  Even as it stands, I'll help with proof reading and redacting.

Mike
