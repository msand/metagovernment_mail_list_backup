SCIOC looks specialized to me, Alex.  I am no expert but it appears to have
to do with categorizing web content by type for exchange.  And although it
does seem to be still alive, most of the references end in 2010.  It
suffers from the critical mass problem - it is not useful until a lot of
sites are using it.

For the big project of categorizing everything, I'm thinking we need
something more flexible than rdf.  For the Voices of Humanity
Project<http://intermix.org>,
I hope we can eventually implement a generalized bottom-up reticulated
hierarchy crosscut by networks/geography/subject as well as by the six
voices of humanity.  The basic idea is to expand the notion of a "tag" to a
"dimension".  So for instance a tag might be "peace", while a dimension
might be

peace
     absence of war
     balance of forces
     no structural violence
     feminist peace
     holistic Gaia peace
     holistic inner-outer peace

There might easily be a completely different analysis of the concept of
peace. which would co-exist with the one given above.  The person adding
the dimension (as one adds a tag) to a forum message or database item
specifies which of the tags that the dimension is composed of apply to this
item.  Dimensions can be altered, thus forming a new dimension.  The meta
tag of a dimension can be specified as one of the dimensional tags of
another dimension.  etc etc.  There are huge problems of usability and
cross-language implementation.  Perhaps it is not practical.  The advantage
is it is bottom up and very flexible.  There is no reason any particular
tag cannot reach the higher levels by different routes.  For instance in a
geographic hierarchy, we can go from a town to a province to a nation to a
continent to Gaia.  Or we can go from that same town to the local bioregion
to the category of bioregions to Gaia.  It needs a ton of work, though,
first to think it through and then to implement with the latest graphical
interface.  (Not to mention the critical mass problem (;-)
