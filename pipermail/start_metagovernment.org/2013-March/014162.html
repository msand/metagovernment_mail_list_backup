Hi Ed,
thanks.

Yes I should send an update.
Here is a fast one:

Vilfredo is progressing very well.
And we are slowly learning how to make people interact while keeping
the math solid. It is not easy, but it is doable.

The most recent progress was to let people see the result of their
vote (in terms of graph), so they could change their vote looking for
a Nash equilibrium. This made many people understand the graph much
better. And many who used Vilfredo before and after said that it is
much better now. The next main jump will be to let people ask
clarifications about proposals, and respond to them. Considering that
in Vilfredo every body must vote for every proposal, and most people
do not understand many proposals, this is also considered very
important.


In the meantime I am aving more and more requests for help in Italy.
In Italy one of the three main parties is the M5S which advocates an
e-democracy approach. But they still don't have their platform. So the
discussion about how to implement eDemocracy just went over the roof.

My input in this has been a series of comments, but mostly few, well
thought entries in my italian blog. One of them I have translated in
English, where I present White House 2 and its derivative works (Open
Active Democracy, yrpri.org, Better Reykjavik, ... ).

I think people who are not familiar with White House 2 might find it
interesting.
http://blog.pietrosperoni.it/2013/03/20/from-white-house-2-to-betri-reykjavik/

For me it was important to write it up, because most people don't know
about Jim Gilliam important contribution, and they all keep repeating
"liquid feedback" as a sort of mantra.

My second post was then a thorough critic to delegated voting. This
has not been translated but is in my todo list.

And then other posts. I will let you know when I translate them, so
you can read them.

Best Regards,
Pietro
