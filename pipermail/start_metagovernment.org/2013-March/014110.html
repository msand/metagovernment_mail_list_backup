I had seen this posting on another list, and have been planning on submitting a proposal for the Metagovernment project.

Ideally, I would like to present several related concepts:
1. The concept of collaborative governance, and how it can (inevitably will) come to fruition
2. Introductions to several member projects
3. An overview of vote mirroring and related cross-project initiatives

I will try to post a draft proposal on the wiki this week. In the meantime, project which may like to participate (either in having me describe them, or in also coming to Strasbourg and presenting themselves as part of #2 above), please chime in. Also, if travel to France is not feasible for you, I could include in the proposal that you attend virtually.

> The 2013 World Forum for Democracy “Rewiring democracy – reconnecting citizens and democratic institutions in the information age” will be held in Strasbourg (France) on 27-29 November 2013.
> http://www.coe.int/en/web/world-forum-democracy/home
>  
> It will provide a platform for emerging leaders and grassroots initiatives that foster participation and re-connect citizens and democratic institutions.  Participants from the world of politics, academia, civil society, business, media and social networks will critically review these innovative initiatives, assess their mainstreaming potential, inject new ideas into them, and identify the most promising ones for further promotion and dissemination. Participants, as well as the “global community” (via social networks and internet voting) will select the most promising initiatives, that will receive the Council of Europe recognition for democratic innovation.
>  
> The Forum will work through a series of informal, creative labs, enabling a frank and intensive debate, in an “open source” format. The content of the Forum will be co-created by the Council of Europe and a wide range of partners – organisations, institutions, civic movements and groups – who wish to submit their ideas and practice to the Forum peer review.
