You probably mean that you do not see any reason to be angry.

Please let me  explain. I joined the fp7 as a research from my
institute, and as a member of this list. I brainstorm on that page
with the list, and discussed with the list what were our proposals.
What were we going to bring to the table. At the fp7 I often explained
that I wanted as much as possible the informations to be trasparent.
This I wanted for various reasons. The idea of taking away the link
seem to me a really bad idea for various reasons:

1) personally I feel it forgets, and disrespects the work that I, and
the other people who have brainstormed this info, did.
(As I feel it disrespect my and other people work, there is indeed a
reason to be angry).
2) it is good for metagovernment. The fact of having permitted
something can help us in future. How? Suppose one of us want to give a
talk. In some cases they might look at where is someone coming from.
The fact that this list produced (or helped to produce) something is a
point in favor. In a moment in which politicians are looking for
people who are involved in eGov, the fact of being a list which has
member that are involved in official EU proposals, is a really good
point.
(as it is a decision that will eventually damage metagovernent, there
is a second reason to be angry, actually worried)
3) OMISSIS (ok, something I can say, and that is that some of what
will be developed can be tested by this list).
4) all the work that will be done will become opensource.
Metagovernment can then use this work.
5) because of 3 and 4, as this work gets developed, to still hold a
connection to the fp7 can give a slight help to the people who are
here to push the work in one direction, or to know a bit more. In
short put it like this, we submitted the proposal. After that Ed asked
me to make a report, and I did it. Why did I do it? I should not have
done it, right? after all I am not supposed to speak about it. Instead
I spoke about it, although in vague terms. I have done this because I
did felt that the work was related, to MG, and it was possible only
because of MG. You might not think of what I said as of any
importance. But you had access to it only because of that link. If
there is no link, on what base should I speak with you guy about a
personal project I am involved in.

So it is logic for  MG to still want to keep the page. Even if it is a
separate project.

I am not speaking about the relations between member of the community,
but about the relations between different projects, and between groups
and projects. The group metagovernment in connection with the freedom
foundation, permitted the project fp7. As such the fp7 is a child of
those groups (as well as University of Rome).

> FP7 acts not like child but arises with the power of money.

The idea that it came because of the "power of money" is your own
personal reading of the situation. I fully reject this reading. I work
for the university. I will see very little of that money, and all in
an indirect way (some of the trips I do to study this thing will be
paid). For now I am paying of my own pocket. So far I have worked 2
weeks in Italy, and one week in the UK to write the proposal. No one,
not the FP7, not metagovernment, not the freedom foundation, not the
university of coimbra will pay me back for the money I spent. This
money was spent out of the desire to study those things intensly. But
you know what? I am paid by the university. I do not have to teach. If
I wanted for 5 years I could work on this, and not do anything else.
But this project might permit other people to work on it too.  I feel
your words are somehow misrapresenting the situation. I would much
prefer if you could avoid trying to guess the motivation behind my
actions. Of course, if you can.



So I think/feel I could explain why I felt angry. Maybe we could write
something above the page, to explain how the proposal was submitted.

the work from this project to converge back to metagovernment, once
the project has ended.

This would clarify the relation, while keeping true to the past, and
