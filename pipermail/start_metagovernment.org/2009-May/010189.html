On Apr 26, 2009, at 5:55 PM, Robert Peters wrote:

> I suppose I've just grown frustrated at MetaGovernment in general.   
> 80% of new businesses fail in the first 5 years.  Of those that  
> survive, a further 80% fail in the next 5 years.  This illustrates  
> clearly that having an idea ("I'll open a business" or "I'll make  
> some software") is much easier than making it successful.  On top of  
> this, we have a leader who actively refuses to lead, and an  
> inability to learn anything from history about what it takes to make  
> a project of the titanic scope we are proposing (the abolition of  
> all governments, no less!) succeed.

This isn't a new business, it's an open project. Its success is not  
measured by whether or not it becomes profitable but only by whether  
it is able to achieve its objective. That could take 50 years for all  
I know. There are plenty of other projects which are rushing much  
faster than we are, and IMO they are thereby forced to make choices  
which are not necessarily right.

And our immediate aim is not to abolish all governments in the world  
but to set up an alternative system of governance which communities  
can use as they please. I certainly do not expect any major national  
government to be replaced by Metascore in the next few years. We have  
to prove ourselves on the smallest scale first, and it is reasonable  
to expect that that process should be slow and considered.

> We're doomed.  We have no idea if what we're proposing is even  
> possible, and we don't seem interested in trying to find out (I've  
> said this before, I'll say it again: we could have a Slashdot-type  
> site set up in less than 48 hours, yet it's been over a year and we  
> have nothing tangible).

We have two active prototypes running. Have you tried the open one at http://prototype.metascore.org/ 
  and given feedback?

If neither of those work for you, then who is it that could have a  
SlashDot-style system running in two days? I cannot. If you can, then  
please do. I'll give you access to the domain if that's what you  
require. I cannot force anyone to set up that software, so it is up to  
you to do so if you think it can be done.
