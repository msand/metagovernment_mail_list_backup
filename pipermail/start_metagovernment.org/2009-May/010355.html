On May 18, 2009, at 5:33 PM, Michael Allan wrote:

>  c. When a voter (V) votes for a proposal (P) in the context of a
>     Votorola issue (I), and another voter (W) votes for another
>     proposal (Q) in the context of the same issue, then both P and Q
>     are thereby made alternative proposals of I.

My question here would be... how are proposals associated with issues?  
The term "issue" is a rather vague concept, and as far as I know,  
different projects handle it differently (if they have even tackled it  
yet). I think DemocracyLab uses a formal taxonomy of pre-defined  
issues. I think the Metascore team is considering using a folksonomy  
to have issues interrelate by organic user tagging. How can such  
things be made compatible? The concept of proposals speaking to issues  
is a very central one to every project, and probably should be  
scrutinized at length.


On May 19, 2009, at 10:33 AM, Michael Allan wrote:

> So maybe we'll soon be able to give a technical thumbs up to Ed,
> Giovani, and other project leaders (anyone else listening) that we can
> do this stuff.  The hurdles don't seem to be technical.  What do you
> think?

At least for the Metagovernment project, decisions aren't up to one  
person. In this particular case, there is no distinction between  
technical and administrative decisions. If the Metascore team--or  
anyone else--can figure out interoperability, then it will happen. I  
do not think anyone in the project is opposed to the idea, so it's  
mostly just a matter of figuring out how to make it happen.

As far as I am concerned, the Metagovernment project is truly meta, in  
that it is an umbrella group for these other organizations: as  
evidenced by this discussion in particular. What you (anyone) decide  
is what this project does, since there is no barrier to membership and  
no levels of control.
