I certainly have not thought this issue through to its logical conclusion.
However, as a matter of general principles, it does seem to me that each
individual should have one and only one digital persona, which should be
maintained in a trustworthy, authoritative system -- rather than being
re-created willy nilly in myriad software programs.

Of course, if the systems about which we are talking don't really matter,
then it doesn't really matter whether people are who they say they are or
whether they have created multiple personalities.  However, I personally
don't care to waste my time in any such systems and I certainly don't want
to participate in any system that enables others to "game" their votes.

The paper I wrote addressing some aspects of this issue 10 years ago to the
month is available at http://ambur.net/PushShove.html See especially these
paragraphs: http://ambur.net/PushShove.html#Cook
