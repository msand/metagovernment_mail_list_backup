On Thu, May 14, 2009 at 5:18 PM, Jacki Buros <jburos at gmail.com> wrote:
> Hi all --
>
> So, in recent months I have been helping to develop a site
> (polladium.com) and I wanted to take a moment to introduce it to the
> community...
>
> I see it as related to online governance, though the reasons for this
> may not be immediately apparent. Allow me to elaborate.
>
> It's a social polling site, integrated with Facebook and twitter.
> Users create poll questions on current news issues and participate in
> polls. They can see what other people are thinking about and what
> their facebook/twitter friends are voting on.
>
> So, you may ask, how is this connected with our aims? I see it as
> improving the rate of participation in community issues through an
> online forum. Because it's social, and not obviously political, it's
> less intimidating than other sites like WH2 which will primarily
> attract people who are already politically active, or who have a
> vested interest in a specific political issue. So the reach of a site
> like this will be more broad than those of our other initiatives.
>
> One issue I have noticed in using the site is the potentially divisive
> affect that polls have on the participants -- basically, people are
> forced to choose one or another 'camp' and there's very little sense
> that a consensus can be reached.
>
> Some positive interface ideas we have come up with are the VoxBox (see
> my voxbox here: http://www.polladium.com/voxbox/jb555) which allows
> one to follow the actions of friends on the site. Also, the publisher
> section (see my publisher profile here:
> http://www.polladium.com/p/polladium.php?p=1003) which can list a
> subset of polls related to a particular interest group.
>
> Would be curious to know if there are thoughts in the group as to the
> site's design, how it may improve and what may be useful about what we
> have done.
>
> PS I define my aims in the online governance initiative to be three-fold:
> 1 - increase transparency
> 2 - improve participation
> 3 - improve accountability
>
> Feel free to disagree with me on these -- they are my personal goals
> and not necessarily those of the group.
>
