> So a certifying authority (like Verisign) does the authentication?  I
> think you're right, they offer that service.  But don't they charge a
> fee?  It might be too high.
>   
The problem is not really the cost here. Is the trust. If Verisign or
any other "proprietary" company does the "certification", how to avoid
corruption and all other problems inherent of dealing with private orgs?
Our user-verifiable telematics project aims to create a
"community-managed certifying authority", that will ensure openness 
and  transparency in this process. We are now writing a new EU grant
about this, and the Free Software Foundation Europe has given their go
to join as a partner (although they don't want to commit to write the
project). The deadline is June 2, I am not sure we will make it this
time because we are in the beginning, but we'll try.
