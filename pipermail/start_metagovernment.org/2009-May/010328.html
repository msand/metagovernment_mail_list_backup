Thoughts of a newcomer on parts of Kris¹ views - apologies for anything
missed in previous threads.

1. It may be worth bearing in mind general possibilities in the future for
Œcommunity auditing¹ of declarations of stakeholder interest, rather than
reliance upon a superior authority.  Assuming that people should be prepared
to have such declarations as public information, then those they know could
effectively moderate their selection of categories.  In areas where it might
not be appropriate to have this information as public (for example, those
affected medically by governmental/industry activity) then other mechanisms
would need to be explored.  Cross-referencing between different social
networking etc web technologies might also be a possibility but could raise
much trickier issues I think, as my assumption is that consent would be
appropriate and would be incomplete across participants.

4. I¹ve put forward some views on delegation in earlier emails.  Just to add
here that I think that people/organisations are stakeholders in so many
issues that a framework for ensuring inclusion, even without direct
participation, would be necessary.

Tom



On 16/05/2009 04:09, "Kris Dev" <krisdev at gmail.com> wrote:

> Hi all,
> 
> Going through everyone's views, i conclude, the following are the main
> subjects of the discussions here - please correct me, if i am wrong:
> 
> 1. 'Stakeholder involvement' rather than anyone's (outsider's) involvement.
> 
> 2. Unique identity of the stakeholder / voter.
> 
> 3. Permitting only single vote of each stakeholder on each subject.
> 
> 4. Delegating for proxy voting.
> 
> 
> The following are my views on the above:
> 
> 1. It is difficult to segregate someone as a stakeholder and another as a
> non-stakeholder; may be a classification of directly impacted, indirectly
> impacted and not impacted, may help. But such declaration must come from the
> individual and cannot be subject to audit, lest a superior authority for
> review is brought it, which may belie the purpose.
> 
> A weightage factor can be introduced for the 3 categories - directly impacted,
> indirectly impacted and not impacted.
> 
> 2. Biometric Smart Card (BSC) can be used for unique identity.
> 
> 3. BSC would limit to a single vote for each, even online. 
> 
> 4. Delegating for proxy voting may help in some genuine cases; but could
> subvert the process of stakeholder direct involvement.
> 
> Kris Dev
