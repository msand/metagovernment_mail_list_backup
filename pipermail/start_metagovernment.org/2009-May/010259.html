On Sat, May 9, 2009 at 10:09 AM, Thomas von der Elbe <
ThomasvonderElbe at gmx.de> wrote:

> Dear people,
>
> first I want to thank you for deeper exploring possible cooperation between
> us!
>
> And I would also like to answer to some doubts concerning this.
>
> I believe, that most of us are very intensively focused on their particular
> tasks in their particular project.
> But if we just for a moment could loosen this focus a bit and zoom out of
> the picture. In this wider picture, isn´t it obvious to see, that our goals
> are the same or at least compatible?
>
> Of course: cooperation would take time and energy to communicate, discuss
> etc. and finally find consensus on how and what to do. And if a project is
> close to completion, it might make no sense to invest that energy for a
> future benefit.
> Has one of our projects already reached this point?
>
> But there might be another hurdle for cooperation: One´s own identification
> with one´s project.
> In my case: I had the idea for global, direct e-democracy a few years ago
> and started to convince people to work with me on it. Then I found
> Metagovernment and realized, that they were much further developed already.
> Retrospective it is funny to see, that at first I saw them as competitors.
> I was not the first one with this idea and probably would not be the one to
> bring it into existence either. This did hurt somehow.
> But slowly my identification shifted to Metagovernment. The new "We" was
> much bigger now, global and much more powerful, capable of reaching the goal
> even faster. Now this felt even better then before!
> But then other projects like Votorola, Democracylab, WH2 appeared. Again
> the same process: fear of not being in the winning team. lol
> A part of me tried to ignore them. Another part compared us, to reassure
> myself, that Metagovernment was better.
> But finally I found in the other projects such beautiful solutions for
> problems I hadn´t even seen yet, that my whole pattern of
> self-aggrandizement (at least in this area ;-)) collapsed.
> Thank you for this !
>
> Now somehow my focus has shifted to the beautiful energy behind all of our
> projects. And I can see, that this energy is the very same in all of us. And
> I just can´t chase away the picture of all of us working together. Just
> imagine how fast we could get really far!
>
> And like I said, maybe the projects are already developed so far, that soon
> we will have a darwian fight among them, which only the fittest one will
> survive. But if not, let´s take a moment together to consider what the dead
> ends of this evolution are most likely going to be !
> After all, one of Metagovernments basic principles is: Consensus through
> Synthesis.
>
>
> With best regards,
> Thomas
>
>
> PS: I just had the idea, that maybe some people with their project are
> hoping for the future to create a job with an income. But that would be
> equally possible in a joint project, wouldn´t it?
