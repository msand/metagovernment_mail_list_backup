Mike, the question you are posing is the start of a strategic plan, namely a
mission statement:  http://xml.gov/stratml/draft/StratMLGlossary.xml#Mission


With respect to your comment about the potential for synergy, among the
purposes of the StratML standard is to:  Facilitate the discovery of
potential performance partners who share common goals and objectives and/or
either produce inputs needed or require outputs produced by the organization
compiling the strategic plan.
http://xml.gov/stratml/index.htm#DefinitionPurposes 

Yes, from my perspective, it would be very helpful if everyone who aims to
accomplish anything that requires the engagement of others (stakeholders)
were document their "aims" (goals) on the Web in StratML format.  While I
recognize that those seeking proprietary advantage may not wish to do that,
I can see no reason that .gov agencies and nonprofit, public service
organizations should not be expected (if not required) to do so as a
precondition for funding.  (I am no longer making charitable contributions
to organizations that do not have a strategic plan.  My view is that there
is no reason for me to waste my money on organizations that either: a) don't
know what they're doing and for whom they are doing it, or b) don't care to
share such information with me.)

In the long run, it also seems to me it is possible that even entrepreneurs
who aim to make a profit will be unable to do so without efficiently and
effectively engaging not only their existing customers but also their
potential customers.

Who in their right mind will want to continue doing business with those who
insist on hiding information from them, if they have a reasonable choice not
to do so?  Moreover, if businesses fail to provide the information their
customers want, it seems to me the risk is great that: 

a) their competitors will step into the vacuum and do it for them (in a less
than flattering manner), and/or 

b) their customers will take it upon themselves to do so (particularly if
they are disgruntled).

It is my hope and expectation that consumers (of all things, including
"governance") will become more educated "shoppers" in the future, and I
strongly believe StratML can play an important role in the pursuit of that
vision.  I also remain hopeful that Metagovernment.org can make a
significant contribution toward that end.
