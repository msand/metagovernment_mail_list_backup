Ed Pastore wrote:
> Matías Battocchia wrote:
>> So, up today a network of trust is the only feasible solution to the 
>> online identity problem?
>
> The more I think about it, the more sense this makes. After all... identity 
> is other people knowing we are who they think we are. So in essence, 
> identity is nothing but a question of trust.
>
> The real issue seems to be about consistency of identity: i.e., "Is this 
> person before me now the same person I know from before." Is there a way to 
> verify consistency-of-identity by one's network of trust?

Answering for Votorola's neighbourhood trust network: Ordinary voters
extend trust only to the people they actually know, at least in
passing.  So I might knock on my neighbour's door across the street,
introduce myself and say, "I see you folks are on the voter list.  I'm
trying to get on too!  I live just over there.  Could you please
extend your trust to me?  Here's my online ID."

Recognizing me, my neighbour would say, "Of course we will!"

"Thank you!"

So consistency of identity is provided by people's memory, and
refreshed by everyday encounters on the street.
