This article may be of interest:
http://www.futuregov.net/articles/2009/may/19/elections-electrified-why-e-vo
ting-better-paper-ba/ 

 

However, it is disappointing that a false choice is still being presented in
such a forward-looking article, i.e., that paper ballots are the only way to
ensure that the integrity of voting systems can be ensured and audited.

 

Electronic representations of the ballots can be stored apart from the
databases in which the votes are tallied; the visual integrity of the
ballots can be ensured by using standards like PDF, XPS, or XFDL in
combination with digital signatures; and the vote tallies compiled in the
databases can automatically be audited (recounted) if the ballots are
maintained in XML format in records management systems under the control of
separate, trusted authorities.
