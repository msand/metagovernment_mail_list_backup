hi Kai,

In principle, using Kickstarter + Paypal as the means through which to 
fund democracy projects is a perfect match. In practice, the choice 
seems harder (but still maybe good enough). Both Kickstarter and Paypal 
are private, for-profit corporations. Kickstarter takes 5% off the top, 
and Paypal takes 3% off the top. They are both monetizing the network 
effect, bigtime, for services that can be and mostly already are fully 
automated. And, as proprietary operations, both are available to be 
commandeered for use by the elites as gatekeepers. (Paypal is already 
being used as such.) And, there is (my guess) generally no tax deduction 
available when funding passes through that conduit, which is another XX% 
off the top. Ouch.

What we very much need (when someones get around to it) are open-source, 
non-profit, democratically managed, fully transparent versions of 
Kickstarter and Paypal. That really would be a perfect way to fund 
democracy projects, and to fund prizes for proposals and many other things.

(If I am wrong about any of the above, let me know. I haven't really 
looked deeply into the Kickstarter alternative, but have definitely had 
it in mind.)
