I can see you've thought a lot about all of this.   And I'm really appreciating the immense amount of work you (and others) have already invested in realizing this ideal/vision/goal.

One big thing keeps coming up for me though: In order for a system/process like this to really catch on...and to work at a population scale....it would need to be both empowering and fun. Most people these days aren't going to spend their 30 minutes, or so, of precious alone time each day rustling through 10,000 mundane proposals. Sorting through a bunch of (meaningless to me) stuff would be a real buzz kill. Personally, I just wouldn't do it. It needs to be organized....and it needs to be *connective* in a meaningful way.

> I am open to being shown that this would be constructive, but the social network platforms we have had presented to us up to this point look to me to be mostly counter-productive from the perspective of begin-healing-the-planet. (There is lots to be said about this ...) (And also, I know: "Awww ... I'm no fun!")


Personally, I don't do Facebook because it's such a time suck...but it's extremely popular around the world for one reason. Humans are very social creatures and most of us want to connect and share our lives and 'business' with friends and family online. Being able to do this is meaningful and fun for a LOT of people. And it's a really great way to *organize*.  Let me say that again...and it's REALLY great way to *organize*....(think "Arab Spring"). That's the "empowerment" aspect. Having access to a dedicated global platform to facilitate the business of creating a better world...that's furnished with all of the toolsets we would need to engage in "communicative action", at a population scale, would be really attractive to LOT of people right now....particularly if it was effective in generating and funding some really intelligent projects. 

I'm imagining a networking platform that I could visit each day in order to catch up on all the latest buzz about what is happening in the/my world. I would want to be able to use this platform as an information filtering mechanism, as well as an interaction/action mechanism. I would want to have my own individual and/or group webpage(s). I would want to be able to organize them so that I could track all of things happening on the network that were important to me. I would want to be able to connect directly to the *library/database* so I could have quick access to any info I wanted about the network and it's/our business. I would want to be able to easily connect to other webpages of members and groups (in the same format) so I could easily peruse and gather info about them. I would want to be able to easily manage my involvement in the proposals/projects that I was a part of. I would want to be able to easily make pledges or donations to projects that I liked....and to connect with them personally to become involved in some way if I wanted to do so.    And I would want it all to be accessible on a mobile ap.   I think you get my point....  :-)

I know...it's an "iPad of complexity"....but, in my mind, it's the only way we will ever be able to organize at that scale in order to heal society and the planet sometime before we totally self-destruct.  

What thinks you?

The individual donor has no direct say in which projects get funded (except when the individual donor donates the full amount needed).
