On Oct 25, 2010, at 2:02 PM, Richard Franks wrote:

> The question will be re-framed by the answers, explanations and
> discussions it generates.  This is to be expected when exploration of
> the problem space discovers information not initially available.  This
> will happen with or without a formal/automated process for re-framing
> the question.  But I agree - a system designed with this in mind will
> be more scalable.

So in Vilfredo, I think this would involve a third stage to each  
round. Or at least a meta-round that interjects into the process after  
every few rounds of back-and-forth.

In DemocracyLab, this is already well incorporated into the process by  
the careful investigation of each individual's and issue's component  
parts.

Can other projects see the value and practicality of integrating in a  
meta-process of re-evaluating questions as they are debated?


On Oct 25, 2010, at 1:53 PM, Owen Ambur wrote:

> Sorry to sound like a broken record, but from my perspective, it  
> would be
> good to frame "questions" as objective (measureable) statements of
> longer-term, more visionary goals and shorter-term, more readily  
> achievable
> objectives, i.e., what we aim to accomplish within the relatively  
> near term
> in support of the longer-term vision.

That makes a lot of sense, particularly in this context. I would think  
it would vary depending on each project's approach. Again, I think  
DemocracyLab already does this fairly well to some extent, while other  
projects do not seem to do it much at all.
