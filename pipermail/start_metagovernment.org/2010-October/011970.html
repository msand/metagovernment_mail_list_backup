On Mon, Oct 25, 2010 at 9:32 AM, Michael Maranda <mm at michaelmaranda.net> wrote:

> Open Democracy does not violate major tenets in my proposed criteria.

I find this interesting - and apologies if this is going off-topic, or
re-treading old ground - but is there anything to be gained by
attempting consensus at the tenet/criteria level?  As in - if you can
agree on criteria for a high-level "direction", and grade the
low-level "detail" options against those - how many people would then
find it easier to compromise on the "details" knowing that their
"direction" was taken into consideration?

There is a problem of recursion here - if you can't find consensus
with the tenets, then you'd need to first work out the meta-tenets,
and perhaps the meta-meta-tenets, etc.  But I wonder if Michael is an
exception here, or if simply the knowledge that your higher-level
input is embedded into the details of a final decision you wouldn't
otherwise support, would enable more people to act the same way and
join the consensus?  Synthesis isn't all-or-nothing, but with a single
conceptual layer of choices, it feels that way.
