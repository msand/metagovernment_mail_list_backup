Yes, I agree that before publishing the book (not sure we have finished the epilogue tough??) we should consider knowing more who we are talking to.

About the blog, yes that would be nice. I'd like to share more about that idea of distributed, p2p, social network infrastructure I proposed at La Sapienza University back when we were preparing EUGAGER, where there is no central datacenter holding all citizen's sensible data, but your data is hosted by yourself and partially by your contacts (people you told you trust). A few "emergency servers" would still be needed to hold data from contacts with little or none connections(ie new users, people less active, ecc...).

I think that is a crucial step towards guaranteeing citizens that their data won't be used to "datamine", "track" and "control" political trends that can be used against democracy, and also that aggregate data won't be sold by a single private or public company.
Unfortunatelly that was not part of the EU call (infrastructure), but in the light of making it all open I think it is very important.
