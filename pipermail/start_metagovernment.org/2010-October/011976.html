Here is something I can answer. It is possible to consider multiple
voting, like rating the answers, or ordering them. But I have a
_strong_ intuition (funded on some partial math results) that the
result of this would be that the Pareto Front would grow a lot, even
to become the whole list of all the proposals. At the end an all or
nothing seem to be the most acceptable solution. You decide where the
threshold for something to be acceptable is. But you are invited to
put it as low as it is actually acceptable.

Sorry, this is incorrect. For example in Science you have very simple
questions (how did life arise on earth?), with very complex answers,
and very complex questions with very simple answers (take any long
equation whose answer is 2). Something that is important is that there
is no "conservation of complexity" principle in Nature; complex causes
can have simple effects and simple causes can have complex outcomes.
That's why it's so interesting.
