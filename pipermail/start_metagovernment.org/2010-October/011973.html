I recommend using perspectives to define the space, the question, and the
response.

http://www.clintfuhs.com/files/pdf/Fuhs_Perspective-taking-Appendix.pdf

<http://www.clintfuhs.com/files/pdf/Fuhs_Perspective-taking-Appendix.pdf>This
is an answer to the old adage "the system that created the problem cannot
solve the problem" or however it goes.  This idea is that the quesiton need
to match the perceived conditions, and the response needs be at least as
complex as the question.  Elsewise, repeat.

Mind you, I am not a fan of consensus, except at "same stage complexity."
