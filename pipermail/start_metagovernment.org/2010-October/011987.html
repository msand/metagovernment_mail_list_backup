On Oct 20, 2010, at 1:28 PM, Alex Rollin wrote:

> I'd like to know, in a week or so, what kind of traffic this generates
> to the wiki, and what the general patterns are once visitors arrive.

Given the results (below) my impression is that, like the rest of the  
world, Slashdot isn't getting it. The spike on 10/20 is hardly  
noteworthy.

If you look at the discussions on that thread:
http://news.slashdot.org/story/10/10/20/1549227/US-Elections-Dominated-By-Closed-Source-Again
there are over 400 posts, and almost nothing about collaborative  
governance. But maybe Slashdot is an anomaly? Though my impression is  
that this is typical: People in this thread seem much more interested  
in expressing their vaunted opinions of how horrible the status quo  
is, rather than giving a moment's thought to actual solutions.

I see this a lot. People are very ready to say that democracy is dead.  
Then I present them with a hint of our project, and they immediately  
launch into criticisms of how it fails to be completely perfect.

As you can see from the numbers below, people continue to come to  
metagovernment.org in decent numbers. But getting them interested in  
the message is a real, heavy challenge. (To be specific, well over 100  
people come to the site each day... and our list has 117 members).

I am open to suggestions for how we can market more effectively. I'm  
getting people to the site, but as we've noted before, it's message  
still isn't working. We may just have to prove ourselves before anyone  
will believe us, but... it would be nice if we could make our case  
more effectively.

So far, my suggestion (sidelined by my ridiculously pressed schedule  
this year) has been:
http://metagovernment.org/wiki/Main_Page/2010-06/v2
but it is a primitive first draft.
