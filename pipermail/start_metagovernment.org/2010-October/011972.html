I think this points toward a deeper issue in all our open-drafting  
systems (and in closed systems too). The way a question is presented  
greatly affects peoples answers. Further, in open drafting, there is  
the liklihood of many very similar proposals.

Given that, a question-framing process seems helpful if not downright  
necessary if we ever hope to scale to larger communities.

Of course, there's also the risk that locking-in to one question might  
hinder synthesis...
