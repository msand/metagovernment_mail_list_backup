Pietro Speroni di Fenizio schrieb:
> Personal Democracy Forum report, EU edition.
>
> I went to the Personal Democracy Forum, here are my impression for this group.
>
> The two days were incredible, full of networking and ideas. In general
> I would say that we are at least 10 years ahead of everybody there.
> Most people there concentrate on trasparency, and open data. Some on
> the digital divide. There is a lot of work and a lot of noise on
> results we would consider trivial or at maximum irrelevant. For
> example, politicians using facebook and twitter to reach more people.
> Yes, that can be done, but it is just using facebook and twitter as a
> 1 to many system. Can't we do anything better?
>
> Every now and then someone would get it. There were presentations were
> a politician would express her frustration: I receive 100 emails a
> day, how can I integrate them.
>
> Some people did not realise that the problem, the key problem, is a
> problem of integration. So we had the CEO of GetUp who would tell us
> how he had millions of people signing his actions, and each sending
> personal emails to their representatives. But there is a catch: the
> emails are personal but the meaning of message is always about the
> same. Then the representatives calling it Spam. I would give reasons
> to the representatives, but of course the man in the street didn't.
> Again the problem is a problem of integration of the multiple voices
> and framing of this problem.
>
> I would say that only a very very tiny minority had framed the problem
> in the right context.
>
> A politician from CDU (christian democrats in germany) described how
> you have thousands of email protesting, but when the same people are
> invited to participate in a constructive production of an alternative
> the number drops to hundreds, and then the original ideas end up being
> about 20-30. Those are good news, because 20-30 original ideas are
> numbers that our systems can absolutely cope with.
>
> Another person who really framed the work VERY well was Paul Johnston
> that in the talk Opening Up Policy-Making to Public Participation,
> gave us some really good insights about how we need to start maturing
> up. How elected representatives just cannot cope with thousands of
> unrelated emails, of how few of those emails actually have real
> content. I hope I can find the video online. Those are people who are
> pushing the group toward the direction we are interested in, having
> citizen work inside the government.
>
> Another interesting talk was about europetitions. Did you knew that
> anyone can petition the EU parliament. Even more, it looks like it
> does not even have to be a european to do it (wow).
> There are two kind of petitions, petitions where you are suggesting a
> law and I think that for this you need to be 1 million signatures from
> the EU. But then there is also another form of petition. For example
> if a local law does not follow EU standard you (anyone, any single
> person) can send a petition. We were presented a website where people
> can write a petition, and send it to the EU parliament. Also when the
> parliament replies the reply is sent to all the participants. So I
> dialogue between the EU parliament and the citizen is potentially
> started. Although petitions have limited power I think this is really
> cool. And I spoke with Reindr Rustma who was presenting this website.
> They have an API, so it is possible to interface it from the outside,
> so now I am considering organising a vilfredo plug in, so people can
> write a petition together, and then send it with a click.
>
> During the forum I also started a new question in Vilfredo and I
> advertised at pdf on twitter, using the pdfeu tag. We are running the
> question rapidly as we want to use the answer to post it to the
> crossroad website.
>
> ouff, the more I go on the more I feel I should be doing this full time...
>
