I maintained a distinction for myself between

1) criteria (which embraces/overlaps with tenets) for the phrase
2) explanatory definitions and statements supporting each proposed phrase
3) the phrase or word itself (in each proposal)

For this task/question - the definition/explanation plays a supportive role.
Correct?  We don't need full consensus on the wording/semantics there.

So, any of our higher level input doesn't necessarily stick to the output in
the way we'd like unless we all have shared commitment to it doing so.  (And
it seems to me we do -- which is why are here after all)

> input is embedded into the details of a final decision you wouldn't
> otherwise support, would enable more people to act the same way and

> conceptual layer of choices, it feels that way.
