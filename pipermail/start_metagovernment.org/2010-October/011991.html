On Oct 26, 2010, at 10:00 AM, Alex Rollin wrote:

> In the Votorola project we've been working on the specification for
> the prototype of something called "crossforum theatre".
>
> While one purpose of the project is to allow voters to use any
> discussion media in order to continue discussion of a position or
> poll,
>
> another purpose is to provide a set of methods for easily accessing
> the current discussions on a position, to follow the discourse, and,
> in later prototypes, to engage through a variety of interfaces (web
> browser, mobile, in person).
>
> The issue of "traversing a tree", of discovering the variety and
> texture of positions within a poll submitted by other candidates,
> seems to be a ... well, it's something worth looking at.  I'd be
> interested in hearing how this fits into what Richard said earlier
> about how to "faily construct the tree."  One issue I have seen is
> that a certain poll may have filters on the votes, filters that
> disregard other voters, and rebuild the tree.  This information is not
> necessarily held within a raw vote count, and may have to do with
> processing the votes after votes are cast; how a poll is actionable to
> a certain group based on who votes and how as a subgroup of all voters
> and their votes.  Building a tree, a separate tree, for that group, is
> not really fair, but is certainly "practical" from a limited
> perspective.  As an example, they only care about the discussion
> happening amongst qualified voters, qualified within their group, or
> at least there's an argument that this is all they care about.
>
