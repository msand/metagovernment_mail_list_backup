Hi Michael,
thanks for the email.

We surely should consider Open Democracy as the official answer, then.

But we also should look at other lessons to take from this situation.
It is now the second time that a major agreement is stopped because a
single person voted differently.

I now wonder if there is any official way we should implement when
those situation occur.

For example should we invite a person to change his vote, and do it
before opening the new "writing" phase?

Just thinking aloud right now.

Still we should go through the writing phase (maybe writing a dummy
proposal), and then the voting phase. There is no other way to close a
proposal than reaching an agreement.

Cheers,
Pietro
