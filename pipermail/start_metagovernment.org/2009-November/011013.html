Hi Giovani,

while Rule 2 Gether seems really good,
the key element in the participatory democracy is not the ability to give
comments
or the experts speaking,
but the statistical sample of the population.

 This is the element that we are all missing.
It is easy to give the possibility to users to comment.
It is also quite easy to have experts tell their experience (at times it is
hard to have them shut up ;-) ).
The problem with all this is that all those system tend to make the people
who scream more, dominate the discussion.

 Look for example at Vilfredo, where the person who have more patience will
inevitably win the discussion. He just needs to hang around long enough when
everybody else is not interested anymore in participating.

Instead in those case they pick some people. Those are taken at random with
the only requirement to be a  statistically relevant, sample of the
population (matched for -I suppose- age, sex, race, education, wealth ?).

Then they get those people to learn about an issue, and then either vote (in
some systems) or comment (in some others).

And the activists, the ones who shout a lot, are excluded.


Can you see hw the systems are different?

Pietro
