It seems to me that there are at least three distinct concepts at issue
here: 

	1) governance -- implying *coercion* (of someone, even if it is
"collaborative" among others)
	2) collaboration -- implying voluntary cooperation (among those who
share common objectives)
	3) polling -- measurement of opinions/attitudes but not necessarily
implying either governance or collaboration, directly or indirectly

Having spent my entire 34-year career in "governance," now that I am free to
spend my time as I wish, I am far more interested in collaboration, which is
why I am spending a lot of time working to foster the specification and use
of Strategy Markup Language (StratML) as an international voluntary
consensus standard. http://xml.gov/stratml/index.htm 

Owen
