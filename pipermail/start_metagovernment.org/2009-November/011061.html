On Nov 20, 2009, at 12:17 PM, Joshua N Pritikin wrote:

> There is polling where you call a bunch of people on the phone (or  
> mail
> out surveys) and people respond off the top of their head without
> thinking about the issue deeply. Then there is deliberative polling
> where you select a random sample of more than 100 people, bring them
> together, deliberative on an issue with expert testimony, and come to
> some kind of conclusion. The two processes have some commonality in  
> name
> ("poll"), but produce vastly different kinds of results.

What most projects here are considering is polling of absolutely  
everyone who wants to participate, and developing consensus out of an  
ensuing deliberative process, managed by software engineered for the  
task.


On Nov 20, 2009, at 9:32 AM, Joshua N Pritikin wrote:

>> We are doing Collaborative Governance.
>
> Well, how are you going to do public consultation? Are you planning to
> convene random samples of citizens?


I also want to note here that Joshua is pointing out a possibility of  
confusion in the term Collaborative Governance. I hadn't thought of it  
when suggesting it in the Vilfredo process, but it turns out that  
collaborative governance generally refers to a much broader scope of  
possibilities than what we are using it to mean. See:
http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Collaborative_governance
