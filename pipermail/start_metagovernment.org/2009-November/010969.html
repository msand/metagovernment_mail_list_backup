> 
> I think the technical challenge is to give meaning to the discussion
> by somehow tying it to the possiblity of a concrete result.  So
> imagine I find the discussion tab and it takes me to a group of 5
> voters, all engaged in a heated debate.  I join them and I ask, "What
> exactly are you guys talking about?"  They point me to the object of
> the discussion (a), and they carry on discussing it.
> 
> Two days later, their efforts come to a successful conclusion.
> Everyone stands back, and there it is: the concrete result of the
> discussion (b).
> 
> What are (a) and (b)?
-- Well, I am starting with (a)... the array of formally nominated 
candidates who will be subjects of a ranked-choice vote. Actually, 
there's a precursor to (a)... the election itself... the concrete 
motivation of the collaborative process. That is, Who should be the 
winner of (x)? Or, what is our consensus on the best way to say (y)? Or, 
what should our collectively-stated policy be regarding issue (z)?  The 
concrete result (b) would correspond to the tabulated results at the 
moment voting closes.

Also, in addition to a general discussion (Opinions) area for each 
election, my goal is to enable focused discussions directly off each 
nomination data point, so that there would be no need to ask what the 
discussion is about.
