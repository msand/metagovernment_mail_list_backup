The repeated proposals are a (small) problem. When I like a proposal and see
similar ones, I vote for all of them, and I imagine that everybody does the
same thing. So, the repeated proposals are likely to reach the next
generation together. Humans can see the consensus but not Vilfredo, who
thinks that --for example-- the 3 remaining proposals are all different when
in fact happens the opposite thing. To make Vilfredo close the question,
only one must left.

To tell that two (or more) proposals are equivalent: hold shift down and
select them, instead of a "tic" the letter "a" appears besides each one (or
a "b" if you clicked twice...). Could this be useful?
