There is no 'glimmer of the promise' without the work of academics and
engineers.
I see the aims of political <-> entrepreneurial as interdependent, but
both are dependent on the academic and the engineer.
That said, entrepreneurs can leverage the political motivations of the
masses to fund the work of academics and engineers.
Where all four exist, there is a confluence of interests and potential
for real positive feedback.
I would argue that these four interests are not *always* compatible.
But when they are, there's potential for real change.

But Michael's distinction leads to the question of whether Gov 2.0 is
the right forum for one/several of these interests -- looking at their
site, it's as much a forum for the engineer as for the politician.

Here's their pitch (http://www.gov2expo.com/gov2expo2010/public/cfp/82) :

> If you’re passionate about the power of the Web and how it can be leveraged to create greater transparency, participation, and collaboration between government and citizenry, share your knowledge and insights with your peers at Gov 2.0 Expo.

> Our speakers reflect our audience, so we’re looking for independents, startups, enterprises, and everyone in between, in a wide variety of industries. We’re also looking for new thinking.

Metagov is relevant to two of their 6 major interests:
"Government-Citizen Interactions" and "Small Entities as Laboratories"

So, it seems applicable to me.

Jacki
