I don't really care much about this "debate" per se.  However, I do care
about what we are actually trying to accomplish together.  Thus, the
passages of greatest interest to me are these:  

"The goal of a project such as metagovernment is to design a bootstrapping
system that meets not the desires of society, per se, but the mechanisms in
which those desires can be best achieved. As such, its goal is to find a
commonly agreeable framework and standard about which every individual can
agree is fair and applicable to all."
http://metagovernment.org/wiki/Healthy_society

First of all, I don't believe "society" has "desires"; individual, human
beings do.  Nor do I believe that "every individual" will ever agree that
any particular "framework" or "standard" is "fair and applicable to all."
(Perhaps the most cogent statement President Carter ever made was that life
is not fair. http://quotationsbook.com/quote/44426/)

What I'd like to think we (the subscribers to the Metagov listserv) are
working toward is: 

1) a standard set of data elements (preferably an XML vocabulary and schema)
by which individuals can not only:

a) express degrees of support/opposition on "propositions" (e.g., goals and
objectives) anywhere on the Web and perhaps (optionally) provide very
*short* narrative feedback, if they choose, but also (and more importantly)

b) indicate their degrees of *commitment*, e.g., the time, money, and/or
other resources they *personally* are willing and able to allocate (toward
realization of the objective);

2) software programming code to facilitate the creation, aggregation, and
reporting of such "data"; and

3) Web-based services supporting such functions.

At this point, it does not seem to me that anyone else shares my interest in
those objectives, particularly the first one.  Thus, I am contemplating how
much longer I may wish to remain subscribed to the listserv.

Owen
