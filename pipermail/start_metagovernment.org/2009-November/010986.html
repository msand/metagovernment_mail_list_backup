I came across this interesting article today:
http://news.cnet.com/8301-13505_3-10394478-16.html?part=rss&subj=news&tag=2547-1_3-0-20

It reminded me of our talks about adopting a common framework, and
Google's Open Social was suggested.
The thing is exactly as Matt Asay puts it, Open Source doesn't
necessarily mean to have an Open Development Process.

Now what I think is that if we are to build a global governance tool,
the common ground should be totally open and transparent. Our FP7
proposal tries to build just that.
Of course, remains the fact that FP7 wasn't approved, and therefore
there is no code. But well, I'd like to inquire about the ethical
decision behind this (using google to build something big, create
something big on our own or... do not create anything big enough)...
