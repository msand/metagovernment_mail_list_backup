Ed Pastore wrote:
> ... I figure we would have a stronger case (and a better
> presentation) if we submitted a group presentation proposal. And it
> makes sense that collaborative governance be presented by a
> collaborative group. :)

It finally occurs to me, we're in opposite domains.  There isn't much
hope of technical collaboration between e-dem and e-gov - not even a
grassroots e-gov. ;)

> Here is my (early!) draft presentation proposal:
> http://metagovernment.org/wiki/Presentation/gov20expo
> In addition to needing input from other projects; the proposal as a whole 
> needs to be more persuasive to the selection panel.
>
> What do you think?

For my part, I think the goal is wrong.  It doesn't matter what
technical domain we're in.  We won't get a healthy society by "bluring
or removing the distinction between citizens and officials".  People
ought to speak for and represent themselves - not as parts of an
all-encompassing bureaucracy - but as people.

Spare a thought too, for the bureaucracy. ;) At the end of the day, it
still has to *be* a bureaucracy.  It doesn't want us moving into its
offices, any more than we want it moving into our homes.
