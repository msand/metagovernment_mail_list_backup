On Fri, Nov 20, 2009 at 11:42:57AM -0500, Owen Ambur wrote:
> It seems to me that there are at least three distinct concepts at issue
> here: 
> 
> 	1) governance -- implying *coercion* (of someone, even if it is
> "collaborative" among others)
> 	2) collaboration -- implying voluntary cooperation (among those who
> share common objectives)
> 	3) polling -- measurement of opinions/attitudes but not necessarily
> implying either governance or collaboration, directly or indirectly

There is polling where you call a bunch of people on the phone (or mail 
out surveys) and people respond off the top of their head without 
thinking about the issue deeply. Then there is deliberative polling 
where you select a random sample of more than 100 people, bring them 
together, deliberative on an issue with expert testimony, and come to 
some kind of conclusion. The two processes have some commonality in name 
("poll"), but produce vastly different kinds of results.

You are all familiar with the Public Affairs Act of 1975, right?
