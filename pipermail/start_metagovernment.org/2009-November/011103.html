On 20 Nov 2009, at 17:18, Pietro Speroni di Fenizio <metagovernment at pietrosperoni.it 
 > wrote:

> As you know, I recently gave a talk in Southampton.
>
> One of the comments over there was really interesting, and I would  
> like to open up the venue for discussing it further. And I find it  
> interesting that the idea seem to appear now in different places.
>
> The comment was from Seth Bullock (in CC).
> Seth pretty much said:
>
> "The IPCC is the group of scientists from the UN who integrates the  
> research on climate change. For anything to appear on the IPCC  
> document there have to be a really strong majority of people that  
> agree with it. Maybe around 90% or similar. As a result the  
> documents that come out do not correctly represent the seriousness  
> of the situation we are in. What Vilfredo could do in such a  
> situation would be to permit to people to vote different versions of  
> the same paragraph, and then just insert the voted pareto front. To  
> show the spectrum of what the people signing  the document think"
>
> We then kept on having a small discussion on how the various opinion  
> should be represented (different documents, one after the other,  
> etc). Until we reached a conclusion similar to the document I am  
> accluding.
>
> This is essentially using a table, with a row for each paragraph. If  
> the paragraph is not supported by everybody, the row will be divided  
> into different column, one for each different element of the pareto  
> front.
>
> I am including an rtf file with an example of what I mean. This is  
> taken from Ed's abstract for the government 2.0 conference.
> http://metagovernment.org/wiki/Presentation/gov20expo
>
> If the list strips the attachment, the rtf can be found on:
> http://dl.dropbox.com/u/2754299/wiki%2Bvilfredo.rtf
>
> It should be noted that the idea is already going around in  
> different ways. Already Manuel, Fabian & C. at Candiwi have  
> installed a system to comment at each paragraph. And you can do  
> something similar on the Mercurial tutorial page.
>
> Also at the wall of text page, someone suggested that each paragraph  
> in a vilfredo proposal should be voted by itself.
> http://vilfredo.org/viewquestion.php?q=67&room=Vilfredo
> (first suggestion, current generation: 2)
>
> And in the mailing list just today someone suggested that maybe  
> vilfredo could be integrated with wiki (sorry, can't find the email  
> right now).
>
> So in short I think that yes, we should consider having the  
> possibility to set up a Vilfredo system to vote for different  
> paragraph in a common document. But I also think we should have a  
> different question being set up for each paragraph, and this can  
> only happen once Vilfredo is so smooth it can be seamingless embedded.
> Also you might want to do this only after the general structure of  
> the document have been decided.
>
> Any suggestion would be VERY welcome.
