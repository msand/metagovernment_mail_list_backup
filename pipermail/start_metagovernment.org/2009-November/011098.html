On Sat, Nov 21, 2009 at 03:37:28PM -0500, Craig Simon wrote:
> Joshua N Pritikin wrote:
> >I believe
> >deliberative democracy works best as a complement to
> >representative democracy.
> 
> It does strike me as a well thought out and truly promising
> complement. If it were more prominent, I think it could prove more
> informative than partisan debates.
> 
> Nevertheless, I presume you intend for it to displace some
> traditional methods of political decision making. So, where (or how)
> would you draw the line on when it should be used instead of popular
> elections or legislative determinations?

Like I suggested before, I think a regular poll would be a good way to 
decide. See the Democracy Act, Section 3-F-2 
http://ni4d.us/en/act_annotated

> Also, has the process for selecting facilitators and their materials
> ever been cause for controversy?

Not that I know of, but I have not read history thoroughly. Inviting 
representatives for all the major positions, giving them equal amounts 
of time, and permitting them to answer questions from the citizen panel 
does not seem to be an easily corruptable process.
