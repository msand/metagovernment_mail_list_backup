Marcos, the only thing that I need to know is how many dimension are the
informations from the voting.

If it is a one dimension, I would need to code a dominant function such that
endorse dominates ignore
ignore dominates oppose.

If this is the case, then yes, there is more information. But then why stop
at 3, move it to 4, move it to n, order them all (A, B, ..., N) (I rather
have this A, than B, B than ..., ... than N). You can even give to each a
continuous  value. Just let people move a sliding rule for each element, and
let them go with their gut feelings. All this works well in terms of pareto
front. And I do not exude to add it at some point. The only problem about
this is that the more valuse people have to chose from the more inconsistent
they will be in their choise. The more hard will be for the function to
converge. SO adding possibilities will not make them easier to converge, but
harder. As I said, I don't rule out the possibility to add this in future.
But I don't want to impose what mathematically I see as an inferior system
to anyone, so it will have to be a choise from the questioner.

A totally different idea is if the ignore, not ignore & approve, not ignore
& oppose possibilites represent elements in a 2 dimensional plane.

In this case I am not sure how would I have to set up the domination
function.

Sure thing
not ignore & approve dominates not ignore & oppose.
But how do those relate to the ignore part?

If you have two questions, and everybody approve the first, and you ignore
the second, does this mean that the first dominate the second, and the
second should be lost?
What if instead everybody opposes the first and ignores the second, whould
we now keep the second?

Because if both those are true, we are back at the unidimensional case, and
you can as well ask people to rate things with 1 star, 2 stars or 3 stars.

And if those are not true, I woudl like you to explicitly tell me how should
I handl those cases.
