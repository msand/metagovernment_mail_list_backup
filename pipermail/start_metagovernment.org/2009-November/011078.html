On Nov 20, 2009, at 11:00 PM, Joshua N Pritikin wrote:

>> People that don't care won't take good decisions, because they just  
>> simply
>> don't care, the situation doesn't affect them at all. In that context
>> objectiveness is futile and so is random drafting.
>
> It's not that simple. Many people won't care about an issue before  
> they
> are selected for a deliberative poll. However, once selected, they  
> will
> likely start to care about the issue due to being informed that what
> they will decide on the issue will have real consequences on political
> policy.

The fundamental difference is one of scope. Selecting a random group  
to deliberate on one issue is great. But it just doesn't scale to  
every issue affecting every community.

But also, there is nothing mutually exclusive about the two ideas.
