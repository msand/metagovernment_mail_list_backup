Manuel and Fabian,

I should have just said what *I* think Candiwi is useful for rather
than pose it as question.  Like you say, it all depends on your use
cases.  I think Candiwi is useful for the case of government record
keeping.  For example, my city has two main legal "articles":

 * Municipal code http://www.toronto.ca/legdocs/municode/1184_toc.pdf
 * Zoning bylaws (currently being amalgamated)
   http://www.toronto.ca/zoning/bylaw/ZBL_NewProvision_Chapter1.htm

It also has "proposals" for amending them:

 * Bylaws: http://www.toronto.ca/legdocs/bylaws/2009/index.htm

Currently the clerk's office has to merge the new proposals into the
existing articles.  It's a labour intensive chore, so they're always
behind on it, and the docs are never in sync.  Candiwi can help there.
(It's just that I often get confused between e-dem and e-gov, and I
assume others do too, so I pose these questions - that's all.)
