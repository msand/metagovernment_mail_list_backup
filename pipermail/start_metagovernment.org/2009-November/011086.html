On Sat, Nov 21, 2009 at 10:12:09AM +0100, Pietro Speroni di Fenizio wrote:
> I think the point of deliberative democracy is really good:
> take people who do not care,
> inform them
> let them comment/make-the-decision
> 
> I wish there was a way to be able to get automatically, through the
> internet, a sample of the population.

I wish that as well.

I do not oppose the important work that you are doing here---trying to 
use technology to improve upon the usual methods of involving the public 
in governance. However, I argue that you should acknowledge the chief 
shortcoming of your approach---that it lacks random sampling. 
Furthermore, I hope you will explicitly support other efforts towards 
deliberative democracy which incorporate a random sampling approach.
