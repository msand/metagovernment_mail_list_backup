On Nov 21, 2009, at 12:59 PM, jburos at gmail.com wrote:

>  People who don't participate aren't necessarily people who don't  
> care -- assuming this is just our way of honoring convenience --  
> they aren't even lazy. They are busy doing other things. Or they are  
> preoccupied. And, when asked, they may care. If one doesn't ask one  
> doesn't know.

I guess that by "don't care" I mean "don't care enough to make time."

But again, it seems reasonable that this level of apathy will diminish  
substantially under a collaborative democracy system. People can feel  
free to ignore things they don't have time for, allowing people with  
more time/energy/interest deal with particular issues. But if at any  
moment an individual feels that the people working on an issue are  
doing the wrong thing, they can jump in and try to shift the consensus.
