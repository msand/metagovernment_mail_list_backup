I guess it all depends on your use case. Different tools for different
jobs. A wiki has the advantage, of being easier to use, since there
is no process to go through. You just edit and save.

If it doesn't make sense to add that overhead, then of course a wiki
may be more appropriate.

If you want to deliberate on the content and establish that there is
actually some form of consensus about the content of the repository,
rather than it being the product of a narrow group that is willing to
invest the effort. Not that there's anything wrong about that, but you
can't tell from that, if there is any kind of consensus.
