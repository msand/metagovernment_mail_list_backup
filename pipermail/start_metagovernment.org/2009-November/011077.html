On Fri, Nov 20, 2009 at 11:29:04PM -0300, Matías Battocchia wrote:
> People that don't care won't take good decisions, because they just simply
> don't care, the situation doesn't affect them at all. In that context
> objectiveness is futile and so is random drafting.

It's not that simple. Many people won't care about an issue before they 
are selected for a deliberative poll. However, once selected, they will 
likely start to care about the issue due to being informed that what 
they will decide on the issue will have real consequences on political 
policy.

> Deliberative Polling® (®?!) works because the chosen ones (minor group)
> belong to a mayor group that do cares.

I am not convinced that your way of looking at things is accurate.

> That system leaves possible interested stakeholders outside the game.

How so? For a credible deliberative poll, all interested stakeholders 
should have been given the opportunity to present their case to the 
randomly selected panel of citizens. Perhaps individual stakehodlers 
will be left out of the deliberations, but normally no serious position 
on the issue of question will be omitted.
