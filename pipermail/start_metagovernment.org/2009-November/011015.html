Pietro Speroni di Fenizio wrote:
> Hi Giovani,
>
> while Rule 2 Gether seems really good,
> the key element in the participatory democracy is not the ability to
> give comments
> or the experts speaking,
> but the statistical sample of the population.
>
>  This is the element that we are all missing.
> It is easy to give the possibility to users to comment.
> It is also quite easy to have experts tell their experience (at times
> it is hard to have them shut up ;-) ).
> The problem with all this is that all those system tend to make the
> people who scream more, dominate the discussion.
>
>  Look for example at Vilfredo, where the person who have more patience
> will inevitably win the discussion. He just needs to hang around long
> enough when everybody else is not interested anymore in participating.
>
> Instead in those case they pick some people. Those are taken at random
> with the only requirement to be a  statistically relevant, sample of
> the population (matched for -I suppose- age, sex, race, education,
> wealth ?).
>
> Then they get those people to learn about an issue, and then either
> vote (in some systems) or comment (in some others).
>
> And the activists, the ones who shout a lot, are excluded.
>
>
> Can you see hw the systems are different?
>

>     This is the core funcionality of Rule2Gether (www.rule2gether.com
>     <http://www.rule2gether.com>) right now.
>     Users can vote on the Council Agenda Items and comment pro/con
>     (see for example http://rule2gether.com/node/28). The next step is
>     to define a period of time before this happens, where only an
>     elected group of experts (by thematic area: health, taxation,
>     sports...) can inform citizens on pro/cons, so when the voting
>     opens citizens will find already pro/con arguments to decide what
>     to do and will be able to reply and/or post their own arguments.
>
>     For an overview of rule2gether and an idea of how elections would
>     work see the 28-slides presentation at:
>     http://static.telematicsfreedom.org/rule2gether/intro-en/index.html
>     You can also register for the demo on rule2gether.com
>     <http://rule2gether.com>.
>     Development is temporarily suspended until we collect more funding
>     to hire drupal developers :-(
>     But the idea is to have a suite of web applications for
>     e-participation (as described in
>     http://www.telematicsfreedom.org/en/project/6/telematic-tools-continuous-democracy)
