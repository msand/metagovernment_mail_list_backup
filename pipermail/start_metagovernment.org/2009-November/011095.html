On Fri, Nov 20, 2009 at 8:00 PM, Joshua N Pritikin <jpritikin at pobox.com> wrote:
> It's not that simple. Many people won't care about an issue before they
> are selected for a deliberative poll. However, once selected, they will
> likely start to care about the issue due to being informed that what
> they will decide on the issue will have real consequences on political
> policy.

Saying it's self-selected is like saying a sale at the market is
self-selected.  Sure at first only the regulars who show up, but as
the people start to notice and communicate, more people show up.  In
the case of this analogy it eventually dries up due to scarcity.  But
with metagovernment, as the system demonstrates a greater efficiency
*and* vision, there's a general accretion of new users, a compelling
enough rationale that the old systems of governance becomes obsolete,
irrelevant.  The traditional apathy of the past few decades would
indicate that it already has to some degree...
