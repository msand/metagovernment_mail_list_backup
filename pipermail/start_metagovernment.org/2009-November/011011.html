Pietro Speroni di Fenizio wrote:
> I was aware of the system where a sampe of people are taken to vote on
> an issue, after having been educated on it by the experts (pro and
> con). I was not aware that in Oregon they are actually using it
> (although just to comment on the options).
> Very interesting indeed.
This is the core funcionality of Rule2Gether (www.rule2gether.com) right
now.
Users can vote on the Council Agenda Items and comment pro/con (see for
example http://rule2gether.com/node/28). The next step is to define a
period of time before this happens, where only an elected group of
experts (by thematic area: health, taxation, sports...) can inform
citizens on pro/cons, so when the voting opens citizens will find
already pro/con arguments to decide what to do and will be able to reply
and/or post their own arguments.

For an overview of rule2gether and an idea of how elections would work
see the 28-slides presentation at:
http://static.telematicsfreedom.org/rule2gether/intro-en/index.html
You can also register for the demo on rule2gether.com.
Development is temporarily suspended until we collect more funding to
hire drupal developers :-(
But the idea is to have a suite of web applications for e-participation
(as described in
http://www.telematicsfreedom.org/en/project/6/telematic-tools-continuous-democracy)

Any feedback is welcome,
cheers,
