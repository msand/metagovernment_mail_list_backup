I believe the wiki is ready to take public. I have done reasonable security
diligence in this thread:
http://www.mediawiki.org/wiki/Manual_talk:LocalSettings.php#Securing_this_file
and as far as I can tell, the wiki is not exceptionally vulnerable.

The only obstacles to taking it public are the copyright (etc.) pages I
mentioned below and some commitment from some members of this group to keep
an eye on it. If it goes public, then it needs to be protected from
spam/vandalism and posts from other people may need to be acknowledged or
responded to. Taking the wiki public should probably be something we
discuss at the meeting, so I am planning on having it ready to go live the
next day, June 8.

Taking the wiki public means the archives of this list server are also
ready to go public. Also note that on the meeting agenda I would like us to
discuss which of our growing list of venues we should focus on:
http://www.metagovernment.org/wiki/Startup/Meetings/1/Agenda

> I prefer the creative commons one. Not because it is substantively
> different from GNU (which wikipedia use) but because the whole raison
> d'etre of the CC project is to make maintain a set of the licences for
> exactly this sort of purpose. They have a very short and snappy "human
> readable" version http://creativecommons.org/licenses/by/3.0/ which links
> thru to the in-depth legalese version. All of this is similar to GNU, I
> just think it's been done a little slicker. It is intended towards
> "cultural projects" such as books, wikis and blogs rather than software
> (perhapd GNU is better at software? - although I've heard some debates
> around backwards compativility of licences and other niggles with GNU).
It
> is also a major international movement and a project we can learn from,
> support, and perhaps down the line get some support from.
