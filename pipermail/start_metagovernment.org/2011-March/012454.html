I apologize for my low participation recently. I think this question  
was left in my court, because David asked me to suggest an alternate  
time/date (since in my time zone 1900 GMT is the middle of the day,  
and always conflicts for me on weekdays).

I started putting together a Doodle calendar to brainstorm some times  
that might work for everyone, but difficulties with the limited  
interface were amplified by the fact that the U.S. is entering  
Daylight Saving Time this weekend.
http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Daylight_saving_time_around_the_world

So instead, I'll just try to work it out here. Again, I don't want to  
stand in the way; so if Monday at 1900 gmt works for others, I say you  
try it, at least once. I do think a moving date and/or time might (as  
opposed to a regularly scheduled one) might work better for  
accommodating a global audience.

For me, weekends are best... the ideal times in my time zone, once DST  
starts, is between about 1400 and 2000 GMT.

On weekdays, I would only be available evenings (most times, anyway),  
which gets to be pretty late in Europe. The earliest I'd be available  
most days would be like 2200.
