> I admit, the 100% rule has beauty to it! Even though it can lead to

> homepage and favor a new version instead ... quite the opposite of
> consensus for the current version.  I'd like to know what the others

I think it *did* work in the past.  If it fails in future, we could
revisit the issue then.  On the other hand, the home page redesign is
currently suffering from low participation, and I think it was your
original idea, Thomas, that the decision mechanism could help here.
This is a priority for me, as well.

Ed, would you consider dropping the 21 days down to 7?  Just for
starters?  If people saw that action was imminent, or in fact taking
place, then I bet they'd be more inclined to participate.

In any case, I feel we ought to be voting for Ed on this particular
issue (G/p/m00).  We can't act legitimately on the home page, so long
as he opposes us (quite rightly) in this particular manner.
