End candidates currently see no footprints:
http://metagovernment.org/wiki/User:Conseo/draft

I have an idea: Suppose a candidate could see the footprints of his
immediate voters, except they're flashed in rapid succession like the
frames in a film projector.  For candidate M, the frames would be:

   J, K, L, J, K, L, J, K, L...


                      (J)  (K)  (L)
                        \ 1 | 1 /
                         \  |  / 1       (A)   (B)
                (P)  (O)  \ | /           | 1  /
       (R)        \ 1 |    \|/            |   / 1
         \ 1       \  | 1  (M)            |  /
          \         \ |     |             | /  (E)  (F)
           \         \|     | 4           |/    | 1 /
        1   \        (Q)    |    (I)     (C)    |  / 1
    (S)-----(T)        \ 3  |    /        |     | /
              \ 3       \   |   / 1       | 3   |/
               \         \  |  /          |    (H)-----(G)
                \         \ | /     (D)   |    /     1
     1       2   \         \|/        \ 1 |   /
 (U)-----(V)-----(W)       (N)         \  |  / 4
                   \ 6     /            \ | /
                    \     / 9            \|/
                     \   /               (Y)
                      \ /           
                      (X)                 8


Call that a "voter flick".  Suppose M could also see a "co-voter
flick":

   Q, I, Q, I, Q, I...

And we (his voters) could see a "candidate flick":

   M, N, X, M, N, X, M, N, X...

You may stop your flick at any point and "turn" it onto a new axis:
from one of your candidates, for example, across to *his* co-voters;
or from one of the co-voters up to *his* voters; and so on.  So you
may traverse the entire tree through the "lense" of your draft.  Or,
more accurately, the entire tree would pass, film-like, through the
lense of the draft.
