I've added CrowdVote to the wiki. Please update it when you can, since  
my understanding of it is still limited:
http://www.metagovernment.org/wiki/Active_projects
http://www.metagovernment.org/wiki/CrowdVote

Concerning your idea of distributed service and Michael's question  
about API, the point is the ability for it to be integrated into the  
Crossforum Theater, which is a way of aggregating discourse among our  
various projects into a unified view. So if a debate starts in one  
medium, it isn't stuck there... it can migrate and/or spread to  
whatever tools serve it best. See http://www.metagovernment.org/wiki/Crossforum 
  for an introduction.

Since you talk about CrowdVote being distributed, I thought this may  
be of particular interest, since the Crossforum is a sort of meta- 
distribution. Enabling the Crossforum developers to tie into CrowdVote  
should help to make both stronger.

On a separate note, apart from distributing the implementation of your  
software, you can also look at the idea of distributing the  
administration. The idea is entirely theoretical at the moment, but  
better to think about it sooner rather than later:
http://www.metagovernment.org/wiki/Distributed_Administration_Network
