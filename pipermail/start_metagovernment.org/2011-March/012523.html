On Mar 25, 2011, at 4:58 AM, Thomas von der Elbe wrote:

>>   The home page is to be modified by consensus whenever the proposed
>>   modification has been agreed by a plurality of members, and the
>>   plurality holds for a week.
>

> http://metagovernment.org/wiki/User:ThomasvonderElbe/draft_rules
>
> Is anybody here, who can not agree to this?

To date, the rule for changes to the home page has been consent and  
consensus: i.e., no active dissent.

If we were to change it to the above, we would have to clearly define  
plurality. Do you simply mean more for one than for any other, even if  
that one has less than 50%? That is a radical departure from zero- 
dissent, and extremely liberal for such a small community as ours.

Also, I think one week is too short for a community like ours. We  
regularly have members who cannot participate for a whole week.

So here's an entry-point-question I still don't get with Votorola. I  
appear to disagree with the above draft. I can see it here:
http://metagovernment.org/wiki/User:ThomasvonderElbe/draft_rules
Now how do I go from there to entering my own draft as a competing  
proposal?
