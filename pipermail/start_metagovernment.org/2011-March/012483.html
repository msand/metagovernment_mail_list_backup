Welcome Jason,

I have a question that falls in line with Thomas's.

> ThomasvonderElbe at gmx.de wrote:
> > I find your idea very fascinating and I like the sense of openness
> > I get from it, because you dont try to pull the users into your
> > app more than necesarry but instead go to where they are already
> > are, i.e. in blogs, twitter, ... . But I'm not sure, if I got the
> > picture right already. Is it like that I can follow the votestream
> > of A who follows the votestream of B who follows C ... D ... F
> > ... ?

Jason Blum wrote:
> Yes, you can put your Vote on "auto-pilot" by setting it to follow any
> number of VoteSources that contains VoteURLs which correspond to votes in
> your docket.
> 
> How you and everyone else in your docket vote is transparently auditable -
> but who your vote follows is your own business.  You might be following
> someone who's following someone who's following you, in which case no vote
> can be calculated for any of you.

There could be something unique about your idea.  I wonder if this is
an accurate description of how it would compare with traditional
voting.  In your own terms:

 (a) Instead of a vote source choosing a community representative, the
     community representative chooses her own vote sources.  Instead
     of the community representative further choosing (as a delegate)
     a community representative of her own, the vote sources may
     further choose votes source of their own.

Translated into the language Thomas and I are more familiar with,
which is closer to tradition, this would become:

 (b) Instead of a voter choosing a candidate, the candidate chooses
     her own voters.  Instead of the candidate further choosing (as a
     delegate) a candidate of her own, the voters may further choose
     voters of their own.

That seems to be the correct translation.  But do I have (a) correct
in the first place?
