On Wed, Mar 16, 2011 at 5:03 AM, Thomas von der Elbe <
ThomasvonderElbe at gmx.de> wrote:

>  Hello Jason,
>
> Welcome to the list!
>
> I find your idea very fascinating and I like the sense of openness I get
> from it, because you dont try to pull the users into your app more than
> necesarry but instead go to where they are already are, i.e. in blogs,
> twitter, ... . But I'm not sure, if I got the picture right already. Is it
> like that I can follow the votestream of A who follows the votestream of B
> who follows C ... D ... F ... ?
>
>
Yes, you can put your Vote on "auto-pilot" by setting it to follow any
number of VoteSources that contains VoteURLs which correspond to votes in
your docket.

How you and everyone else in your docket vote is transparently auditable -
but who your vote follows is your own business.  You might be following
someone who's following someone who's following you, in which case no vote
can be calculated for any of you.


> One thing, that feels limiting is the fact, that I should only be able to
> vote yes/no on given proposals.
>

No, you can have multiple choices on votes.  For instance, VoteURL's for
what color to paint the community room might be:
http://CrowdVote.org/H83P7-1 = "red"
http://CrowdVote.org/H83P7-2 = "yellow"
http://CrowdVote.org/H83P7-3 = "blue"
etc.

Or even a range, for instance in how much to raise the annual fee:
http://CrowdVote.org/YE38S-5 = "$5"
http://CrowdVote.org/YE38S-10 = "$10"
http://CrowdVote.org/YE38S-15 = "$15"

But that can be changed easily. This is the field we have focused most of
> our work on so far: to enable the voter to make own proposals or improve
> given ones. The first problem that comes to mind here is scalability, right?
> If you allow this, you'll soon have thousands of cloned proposals! But this
> is solved. Now I would be interested in looking, if there are possiblities
> to combine both.
>
>
CrowdVote still presumes an elected official to manage the docket - that is,
to "set the agenda."  Maybe a future version could support distributing this
responsibility among constituents, but for now I just want to get a free
market of representation up and running.


> What is your opinion on this draft of an open architecture? Your concept
> would (beside others) mainly adress the personal autocasting, right? :
> http://u.zelea.com/w/User:Mike-ZeleaCom/Opening_an_architecture
>

I'll have to spend more time with this - very interesting stuff.  Yes, I
guess CrowdVote would provide the personal autocasting, but not through an
elaborate prediction engine, but merely through social media.

Thanks again for your thoughts!
