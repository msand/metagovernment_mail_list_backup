As longer-term subscribers to this listsev know, I believe it would be good if candidates for elective office were to document their campaign platforms on the Web in open, machine-readable format, like Strategy Markup Language (StratML) -- as U.S. federal agencies will now be required to do with their plans and reports under section 10 <http://xml.gov/stratml/references/PL111-532StratML.htm#SEC10>  of the GPRA Modernization Act.

 

I also believe it would be beneficial to focus on contributions (inputs and processing) toward the realization of shared objectives to be accomplished by the “voters” themselves, rather than merely delegating authority without resources to others (which creates scapegoats while absolving the rest of us from responsibilities that only we can assume).

 

While the notion of one dollar/one vote may be unpalatable for many purposes, the practice of casting votes without providing resources is at best ineffective, if not counterproductive in the long run.  The results are becoming more and more apparent at all levels of government.  What is required is not more of the same (paving the proverbial cow path) but something different.

 

Toward that end, I hope the StratML standard (ANSI/AIIM 21:2009) can play a useful role.  Something very much like it is required to mature the social networking services for business-quality purposes, including the business of governance.

 

Owen
