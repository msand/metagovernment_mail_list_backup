Jason, Thomas and Ed,

Thomas von der Elbe wrote:
> I find your idea very fascinating and I like the sense of openness I
> get from it, because you dont try to pull the users into your app
> more than necesarry but instead go to where they are already are,
> i.e. in blogs, twitter, ...

I had the same impression.  It's potentially a very open and very
extended voting UI.  It brought to mind Mark Murphy's POSH
interface. [1] I recall, too, that it was our discussions with Murphy
here in the list that eventually inspired us (Thomas being a
contributor by then) to turn Votorola's own UI in a radically open
direction.  Thomas's invention of vote mirroring has been a pillar of
that openness on the voting side. [2]

Jason, will your Docket and VoteSource tables have a public Web API?
When your code is running, we could probably teach our vote-server to
do the jobs of Queuer, Scraper and Calculator, and also to publish its
own votes in a compatible Docket/VoteSource protocol. [3] I don't know
what you may think of that proposal, but I offer it.

I also don't know what you may think of Ed's suggestion, but I share
his openness to critique.  I hope you find time to read up on our
efforts and give us your feedback, most especially on the technical
architecture (since I'm a technical guy).  How do our two approaches
differ?  What is your critical appraisal of the difference?
