Interesting.  Not sure I completely follow.  My first reaction is that this
is a "term" of the Docket Owner's representation of his community, and as
such, is strictly between him and his community - i.e. a matter for him to
sell them on in the first place during his campaign.

But I agree CrowdVote should offer him features to toggle on or off to
support those terms.  And perhaps one could be a way to diminish the
influence of votes inherited from a distance?  If I am understanding you
correctly?

On the other hand I think "Crowds" like the Twitter-sphere or the New York
Times Bestseller list all somehow still provide a consensus...  In any free
market, there's always somehow a "best-seller."  That's why we have this
expression that you can vote with your dollar.  CrowdVote just wants to give
you a wallet-full of votes, so that you can also vote with your vote in a
free market of representation.

Heh I am sure my terminology is all over the place.  I have no formal
training in anything like this so I really appreciate the suggestions and
clarifications.
