Ed Pastore wrote:
> Would this work?
> 
> Three friends are ordering from a Chinese restaurant. Akim wants 
> something vegetarian and spicy; Belinda does not care about spiciness, 
> but wants something with beef; Cari is not particular about ingredients 
> but wants something that is not spicy. While they could order more, the 
> minimum number of dishes they could order would be two: a spicy 
> vegetable dish and a mild beef dish.
--
That states a problem and an adequate result under parameters beneficial 
to people like Akim who will learn to insist on accepting nothing other 
than their first choice. In any case, the challenge, as in high school 
math, is to "Show your work."

Perhaps the summary statement can include an explicit strategy for:

1) formally nominating specific menu items;
2) letting participants announce their preferences;
3) tabulating and presenting results, and;
4) determining who may nominate and/or vote.

The third point is clearly the most important for getting the kernel of 
the idea across.
