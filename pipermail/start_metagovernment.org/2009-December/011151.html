I had brought this up with Pietro previously, off-list. The best  
explanation I can provide for it is that (roughly) Vilfredo asks  
people to vote for *sufficient* answers (ie, all answers they agree  
with), but then the elimination process acts as if people thought they  
were *necessary* answers (ie, the best answers).

This is a common distinction made in formal logic. See:
http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Necessary_and_sufficient_condition

The only solution I can come up with would be to have two kinds of  
votes, for "acceptable" and "preferred." But I believe that has  
drastic effects on calculating the Pareto front. And it complicates  
the voting process even further, making it more difficult for the  
beginner.

But I still think there is a way to make it work. Maybe we should ask  
Vilfredo? :)
