> Let me answer those questions according to the Vilfredo Project.
>
> The aim of the Vilfredo Project was not to make a coll website, but  
> to give an example of a different form of decision making;
> traditional decision making in democratic societies is rooted in  
> Voting theory (add 5 minutes of crash course on voting theory).
> All of voting theory is based on the idea of answering a closed  
> question by counting how many people support the various options.
> And then there are various ways of voting and of counting.
> Vilfredo rejects the assumtpion that we already know what are the  
> options and instead proposes a search algorithm that uses human  
> beings to look for the possible alternatives.
> The two things we are doing that are new are:
> a) using a -human- genetic algorithm to find the possible alternatives
> (instead of simply having the people in charge decide them)
> b) Comparing the sets of people voting for the various solutions,  
> and taking the pareto front of the solutions thus generating a set  
> that represents all the users
> (instead of simply summing up the votes)
>
> We can do (b) because we don't have a fixed number of solutions that  
> must pass from one generation to the other.
>
> The results that we found where:
>  a)   people agree more on important things: we tend to agree on  
> values while we tend to disagree on the  weight we give to the values
>  b)   there ARE solutions out there; most question reach an answer  
> fairly soon (in less than 20 generations, often 5 to 7)
>  c) the solutions are usually more complex
>
> problems we did find was that people get bored quickly
>
> while problems we did not find: most people would have predicted  
> that ignorance of the participants was going to be a problem, but in  
> fact we never found that to be a big problem. AT the worse some  
> solutions had to spell out why they were presented, and why other  
> solutions were not really viable. But the fact is that the solutions  
> from the experts will never be destroyed by the solution from the  
> non expert because the expert will not vote for them, and then both  
> will make it to the pareto front.
>
>
