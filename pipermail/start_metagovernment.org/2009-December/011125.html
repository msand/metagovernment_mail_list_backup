There are several styles of open-ended questions in sports, like 'how
to market football/soccer in the US'

or, how to reduce a baseball team's dependence on a single player --
the pitcher (perhaps require 4 outs per inning instead of 3 ?? )

or, how to promote competition in a league including teams with
unlimited resources (salary caps, ?? )

Basically, take any controversial idea in sports, determine which
problem it is trying to solve, and rephrase as a question 'how to
solve this problem'? (implicitly, better than said controversial
notion)

An example is to consider the instant-replays in tennis (player
challenges) -- what problem is this trying to solve ? that's an
open-ended question where 'Do you approve of player challenges in
tennis' is not.

> much more involvement than esoteric questions.
