The President's open government directive is now available in StratML format
at http://xml.gov/stratml/index.htm#FedAgencies or, more specifically,
http://xml.gov/stratml/OGD.xml

Objective 1.f, Open Government Input and Feedback, offers a tremendous
opportunity for Metagov applications.

Needless to say, as implied by objective 1.b, Open Formats, the agency open
gov plans required by objective 3.a, Open Government Plans, should be
published in StratML format.

FYI, the U.S. federal government today released its anticipated Open  
Government Initiative.

See
http://www.cnewmark.com/2009/12/making-govt-work-a-huge-step.html
for quick links and
http://www.whitehouse.gov/open
for the news "from the horse's mouth."

Note that this is an initiative of the Executive branch of the US  
government; a nice attempt to open up data and even decision-making in  
departmental institutions. It does not however affect the Legislature,  
where citizens are still entirely dependent on their representatives.  
Nor does it have any direct impact on other levels of American  
government.
