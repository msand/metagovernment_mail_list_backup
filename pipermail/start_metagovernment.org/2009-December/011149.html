If we introduce categorization, for which different voting rules
apply, I would suggest that it be on the level of the proposals rather
than the articles. The logic being, that if you make a spelling
correction, it doesn't matter in what kind of article it is made.
Likewise substantive changes that many people would like to weigh in
on, can be in informative articles, not just legislative.

I had an idea like this, but haven't thought it through yet. The idea
was to have different levels for a proposal and anybody can escalate
to a higher level. The higher the level, the more stringent the voting
requirements. The lowest level would then be spelling corrections and
it would require a very limited amount of votes, just to ensure that
the level is appropriate and somebody isn't trying to sneak something
in through a lower level.
