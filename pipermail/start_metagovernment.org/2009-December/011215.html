Hello all,

I think collective funding can be a great thing, especially if we let the
public fund the social projects.This may be a way to get large sum of money
for funding projects for the public.

But it has its problems to. When large sums of money are passed through
small group, it may cause corruption and unjust decision .

To avoid these problems, I think there should be some measures that must be
taken.

   1. highly transparent decision process.
   2. Check and balances between several groups of decision makers.
   3. laying a set of rules for a just money distribution.

> For my 2 cents, I have another proposal.  I don't know if this is
> counter or complementary to OpenKollab's.  It comes out of private
> discussions held earlier this month:
>
>  1.  Demonstrate an open architecture with:
>        a) Input of two or more cooperating projects; and
>        b) Output of interoperable tools.

>
>  2.  On the basis of that demonstration, apply for funding.
>
>  3.  . . . continue working together on the common parts.
