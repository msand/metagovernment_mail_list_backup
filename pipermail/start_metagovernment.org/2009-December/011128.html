It's not an academic conference, so there are no papers. In essence,  
the presentation is the document. It seems to me like it is basically  
a showcase of emerging trends in the nebulous concept of "government  
2.0." If it's like the agenda from 2009, I would guess most of the  
presentations will be about websites which open up government data and/ 
or mash up government data with other things such as Google maps;  
initiatives for local governments to share data with upstream  
governments; governments providing self-service tools to citizens, and  
so forth.
