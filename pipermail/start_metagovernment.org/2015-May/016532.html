I don't think there is a way around programming.
Capitalists (Investors, Business folks, Bankers, Entrepreneurs, Big
Shareholders) ... they all usually are not programmers.
There are well paid corporate programmers, but the majority of programmers
share the same fate and are paid little money compared to the skills they
are/were able to learn.

So, if people on large scale, know how to program, they can build an
alternative future, because practically they share the same fate.
If people do not go into programming, then the elite and their paid
programmers will build big data analysis farms, machine learning and lots
of algorithms to build a future that belongs to them. If people go
massively into programming, they can build an alternative.

I believe its vital that mainstream people go into programming, otherwise I
do not see a bright future.
My girlfriend studied social sciences and slavic languages and she is in
her mid thirtees and became a programmer just 2 years ago and already
managed to built http://www.zweidesign.co all by herself (a plattform for
construction workers to outsource work to each other), so its definetly
possible for everyone to become a programmer.
