Here our designs split a bit and the question of free speech comes up. I
think that there should be complete transparency in all communications and
that punishable actions should be taken to these trolls in the form of time
penalties before they're able to post again. Your concept would allow
trolls to run rampant but allow people to ignore them by filtering them
out, which I think most people would choose to do.


I think it dangerous to hid away and marginalize an entire group from
having their opinion heard and leaving it up to past flagging to determine
their legitimacy. Again at some point one of these trolls might reform or
actually have something valuable to say. I don't feel it's right to hid
what they say because a group of people have deemed it irrelevant. I
instead argue that every post should remain public and viewable but that
individuals be given a time out for abuse of the posting code. One by one
we treat these trolls like criminals and give them a trail where the
evidence is produced and a verdict is reached by an outside jury,
everything remains transparent.


We can draw from the shouting fire in a crowded theater analogy or crying
wolf. You prefer to automatically block these people from ever being heard
except by others like them (which might only serve to encourage them
further), I say we give them the benefit of the doubt and punish them
later.

Posts that come later in my system have the effect of bumping all its
parents posts to the top of their feed lists temporarily, they all take on
the vitality of the brand new post but as that post grows older and starts
to lose overall score the parent posts revert back to their score once it's
overtaken. Because the brand new post has a lesser chance of receiving as
many props it loses score at a much quicker rate than it's more visible
parent posts.

They're only making 6 figures because there's a market for it. Removing
much of the personal financial gian from politics lowers these wages
dramatically. I think there would be interest in getting paid for activity
that you had expertise in. Like karma for Reddit, there's always somebody
stepping in and sharing their knowledge. Here you're helping run the world,
and getting money for it, it's even more incentive to contribute.
Competition breeds innovation. If there's money to be made in finding the
greatest solution to a problem, I believe innovation will occur and we'll
all benefit from it.
