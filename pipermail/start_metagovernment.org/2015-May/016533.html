> If people do not go into programming, then the elite and their paid
> programmers will build big data analysis farms, machine learning and
> lots of algorithms to build a future that belongs to them. If people
> go massively into programming, they can build an alternative.

I would say they should go into organizing themselves. Programming is
just one way. There are advantages and some disadvantages. I hope you
will add some.

+ large scale

- more suitable for simple commands
- limited flow of information
- limited trust
- higher dependence on network technology that is in hands of elites

> 
> I believe its vital that mainstream people go into programming,
> otherwise I do not see a bright future.

There is some need for programming to create the large scale. But there
should be also other "programming" - learning people how to organize
themselves.
As I hate when somebody compares people to computers, for a hardcore
programmer like you it might be useful :) We may need to develop and
update some social software into people. Replace fear by hope, guilt by
confidence, competition by collaboration.... without that even if
everybody could program, we will be slaves.

What do you think ?
