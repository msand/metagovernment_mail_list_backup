Hi Ronald,

Thanks for translating this.

My own analysis tells me that opinion forming (5.5.1 in your document)
will break up the political parties (rationalize them) into pieces
that are largely unrecognizeable as parties.  Maybe the pieces closest
to traditional parties will be these structures (left and right):
http://zelea.com/w/Stuff:Votorola/p/power_structuring#SN
But these are power structures divorced of all normative substance,
such as policy.  Inside they are pure executive power, pure politics.

I think the norms will be formed outside of these "parties", not
inside.  The structures that produce the norms will look more like
this: http://zelea.com/w/Stuff:Votorola/p/legislative_action
Those two structures (left and right) might be "parties" too, but they
are not tied to the structures in the previous figure.  This is what I
mean by "breaking up", and "rationalizing".  The general purpose
political organization that you describe in your document (the party)
no longer exists here.

This is just my own analysis of what opinion forming (or "primary
voting") will do to the parties.

Mike
