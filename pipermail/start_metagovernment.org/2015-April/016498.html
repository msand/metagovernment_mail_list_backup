We watched Commune a couple of days ago and I've also done some
followup research. Here's my take on it:

1) BBR still going, but population has dropped below 20 several times
in recent years. And this is for a place which owns the property so
there's no rent: According to the research, owning the land is by far
the single most important factor in predicting community longevity.

2) The population turns over completely about every two years (i.e.,
people show up with the fantasy of living communally, last a year or
so during which they discover it sucks, then they bail).

3) There is no capital acquired and so no improvements are possible.
The buildings look like they did when they bought the place 40 years
ago (and probably pretty much like they did when they were built in
the late 1800s). My guess is that this is a major reason why people
leave: Avoiding resource scarcity and physical discomfort are two of
the strongest drives in all animals (and I'm sure especially humans
who have experienced life without these things).

4) Community childrearing was a major fail (as it was for the
Kibbutzim), as was the attempt to reject pair bonding. These proposals
are just too far from our Clinations as every experiment in this area
has clearly shown.

Another key point for me is that these are self-selected samples: If
you can't make it work with them, how can you possibly hope to run a
whole society using these rules? The only wiggle room I see is the
same argument I use against SDAP leadership (in that case, the
personality characteristics that make people seek power are highly
correlated with characteristics (particularly prejudice and
corruptibility) that make them unfit to wield it). The argument for a
consent/consensus based government would therefore be that
Neurotypicals would be able to do it better than the self-selected
groups that start and join these communities. I don't know of any
research that shows that this is possible, and of course it ignores
the entire issue of SDAPs and their ability (indeed preference) to
game any consent/consensus based decisionmaking system.
