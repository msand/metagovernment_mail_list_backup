Is there a branch of research, that analyses systems, where "votes" are
given for/against certain proposals or people and then are gone until
regained?

STATUS QUO:
Traditionally, people receive "one vote" per "voting period" (usually
years), then they spend that vote and lose it, so they are left with zero
votes.
In the next period, they get another vote.

ALTERNATIVES:
People get a certain amount of "votes" per "voting period" and can vote on
proposals and people after which their vote is gone.
They regain new "votes" with the rate X, which can be dynamically adapated.
If others, for some reason, "vote" for a person A, that person get's "new
votes" additionally to those she/he gets at rate X by default.

Does that make sense?
