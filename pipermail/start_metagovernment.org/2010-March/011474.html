Thanks Ed, thanks Owen for the suggestions.
I tend to agree that it is often possible to avoid voting but just make a
change and then see who joins in.

In this case I think we should look for a simpler relation.
Just something like: we list the active project, they put the metagovernment
logo (with a link on it) in the webpage.
And an acknowledgment that we are going in a similar direction, that the
project is trying to solve ... problem that fits in the general direction of
... .
This should be the basic basic membership. There might be other forms of
membership, but I would suggest to have a simple and wide one first.

The fact of asking to be put as an acknowledgment in the paper normally goes
in the opposite direction of the money flow. If money flows from A to B
generally B acknowledges A. It's hard to pull off that someone sends you
money, *and* acknowledges you. But it can be tried :-).

> For example:
>
> Active Projects:
> - display an affiliation blurb or logo on their websites
> - commit to a certain level of participation on the list, wiki, etc.

> - pay a small amount of monthly or annual dues (sounds controversial)
>
> Metagovernment:
> - actively promotes all projects (and the general principles of
> collaborative governance) on the web, at conferences, etc.

> - encourages each of its individual members to commit some time to at least
> one active project
> - coordinates the Standardization project
> - seeks out additional forms of cooperation between some or all projects;
> actively promotes synergy when possible
> - works toward hosting a conference to promote collaborative governance and
