I hesitated at the time of writing "contract", it's a strong word that
normally implies the existence of an upper organism to which the parties
that hold that contract accept as arbitrator. What I intended is soft use of
the word, where we simply write down "active project does this and
Metagovernment does that" and if the pact is respected, then we can say that
both projects are working together. Of course, this contract is not
enforceable against law. Wikipedia says:

"According to legal scholar Sir John William Salmond, a contract is "an
agreement creating and defining the obligations between two or more
parties"."

Maybe that's the word... *agreement*. (See
http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gentlemen%27s_agreement.) The word "plan"
didn't convince me.
