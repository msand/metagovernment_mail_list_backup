On Feb 22, 2010, at 3:45 AM, Matías Battocchia wrote:

> Answering this question may lead us to more answers, for example, to
> the inquietudes 'What really does Metagovernment?' and 'What we have
> to do to increase participation?'; which in turn would help us to
> define objectives and goals (thanks Owen for pushing this out) that
> would make possible the accomplishment of our vision. I transcribe
> here what you can read in Wikipedia about umbrellas:
>
> "An umbrella organization is an association of (often related,
> industry-specific) institutions, who work together formally to
> coordinate activities or pool resources. In business, political, or
> other environments, one group, the umbrella organization, provides
> resources and often an identity to the smaller organizations.
> Sometimes in this kind of arrangement, the umbrella organization is to
> some degree responsible for the groups under its care. Compare to
> franchises and subsidiaries. There may be many reasons for
> establishing/joining an umbrella organization;
>
> the ability to carry out activities which could not be accomplished
> alone, due to:
> * economies of scale,
> * a better pool of experts and experience,
> * shared apprenticeship and exchange of know-how;
>
> a sense of community and support that makes people and corporations
> derive utility from multilateral and shared activities:
> * an increased brand/public awareness,
> * legality of pursued action(s)."
>
> Let's work this out analyzing it by parts.
>
> "An umbrella organization is an association", then we should have a
> contract with active projects, specifying roles and obligations, this
> way we know when the association starts and ends.
>
> "who work together formally to coordinate activities or pool
> resources", Metagovernment has the kind of activities that could be
> coordinated, like the Conference or the Standardization. Obligations:
> we do the coordination, then the active projects follow the plans
> (top-down); active projects tell us what to coordinate (down-top).
> Pooling resources... this is harder; what can active projects actually
> throw in a common pool?
> * Work force. Difficult, programmers have enough with its own  
> projects.
> * Knowledge. Maybe. Not about programming, there are better resources
> elsewhere. About voting systems, could be.
> * Money. Even worse. As an individual, I feel I should donate; what
> about a $10 monthly fee, for those members who want to do it? Let's
> open the road for that, using a PayPal account. (Ed's?)
>
> "provides resources and often an identity to the smaller
> organizations", Resources, mmm, we don't have much to offer, the wiki
> and some space in the server. We could take this further, if we had a
> dedicated server or a virtual private server ($700 yearly) to expand
> our services. Obligations: provide free full hosting to active
> projects, while they use this service exclusively for developing
> collaborative governance projects. (Identity? Well, in the future.
> Metagovernment isn't famous... yet, and identity happens when others
> recognize you.)
>
> "the umbrella organization is to some degree responsible for the
> groups under its care", sometimes, not this case.
>
> "the ability to carry out activities which could not be accomplished
> alone", can you add one more activity?
>
> "a sense of community and support that makes people and corporations
> derive utility from multilateral and shared activities", ...public
> awareness... Obligation: Metagovernment maintains a list of active
> projects in the wiki's main page, active projects add a little banner
> of Metagovernment on their sites. If we go to a conference, we present
> all active projects, if an active project goes to a conference, it
> introduces Metagovernment.
>
> I invite you to enhance to list of things that Metagovernment can do
> for its active projects, and vice versa.
