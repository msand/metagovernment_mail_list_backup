Sorry to have been out of touch lately.  My wife and I were on a 24-day tour
of New Zealand and Australia.  We're still recovering from jet lag.

In general, I agree with what Matías is proposing.  One difference is that I
would not use the word "contract" since it implies authority we do not have
(and I suspect that most of us do not desire).
http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Contract  Instead, I'd use the word "plan".
http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Plan 

I do not believe we need to establish any large objectives.  Indeed, if we
were to try to do so, I'm pretty sure we'd fail.  However, I do believe we
should establish some relatively small objectives against which progress (or
lack thereof) can be measured.  If we do that, the results will speak for
themselves (and we can each decide for ourselves whether they are worth the
continued investment of our time, effort, and perhaps money).

At this point, it is unclear whether we actually have any shared objectives
or not.  For example, I have not seen any evidence that any of the
subscribers to the listserv share my objective of facilitating feedback on
goals and objectives documented on the Web in StratML format (which is the
reason I subscribed in the first place).

To the degree that any number of the subscribers (however small) may agree
upon any particular objective(s), my offer to document it (them) in StratML
format still stands.

Since AIIM's StratML Committee is now specifying Part 2 of the StratML
standard, addressing the elements of performance plans and reports, I would
welcome the opportunity to use Metagov's plan as a pilot test/use case for
it.  Hopefully, it will help organizations like Metagov become more
effective and, in turn, feedback from folks subscribing to organizations
like Metagov will help not only to make the StratML standard better but,
ultimately, to improve the planning and performance processes themselves --
for all organizations worldwide.

Owen
