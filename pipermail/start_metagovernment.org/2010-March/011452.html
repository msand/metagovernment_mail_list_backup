I am a little hesitant to change the name yet again, but I do agree  
that "collaborative governance" is somewhat too broad. And it is too  
many syllables. :) Also, I just recently told Richard Stallman that we  
finally moved away from the word "open," much to his delight.

But so far, I do tend to prefer "open governance" to other things  
proposed. If anyone wants to see the history of the Vilfredo  
discussion, it is here:
http://vilfredo.org/viewhistoryofquestion.php?q=53
Strange that "open governance" wasn't even proposed there...

Does anyone have a better idea? Feel free to either present it here,  
or on Vilfredo:
http://vilfredo.org/viewquestion.php?q=53


On Mar 7, 2010, at 1:19 AM, Matías Battocchia wrote:

> Hi. I'm thinking in making the rebrand reality next week in the  
> wiki, if nobody disagree during the this 7-day term: from  
> "collaborative governance" to "open governance".
>
>
> 2010/2/21 Matías Battocchia <battox at gmail.com>
> I would to like to call the attention for the name of what we're
> promoting. It happens that *collaborative governance* is "a process
> and a form of governance in which participants (parties, agencies,
> stakeholders) representing different interests are collectively
> empowered to make a policy decision or make recommendations to a final
> decision-maker who will not substantially change consensus
> recommendations from the group". This comes from Wikipedia, but it
> also appears that this meaning is present in other places of the
> Internet as well. (Google it.)
>
> To make things worst, open source governance has *considerably* weight
> in the Internet; one can be tempted to return to that previous name,
> although it's an unfriendly one. Anyway, I feel that we can move
> forward and try to pick a better name. I see two ways we could follow,
> if the group agree:
>
> a) To do some bibliographical research among science fiction,
> political science (and the combined genre 'political science fiction')
> books, looking for the first term coined. Recently, while reading, the
> word *computocracy* assaulted me. Should we revive it? There is even a
> 1985 book by Archie J. Bahm with that name.
>
> It seems to me that a term lasting with '-cracy' is a great choice:
> technical and elegant.
>
> b) To hear what Internet has to say about this subject, analyzing
> search results. People use several synonyms for open source
> governance, like democracy 2.0, open politics, open governance,
> collaborative democracy, participatory democracy, and so on. The 2.0
> suffix won't help, but the word 'open' tends to appear a lot, also
> 'democracy'. (For some reason open democracy isn't suitable.)
>
> I like open governance the most, because is simpler than open source
> governance, yet it can be easily related to it. We could convince
> Wikipedia editors to point open source governance to open governance,
> not otherwise, as actually occurs.
