Owen Ambur wrote:
> Thomas, here's part of the mission statement of OASIS's Election and Voter
> Services Technical Committee:
> "Develop a standard for the structured interchange among hardware, software,
> and service providers who engage in any aspect of providing election or
> voter services to public or private organizations..."
> 
> They also say: "The messages that form EML are intended for transfer between
> systems."
> http://docs.oasis-open.org/election/eml/v6.0/cd01/EML-Specification-v6.0.pdf

That's true.  But I couldn't find anything useful in EML.  They're
concerned with traditional elections, mostly.  They already know what
the top-side of a traditional democracy looks like (we all do, it's
the status quo) so they took a bottom-up, data-centric approach.

http://u.zelea.com/wiki/User:Mike-ZeleaCom/Opening_an_architecture#Other_approaches

OPOL and xDebate are more forward looking, but they take a similar
data-centric approach, and again I couldn't use them for much.  IMHO
it's premature to standardize the data, when we don't know what the
applications on top look like.

So our own approach is to prototype mostly from the top down.  We try
to hack the whole thing into a roughly workable shape - and make it
actually work - before firming up the nitty-gritty details.
