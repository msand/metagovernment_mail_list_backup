Hi all,



the system of "liquid democracy" or, proxy voting, is a system that has a proven reputation for decades now in, even the biggest, corporations. It is slightly different from what we present in the political area but nevertheless the technique is very similar. 

This technique is called "shareholders meeting" and shareholders can appoint a "delegate" when they want and who they want. In most cases that I know of the "delegate" is a specialised law firm that represents a number of shareholders who have a specific view on things.

Of course we, as citizens, must have each an equal share in our community and we want to express our opinion when we want and not once a year.

But with those exceptions there are strong similarities in my view. 

>From there, and somewhat related with the "zombie delegation" I came to the question "will or can delegates be paid" like the delegates in the shareholders meeting? If paid the chances for "zombie delegation" will be less obvious. Now we are also paying our "delegates" (the chosen representatives). Is there any opinion about this? Maybe we can think about this along the lines of http://flattr.com/ . Every citizen receives a budget funded from taxes that he can spend, or not, for delegation (nothing else). Just a first idea. This is not compatible with my first statement that it can avoid "zombie votes" but I have to start somewhere.
