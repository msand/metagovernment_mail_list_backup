On Mon, Oct 31, 2011 at 1:43 AM, Ed Pastore <epastore at metagovernment.org> wrote:
> Hi, folks. Just to clarify: this organization process is not just about
> becoming eligible to receive grant funding. It is also to prove to ourselves
> and the world that we can do this. Are we able to create a real-world,
> formal organization that has no individual leader or leaders?
> Probably the most crucial test will be to see if we can manage money. Once
> we have a basic charter we agree on, I would like to contribute money and
> ask others to do so as well, and then make our first spending on legal fees
> and filing fees for the organization (if we have consensus, of course). If
> we can pass that test, we can begin to prove that we are capable of
> governance without empowered leaders.
> I have continued to look into the requirements for organization. At least
> here in the U.S., it looks like a board of directors is a requirement. I
> believe we can get around this requirement by promoting everyone who
> participates in the project to the board of directors.

You may need to file paperwork every time there is a change of
directors which would make this approach costly.

You should be able to set up the rules (constitution/charter) such
that the board of directors are powerless to do anything without
member consensus.

Someone else has mentioned a rotating board of directors; I think that
would be good to prevent it becoming a role with power or status.

> If we have to do
> this, it wall affect the charter below: we will have to lay out in clear
> language when someone becomes a voting member.  We can't just let anyone
> immediately become a voting member, or else a group of people could easily
> hijack the organization's funding for their own purposes.

Over at wikimedia we usually disenfrancise community members who
havent reached a certain edit-count, and we also require a limited
number of edits in the preceding twelve months.

the last requirements were: "
# have made at least 300 edits before 15 April 2011 across Wikimedia
wikis (edits on several wikis can be combined if your accounts are
unified into a global account); and
# have made at least 20 edits between 15 November 2010 and 15 May 2011.
"

https://meta.wikimedia.org/wiki/Board_elections/2011/en
