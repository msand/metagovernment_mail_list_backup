You ask,
"do we really want to start out our formal organization by violating our
core principle"
not sure if it is a good core principle if it does't work in practice.
How about this principle, "all things exist but not equally"? Both
sub-atomic particles and solar systems exist but not equally.Why introduce
a principle of equality when in reality all there are are singularities?

> On Nov 6, 2011, at 3:43 AM, Thomas von der Elbe wrote:
>
> Conseo is working on a solution for that: To each vote the voter can
> connect the amount of resources he is willing to contribute. This could be
> Dollar, working hours, ... . So everybody can decide for himself where his
> money goes.
>
>
> But what if we have a formal organization which has received grant money?
> Who gets the power to determine how that money is spent? In a sense, I
> suppose you could say that the foundation which provides the grant has made
> a resource allocation to it, but that allocation may be rather broad,
> sometimes something as general as "seed money" to help the organization get
> off the ground. The details particular allocations will still have to be
> decided by people who do not need to input further resources to get it done.
>
> On Nov 6, 2011, at 3:59 AM, Steven Gans wrote:
>
> Not sure you want to give up the role of leadership. What if we do the
> following, plan an end date to phase out the involvement of leaders? If
> leadership rotates we become a more democratic community.
>
>
> This was my original idea back before the Metagovernment page was even
> editable. Here's the first web version, from early 2007:
> http://www.metagovernment.org/archive/2007-03-20/
> (See the third-to-last paragraph.)
>
> That gets the job done, but I was quickly turned from that idea by others:
> do we really want to start out our formal organization by violating our
> core principle?
>
> Perhaps more to the point, the purpose of a board of directors, in the
> view of sanctioning governments and granting foundations, is to provide
> accountability and management so that they know the organization will act
> responsibly. Having a powerless board does not provide those guarantees.
> Instead, we need to find a way to provide those guarantees without having
> any board. That's our purpose anyway. This is our test to see if we are
> actually able to eat our own dogfood.
>
