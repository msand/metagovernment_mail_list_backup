@ Pietro

> Regarding the idea of using a Votorola like system, that is a proxy
> voting, I think this is not really acceptable.

In regard to the cluster-forming my suggestion was not to use proxy 
voting, but to simply let the users decide what cluster they want to 
belong to. Votorolas proxy-voting has this as a side-effect, but there 
might be other ways.

> First of all a problem with proxy voting is that people give support
> to someone, then they forget, and that person remains with an
> inherited power. This is very dangerous.

There are solutions for that. My favorite is from the german 
"Liquid-Democracy-e.V.". They call it "rusting of the votes", i.e. votes 
gradually melt away over time if they don't get refreshed by the voters.


> Also an important principle that I am following is a principle of Fair
> participation, which require that proposals are all evaluated only by
> the content. This means that people enter this system without knowing
> who else is there. They do not know the others.

The issue of anonymity is not bound to proxy-voting, is it? The problems 
of anonymity and its possible solutions apply to all e-dem-systems, 
don't they?

> Indeed every voter has the right to participate to both a clarity
> working group and a compromise working group. But, as you pointed out,
> it is probably unfeasible if the groups are too big, in which case
> some groups can be split, and have two groups work in parallel on the
> same cluster.
>
> Also remember that work groups usually require extra effort.
> Although everybody is welcome to participate, on average less than
> everybody will participate in the workgroups.

Ok, but since it is all about politics and sometimes about really 
important decisions you can still expect thousands of participants in a 
european-wide poll, can't you? Not to mention a global poll. And then 
the question comes up, which brought us all here in the first place: How 
can we technically support thousands of participants which try to reach 
a consensus on something? How can they all communicate with each other, 
bargain with each other, ... ? I feel this needs to be structured, since 
more than 20 people can not talk to each other directly.


> Have you seen the two taks I gave on Fair Participation and on  System
> Generated Requests for Rewriting Proposals
> http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=o8Ty-E9xlDA
> http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=m_odnkfaH5c

I saw the first. One thought of mine that I remember: I don't think it 
is technically possible to prevent someone pointing his friends to his 
proposal.

> you mention the system of groups within groups that is present in
> Votorola. I am not sure I believe that will actually work. I mean, yes
> you can have a fractal system of split groups, into smaller and smaller
> units. But then I am not sure you can so easily come back and
> aggregate proposals in the bigger groups.

Well, each group is represented by its candidate. Those candidates form 
another group which is represented by their candidate ...
So you are right, it won't be easy for a group of candidates to form a 
consensus, because they all have to get their voters to agree. But this 
is politics, isn't it? It's not easy, thats for sure.
