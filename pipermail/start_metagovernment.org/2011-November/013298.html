Thank you Paul,
indeed I was not aware that this exchange happened.

I disagree with Prof Chouard that there is anything intrinsic in
internet voting that makes it impossible to be secure.

But there are a number of issues on the table. If banks can be secure
enough, I don't see why voting cannot. With the correct mixture of
real life authentication, open source of the code, and pgp
cryptography I think even Italian voting can be made secure.

I am not following very much helios voting project. Thanks for
bringing this to my attention.

In general I consider the problem of security as an independent
problem that I do not face or tackle. I know some serious research is
going into this, and I know it WILL have to be solved before voting is
accepted at a EU level. But this is not what I am working on. And this
is also not what I will be presenting about.

I think the algorithm that permits to millions of people to
participate at once in a brainstorm session in a mathematically fair
way is more complex that just security and authentication. That is,
even if you have a perfectly secure system you would not be able to do
it, if you did not solve some specific problems. And once you have
solved those problems you can pretty much superimpose on your solution
the algorithms and requirements to make the whole process secure. I
see the two requirements as quite independent to each other (which is
rare in science, but in this case I think they are). So I concentrate
on this other problem.


Best Regards,
Pietro
