Hi Stephen,

""The question for me is what is the minimal structure/process necessary to 
*seed* an emerging global organism of creative, self-organizing 
evolution......online.""

I think we have the minimal structure to force our entry in the existing 
"representative system" with realy free delegation (liquid democracy), 
instead of forced delegation, and direct democracy that has a place for free 
association of free communities. That is our E2D organisation 
http://e2d-international.org/forum/ with the Demoex system. Some of us are 
also active here and in other groups that try to combine forces.
If you are interested just let us know. 
http://e2d-international.org/contact/

How E2D it works: (from SOL Australia http://senatoronline.org.au/ )
Here's how it works (in short) :
  1.. The E2D post-election website will provide you balanced, unbiased 
information on all the issues, bills, and policies in Parliament, Community 
Counsil, ..
  2.. You vote on the E2D website how you want our representatives to vote - 
every bill, every important issue, every time; When you want (Liquid 
democracy can be used)
  3.. E2D representatives then vote exactly how the majority directs.
Regards

Paul

>>
>> But the fact is that even when you are deciding questions that have to

>> solution that was obviously not considered by the person asking the
>> question.
>>
>> And then there is another issue, even questions that are "only a
>> number" often hide the complexity inside. For example some people
>> might be willing to pay a certain tax rate, if then the services they
>> receive are of a certain kind. In Italy many people protest for higher
>> taxes, but they often claim, the problem is to pay so high taxes and

>> prime minister, Monti, explained that we will now have to make some
>> sacrifices, but it will be presented in packages that touch everybody,
>> so no one will feel an economic maneuver is just against them.

>> Regarding the idea of using a Votorola like system, that is a proxy

>> inherited power. This is very dangerous. Look how now in
>> Metagovernment you have 5 votes that you can move as you want. Yes,
>> people can take them away from you, but some of those people are not
>> active anymore, and many simply do not care. They will vote for
>> someone because they trust him. Because he is a cool guy, or because
>> he is an actor. Sorry, I do not think this is ok.
>>

>> friends or their worse enemies. They only read what those people
>> propose, or comment. If you take away this principle you are inserting
>> in the system all sort of social dynamic that da facto make it harder
