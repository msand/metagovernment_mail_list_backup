You guys are talking about Pietro's call for (1) strict objectivity or
"fair participation" and (2) a solution to zombie voters.

(1) I guess I disagree about strict objectivity.  Habermas says the
subject and social worlds just as important to consensus making as the
objective world.  If we we de-personalize and de-socialize the medium
by focusing exclusively on the content of the text, as Pietro
suggests, then we end up with a one-sided rationality.  In consensus
making, it matters greatly who the author is, and it matters greatly
who supports the text and who opposes it.

(2) Has anyone actually implmented "rusting votes" Thomas?  It sounds
like a good solution.

I think the design of Votorola allows for a better one.  Our users can
solve the problem themselves by simply talking to the zombie voters.
They can ask, "Do you really want to continue voting for
Mr. Superstar?  Don't you realize he's changed his position since you
first supported him?"

Then they listen to the answers.  (Maybe this is an example of how
socializing the medium solves a problem of irrationality.)
