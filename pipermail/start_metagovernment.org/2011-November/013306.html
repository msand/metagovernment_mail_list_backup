Wow, this is a very interesting discussion between you :-)

I have a feeling, that right now, votorola is the best system concept we have.
On the other hand, i totally agree with pietro about:
1. principle of Fair participation
2. problem of proxy voting (that people give support to someone, then
they forget, and that person remains with an inherited power.)

Maybe votorola could evolve a bit, like:
A. Would it be possible to create proposals and discuss them, but
until you give ur voice for a proposal u can be anonymous?
(the creator of the proposal could always stay anonymous)
=> This might solve the issue with "fair participation principle"

B. What about a mechanism, that will freeze one's voice with an
accepted proposal, i mean:
The anonymous author of a proposal is not allowed to shift the votes
as soon as a proposal becomes "active law".
...votes will stick with that "active law" as long as its active.
- if it was a project that gets executed, the votes vanish after the
project has been executed.
- if it was a something like "The dignity of men is unimpeachable."
the vote stays with it until one die's?
...so that means children that are born should "replenish" this core
constitutional principle, as soon as they get the right to vote.

What do you think?
