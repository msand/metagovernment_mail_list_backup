Thanks for the replies Paul and Jonas.

There are some similarities to liquid democracy, particularly the idea of
delegation. However, liquid democracy is still geared towards voting on
issues in order to make decisions.

I'm proposing we take a step back from decision-making in order to build a
trust network that is purely and simply a resource - a measure of trust in
distinct areas that people can draw upon as they see fit.

An application of this resource in democracy would be to give the most
trusted users a prominent voice in debate in their specialist fields. But
it has a whole range of other social and economic applications: finding a
trustworthy babysitter, maintaining a high trust score as an asset on your
CV, expressing your distrust of a business that causes social harms...

So it's about empowering people, encouraging responsible actions and
nurturing the right conditions for better democracy.
