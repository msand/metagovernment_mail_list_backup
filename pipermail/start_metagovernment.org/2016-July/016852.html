> Here are the five steps of the typical direct democracy project
>
>
>    1.
>
>    The *creative phase*: the single or group proponents of the project
>    gather and start to discuss about the project. A document is written, and a
>    site is opened.
>    2.
>
>    *euphoric phase*: the group starts to involve other people, the
>    project is developed, people work hard. The atmosphere around the project
>    is euphoric .New functionalities are added every day.
>    3.
>
>    The *stagnation phase*: the project fails to get much traction. People
>    are starting to get frustrated and there is a deep gulf about the big
>    expectations and the poor results.
>    4.
>
>    The *decline phase*: the group accuses the public at large of being
>    lazy, the media of being non collaborative and biased, the governments of
>    mobbing and the other groups of sabotaging. There is little or no
>    self-criticism about why the project is not going as planned. People start
>    to leave the project.
>    5.
>
>    The *death phase*: the once big group is now very much slimmed down
>    and only a few people survive. There are no major addition and changes to
>    the project in months. The project is not being discussed upon in the media
>    anymore. In a few months or years, the project is definitely shelved for
>    good.
>
>
> At the moment, all direct democracy projects do follow this pattern, no
> one excluded.
>
> It is yet to be seen if there will ever been one direct democracy project
> to be used on permanent
>
> basis.
>
> Votorola is now at step 5
>
> Airesis at step 4
>
> Matchism, Blinap at step 1-2
>

>
