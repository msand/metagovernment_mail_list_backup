Just to add my two cents,
in Italy by law the speedometer in the car are biased showing you a false
information. If you ever go to the "motorizzazione civile" you would
actually find it out. They make them say 140 when in reality you go to a
lower speed. So to get the actual speed you are much better off getting the
speed from a navigator. Add to this that not only the speed limit is 130,
the speedometer are biased, but also there is a certain amount of speed
that you are allowed to go over the speed limit without getting a ticket
(also by law). Add all this out and you get that you are quite secure if
you go with your speedometer at 140 km/h. This does not mean that people
are not following the laws. Only that they know the laws so well that they
know when they can push and when they cannot.
