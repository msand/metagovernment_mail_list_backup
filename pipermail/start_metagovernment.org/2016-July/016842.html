Yes make me test blinap I believe there is good chance that Ned have in

On Sun, Jul 24, 2016 at 8:33 PM, Ned Conner <npconner at earthlink.net> wrote:

> >>Ned> National governments and multi-national corporations and top
> capitalists make tens of millions of public-matter decisions per year
> (decisions that affect the public). The decisions that produce the
> atrocities are among those tens of millions. (Not all of the decisions
> produce atrocities, but reliably filtering out those that do requires
> passing all of the decisions through the direct democracy filter.)
>
> >Scott> Seems like a big project: Review every decision that anyone makes
> that could affect another person?
>
> Ned> Yep. They are Really Big Projects. (Both the project of developing
> the software, and the project of using the software). (Which is why I
> continue the grueling work of trying to entice top talent to switch from
> playing around with the "propose -> vote" design pattern to playing around
> with the "propose recursively" design pattern.)
>
> (Actually, the Blinap process in particular is intended to *make* every
> "sufficiently consequential" public-matter decision -- it does not just
> review decisions already made by other means. Blinap is intended to
> entirely displace (render obsolete) decision-making by national
> government-bodies and multi-national corporations and top capitalists.)
>
> And also, the answer to your implicit question ("Is that possible?") needs
> to be an actual demonstration, not just words. (I will be really glad when
> we get to the testing phase for Blinap and the Matchsys and Matteo's
> decision system, and past the competitive chest-beating phase that precedes
> the testing phase.)
>
> In that testing, the Blinap design will indeed be found to be capable of
> making millions of decisions per week: democratically, reliably,
> efficiently, affordably, transparently, and quickly. You'll see. (This is
> me competitively beating my chest.)
>
> (Anyone interested in discussing details of the Blinap design can post to
> this thread.)
