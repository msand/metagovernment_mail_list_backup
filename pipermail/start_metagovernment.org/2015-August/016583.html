*Just think about the following:*
*Each "unit of money" is a vote*

   - Successful selling or fundraising = Being elected
   - Buying or Donating = Voting

Now there is a flaw with our current money. A completely passive
politician, sitting on all his votes, doing nothing with his power gains
more power over time.
A Politician who is highly active and spends all his votes loses all his
power - BUT: if done right has the chance to be re-elected.

Now imagine the amount of votes in a society is finite and some elected
politicians hoard all the votes and do not use them, then the rest of the
society is forced into some kind of "standby mode", because they cannot
vote on each other to elect each other in order to do something for them.
Everyone needs to wait for the few elected ones to start voting on
something to re-gain the votes they spend on the ones hoarding them all.

But of course, there is a way out - instead of waiting for powerful
politicians (which means here - those who have a lot of votes behind them -
which here stands for money) - certain institutions, let's call them
"BANKS", can lend money to those who are found to be "trustworthy".
So "BANKS" as representatives of "ALL THE PEOPLE" decide to give votes to
certain politicians... and because "BANKS" are not really representatives,
let's call it "LOANS" instead, which means the politicians elected by banks
have to re-elect banks in the future, giving them the same or even more
votes back.

... which in reality works like a charm i guess (Just think of governments
in debt around the world - a current example shows in the soap opera
"greece" vs. "germany")

Just for inspiration - start equating "economy & money" with "democracy &
votes" and contemplate a bit of what "money & economy" means in this light,
if you apply the knowledge of "political science", or whatever your
background is, to that.
