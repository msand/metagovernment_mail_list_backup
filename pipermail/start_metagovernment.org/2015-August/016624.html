I would like to comment on all this, but I don't have the resources
right now to comment on each claim and point made.

There is a lot of confusion about this topic and about money.

Here's how it was supposed to work:

1. Leaders were supposed to have a vision for their
nation/state/county/town in order to justify growth (run by the Fed)
and the printing of money (Treasury).
2.  Banks were supposed to lend money to people who were making value
in capitalism towards such goals.
3. Borrowers were supposed to turn that money into greater capital: a
mixture of social and economic value.

But here's what happened.

1.  Leaders were being placed into enormous positions of power, simply
because the Constitution requires it with no vision whatsoever, or
pseudo-visions created to justify their enormous power.
2.  The economic system was led by other-worldly powers from the
all-seeing-eye who had no human experience, so could not create a
system that was of human value apart from housing loans, which was
artificially inflated by real estate speculators.  (For more on that,
I refer you to the Bible.)
3.When the facade started fraying, panic set in and a temporary
stability was created through the media.

That's were we are now.  The system has not corrected itself yet.  A
currency like bitcoin will not fundamentally fix the central problems:
 the accounting for a planetary transition predicated on various
secular factors and prophesies, and the shift from a production-based
economy to a creative and cultural economy.
