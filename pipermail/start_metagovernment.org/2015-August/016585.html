I've no problem with a "manager" taking on some of these roles, and
indeed have specified exactly that in the Matchism template
(www.matchism.org). But there is a key difference between this
proposal and the kind of "leaders" we have now (elected or otherwise):
"Managers" are *employees* of the government, not elected officials or
inheritance or appointment-based monarchs. This gets around the
majority of the corruption and incompetence problems endemic to
current systems because these managers of the executive branch do not
make law (a serious problem with US presidents in particular), they
only execute it. And if they fail to do that properly, we don't have
to impeach them or wait years to vote them out of office, we will
simply fire them.
