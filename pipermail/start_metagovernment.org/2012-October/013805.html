On Tue, Oct 9, 2012 at 11:39 PM, Michael Allan <mike at zelea.com> wrote:

> Alex and Marc,
>
> Thanks again for inviting me to yesterday's meeting.  Thanks also to
> Ronald for presenting.
>
> Marc said:
> > So that way I personally see Votorola (as far as I understand it) in
> > the genre of 'Discussion Systems'. ...
>
> Yes, I see my mistake.  You and Alex are correct.
>
> > But the most important point is, that there is an evolution of the
> > 'Discussion System' by design due to competion between different
> > implementations of specific solutions for 'discussion techniques'
> > and 'opinion forming'.
>
> I agree.  The crucial thing is to have a free choice of tools and
> practices, especially for the individual user.
>
>
> Alex said:
> > It's no problem that the OVN is defunct. It's about the concept
> > which is of interest to us.
> > Would be great if someone could explain the idea behind it.
>
> The technical idea is summarized here: (see also links at left)
> http://zelea.com/project/outcast/_overview.xht
>
> This shows a "discussion system" in AG terminology.  It shows the
> discussion media themselves at right (mailing lists, chat rooms, etc)
> plus other facilities that are related to discussion and necessary for
> opinion formation (Meinungsfindungs).  In this picture, however, the
> problem of free choice is shown only for the voting media, where it is
> labeled "free range voting".  For the other media, we assume the
> individual already has freedom of choice.
>
> > What about the "Diff Functionality"?
>
> It shows up as the "difference bridge" in this more detailed picture:
> http://zelea.com/project/votorola/d/architecture.xht
>
> This picture also expresses the problem of free choice in drafting and
> discussion media in the context of Votorola.  Here the question is,
> "How does the Votorola vote-server connect to all the various drafting
> media that users might choose?  And discussion media?"
>
> Part of the answer is the difference bridge and part is Crossforum
> Theatre (a UI framework).  But these solutions won't necessarily
> translate to other voting media, or to other opinion forming tools.
> So this second picture is probably too specific; it's not the whole
> "ecosystem".
>
> > So did you ever think of trying to incorporate "Vilfredo goes to
> > athens" as a micro discussion mechanism where votorola has none?
>
> Yes, and this remains open as a possibility.  But no special interface
> is required to connect with a brainstorming medium like Vilfredo, at
> least not in the first instance.  The users can simply carry the
> results across manually.
>
> > Will Votorola offer plugins to enable implementing different
> > discussion mediums?
>
> Yes, or rather to connect with existing discussion media that are
> already implemented *external* to Votorola.  Likewise for drafting
> media and any other facilities where the day-to-day work is done,
> including competing vote-servers.  Our strategy is to connect with
> what's already out there, and to facilitate others connecting with us.
> Plugins and other specializing components are required to implement
> the connections, just as you say.
>

> >

> > after tomorrow.
> >
> > Maybe you could talk to Thomas and eventually you could give a
