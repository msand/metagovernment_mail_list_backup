Hello,

On Fri, 12 Oct 2012 8:12, marc wrote:
> Decision-Making (Willensbildung) as the overall social and political 
> process could be devided into to following areas:
>
>
> 1) Information Gathering
>
> 2) Opinion-Forming

I think, "Position" would be a better term than "Opinion". I have in 
mind the common situation, where 100 people have 100 opinions about an 
issue, after a long time of brainstorming and discussion they have 
reduced the number to 20 opinions. Further discussion (which in my 
"german" understanding is about exchanging rational arguments and trying 
to convince the others about the "truth") is at some point futile. Here 
negotiation comes in and is the only way to transform 20 opinions into 
one or a few positions. Negotiation is imo more like bargaining about 
the price on an Asian market, it is not so much about the thruth 
anymore, but nevertheless imo an integral part of politics and 
"Position-Forming".

therefore imo:

2) Position-Forming
> 2.1) brainstorming
> 2.2) discussion
2.3) negotiation

> 3) Decision Forming
> 3.1) voting

Since "Voting" can also be part of discussion (voting for arguments) and 
negotiation (voting for positions) we should maybe add 2.4) voting.

... my 2 cents.
