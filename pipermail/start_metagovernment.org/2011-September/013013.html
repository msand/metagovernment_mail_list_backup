Thanks Matías, and thanks Paul for your feedback.

The reason I was thinking of Belgium's non-profit is because as far as  
I know, they are the only country that supports an International non- 
profit or internationale vereniging zonder winstoogmerk. It is my  
understanding that this organization is not necessarily tied to  
Belgium. Every other kind of non-profit I see appears to be integrally  
tied to its host country, and that does not appear to be appropriate  
for Metagovernment, which is "meta" to nation-states and which has a  
very international membership.

As for becoming a chapter of Democracy International, I don't see how  
that solves the issues above. It ties us to Germany and also to  
another organization (which appears to be in favor of referenda, which  
I believe to be a poor form of direct democracy doomed to failure). We  
have a similar offer from DemocracyLab which has a mission somewhat  
closer to ours, but again that would tie us to being a subset of an  
American organization.

To clarify, Paul, what we are seeking is a way to be formally  
organized such that we can apply for grants. But we do not with to  
have a board of directors nor a close affiliation with one nation. I  
don't know if anyone has ever tried to make such an organization, so  
it may simply be beyond any existing laws. My hope was that  
internationale vereniging zonder winstoogmerk might be something  
close. Am I looking in the wrong place?

Thanks again.
