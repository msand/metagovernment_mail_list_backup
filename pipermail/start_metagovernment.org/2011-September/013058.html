alex at twister11.de wrote:
> @About MIRROR VOTING
> Thats another one. Who should decide about the rules for mirror
> voting?  Sure, there should be experts that should give their input
> for the aspect of the software that will support the "protokoll" of
> mirror voting between 2 micro communities, but it should be the
> communities that negotiate how mirror voting takes place.  Maybe
> experts could decide on a mechanisms between every 2 software tools
> that implement a different decision finding process and how the
> results map to each other...
> ... but my guess is that many assumptions have to be made and you
> cannot be sure if a micro community that uses a tool will agree to
> these decisions, so they should be allowed to negotiate their own
> translation of votes to other systems with the other community that
> uses a different tool.

I agree, it's a concern.  But provided vote mirroring works as
planned, the user will have a maximum of freedom in choosing his/her
own voting facilities, including the mirroring service:
http://listserv.aoir.org/pipermail/air-l-aoir.org/2011-July/024104.html

So this is maybe a case where it's better to vote with your feet than
to spend lots of time negotiating settlements.  In extremity, you can
always set up your own mirror and do the translations as you like.
Openness to such innovation is one of the purposes of vote mirroring,
and it applies recursively to the mirroring system, as well.
