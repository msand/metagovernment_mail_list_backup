On Sep 20, 2011, at 7:11 AM, Michael Allan wrote:

> If we transcend partisanship, then all that is left is the common
> goal.  Your movie script brought to mind my past attempts to
> communicate the moral argument of freedom.  Those attempts always
> failed because I failed to put my finger on the precise point of
> injustice in the status quo.  That point was somehow hidden from view,
> and I don't know if anyone ever noticed it till now.  Here is how I
> see it:
>
>   I used to believe that I was free because I lived in a democracy
>   and had a vote; but the truth is, I have no political freedom at
>   all.

When I first read the above sentence, the first thing that came to my  
mind is, "this is how our movie should start." That's a powerful and  
moving statement. It also gives me the idea of making the entire movie  
script first-person, and yet still crowdsourcing the narration. So  
each paragraph is a different person talking about how he or she is  
not enfranchised (within the overarching thread of the narrative).

I think that would work really well for part one (which is the only  
part that I have a first draft of at http://metagovernment.org/wiki/Promotion/Movie/Script 
  ), but I don't know if it would work for part two: how we can do  
anything about it. Maybe once the narrative shifts from the flaws of  
representative democracy to the possibilities of collaborative  
governance, it should switch from "I" to "We." How does that strike  
anyone?

In other words, so sum up the whole movie in two short sentences:
I am not free because representative democracy has failed me.
We will be free when we take control of our own governance.


>   Whether I vote or not, and regardless of who I vote for:
>
>     (a) the candidates are chosen ahead of time, and I have no
>         influence over the choice;
>     (b) the course of the election is unaffected by my vote;
>     (c) the outcome is the same, regardless;
>     (d) state power is unaffected; and
>     (e) the laws are unaffected.
>
>   If I disobey (d) state power or (e) the laws, then I am brought
>   into submission by force.  The powers that affect me are unaffected
>   by any comparable power of mine.  In regard to my political
>   freedom, I might as well live in China or Saudi Arabia.
>   Disobedience is my only freedom, and yet the cost of exercising it
>   is physical confinement or worse.  In this regard, it follows that
>   I am a slave.
