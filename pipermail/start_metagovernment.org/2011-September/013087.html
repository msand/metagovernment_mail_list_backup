Anyway, I call those "solutions".  It's been dawning on me that we
need *first* to speak of the problem.  I was hoping this would be a
constructive point to raise in this thread.  In the movie script (to
explain), you gave "the problem" a starring role.  I think that's what
we need to do in reality, too.  We need to speak of the problem
*without regard* to any solution.  Only then can it be a guide for
societal engineering.  This is crucial.  The rationalization of
society is the structural embodiment of reason, and all our work of
restructuring (and communicating) must be in reference to that reason,
which begins with a concrete and immediate problem.

See how that reason explains even the rhetorical power of the first
person narrative - "I used to believe that I was free..."?  No
abstractions in that, it's all factual, sincere and above the table.

Moreover, what reason cannot explain, reason hits like a hammer.
