Hi Ed,

for starters nobody is telling that "direct democracy" (initiative and 
referendum)  is a perfect system. But in my opinion it has proven to be 
better than representative democracy. But it needs to be worked on. Direct 
democracy in a state like California (some 30 milion people) is not 
comparable with direct Democracy in switserland (some 8 milion people) that 
is furthemore divided in very autonomous canton's. Even between those 
cantons there is a "cultural" difference with French speaking canton's more 
relying on Representative democracy then German speaking kantons who are 
very much deciding themselves with direct demiocracy. Altough all Swiss 
people could have the same system if they wanted. I isn't.

The rest in between

We, in Belgium, propose to have several rounds
1. a proposal of law
2. anyone can propose and discuss amendments but the person(s) who submitted 
the proposal stay in charge of the proposal
3. a first round of voting give an idea of the social support and has to 
reach a threshold (2%) comparable with the signature gathering
4. if the threshold is reached a vote can be launched for all citizens 
concerned (in Belgium, like in Spain ,... we have all an electronic identity 
card like a bank card)

>
> 3. If any of those above malicious interests play with the inputs or  if 
> anything else goes wrong, it can make a very bad name for our  entire 
> movement. What if I find that the website ends up having me  support the 
> legalization of rape and murder? That could be easily  accomplished by 
> people deliberately getting together to make me look  bad. What is to stop 
> that? Or what if the software just doesn't work  right: then what am I 
> supposed to do in the legislature (or whatever  body I am in)? Just 
> abstain until a new version of the software comes  out? How would that 
> look?
>
That are all assumptions that do not materialise. If you don't trust people 
(in great numbers) democracy is not possible.
Our example in sweden (demoex) shows that it did not happen. And initiatives 
in direct democracy are also proving that it is not happening.

> 4. Even if everything goes right, our movement could still get a bad

> consensus decision-making only allows the best decisions to pass.  But in 
> a less complex system, what is there besides majority rule? 
> Super-majority rule? That's still nothing like real consensus-through- 
> synthesis.
>
consensus has to be reached if possible and it is more often possible then 
we might think. But thinking that it is always possible is an illusion in my 
view.
