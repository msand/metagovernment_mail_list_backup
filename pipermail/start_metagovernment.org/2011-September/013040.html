Matteo,

While the Votorola theory document may seem complex, almost all of the
underlying principles (unless I'm missing something) are astonishingly
simple.
I believe an animated ("infographic") version of the same document could be
quite concise, and something even a child could grasp intuitively.
I will try to sketch something out one of these days although my programming
chops might not be up to the task.
On Sep 20, 2011 5:41 AM, "Matteo Martini" <matteomartini72 at yahoo.com> wrote:
> Just few words about Electronic Direct Democracy or Internet democracy (as
I call it).
> There are quite a lot of initiatives about direct democracy around the
world, still the global movement for direct democracy (if we can call it so,
since there is not official "one" movement) fails to pick up.
> Fails to pick up means that most of the current political decisions are
still taken by the conventional political system and that in no country the
direct democracy system is out there working.
> My idea about why this is happening is that big changes start from small
things.
> This is true especially in the realm of internet.
> Big successful sites like Facebook started as simple sites to exchange
pictures among friends.
> Internet democracy needs to grow in the same way.
> We need to start looking at who needs this tool first, and help such
people use the tool of internet democracy.
> At first, I believe, Internet Democracy should start by targeting groups
like GreenPeace, Sea Shepard down to the local group of people that want to
protect their children from the pollution of the local power plant.
> When targeting such groups of people, it is possible to offer them a
software that allows them to get connected together and create a joint
document to be sent to the local mayor or senator.
> This is where we should start from, I believe.
> The potential users are already out there.
> All the big things start from the small, and we can not think to
immediately start a country or world government from the start.
> I believe we may need a simple tool to offer to people and invite them to
use it for what they really need it.
> If the system works out, then it would be easier to implement the system
at wider and wider scale.
> I have received the mailing list of Votorola since few months ago now, and
I believe that Votorola can potentially become a very powerful system.
>
> But my point is that the power of Votorola can be its main weakness.
>
> I have tried to read the specs of Votorola under the chapter "Theory"
> quite a few times and I have to admit that I could not understand few
> main points of what is written there.
> I think Votorola is bound to be a very flexible system, but will it be
simple enough to be used by the average user?
> Please believe that I am not willing to criticize the excellent work done
by Mike Allen and Conseo so far (and Thomas), I believe you are excellent
people and you are working hard to create a good tool that many people in
the world need to use.
> But sometimes I wonder if people should not instead start to use a much
simpler tool, without many functionalities, that just allows them to vote
between three or four different proposals of law and send this proposal to
the local mayor or senator.
>
> Matteo Martini
