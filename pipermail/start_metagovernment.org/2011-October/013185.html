Can anyone else provide input on this? The idea is to create a basic  
charter for a Metagov organization, then to figure out how we can  
institutionalize it in a way that it is recgnized as a formal  
organization. After that I (and others) can start writing grant  
proposals to foundations in order to fund projects (code development,  
outreach, sponsoring member projects, etc.).

Here is the modified (skeleton of the) charter I am proposing, based  
on Thomas' input:

1. High-level decisions of the organization (including budgeting  
decisions) are to be made by using Outcast and/or governance software  
of a member project. These decisions will follow a decision-rule  
similar to the one we defined for editing the home page:
http://metagovernment.org/wiki/User:ThomasvonderElbe/draft_rules

2. Day-to-day activities may be handled by individuals ad hoc, or more  
formally by individual employees or volunteers of the organization.  
Any decision made by an individual must be publicly documented and can  
be overridden by the process in #1.

3. Modification of the charter can only be done by a supermajority  
similar to the ruleset modification-rule set out in the link above.


Is this reasonable, workable, and appropriate?



On Oct 21, 2011, at 7:56 PM, Ed Pastore wrote:

> Hi, Thomas.
>
> When I look at other charters (focusing on the bylaws of nonprofit  
> organizations) they are mostly about how the board of directors is  
> constructed and elected and how the bylaws can be amended. So for  
> us, the decision-rule is in a sense the charter, and what we are  
> discussing here is a wrapper around it.
>
> A charter needs to be solid enough that a foundation would feel like  
> it could provide us with a grant and know that the money would be  
> handled responsibly. For that reason, I think the ad hoc part needs  
> to be downplayed some. I don't think we need to functionally  
> decrease it, but the charter itself shouldn't sound so explicitly  
> like there is no accountability.
>
> For the sake of simplicity, I think the third bullet could be  
> removed from my version (keeping numbers 1, 2, and 4), since it is  
> logistical and not constitutional. Would that make it workable for  
> you?
>
> Anyone else have any comments?
>
