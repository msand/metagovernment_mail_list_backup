  Hi all,

  I don't know anything about the votorola system but I know that we have a 
  good respons when we are showing the "liquid democracy" picture from the 
  "aktiv democrati" (ENA workshop). Maybe it need some more explanation but it 
  invokes questions and the respons is positive.
  It is also a good point that we can mention that the system has a long 
  record of good performance in corporation application. It resembles the 
  share holders assembllee. A shareholder can vote himself or he can give his 
  vote to anyone he wants to represent him. Of course there are some 
  differences. All citizens have one equal vote. The chareholders meeting (and 
  we all are shereholders of our community) of a company can be annual while 
  the political shareholders meeting can be continuous.
  There are some unsolved questions (does a representative in this system has 
  to be payd and how, how frequent can we change delegation ) but the system 
  attracts positive attention. Personally I think that changing 
  representatives will not be to volatile and become unworkable that way , but 
  it has to be experienced. There are of course several possibilities to, 
  changing to personal vote can always and imediatly, changing from one 
  representative to another has to be a little bit more difficult ... and so 
  on.

  Paul
