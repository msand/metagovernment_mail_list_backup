>
> Site capabilities overview:
>
> 1)      Most crucially, WikiPeace will enable horizontal discussion to
> occur about any problem of any scale. This will be accomplished with the
> ability to scale one’s search to a desired size, (I.E. city scale, global
> scale, even down to individual households, organizations, and/or
> workplaces), and from there to determine what space of that scale, (I.E.
> what city, household, etc). The most valuable input will be the most visible
> by use of user ratings, as WikiPeace aspires to embody meritocracy as well
> as complete transparency and horizontalism, (the abolishment of inequality
> in decision making power).
>
> The user selects what issues are relevant to them, then what strategies
> make the most sense, and from there directly interface with the selected
> strategy’s authoring organization, with the ability to see their calendar,
> update events to their own user profile calendar, and even set text message
> reminders for events. The site is designed to bridge the largest gaps in the
> process of nonviolent social organizing. In this way not only do the best
> ideas for the worst problems get the most attention, but each person is
> connected directly with what matters to them. Additionally, organizations
> and member are transparently rated in effectiveness as well as 'karma', (in
> case anyone's effective but say, sexist)
>
>
>
> 2)      A ‘haves and wants’ section enables individuals to find what they
> need from people around them as if everyone interested was running a 24/7
> yard sale. What’s more is that there will be space for people to
> offer/request intangible goods, such as skills fermenting, or repairing
> electronics. Because everything is transparent, using these classified ads
> to do crime will become virtually impossible, such as the craigslist
> killings. –Individuals will have space to barter if they choose, reducing
> the amount of dependency upon ‘a-moral’ corporations, corrupt governments,
> and currency in general, to meet the needs of real people. -furthermore this
> is invaluable because it makes users return to the site and tell their
> friends.
>
>
>
> 3)      A critical factor in the resolution of today’s problems is that
> there is an immense history of the ruling classes reframing the messages of
> the oppressed in ways that makes change not happen. Propaganda has proven an
> invaluable tool for the generation of a public that cannot hear its less
> fortunate members, for the most valuable of terms have become the most
> loaded. WikiPeace is designed to leave infinite space for its users to
> debate cultural myths, as well as providing a space for the most universal
> myths to be sorted out on global scales, with tremendous amounts of input
> from all sides, and everything rated, (transparently).
>
>
>
> 4)      The default presentation of any page will be written in ‘common
> speak’, as in the message will be phrased specifically so that the most
> people can understand it. Each page will have a ‘side’ one may choose to
> display that is for technical vernacular, that WikiPeace may contain both
> the specialized language of professionals, while not leaving ‘lay-people’
> behind. Another side one may choose to read and/or write in is in the
> language of Marshal Rosenberg’s NonViolent Communication, which is
> specialized for the most effective conflict resolution. It is based on
> stating needs, as well as circumventing inflammatory, counter-productive
> language.
>
>
>
> 5)      There is a system of ‘tokens’ which allow users to do everything
> from openly debate concepts without disrupting a page, to tracking a page to
> be notified if it has been altered either slightly, or entirely. The
> ‘tracking token’ will ensure that site disruption is ineffective, as
> complete transparency will enable users to flag disruptors and discontinue
> their editing privileges, and important pages will be tracked by potentially
> hundreds of users. The ‘debate token’ enables users to place anywhere in a
> pages text essentially a link to the debate. This debate format is
> transparent, and starts with a ‘for’ and ‘against’ section, where the users
> rate the best arguments on either side to display at top. There will be
> space for informational links, as well as a section for arguments that are
> neither  for or against but somehow relevant. The ‘note token’ is similar
> to the debate token, except its meant as a gentle addendum to a pages text,
> usually transitional, because either the addendum should be written into the
> page, or it’s on its way to becoming a debate. Sometimes it is a tangent and
> in this way information can flourish separately from the realm of
> distraction.
>
>
>
> 6)      There will also be a map generator which can display the
> popularity of one issue's strategy over another in relation to its rater’s
> general geographic location. In this way, it will be demonstrated that a
> ‘law’ doesn’t need to be something everyone in an arbitrary, legal, division
> of land has to do battle over. Where ever and however a consensus decides
> they want their rules/boundaries can be the very skeleton of a law,
> determining it’s geography based on how actual people feel about what should
> be done. I know it sounds potentially confusing, but what we've got running
> now is outright criminal, requiring 'laypeople' to abide by a code that is
> so complicated it literally requires specialists to interpret.
>
>
>
> In summary, these features combined will make participation and learning
> streamlined, specifically for the purpose of implementing civic intelligence
> to overcome the greatest threat our species has ever known, what can only
> best be described as global corporatocracy, or the profit agenda. Once this
> site is up it can enable everyday people to make actual change by not only
> effortlessly connecting them to likeminded people, but by creating an
> abundance of necessary information, and by helping all collaborators to
> organize via interest-based education and text message reminders.
>
>
