Sounds like a great idea, but I think it would be a major effort to turn it into a reality and properly market it and maintain it.  Would easily require several people working full-time for a couple of years.  I think you will need to find some investment to make that happen.  I'd be interested to work on something like that (I've been doing PHP/SQL/Linux/C/C++for 15+ years) but couldn't do it for free.  There would be a lot of opposition to such a system and it would be a maintenance nightmare by the time you had 1000's of users, trolls, and flame wars left and right.  You could get some investment through crowdsourcing e.g. kickstarter.com, and I think you might find a surprising amount of support if you could present the idea in a clear way with well defined use cases for some example issues you'd like to address, clearly partitioned implementation phases, and a thorough summary of existing work that has been done in the domain.  But I wouldn't
 try to do it on less than $1/4M in starting capital.  There are also many other people working on similar things, and so the ideal approach would be to leverage the efforts of others in the community and try to get people to work together.  The software world is full of examples of small groups working on small separate projects that 98% of the time never achieve critical mass and I think to take it beyond that is a full-time job to find the right people and coordinate everyone, and definitely requires some investment capital to get it off the ground.

>>5)      There is a system of ‘tokens’ which allow users to do everything from openly debate concepts without disrupting a page, to tracking a page to be notified if it has been altered either slightly, or entirely.
