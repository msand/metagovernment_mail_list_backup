I don't believe it's possible to buy enough people's votes, and you
really have to buy A LOT of vote's in order to have a measurable
impact on important issues, without other people noticing it. If u
really buy or force a lot of people, it is very likely, that some of
them will make it public somehow (maybe whistleblowing mechanisms) or
people who are not affected will notice strange behavior and will
become suspicious.

Therefor, I don't think it will ever become a big issue. Beside that,
you could always delegate ur voice if u are afraid of casting a vote
on your own.

Probably you could make that argument in a much more sophisticated way.

Normaly I refer to that by the statement "Form FOLLOWS Function" or
like a TED Talk i saw recently mentioned it: "Form IS Function".
I think of it as "Form follows Function", where "Form" follows
"Function" so closely, that "Form is Function" becomes true.
As long as people are disconnected from ballots, "Form" obviously IS
NOT "Function" and therefore improvement is needed.
As long as "Form" IS NOT "Function", there is room for all kinds of
defects that will surface and make you realize that you have to
improve "Form".
