We're probably not "willing to attract users" by marketing strategies.
I agree with C's reasons for this, and I offer the example above to
complement his own explanation.  We aren't drafting a law up there,
but it's a live demo of collaborative re-writing.  In that sense, it's
better than any video for promotional purposes.  Do you see?  As long
as we're developing the real goods, then we won't need to be staging
promotional shows on the side; the goods are a live, travelling show
in themselves; all are free to participate, and freedom is the name of
the show. *

Ed's charter is a kind of law (surely more interesting than Votorola's
home page).  If it attracted just a little more participation, then
probably it would become a re-writing effort too.
http://metagovernment.org/pipermail/start_metagovernment.org/2011-October/004379.html
But it's the charter (or law) that must attract the interest in the
first instance, and not the software.  What I'm saying here is, "Show
us folks who are actually engaged in drafting a law (or any consensus
text), and we could show them some software."


 * Marshall McLuhan may be a "manic exaggerator" as they say, but
   aren't some of his exaggerations coming true?  The medium is free,
   and freedom is the message.

   What I'm saying is, "If we do it right, then it will sell itself."
   But doing it right ain't easy, so we focus all our efforts on that.
