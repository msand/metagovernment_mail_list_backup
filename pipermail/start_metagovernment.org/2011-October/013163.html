Craig Simon wrote:
> ... How does Votorola notify supporters down the delegation tree
> when a lead voter switches his or her position?

Shifts his or her vote?  No notification is built into the vote-server
itself.  It could be built atop the web API if needed, and run as an
external facility. [1]

UI's are external facilities themselves.  One UI we're coding (not yet
in Thomas's demo) looks something like this: [2]
http://zelea.com/project/votorola/a/xf/vote/_/3.xht

Note that if Frank shifts his vote from Anne, then his upstream
branches also shift visibly in the tree.  The shift will be dramatic
in most cases, involving both a translation and a rotation.  (Picture
George and his 1515 voters swinging off to a different quadrant.)  If
the shift came as a surprise, then the social network would carry the
news.  But it shouldn't come as a surprise; not if Frank and his
delegates are doing their jobs and earning all those votes; they
should keep in communication with the voters.
