Hi Ed, (and everyone),
Thanks responding to my suggestion. I resonate with the basic concept of what you've written in the promotion. I particularly like the idea of having multiple individuals reading the script. I think making it human or personal is key. I wonder though if it matches the feeling or mood of what is currently happening on the streets around the world. I find myself asking...where's the passion, the frustration, the outrage....where is the dream of something viable and enchanting. My guess is that all of those elements are alive in you....and for some reason they're being tuned to a lower octave (?).

Most of all I'm wondering where's the connect to the #occupy movement. There are general assemblies happening all over the world practicing collaborative governance....right now...however simple, raw and makeshift they may be. It seems to me they will be the early adopters of the tools Metagov is developing. I think that in a very short period of time those assemblies will be wanting to connect with each other in more efficient, complex, and empowering ways. There's a global movement developing "centered around openness, inclusion, and freedom". Seems like Metagov manna to me.

I imagine a short video outreaching to that community, clearly describing your vision(s) of "collaborative, consensus-based, and synthesis-oriented solutions", would put Metagov at the creative hub of a self-organizing mass of passionate, committed, and focussed individuals. 

I apologize if I'm missing something already in the works. I've spent several hours on the website and, not being a programmer, there's a lot I can't quite grok. That said, I get what you are up to. That there is actually a group working on "code" for collaborative governance totally blows my mind. You have know idea....
Thanks again for responding,
Steve
