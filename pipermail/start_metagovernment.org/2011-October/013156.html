I am perfectly willing to agree to disagree here, but I have yet to see a
single argument for the use of secrecy that I couldn't find serious flaws
in. People are used to the idea that secrecy in 'voting' is corrupt, it
doesn't mean it's going to make people stand behind your organization or
trust it enough to use it in the fight for a better way. honestly, what
purpose is this secrecy serving?

If you can't convince me, (who wants to be an ally), I doubt your critics,
particularly the ones in power, are going to have a very hard time writing
you off. Are you doing this for the people? then you'll need to really
convince them that you're different, not more of the same. And there is not
a rational explanation yet that I've heard on behalf of secret voting that
doesn't make it sound like someone's basically trying to validate their own
regime building tactics. -you know what I mean? particularly because the
only way for a regular person like me gets to see if all the votes are
actually being counted and produced by real people, is to see A)what people
they know voted for *is* in the list, and B) to be able to check if
strangers votes are from bots or fake people. without that you have a
breeding ground for false democracy, it just takes a skillful hacker and
anyone with enough money or force to employ them. I hope you'll take this to
heart long enough to see why I'm pestering you about it, it really is a deal
breaker.
