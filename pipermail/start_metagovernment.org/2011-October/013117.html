Hey C,

I like the idea of eating our own dogfood (tools).  In order to reply,
I first need to understand the problem you're chasing, and exactly how
it calls for tooling.  The GUEPO plan is currently executing in its
development phase [1], and you're contributing the track design for
q-sum accounts [2], a core component of the RAC.  What problem do we
have *there* that tooling could solve?  I understand how the tools
might help if: (a) we disagreed between us on the plan; or (b) a third
person wanted to contribute but was forestalled by a disagreement over
the plan; or (c) an entirely different plan called for your help (or
mine), but again there was a disagreement to clear up first.  In those
cases, the need is clear.

> To show the attraction of consensus based action ...

You want to attract a project such as OpenSourceEcology that depends,
like we do, on a consensual assignment of resources, and you plan to
showcase our RAC for this purpose.  We probably could do that if we
had a useful application to showcase, but "useful" brings us back to
the question of the need fulfilled by the tooling *in that
application*; we have to flush that need out into the open, first.

Sorry if it's obvious, and I just miss it.
