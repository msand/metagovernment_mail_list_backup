Hi Paul,

Thanks so much for your thoughtful reply.  I feel that a shared
understanding of the possible solutions begins not with the solutions
themselves, but rather with the problems they attempt to solve; it
begins especially with an understanding of each problem by its own
lights, and in a concrete situation.  Your reply was the first
instance of that I've seen in all the years I've worked in this field.

The problem of exclusion you describe also occurs in legislatures,
where it's the lawmaker who's excluded.  The crucial thing I feel is
to understand why this kind of thing happens, how it unfolds, and what
the broader consequences are.
