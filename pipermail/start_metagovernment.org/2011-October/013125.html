> On Sun, 09 Oct 2011 16:54, Ed Pastore wrote:
>
> 1. High-level decisions of the organization (including budgeting decisions)
> are to be made by using Outcast and/or governance software of a member
> project. These decisions will follow a decision-rule similar to the one we
> defined for editing the home page:
> http://metagovernment.org/wiki/User:ThomasvonderElbe/draft_rules
> (This process will in every way take the place of a traditional Board of
> Directors, of Annual Meetings, and of other governance-related rules that
> appear in traditional bylaws/charters/articles.)
>
> 2. Day-to-day activities may be handled by individuals ad hoc, or more
> formally by individual employees or volunteers of the organization. Any
> decision made by an individual must be publicly documented and can be
> overridden by the process in #1.
>
> 3. Any money controlled by the organization will be kept in a bank account
> which can be accessed by at least two people designated by the process in
> #1, and who may be replaced at any time by that same process. All income and
> outlay must be publicly documented (contributions may be made anonymously).
>
> 4. Modification of the charter can only be done by a supermajority similar
> to the ruleset modification-rule set out in the link above.
