It should be noted that even if the authenticity, auditability, and 
anonymity challenges of online voting could be overcome, there's another 
critical obstacle. Some experts make principled opposition to internet 
voting and even absentee ballots because there's no way to guarantee 
whether people voting from home or from convenient locations would be 
free from intrusive oversight, coercion, or bribery. A private voting 
booth is considered the surest remedy.

That's another reason I try to be consistently clear that Indaba is not 
anonymous, and is not positioned as something that can solve all the 
problems of building an online government. It's focused first of all on 
raising the quality of discursive tools and open problem solving.
