Dear Nillus,

I'm a software engineer for one of the projects mentioned by Ed.
Unfortunately, I know of nothing that could handle your requirements
off shelf, which I guess are: (a) consensus making; (b) free choice
and free expression; and (c) scale.  At best you could run trials of
experimental tools at small scales.  The tools wouldn't be much
immediate help though in furthering any particular political or social
issue; rather the opposite, as your efforts would aid in the
development of a general purpose technology that is still immature.

There is also theory.  Tools are designed to solve problems of course,
and the underlying theory often concerns itself with problems as much
as with solutions.  Likewise for the occupy movement, where a shared
recognition and understanding of the social problems is probably a
prerequisite for any kind of effective action.  Theory and practice
must go hand in hand, of course, but where the one is lagging the
other may sometimes take the lead.

This are just my own thoughts.  I'd be happy to answer any questions
you may have.
