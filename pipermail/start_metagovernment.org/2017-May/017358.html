Yeah, right: A "revolution" that's a few years old and which governs a
population the size of a small city is a shining beacon? I think you
would be far better served studying how the soviet system, which
started out a lot like Rojava, eventually failed when the SDAPs
transitioned it to totalitarianism. "libertarian municipalism" is just
another variant of the same kind of amateur social engineering we've
seen repeatedly fail: You either understand how psychopaths create and
control hierarchies or you repeat the mistakes of the past.

> Looks like hardship always brings out the best from humans.

Sure, but it also brings out the psychopaths: Hitler, Mao, Stalin, and
Pol Pot *all* came to power exactly because the people were
experiencing severe resource deprivation and so were inspired to
revolution. Taking this as a lesson will only result in you creating a
system that psychopaths will use to exploit The People. For example,
you can learn a lot more about "cooperatives" by studying how the
Soviets and the Asian communists figured out how to exploit them than
by studying some fresh new example that only hasn't had time to
promote a psychopath that has figured out how to game that particular
system.
