You can read the whole thing at:
https://read.oecd-ilibrary.org/economics/oecd-guidelines-on-measuring-subjective-well-being_9789264191655-en#page1

It's wide ranging document with a *lot* of (tedious) detail on how to
do surveys (i.e., the kind of stuff you learn in graduate school in
psychology/political science/public policy/etc.) But it does address
this issue of cultural differences in responses and the various
analogs of SWB, so it's worth skimming for anyone interested in this
stuff. Again, IMHO it's good stuff for evaluating social engineering
design, but I still don't see how setting SWB as a *goal* is really
going to get you anything near what we'd get from simply empowering
The People to make the public policy decisions that affect them.
