  Direct Democracy in Spain

  For the first time in history, a Spanish congressman has decided to transfer his vote directly to the citizens through an online voting platform.

  Joan Baldoví, congressman of the Spanish green party Compromis-EQUO, will vote in Congress the "Transparency Act" according to what the public will vote using the web https://congresotransparente.com . The citizen voting process started on Sunday and will finish this Thursday 12th September 2013.

  https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=LS678iuekqM

  Your voice will be my voice, your vote will be my vote. Do you want to enter Congress with me? If you want to participate in the first direct democracy experience in Congress, go to https://congresotansparente.com, a free space that allows your voice to be heard in parliament, don't wait four years to speak up. Do it now.


  Hacking the Parliament

  Agoravoting is a free software project that started to allow direct and liquid democracy online. This summer the developers of Agoravoting proposed to congressman Baldoví to use his vote in a first attempt of direct democracy in Spain. To their surprise and joy he took the challenge.

  http://i.imgur.com/f6z7lBh.jpg

  Since then the Agoravoting team has been working hard to improve the software, adding cryptography and authentication.

We could add all this stuff to a facebook timeline, but we shouldt become to nostalgic about it :P
what about ongoing stuff?
what are metagovernment people currently up to and where could they need help or what would they like to accomplish if others would join?

i can only speak for me and i would like to "advance email". A website, where people could create "discussion mailinglists" for free.
to make it secure, people who do not join by themselves, but are invited in or added by others first must confirm that they want to participate.

there will be summary emails which can be configured by each participant, so he receives news around those topics that are of interest to him/her.
And then of course, there has to be some kind of "semi-automated" wiki, to which one could add content or update or even delete content, so everyone
could link those snippets or whatever it is, back into the email discussion stream.


I have already written a javascript based authentication mechanism, where one could receive credentials via email and had to confirm it afterwards.
there was also a "forgot password" mechanism and then of course a "user profil" site, where a user could change all those settings...


So, i guess it's not that hard to use javascript/nodejs to host a website that allows users to create ne mailing lists.
The hard thing will then be, how to structure the discussion content by adding additional html/css to the email and if possible maybe even javascript.
Maybe instead of writing the email in ones usual email program, there could be lots of links that open a browser with the content of the currently viewed mail and where a lot more options are allowed, when it comes to commenting, voting, discussion, quoting, persisting, forking, whatever....

So that's it and its what i would add to "currrently" on that timeline ;-)
