Whats the use of categorizing what kind of "thing" is written in a
discussion?
(opinion, quote, justification, question, proposal, fact, argument,
putYourFavouriteHere)


>       [>1.1] Do you mean why to propose rules for discussion? [1.1<]
>

Rules are stupid. => doing stuff manually (disciplined) will not work
Mechanisms are awesome. => providing mechanisms which offer users value,
because they make things easy, is cool!



>       [>1.2] Or do you mean why to try to formalize an informal process at
> all? [1.2<]
>

Give people tools that cater to their needs. What do people want when they
discuss?
How can you aid them, so that things go faster and smoother?
If you give them tools, they will create a process all by themselves.
Give them more tools and better tools and they will improve the process by
themselves.
Watch how they discuss and what kind of patterns emerge and invent tools to
overcome those patterns.

There is no one process to rule them all, there is different people with
different needs and different habits.
All they want is sync their habits/knowledge with others in a way that
makes life easier.
One major pattern is repetition of content in discussion, because its never
formalized into a definition for those who discuss and then referenced in
further discussions.


Sure, you can do it all today, but majority of people takes the path of
least resistance.
Currently its too much of a hassle to manually copy stuff to a wiki, then
link to it, then vote on it, then make minor changes based on more
discussion, rinse and repeat.
Solve that instead.
