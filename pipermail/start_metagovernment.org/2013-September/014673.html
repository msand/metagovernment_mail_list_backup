No, i believe you might confuse the spirit of money and stock.
Stock means, those who participate decide amongst each other what their
work is worth.
With money, the decision is external to the subsystem.

Beside that, the decision between "who gets how much stock" is in essence
the same as "who gets how much money", because:
1. After it has been decided upon who gets how much stock, then the
"incoming money will be distributed according to the distribution of shares.
2. If participants would directly argue who gets how much money, then you
would be able to DERIVE what that would have meant in stocks,

...so in essence its about the "distribution key" that is decided on, and
thats what SHARES or STOCK is :-)

>  26. Payment Through A Bank
>
>      To enable payment, the decision system needs to be associated with
>      an online bank.
>
>      (In the beginning -- as our alpha testing phase -- we can if we
>      wish create our own community money, and have our own private bank
>      that handles that money.)
>

The "bank account" will hold all the revenue.
The "stocks a.k.a shares a.k.a distribution key" will decide how the money
is distributed upon participants.
Therefor, its never about money in the first place, but about shares.
You can avoid the word and not give the process a name, but its what
happens.

Everyone who is paid money leaves the circle of participants, thus,
he explicitly or implicitly sells his "shares" to the others :-)
It's totally ok if someone wants to do that, but this should not be a
default mechanism. What if i dont want to sell my shares to a subgroup of
participants which for some random obscure reasons have "the right" to get
all the shares, while some other participants may ask them to buy some
shares from them, but wont get any if those random few do not want to sell.
