> Scrum's planning poker can be useful in planning and prioritizing and
> such. But it seems to me that the best basis for determining COMPENSATION
> for people is measuring and appraising what has happened in the past (work
> accomplished). It would be better to base compensation on what has
> happened, not upon guesses about what may happen. For example, when
> predictions turn out to be bad, the compensation to the predictors should
> reflect that. Yes? (Maybe I am not understanding how you wish to apply
> scrum's poker to determining compensation?)
>


Sounds good. How would u do it?
Maybe the approach can be combined.
One tries to guess the future, but then does a review as soon as the future
becomes the present and then again when it becomes the past... and then
again when it becomes even more "the past" ....
And because its a process, because there will be a continuous stream of new
tasks, that review process has to be "baked into" the whole.
How would u do it?

>>
>>
>> Proposals as such are not and if it were possible one shouldnt do it,
>> because otherwise nobody would use them.
>> But a proposal is only a proposal and it will only come to life if there
>> is software implementing the proposal.
>>
>>
>>     At this point, the Metagovernment entity has no national currency
>>     income to distribute, just as startups, generally, have no income
>>     at the outset.
>>
>>
>> Yes, but that does not prevent metagovernment from creating its own
>> "artificial currency".
>> Metagovernment could make it transparent what it is that people work on
>> and reward people for doing people, by adding points to their account.
>> Those points could be "shares" and those shares could depreciate too. If
>> there will be "income" at some point in time, those "points" could be used
>> to distribute it among those who have "points".
>>
>>
>>
>>     Until we have an income (from donations, from individuals choosing
>>     to "invest" in a better future for the planet), we can do what
>>     startups often do. We can create our own "stock-option green

>>     the possibility that at some future time the green stamps can be
>>     traded in for "real" currencies (from the future donation income).
>>
>>
>> yes :-)
>>
>>
>>     If we do this, it puts us immediately in the position of having
>>     money to handle.
