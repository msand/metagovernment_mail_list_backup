As i understand the "mirror front", it is:
1. Just a "News Feed", which lists all the "stuff/messages" that belong to
a certain "topic", no matter if the source medium of a message is a forum,
email, twitter, blog or whatever
2. This "News Feed" does not include ALL "stuff/messages" written on these
different kind of mediums, but instead,
3. it's the people who discuss, who somehow themselves or if they choose,
then elected representatives, use "FILTERS", to filter out all those
messages on the source medium, which do not belong to the chosen "topic".
4. For multiple "source mediums" (e.g. a forum about drugs, a mailinglist
about drugs, tweets about drugs, blogs about drugs...) there can be
multiple "News Feeds", maybe because of the first "News Feed" and their
representatives did not allow all messages in, so some people of the same
"topic community" creates another one.
5. They can FORWARD their "news feeds" to other "News feeds", and as soon
as they aggregated to only ONE "NEWS FEED", this is called a "MIRROR FRONT"

Currently, there exists at least two "source mediums", thats the
metagovernment mailinglist and the megatovernment IRC channel.
They produce "stuff/messages" and Conseo and Mike and now Me, can press
"ack" or "nak" to vote on whether the message will go into the "News Feed"
(the REDDIT PAGE), which will represent the whole community and thus be the
"MIRROR FRONT".


I guess it's called "MIRROR FRONT"
FRONT: because its what the general public sees as an image of the topical
community (a community which is a community because they discuss certain
ideas, even if they do not know each other in person and use different
"source mediums")
MIRROR: because it reflects that which is written as "stuff/messages" back
to the general public, but also the topical community, so they can reflect
themselves and improve their discussion to produce a next iteration of the
"MIRROR FRONT"

So thats my guessing on what the name does stand for, but all i'm writing
is guessing in the wild and Michael Allan might provide some feedback ;-)
