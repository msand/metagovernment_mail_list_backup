> Hello Mark,
>
> it's not about direct payment, it's about "shares".
> Everything people do is not a "one time task", but it has a purpose, thus,
> its transformational and not transactional.
> Therefore the task should not be paid for directly, but instead it should
> be paid in shares, so the one who did the task is in part "owner of the
> purpose (or project)".
> The sum of "shares" for every purpose decide how actual payment is
> distributed amongst those "shareholders".
>
> How many shares there are at every moment and so on... should be
> transparent.
> Planning Poker (a wideband delphi based method) can be run time after time
> again to decide how much shares should be offered to people who accomplish
> certain tasks.
> In addition there can be constant review to correct those initial guesses
> again and again.
>
> So its not a "bank check", but instead its a process where the amount of
> shares everyone under a common purpose has is evaluated time and time again.
> You can never know how much "revenue" you create, but the revenue is just
> distributed amongst the "shareholders". There is no employees and no wage,
> only shares for every task.
>
> The question is, how to refine this "planning poker process" ? :-)
