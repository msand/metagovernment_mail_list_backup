Hi Mike,

(At this point, I take this thread not to be about making changes to the 
mission statement or the vision statement or the elevator pitch, but 
about making a reflective start ...)

------------------------------------------------------------------------

    Mike> (II.) Metagov should be open to all start techniques and do
    its best to support each.

My view (to state it /sans/ nuance and qualification, for clarity) is 
that adopting either your original or amended *Principle II* will cause 
us to fail with respect to the mission of becoming able to usefully act 
as catalyst with respect to the global population.

Our openness to supporting any and all tools is not what will lead to 
that success. Our coming up with a really good set of tools is what will 
lead to that success.

Here is my alternative proposal for Principle II:

    ANYONE CAN PROPOSE ANYTHING. 

------------------------------------------------------------------------

    Ned> ... (Example: Ed proposes that, in our decision systems, there
    be no distinctions between governors and governed. I heartily agree
    with that proposal. Does that make Ed and me partisans?)

    Mike> No, it shouldn't. If you believe that a particular start
    effort needs to enforce a policy of "no distinctions", then you
    could enforce it within the context of that start effort. But you
    shouldn't enforce it universally within Metagov if doing so would
    violate (II).

Ned> In a community (or population) that makes decisions with respect to 
proposals through reaching consensus ("no unresolved objections"), there 
is no "enforcement" at the level of decision making. When (II) is stated 
as "anyone can propose anything", this is clear. (Yes?)

Our opportunity/responsibility as a community is to come up with good 
proposals, and to make good decisions with respect to those proposals, 
and to do good work in funding and implementing the proposals that have 
successfully passed through the "no unresolved objections" gauntlet. 
(Note that I have very slyly slipped in my preference that we use "no 
unresolved objections" as our decision rule ...) :^)

As a community, we do not have enough resources to support every 
proposed "start effort" (meaning project start?). We must pick and 
choose, carefully. As a practical matter, the openness of the community 
must be at the level of receiving any proposal, not at the level of 
indiscriminately supporting every proposal.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
