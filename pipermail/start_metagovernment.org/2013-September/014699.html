>      WHAT NOW?
> 
> We now have in hand 28 proposals. A couple of the proposals might even be seen as "bylaw" sorts of things.
> 
> For each proposal, our aim (as a "start group" I guess -- not clear who "we" is at this point) is to decide whether to implement the proposal as it stands, or to not implement the proposal as it stands.
> 
> (At the scale of large-populations, we must conceptualize that a modified proposal is a new proposal. However, at this informal, community-scale stage of the game -- since we have no need to notify strangers all over the world (who are not on the mailing list) when a modification is made -- we can get away with on-the-fly informal modification of proposals. So "as it stands" becomes "as it stands at the point that there are no more unresolved objections".)
> 
> Speaking to all readers of this email: When you read a proposal and have a response, the first thing you do is select the venue in which you wish to respond. You can respond by email to the list (create a new thread, for just that proposal), or you can make the proposal a new topic in AllAngles and respond to it there, or you can make the proposal a new topic in Vilfredo, TruthMapping, or any number of other venues, and then respond to it there. If you have selected a venue other than the email list, post a notification email to the list with a link to the proposal topic.
> 
> Any proposal to which no one responds will be taken to be "good to go". Since none of the proposals actually do anything so far as direct implementation, those "good to go" proposals will simply be available in the library (if we had a library) to serve as foundations for further proposing.
> 
> (The "question proposals" are a special case: if no one responds to them, they remain unanswered -- though they will still be "good to go" as questions.)
