Yes, but "consensus" is always present, only its scope varies, thus, how
many people are included.
If i propose something and am the only proponent, then "consensus" does
only include me.
As soon as someone else joins me, "consensus" exists between me and that
other person.

So, if i propose something, like go and play tennis, consensus between me
and that other guy is enough in order to make it happen.
There are other issues, where it needs more than a 2-person-consensus.

To me it sounds, as if you want nothing to be done as long as consensus is
not reached and people HAVE to discuss stuff, until consensus IS reached.
That could render lots of people inactive and unhappy with the situation.
To me it sounds, as if Mike wants everyone to do as they please and work
for themselves and with those who want to followa certain approach, and
listen occassionally on whats happening "in the community" and if there is
"room" for cooperation, then cooperation might happen.

So basically, those two different perspectives will converge at the same
thing, but come from opposite directions.
Your proposal to me sounds a bit to beaurocratic and people will soon start
to turn away or at least complain that nothing gets done beside all the
talking, while Mikes proposal might tend to prevent cooperation, because if
it doesnt perfectly fit their values or whatever, they rather mind their
own business and do their stuff rather than trying to build consensus or
cooperate on things.

So how can the situation be improved? (and as soon as real resources will
be involved, its necessary to have working mechanisms)



>
> Our opportunity/responsibility as a community is to come up with good
> proposals, and to make good decisions with respect to those proposals, and
> to do good work in funding and implementing the proposals that have
> successfully passed through the "no unresolved objections" gauntlet. (Note
> that I have very slyly slipped in my preference that we use "no unresolved
> objections" as our decision rule ...) :^)
>

Thing is, things that pass through the "no unresolved objections" gauntlet
are by no means guaranteed to stay there for long.
As time moves on, people learn, new "facts" occur and eventually people
change their mind.
So i would like all of this to be formulated as an iterative process loop
or something, rather than as a one time thing.



>
> As a community, we do not have enough resources to support every proposed
> "start effort" (meaning project start?). We must pick and choose,
> carefully. As a practical matter, the openness of the community must be at
> the level of receiving any proposal, not at the level of indiscriminately
> supporting every proposal.
>


No - again - everyone can use his/her own ressources and those who are
willing to follow a certain approach, to move something forward and through
this, it might be possible to show some results and eventually that will
convince skeptics, because sometimes its not possible to argue things
through, because at the end of the day, there is "assumptions" (like
axioms) in every discussion and those are based on personal experience or
believes and those can never be challenged by just discussing them.
sometimes it is necessary to provide a new experience which might be able
to shift certain believes or assumptions and only then can discussion move
forward.
