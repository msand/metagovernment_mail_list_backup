> On Fri, Sep 20, 2013 at 11:31 AM, Michael Allan <mike at zelea.com> wrote:
>> I'll try to answer questions about scoping and such, so you understand
>> the test setup.  Then I'll join you as a participant.
>> 
>> All of us in this thread are participants in the governance test.
>> Just to speak is enough.  We're an open, topical community defined by
>> the boundaries of the topic we're discussing.  It's like we're in a
>> country where the borders, rather than being drawn in a geographic
>> space, are instead drawn in topical space.  But the topic hasn't been
>> defined yet.  So we're in a country with no definite borders and no
>> name.  This is why we have an opportunity for self-governance.  If we
>> do it right, then we can govern the whole community (whatever we
>> define it to be) as such.  Here's a device that'll tell us if we're
>> doing it right: http://www.reddit.com/r/No7Mir/
>> 
>> It's a public mirror.  http://metagovernment.org/wiki/Public_mirror
>> A public mirror shows the total image of a topical community without
>> respecting any borders aside from those of the chosen topic.  Our
>> particular mirror assumes that we're discussing that topic right now,
>> which is why it shows this thread.  Otherwise it knows nothing about
>> us.  It'll just sit there waiting for further instructions.  It'll
>> listen to all the participants in the community (initially this
>> thread) and accept our governance.  The success of that governance
>> attempt will then be reflected in the image that the mirror shows us.
>> A public mirror is fairly easy to operate on a small scale, even a
>> clumsy prototype like this one (with just C and I cranking the gears
>> at the moment); but still, it cannot reflect a true image of the
>> community, one that we'll accept and identify with, unless it's well
>> governed by the community as a whole.  It's technically infeasible
>> within an open society.
>> 
>> So the mirror is like a canary in a coal mine.  But it's also useful
>> for other things aside from breaking when it's abused.  It's the only
>> thing that shows an open, topical community as a whole.  Nothing else
>> provides that service.  It's ours to provide, or it's nobody's.
>> 
>>
