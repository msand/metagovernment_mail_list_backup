(branching off from "Reflective start")

Two possible policies in regard to what's allowed, and what's not:

  IIa) Metagov is open to all start approaches and will do its best to
       support each.  You may do as you please with Metagov resources
       in the way of "start", provided it's compatible with the equal
       freedom of others in this regard.

       (what I suggested earlier)

  IIb) Metagov is open to all proposals concerning the use of its
       resources, but every proposal must meet with the unanimous
       consent of the Metagov group before being implemented.  There
       must be no unresolved objections.

       (paraphrased from Ned's response)

Ned Conner said:
> Our opportunity/responsibility as a community is to come up with
> good proposals, and to make good decisions with respect to those
> proposals, and to do good work in funding and implementing the
> proposals that have successfully passed through the "no unresolved
> objections" gauntlet.  (Note that I have very slyly slipped in my
> preference that we use "no unresolved objections" as our decision
> rule ...) :^)
>
> As a community, we do not have enough resources to support every
> proposed "start effort" (meaning project start?). We must pick and
> choose, carefully. As a practical matter, the openness of the
> community must be at the level of receiving any proposal, not at the
> level of indiscriminately supporting every proposal.

Neither variant (a or b) is actually in force.  The only rule in force
is the traditional one, which hovers somewhere in between.  It's not
written down anywhere that I know of.  It goes something like:

  IIt) Metagov is open to all sorts of initiatives.  But if you've an
       initiative you'd like to undertake here, please discuss it with
       the group first and try to accomodate people's sensitivities.

       (please correct this characterization if it's wrong)

I guess I'd prefer (IIa) myself, but I'm almost as happy with (IIt).
So it was unwise of me to raise this issue in the first place.  Better
to let sleeping dogs lie.
