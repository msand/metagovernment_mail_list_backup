The difference bridge might not fit here, in practice.  Votorola's
approach to argument modeling differs from what these folks are
attempting.  Although our trees/forests can be seen as formalizations
of argument, they're not in *discussion* form.  We take the argument
from discussion media where it's happening (actual argument) and we
reflect it in drafting and voting media.  The resulting image is
tree-like, as shown here: http://zelea.com/project/votorola/home.html

That's a view of a living argument as resolved through the "compound
lense" of text and dis/agreement.  It depends on a rule similar to
"one concept" that I call "atomic variation", illustrated here: *
http://zelea.com/w/Stuff:Votorola/p/position_space_rationalization
Atomic variation relates to difference bridging (as you mention) and
to various broader practices associated with patch flow. **

To be sure, these two forms of argument model (discursive form vs.
textual-dis/consensual) can be used in parallel; they're non-rival.
Which is cool, because I think we can benefit from both.
