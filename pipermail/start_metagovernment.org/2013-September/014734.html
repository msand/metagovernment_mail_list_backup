I wonder what the problem is with talking about "how to run specific
things"?
I think it might be helpful to give specific examples, so theory can be
grounded in reality or experience that people might have made or can relate
to.

Another thing is the "enforcement mechanism".
If i can agree to it or not strongly depends on the mechanism the vetters
use to decide whether something wil make it to the trunk or not. If its
consensus based, then i do not agree.
Because basing it on consensus will mean, single people have a veto to
EXCLUDE discussion participants from the trunk.
If the excluded participant becomes then a vetter himself, he can exclude
all messages from that vetter who always "veto"s to exclude him. So those
two fighting each other exclude each other from the trunk all the time and
nobody could do a thing...

Why not put it the other way around? Things by default go to the TRUNK and
only if its CONSENSUS to EXCLUDE them, will they not go to the TRUNK :-)
That way, nearly everything will go to the TRUNK and only if its really
something extreme, will it eventually be excluded.
