Hi Alex,

    Mike> Principle II, amended: Metagov should be open to all start
    techniques and do its best to support each.

    Ned> My view (to state it /sans/ nuance and qualification, for
    clarity) is that adopting either your original or amended *Principle
    II* will cause us to fail with respect to the mission of becoming
    able to usefully act as catalyst with respect to the global population.

    Ned> Our openness to supporting any and all tools is not what will
    lead to that success. Our coming up with a really good set of tools
    is what will lead to that success.

    Alex> Why?

Ned> Alex, I cannot begin to tell you just how *RIGHT ON!* I take your 
question to be. Your question is in my view precisely the sort of 
question we need to be asking at this point, as a community (as we 
prepare to handle money). Our decisions (such as whether to support all 
tools, projects, and "start techniques", or only the agreed-upon best) 
will have practical consequences.

The first general step toward answering your question is this: We (the 
community) need to figure out and agree upon what will constitute 
success, and how we will measure the degrees to which we are succeeding 
in the various dimensions of success.

The straightforward way to begin the work of taking this step is to open 
the floor to relevant proposals: what will constitute success, and how 
will success be measured?

There are various pages in the wiki that contain material responsive to 
this "request for proposals", and if we searched through the list 
archive, we would find lots of material responsive to the request.

All those "buried" proposals, together with new proposals, need to be 
codified and considered. We need to reach agreement, and then bring the 
agreed upon definitions and measures of success systematically to bear 
upon our decision making. (Easy to say, hard to do ...)

------------------------------------------------------------------------

Having applauded your question generally, I will now turn to responding 
to it specifically.

In Mike's (II), he is talking specifically about "start techniques". As 
a preliminary step, we need to ask Mike to explain to us what he means 
by the term "start technique", and maybe give us some examples. Then we 
can go from there. :^)

------------------------------------------------------------------------
END
