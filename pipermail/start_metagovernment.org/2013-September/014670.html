Hi Alex,

I went through your email and "mined" it for proposals that might be 
ready for formal consideration. Here is the list. (The list includes 
questions, which are one particular type of proposal.)

(Anyone/Everyone: feel free to edit the way that I have stated each 
proposal and to extend the list. Also feel free to start a whole new 
alternative list, that includes yucky things that I have left out, such 
as voting and polyarchy and being mean to babies.)

------------------------------------------------------------------------

   1. Membership: Uncoerced

      Any sentient being of the world (or elsewhere) may freely choose
      to become a member of [the start group named Freetimea?]. Any
      member may freely choose to cease to be a member of the group.

      ------------------------------------------------------------------------
   2. Decision Making: Direct Democracy

      Each member of [the group] is free to participate in making any
      decision that the group makes. Each member participates in
      decision making on equal footing.

      ------------------------------------------------------------------------
   3. Participation: Through Proposing

      Members participate in decision making exclusively through
      formally proposing. Votes cast apart from proposing (through
      ballots, polling, "like buttons", "dislike buttons" and such)
      provide information only, and have no formal effect on decision
      making.

      ------------------------------------------------------------------------
   4. Participation Scope: Anyone Can Propose Anything

      This rule is intended to provide to each individual the greatest
      possible amount of public autonomy while maintaining equal public
      autonomy across all individuals.

      ------------------------------------------------------------------------
   5. Decision Rule: No Unresolved Objections

      The decision rule for group decisions is that a proposal is
      cleared to be implemented at the point that it faces no unresolved
      objections. Objections are proposed. Objections are resolved
      through successive, recursive proposing.

      ------------------------------------------------------------------------
   6. What resolves (can resolve) an objection?

      One form of answer might be to accumulate a list of use-case
      examples, the way that case law accumulates precedent-setting cases.

      (We resolve objections routinely, in ordinary conversations.
      Example: "Now I know what you are going to say, but ... ")

      ------------------------------------------------------------------------
   7. What is a valid objection?

      (list of criteria?)
      (list of types?)

      (Alex: You asked ...)

      ------------------------------------------------------------------------
   8. List of Intersubjective Criteria

      Can we create a list of "intersubjective criteria", that we use in
      ordinary discourse to discriminate between valid and invalid
      proposals (including proposed objections)?

      (Alex: You requested this list.)

      (I seem to recall reading somewhere that some say three types of
      criteria are used: logical, true, and authentic.)

      (Somebody with a strong philosophy background might be able to
      score us this list, or show us that it does not exist.)

      ------------------------------------------------------------------------
   9. Process To Create Relevant Criteria List

      Can we formulate the general rule/process that generates the
      criteria list for a specific proposal that is made?

      (Alex: You asked for this.)

      ------------------------------------------------------------------------
  10. Apply All Relevant Proposal Filters To Each Proposal

      Our new decision process must adequately solve the problem of
      reliably and objectively applying all existing and relevant
      potential objections (a.k.a. "relevant criteria", a.k.a. "proposal
      filters") to each new proposal. (There may be hundreds of relevant
      filters for a particular new proposal, that must be drawn from the
      general filter pool that may contain millions ...)

      (The Blinap design addresses this problem. If there are other
      designs that do, I don't know about them.)

      ------------------------------------------------------------------------
  11. Objective Interpretation of Proposal Filters

      Our new decision process must adequately solve the problem of
      making the intrepretation of existing (agreed-upon) "relevant
      criteria" (a.k.a. "proposal filters") as objective as possible.

      (There is probably a better way to state this proposal. When
      judges and juries apply laws made by others, they are doing the
      same sort of work, which is held to an "objective interpretation"
      standard.)

      ------------------------------------------------------------------------
  12. Professional Application of Proposal Filters

      Our new decision process must automate/professionalize application
      of agreed-upon proposal filters, so that general participants can
      concentrate on edge cases and considerations, where experience is
      lacking and/or opinions differ.

      (Alex: this is my attempt to state your proposal.)

      (Interpretation by professionals in tandem with interpretation by
      general participants can make for a more reliable system. The
      Blinap design has this, and includes checks on the professionals.)

      ------------------------------------------------------------------------
  13. How many objections can be raised by people?

      (No limit to the number or recursive depth of proposed objections?)

      ------------------------------------------------------------------------
  14. How long will the process of resolving objections take?

      (Decision made in three days if there are no new objections?)

      ------------------------------------------------------------------------
  15. There has to be a "fair process".

      (Alex: this is your proposal. I guess you intend that "fairness"
      be a criterion in assessing proposed decision processes. If you
      could elaborate the proposal a bit -- on its own thread -- that
      would be great. What constitutes "fair"?)

      ------------------------------------------------------------------------
  16. Structured Decision Process

      There has to be a structured process, or at least a set of
      tools/options that each participant could take in order to raise
      objections or mark objections raised by others as invalid.

      (Alex: again this is your proposal. Is the proposal merely
      distinguishing between structured and unstructured, or are there
      more specific criteria that the structuring must satisfy?)

      (Feel free to consult with Bentley, who is all over the
      structuring issue.)

      ------------------------------------------------------------------------
  17. Meta Process to Improve Decision System

      There must (eventually) be a meta process that can improve the
      rules surrounding that structured process or set of tools/options.

      (Alex: again your proposal. Ready to be put onto its own thread,
      and elaborated.)

      (And also, the Blinap design has this as an intrinsic feature.)

      ------------------------------------------------------------------------
  18. Decision System Must Evolve.

      It must be ensured that people can evolve the process on their
      own, based on their experience.

      (Alex: Do you have a list of design criteria to propose that can
      be used in evaluating proposed process designs, and that will
      ensure that the selected process can evolve?)

      (And also, the Blinap design has this as an intrinsic feature.)

      ------------------------------------------------------------------------
  19. The process must be FAST.

      Speed is important, because the faster it is (even if its less
      perfect), the more objections can be handled. A "perfect process"
      that takes forever is not perfect. Speed has to be included in
      measuring the value of the process.

      (Alex: Do you have a list of design criteria to propose that can
      be used in evaluating proposed process designs, and that will
      ensure that the selected process is FAST?)

      ------------------------------------------------------------------------
  20. System Should Survive Stupid People.

      The decision making process must be able to operate efficiently
      and effectively even when "stupid people" are participating.

      (Alex: This proposal of yours is laying the groundwork to enable
      objecting to a proposed process design on the basis that stupid
      people could gum up the works. This proposal would create a type
      of valid objection with respect to proposed process designs.)

      ------------------------------------------------------------------------
  21. No Gaming The System

      The decision process must adequately solve the problem that rich
      and powerful special interests will attempt to game the system,
      and corrupt it, as soon as doing so seems worthwhile to them.

      (Alex: again, this proposal of yours is laying the groundwork to
      enable objecting to a proposed process design on the basis that
      special interests could game it. This proposal would create a type
      of valid objection with respect to proposed process designs.)

      ------------------------------------------------------------------------
  22. No Veto or Filibuster

      The decision process must be effectively immune to filibustering
      and such, and must not give anyone straight veto power.

      (Alex: This proposal again creates types of valid objections. We
      may need to sharpen the statement of the proposal, and may need to
      put in a list that shows what "and such" includes. Our design
      requirement proposal must be sufficiently clear and detailed to
      enable "objective" interpretation when applied to new proposals.)

      ------------------------------------------------------------------------
  23. Teaching/Training Newcomers

      The decision system must adequately solve the problem of rapidly
      and efficiently and inexpensively bringing newcomers up to speed
      to enable them to begin participating effectively.

      (Sometimes this proposal will function as a basis for objections,
      and sometimes it will function as a basis for justifications --
      that defend against objections.)

      ------------------------------------------------------------------------
  24. Compensation For Work Done

      The decision system should appropriately compensate workers for
      valuable work done. It should not depend on people "working for free".

      (This requires that there be processes that determine what the
      "appropriate compensation" will be.)

      (Blinap has such processes.)

      ------------------------------------------------------------------------
  25. Payment in Money (Not Stock)

      The decision system should pay for work done in the form of money
      (not stock).

      (Alex: :^) Since I am writing this draft of the proposal, I get to
      pick which way it goes. You are free to reverse the preference
      when you start your new thread, in which the relative pros and
      cons of the two forms of payment are discussed.) :^)

      ------------------------------------------------------------------------
  26. Payment Through A Bank

      To enable payment, the decision system needs to be associated with
      an online bank.

      (In the beginning -- as our alpha testing phase -- we can if we
      wish create our own community money, and have our own private bank
      that handles that money.)

      ------------------------------------------------------------------------
  27. Partnerships & Projects

      It is proposed that all of the work of the decision system be done
      by partnerships, working under project contracts. (A partnership
      may have from 1 to 24 partners.)

      (Alex: this proposal is my agreement with and elaboration of your
      objection to the existence of the employer/employee relationship.
      If we say "no" to the employer/employee relation, we must say
      "yes" to some other arrangement.)

      ------------------------------------------------------------------------
  28. Project Contracts

      Project contracts are proposed into the system library (which
      makes them available for selection), and then are selected from
      the library when it is time to initiate a project.

      (Alex: I *DO NOT* want us to create tasks/goals on the fly
      (without opportunity for all stakeholders to be notified and
      respond), and I *DO* want the process of initiating a project to
      be fast and easy. The two-step process above can in many cases
      accomplish both.)

------------------------------------------------------------------------


      WHAT NOW?

We now have in hand 28 proposals. A couple of the proposals might even 
be seen as "bylaw" sorts of things.

For each proposal, our aim (as a "start group" I guess -- not clear who 
"we" is at this point) is to decide whether to implement the proposal as 
it stands, or to not implement the proposal as it stands.

(At the scale of large-populations, we must conceptualize that a 
modified proposal is a new proposal. However, at this informal, 
community-scale stage of the game -- since we have no need to notify 
strangers all over the world (who are not on the mailing list) when a 
modification is made -- we can get away with on-the-fly informal 
modification of proposals. So "as it stands" becomes "as it stands at 
the point that there are no more unresolved objections".)

Speaking to all readers of this email: When you read a proposal and have 
a response, the first thing you do is select the venue in which you wish 
to respond. You can respond by email to the list (create a new thread, 
for just that proposal), or you can make the proposal a new topic in 
AllAngles and respond to it there, or you can make the proposal a new 
topic in Vilfredo, TruthMapping, or any number of other venues, and then 
respond to it there. If you have selected a venue other than the email 
list, post a notification email to the list with a link to the proposal 
topic.

Any proposal to which no one responds will be taken to be "good to go". 
Since none of the proposals actually do anything so far as direct 
implementation, those "good to go" proposals will simply be available in 
the library (if we had a library) to serve as foundations for further 
proposing.

(The "question proposals" are a special case: if no one responds to 
them, they remain unanswered -- though they will still be "good to go" 
as questions.)

> people have time to learn it and change their habits.
> Everyone knows that. In school you dont learn everything at once, but 
> some thing after another.
> So not even is it possible to introduce a "partial solution" a.k.a  a 
> single feature x and introduce the next feature later, it is instead 
> very much desirable to do so.
> You start with basic features and bolt on more complex ones.
> The same way you learn math ...not starting with universal sets 
> algebra or category theory or calculus or game theory, but rather with 
> basic stuff.
>
> BUT: If people have to learn basic stuff ANYWAY before they are able 
> to use more advanced features, why not introduce the basic features 
> first (our "lowest common denominator") implement it, make them use it 
> and learn on the way, so that we not only have our thoughts and 
> theory, but also get feedback from the experience of implementing it 
> and having it used. So chances increase we get feature x and y "more 
> right" than without that practical feedback.
>
> And even if our tools grow to hundreds of features, its still 
> important to only have basic features available at entry level and to 
> not require all those advanced features, because people new to the 
> system, because they joined the usage late, still have to learn the 
> basics first.

> the things to be intuitive and want to hack right away... like with
