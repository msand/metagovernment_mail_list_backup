In your vision, how does ownership of shares relate to group/project 
decision making?

On 9/26/2013 4:39 PM, Alexander Praetorius wrote:
> 1. New shares will only exist when todos/tasks are done by people.
> 2. If people are willing to SELL their shares, then thats ok, so be 
> it. If ALL people are willing to SELL their shares, then thats ok, so 
> be it.
> 3. All shares which are bought will deprecate, so even if people have 
> sold all their shares and start earning new shares by finishing new 
> tasks or todos, the one who bought all the shares previously will 
> constantly use shares with the average rate of depreciation. The only 
> way to get new shares it so buy new shares (if people are still 
> willing to sell them) or to accomplish todos/tasks, so that new shares 
> are created.
>
> I do not see a problem here.

>                 Hello Mark,
>
>                 it's not about direct payment, it's about "shares".
>                 Everything people do is not a "one time task", but it
>         has a
>                 purpose, thus, its transformational and not transactional.
>                 Therefore the task should not be paid for directly, but
>                 instead it should be paid in shares, so the one who
>         did the
>                 task is in part "owner of the purpose (or project)".
>                 The sum of "shares" for every purpose decide how actual
>                 payment is distributed amongst those "shareholders".
>
>                 How many shares there are at every moment and so on...
>         should
>                 be transparent.
>                 Planning Poker (a wideband delphi based method) can be run
>                 time after time again to decide how much shares should be
>                 offered to people who accomplish certain tasks.
>                 In addition there can be constant review to correct those
>                 initial guesses again and again.
>
>                 So its not a "bank check", but instead its a process
>         where the
>                 amount of shares everyone under a common purpose has is
>                 evaluated time and time again.
>                 You can never know how much "revenue" you create, but the
>                 revenue is just distributed amongst the
>         "shareholders". There
>                 is no employees and no wage, only shares for every task.
>
>                 The question is, how to refine this "planning poker
>         process" ? :-)
>

>                                     Hi Alex (and anyone else interested),
>
>                                     Metagovernance work is not like
>         the work
>                                 of growing zucchinis, or
>                                     the work of writing code. The
>         "products"
>                                 of metagovernance work
>                                     (proposals, and agreements/decisions
>                                 concerning proposals) are not
>                                     generally amenable to free market
>         pricing.
>
>
>                                 Proposals as such are not and if it were
>                                 possible one shouldnt do it, because
>         otherwise
>                                 nobody would use them.
>                                 But a proposal is only a proposal and
>         it will
>                                 only come to life if there is software
>                                 implementing the proposal.
>
>
>                                     At this point, the Metagovernment
>         entity
>                                 has no national currency
>                                     income to distribute, just as
>         startups,
>                                 generally, have no income
>                                     at the outset.
>
>
>                                 Yes, but that does not prevent
>         metagovernment
>                                 from creating its own "artificial
>         currency".
>                                 Metagovernment could make it
>         transparent what
>                                 it is that people work on and reward
>         people
>                                 for doing people, by adding points to
>         their
>                                 account. Those points could be
>         "shares" and
>                                 those shares could depreciate too. If
>         there
>                                 will be "income" at some point in
>         time, those
>                                 "points" could be used to distribute
>         it among
>                                 those who have "points".
>
>
>
>                                     Until we have an income (from
>         donations,
>                                 from individuals choosing
>                                     to "invest" in a better future for the
>                                 planet), we can do what
>                                     startups often do. We can create
>         our own
>                                 "stock-option green
>                                     stamps" out of thin air, and
>         distribute
>                                 them for work done, with
>                                     the possibility that at some
>         future time
>                                 the green stamps can be
>                                     traded in for "real" currencies
>         (from the
>                                 future donation income).
>
>
>                                 yes :-)
>
>
>                                     If we do this, it puts us
>         immediately in
>                                 the position of having
>                                     money to handle.
>
>
>
>                                 yeah ;-)
>                                 But if we do so, this will be challenging.
>                                 There needs to be transparency and a
>         machenism
>                                 to assess the work that is done by people.
>                                 "Scrum's Planning Poker" comes to mind:
>         http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=bEOd5G_p_1Y
>                                 (afaik a variation of the Wideband Delphi
>                                 estimation method)
>
>
>
>                                     Once we have the money in hand, my
>                                 proposal is that compensation
>                                     for Metagovernance work be determined
>                                 democratically, through
>                                     proposing and agreeing/deciding.
>
>
>                                 I feel that there has to be a predictable
>                                 mechanism which prevents dictatorship of
>                                 majority. It should be about process
>         and tasks
>                                 and not about people. I'm not sure how
>         to do
>                                 it, but its important to put a lot of
>         thought
>                                 into this.
>
>                                     (I have written out detailed
>                                 specifications for one way that this
>                                     might be accomplished over on the
>         wiser
>                                 website. But in this
>                                     thread, we are only considering
>         whether or
>                                 not, in principle, to
>                                     determine compensation democratically.
>                                 After we have agreed in
>                                     principle, then we can get into
>         deciding
>                                 the details of how to do it.)
>
>
>                                 Do you know "Scrum's Planning Poker"
>         or the
>                                 "Wideband Delphi Estimation Method"?
>                                 What do you think of this approach? The
>                                 average business day involves lots of
>         tasks
>                                 which are not easily measured and some of
>                                 those tasks cannot be measured at all,
>         because
>                                 during the time you would like to
>         measure it,
>                                 its not even clear that a specific
>         tasks must
>                                 be accomplished in order to fullfill some
>                                 other task.
>                                 Such a mechanism should not prevent people
>                                 from doing some work which is
>         necessary, but
>                                 not rewarded with points (so maybe the
>                                 software should include an option to
>         create
>                                 new tasks on the fly and have them be
>                                 estimated in "points", or maybe there
>         has to
>                                 be a review/retrospective process)
>         ....it may
>                                 not be a beaurocratic nightmare too....
>                                 And last bot probably not least,
>                                 principal-agent-conflicts should be
>         avoided,
>                                 so people do not try assign points to
>         tasks
>                                 for other political reasons...
