What really makes everything hard for me, and eventually for others, is the

So, if you don't care how things are "named", then eventually we can change

My current understanding tells me:
1. There is many *"discussion mediums"* (forum, mailinglist, facebook,
twitter, googlePlus, blogs, list_goes_on_forever....)
2. *"People"* write *"Messages"* on those mediums (chat messages, blog
postings, blog comments, short messages, emails, forum postings,
list_goes_on_forever....)
=> creating a feed of messages
3. Every time, someone writes a message, all *"Owners of a Tributary
Node"*decide upon messages they believe are in scope of the
*"Topical Community"* they wanna build.
(they use 'ack' and 'nak' and might even choose a different mechanism,
apart from consensus)
=> creating a filtered feed of messages
4. Multiple owners of tributary node's can negotiate with *"Owners of a
Trunk Node"* to forward their input to them, which will again...
=> create a filtered feed of messages


For me, it's totally possible to imagine another "trunk node" after the
current "trunk node", thus, building a tree like structure.



I do not believe, that its possible to create "many trunks" within
metagovernment.org itself.
I think, its possible to create at least "one tributary node" and provide a
very very nice interface to the
=> feed of messages that come out of it
(one that makes people curious and which people like to read and "subscribe
to"

EVENTUALLY, it might be possible to create "many tribulatory nodes" for all
metagovernment projects and PERHAPS, there will be a chance to create a
"TRUNK NODE" that gathers all the inputs from all those projects....

...in essence, there will be a "twitter like structure"
...but metagovernment.org will only be a small part of it
There is different communities in the world, trying to achieve the same
thing as metagovernment.org does and they might be "tributary nodes too" or
"trunk nodes" for their associated projects, which are the "tributary
nodes".

So then again, assume metagovernment can build some kind of "trunk node",
and those other communities, that might even come from slightly different
backgrounds, so that they currently do not appear as "potential partners",
will have "trunk nodes" too and then again, we could create a NEW "trunk
node" that gets all the input from the metagovernment.org "trunk node" and
their "trunk nodes".


So my current oppinion about what we should do is:

Try to come up with a SIMPLE SOLUTION (simple is hard), to create an
absolutely awesome mechanism and easy to use tools, so that we can somehow
decide as a small fraction of the overall community (thats us, talking
right here) upon WHAT kind of stuff goes to OUR "trunk" or lets just call
it "tributary node".

Our "tributary node" will become a "trunk node", as soon as we get input
from other communities...


If those tools are nice and our mechanism to decide what content we want to
"publish", so to speak, are awesome, then there might be a chance, that
others will FORK our approach and it might go VIRAL :-)


------------------
In essence, this whole thing will still build up a "pyramid", but at least
it might be a very flexible pyramid where "coalitions" can constantly
change, because the "switching costs" are very small ....eventually, but
this is another thing we might need to discuss :-)
