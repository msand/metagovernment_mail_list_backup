Whats the process of deciding upon this? How many objections can be raised

> Any time anybody sees a problem with the existing set of recognized valid
> objections, the existence of the problem can be proposed, and solutions
> (new kinds of valid objections, removal of existing valid objections,

>

I don't believe in this.
Objections can be very specific to each proposal that is made, you cannot
list all possible objections that could be made in response to all possible
kind of proposals.
That approach seems to be doomed. There has to be a "fair process", that
might not be perfect all the time but at least might be a good start that
could be improved when experience gives one new insights. I think there has
to be a structured process, or at least a set of tools/options that each
participant could take in order to raise objections or mark objections
raised by others as invalid. And eventually a meta process that can improve
the rules surrounding that process or tools/options.
This must be a fair process (at least as fair as possible) and it must be
ensured, that people can evolve it on their own, based on their experience.
In addition, it has to be a FAST PROCESS. Speed is important, because the
faster it is (even if its less perfect), the more objections can be handled.

A "perfect process" that takes forever is not perfect. Speed has to be
included in measuring the value of the process.

But it should not be a beaurocratic process, but a fast one. The issue we
have to solve is, how to evaluate how many shares which people get and for

There needs to be a system where people can log in to see their amount of
shares and what to do with them.
It's a huge undertaking and if you can spend those kind of shares or use
them to have more votes than someone who just joined and so on...

its own special set of rules surrounding it.

I very much hope, some day we will implement something like that, but it
has to start somewhere.

For Example:
Michael Allan has put up some "job vacanccies" around votorola.
This is not a good sign when it comes to shares/money, because Michael
Allen creates a employer/employee relationship, that means, Michael Allen
PAYS the employee so that the employee DOES what Michael Allen as "the
boss" will COME UP with.
A better approach, but a much more difficult one would be to: SPLIT UP the
project into "tasks" or at least "goals" that could be or should be
accomplished by someone else + set a reward/bounty on those (a portion of
shares or something).
These shares have to be granted by Michael Allen OR ... if he opens the
issue to the community and the community agrees on its importance, it will
be granted by several people, not just Michael Allan.
The one who takes on that "task" or "mission" could himself invite others
to help him and give them part of the bounty.

In a perfect world, this bounty will always be SHARES of the Metagovernment
Project as a whole.

But if you want to be able to create those "tasks/goals" on the fly and if
you want it a fast process to determine the bounty (in terms of shares)",
it has to be an easy and fast process.
the faster and easier it gets, the more often people might use it because
the barrier in doing so is lower than anything else.
If its a difficult process, most simple tasks will not use the mechanism,
but only huge tasks will and simple ones will be solved in non-standard
ways between people who know each other...

I agree, we need all of the above.
I disagree that "partial solutions" wind up being no solution at all.
And my reason for this is:
We already HAVE a complete system that solves all the issues life throws at
us, you, me, everyone else.
That solution is the status quo with all its imperfections.

A "partial solution" that would be no solution at all is to me:
A transition from one complete solution (status quo) to another complete
soltution (status quo COMPOSED/MERGED with feature x), where feature x is
what you call partial solution.

What you say with "all-of-the-above" or that we need a complete system
means to me:
Merge the status quo with LOTS OF FEATURES at once.
Its hard to get that right and even if the theory is sound, its even harder
to make lots of people change lots of habits over night, so they wont
accept it.
Even if its perfect, it has to be introduced one feature at a time, so
people have time to learn it and change their habits.
Everyone knows that. In school you dont learn everything at once, but some
thing after another.
So not even is it possible to introduce a "partial solution" a.k.a  a
single feature x and introduce the next feature later, it is instead very
much desirable to do so.
You start with basic features and bolt on more complex ones.
The same way you learn math ...not starting with universal sets algebra or
category theory or calculus or game theory, but rather with basic stuff.

BUT: If people have to learn basic stuff ANYWAY before they are able to use
more advanced features, why not introduce the basic features first (our
"lowest common denominator") implement it, make them use it and learn on
the way, so that we not only have our thoughts and theory, but also get
feedback from the experience of implementing it and having it used. So
chances increase we get feature x and y "more right" than without that
practical feedback.

And even if our tools grow to hundreds of features, its still important to
only have basic features available at entry level and to not require all
those advanced features, because people new to the system, because they
joined the usage late, still have to learn the basics first.
...and they wont read books, they want immediate results, they want the
things to be intuitive and want to hack right away... like with writing an
email :-)

So what we need to find is a "merge process" :-) that means tools and stuff
that helps us to "increase synchronisation" between our thoughts through
"constant communication" that is productive. There for we need helpful
tools to automate parts of the discussion that can be automated (e.g.
instead of someone to manually copy stuff from the email list to our wiki
or instead of having someone to look up the email list archive.... and
whatever else, maybe the system can do it for us)

I believe, even though all the "metagovernment parties" co-exist with the
same goal, its ONLY because the degree of communication is not dense enough.
If it were, we could use every imaginable synergy effect and concentrate
only about the edge cases where our experience and oppinions differ. we
would learn about each others way to think and pull off experiments
together to check our assumptions, and so forth...


The process doesnt have to be perfect, it must only be so good, that the
"rate of benefits through synchronisation via communication" exceeds the
"rate of benefit by doing something on his own".
As soon as thats the case, people will collaborate, because it gives you
benefits faster.
So all we have to do is to lower the barriers and thats why SPEEED is
vitally important, no matter what kind of process or system you come up
with.
