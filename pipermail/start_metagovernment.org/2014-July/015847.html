On Jul 17, 2014, at 7:49 AM, Pietro Speroni di Fenizio <metagovernment at pietrosperoni.it> wrote:

> We are a group, where each of us has his own idea of how we should proceed. How can we possibly agree?

Exactly!  

The way I see it, we’re a disparate group of individuals, each with our individual visions of how to successfully manage the decision making of an enormously (if not infinitely) complex system. Perhaps it’s a bit idealistic to imagine that any single vision (system or method) will ever sufficiently address the massive diversity of demand encompassed by the whole. Any comprehensive solution to the question "How can we best govern ourselves?” will very likely include *most of the above*.

On a similar note...I personally don’t think our coming to *agreement* is either crucial or significant for what is actually developing within our group. In my mind, this list is a crucible. We discuss, challenge, argue, and debate in order to continually refine our individual visions with the feedback that comes from the unending brawl of 'justifiable' opinion. I think it’s futile to imagine that we will ever agree on anything substantial.  Or that we will ever really need to.   Maybe someday…..it’s a nice thought anyway.  ;-)

Until then, let’s put the *fun* back in ‘dysfunctional' and continue on with the “I’m right vs. No, I’m right” method.   I think it has potential….as long as we don’t take ourselves (and our strategies to save democracy) too seriously.
