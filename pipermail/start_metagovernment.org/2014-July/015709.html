Hi Max,

Sure, you could choose any technology or tooling or plattform you wish to 
build upon. The qK do not enforce anything here.

Please note that the "qKonsens" (qK) is an open methodology rather than a 
concrete implementation. The qK describes how an online decision-making 
process could look like by defining processes, tools and actions that are 
based on a theory of a corruption-, hierarchy- and authority- free 
discourse.

Even if we at the working group AG MFT are currently developing a specific 
implementation of the qK - based on d!sco (Discussion Ontology) as the 
communication protocol - this should be seen as a proof of concept. Everyone 
is free to develop her own implementation of the qK.

Cheers
