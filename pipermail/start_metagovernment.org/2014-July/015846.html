> On 17 July 2014 15:26, Scott Raney <scott at metacard.com 
> <mailto:scott at metacard.com>> wrote:
>
>     On Wed, Jul 16, 2014 at 9:09 PM, Mark Frischmuth
>     <mark at democracylab.org <mailto:mark at democracylab.org>> wrote:
>     > Dunno about the better system.  A google search returned this:
>     > http://www.openstv.org/, which seems to allow us to create a web
>     based
>     > instant runoff vote.
>
>     Nice system.  We should definitely run an election on it sometime to
>     put it through its paces.
>
>     > I think this would be preferable to voting on
>     > competing proposals yes/no one at a time, which doesn't properly
>     frame the
>     > choice.
>
>     I disagree, at least for this particular vote.  Evaluating
>     proposal-by-proposal is definitely less efficient than being able to
>     rank order them (something I suggested earlier as a feature for
>     Vilfredo) but this is the *only* method that is guaranteed to provide
>     a decision acceptable to the majority.  The problem with rank ordering
>     is that the top vote getter will be installed even if everyone thinks
>     it sucks.  Better to be able to just reject the proposal outright, and
>     modify it or start over if that happens.
>
>     > Concerning the irony, I agree, it's delicious.  Reinforcing a
>     past point, I
>     > think deliberation and decision are separate steps in a process,
>     and we're
>     > muddling them together.
>
>     Agreed, which is what my system will do: During the creation phase
>     only the most interested parties will rate and comment on a proposal,
>     which may be modified to improve it.  During the vote phase, the
>     previous ratings and comments can be used by the majority of the
>     population to evaluate the proposal.
>
>     So, I request that you and Ned redo your votes: If you object to the
>     *process* and don't want to legitimize it, change your vote to
>     "abstain" (since there is no way to "unvote" on Loomio).  If you
>     object to particular features of my proposal, please follow the
>     instructions in the proposal and in the announcement letter and vote
>     "no" and include a comment as to why.  The idea of updating a proposal
>     while it's being voted on is kind of wiggly, but it's the system I
>     (and Loomio) defined and you'll just have to trust that I won't pull a
>     fast one and totally replace the content one second before the vote
>     closes (and if I do, I'd expect someone to immediately make another
>     proposal to replace it, which would be an example of cycling or
>     "flapping", but that is a superior mode to be operating in than the
>     current "noop" mode).
>       Regards,
