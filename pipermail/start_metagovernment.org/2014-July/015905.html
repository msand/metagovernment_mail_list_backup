>>>>> Groups No!  Looks to me like you're just kicking the can down the
>>>>> road.  Assuming you can even get multiple groups (which I doubt), how would
>>>>> you integrate the results?  It'd end up just like the Vilfredo experiment:
>>>>> Lots of candidate material, no decision.  Process rather than results.
>>>>>
>>>>> Speaking of which, for those who aren't on Loomio, the Loomio vote for
>>>>> my Home Page proposal failed 3 to 2.  None of the 3 no votes made any
>>>>> comment about *why* they voted no other than a vague claim that it wasn't
>>>>> proper procedure to even hold a vote.  Which puzzles me.  I've read
>>>>> thousands of pages of the research in this area over the last few months
>>>>> and yet never came across a single case like we have here where people
>>>>> insist on voting on the voting method before they vote on any particular
>>>>> issue.  Voting systems like Condorcet and Borda are *optimizations*, and
>>>>> will not fundamentally alter the decisionmaking process.  Indeed, I'd be
>>>>> willing to bet that we'd come up with the same decision voting on each
>>>>> proposal separately on Loomio and just picking the one with the most yes
>>>>> votes.  Especially considering the main benefit of these other voting
>>>>> algorithms is to thwart people trying to game the system by appealing to a
>>>>> subset of the voters and relying on "tyranny of the majority" to deal with
>>>>> the rest (none of us are trying to game this Home Page decision, are we?)
>>>>>
>>>>> Since none of them will deign to explain the justification for their
>>>>> behavior I have very little to go on, but my gut tells me that the main
>>>>> problem is still the fantasy about collaborative government and consensus,
>>>>> disagreement about which is the very reason why the Home page was taken
>>>>> down.  It also explains why Loomio gets such short shrift because it just
>>>>> doesn't even claim to support either even though it would work fine (albeit
>>>>> somewhat inefficiently) for any decision we have to make.  As I see it our
>>>>> main problem is not choosing a voting method, it's that members of this
>>>>> group can't even agree on what it means to make a group decision.  A
>>>>> secondary problem being that those members who insist on
>>>>> collaboration/consensus *also* refuse to debate that issue (e.g., *why*
>>>>> isn't a vote on Loomio binding?) or indeed the issue of whether or not the
>>>>> old decision rule (80% majority according to Ed) is even still in effect.
>>>>>  Propose all the clever things you want, but if you're not willing to face
>>>>> the facts about human behavior (even *our* behavior) this group will
>>>>> continue to shed members who actually value getting things done over
>>>>> handwaving, in which case we might as well ask Ed to just take down the
>>>>> site and sell the domain to somebody who actually wants to use it for
>>>>> something productive.
>>>>>
>>>>> As for Airesis, I'm willing to give that a try, but as I told Jacopo
>>>>> previously, it didn't seem to be working correctly last time I tried it,
>>>>> and the language barrier goes both ways (certainly his English is vastly
>>>>> superior to my Italian!)  If he wants to run a full beta-test of the
>>>>> software, count me in, but *he* will have to organize it and lead us
>>>>> through the process or it just isn't going to happen.
>>>>>
>>>>> Another proposal: It occurs to me that another one of our problems a
>>>>> sort of reverse Luddism, where we seem to be expecting some fancy new
>>>>> software to magically reach inside each of our brains and extract (or
>>>>> worse, manufacture) consensus.  Ain't going to happen.  There are few
>>>>> enough people involved now we could collaboratively design the Home Page
>>>>> using just this mailing list, each of us just posting our requirements (as
>>>>> I have done *twice* now) and any one of us integrating the result to be
>>>>> confirmed with another mailing-list vote.  If you agree, please post your
