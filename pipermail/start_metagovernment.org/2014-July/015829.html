If a proposal is created and 5 people support it. The "island" consists of
5 people and the rule can be binding for them.
(contract law) or it could also be seen as "moraly binding".
If 10 proposals pop into existance, each supported by 5 people, that
translates then into 10 "islands" each with 5 people.
If 2 "islands" decide to work an a proposal which is then supported by all
people on those islands, then the 10 "islands" are ideally reduced to 9
islands, where one of the 9 islands has now 10 supporters, because 2
islands merged into 1.

Of course, maybe the "proposal" that merged 2 islands into one, reducing
the total amount of islands from 10 to 9, doesnt manage to do so, but
instead convinced 4 people of island A and 3 people of island B, then
afterwards, its 11 islands, but now one of those 11 islands has 7
supporters and two others have 2 and 1 supporters.

Maybe those remaining supporters are hardliners or maybe they start attract
new supporters or refine their proposal ...to something more specific, that
caters more to their needs then what was possible when they were still 5...


Who knows what this all grows into.
what do you mean with "gridlock" or "status quo" in the above described
context?
