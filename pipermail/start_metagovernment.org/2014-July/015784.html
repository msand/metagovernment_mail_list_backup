On Wed, Jul 9, 2014 at 7:56 PM, Ned Conner <npconner at earthlink.net> wrote:
> I vote "no". My "no" means: I never agreed to the 80% rule, and now I am not
> agreeing to a 65% rule.

Yeah, that's the thing isn't it?  I never voted for the 80% rule
either, and hope that I was never naive enough to even do such a
thing.  But now Ed says we're stuck with it, like it or not.  Is that
democracy?

> Counting votes transforms popularity into power, without reason/discourse
> playing any formal part in the process: mob rule.

This applies to representative democracy, sure, but it's hard to see
how people voting directly for a law that affects them too is an
example of "mob rule".  Voting is a necessary part of the process of
reaching, or at least verifying, a decision.  Without it, we're left
with a bunch of unfinished business (Exhibit A being the MG Home
Page).  You can join Ed in the category "more interested in process
than in results".  I'll occupy the other category, thank you very
much, and we'll just see which of the two philosophies results in the
Home Page getting redone.
