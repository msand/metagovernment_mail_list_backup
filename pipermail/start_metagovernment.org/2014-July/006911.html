A selective overplan (to answer) is one with *at least* this sequence
of four steps: http://metagovernment.org/wiki/Overplan/d/SoP

   CoP    Compare the overplans
    |
   SoP    Select the best overplan
    |
   ToP    Track discussion of the overplan
    |
   XoP    Execute the overplan
    .                               
    .

I think probably every rational overplan is a selective overplan (has
this sequence of steps).  Therefore it is never top-down in the sense
of being imposed by force, but always bottom-up in the sense of being
written and chosen (SoP) by individuals at the grass roots.

We currently have just three overplans in our catalogue to choose from
and all are selective: http://metagovernment.org/wiki/Overplan/d

Only one (NxRO) is actually executable, however, because the other two
are only abstract (they have holes, or "hotspots").

I'll try to discover and catalogue some more executable overplans
soon.  Then we (each of us) will be able to start making a rational
choice of how to proceed; i.e. selecting which overplan to execute.
