Definitely not.  Although Plato and Socrates were fascinated by
authority, they never sought to claim it for themselves.  They were
also interested in ethics, but again, never committed the kind of
ethical breaches Cephu did (rabble rousing against existing authority
notwithstanding).  On the other hand Cephu displays many of the
characteristics Altemeyer classifies as RWA and/or SDO:
1) Highly dominant (SDO)
2) Highly concerned with his own and his group's status and power (RWA)
3) Selfish and unethical behavior (RWA)

Again, if you read nothing else in his book, the Global Change Game
experiment clearly demonstrates these things, and makes specific
predictions as to the fate of our species if we allow the Cephus of
the world to influence international relations, let alone allowing
them to rise to power.
