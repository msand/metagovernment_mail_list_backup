> http://metagovernment.org/wiki/Overplan/c/NxRO
> http://metagovernment.org/wiki/Overplan/a/NxR
> http://metagovernment.org/wiki/Overplan/a/SoP

> should be written from first person perspective, to not give the impression
> to a reader that some group (metagovernment) that he doesnt know yet, seem
> to have already decided for everyone and want to implement some weird thing
> called "Overplan" that will force everyone into something made up by a few
> (us), thus...
> 
> 
> *http://metagovernment.org/wiki/Overplan/a/NxR
> <http://metagovernment.org/wiki/Overplan/a/NxR>*
> "This abstract overplan <http://metagovernment.org/wiki/Overplan> tells how
> to *eliminate existential risk*. It builds on Overplan/a/SoP
> <http://metagovernment.org/wiki/Overplan/a/SoP> with the addition of steps
> for existential underplanning. The rationale behind existential
> underplanning is that we can reasonably concern ourselves with important
> things in the present and future, such as the difference between right and
> wrong action, and the choice of the best possible future, only if we plan
> on being around in the future. The added steps are designed to make sure of
> that."

> 
> "This abstract overplan <http://metagovernment.org/wiki/Overplan> *IS YOUR
> IDEA OF* how to *eliminate existential risk*. It builds on Overplan/a/SoP
> <http://metagovernment.org/wiki/Overplan/a/SoP> with the addition of steps
> for existential underplanning. The rationale behind existential
> underplanning is that *YOU* can reasonably concern *YOURSELF* with
> important things in the present and future, such as the difference between
> right and wrong action, and the choice of the best possible future, only if
> *YOU* plan on being around in the future. The added steps are designed to
> make sure of that."
> 
> 
> *AND*
> 
> 
> *http://metagovernment.org/wiki/Overplan/a/SoP
> <http://metagovernment.org/wiki/Overplan/a/SoP>*
> "This abstract overplan <http://metagovernment.org/wiki/Overplan> tells how
> to do *selective overplanning*. An overplan is a step-wise, comprehensive
> plan for moving forward in the field of participatory democracy, or more
> generally a comprehensive plan for the future of humanity. Today, most of
> us know little or nothing about overplans. We're almost completely in the
> dark about the steps required to move forward, and therefore unable to make
> much of a contribution. Even if you already know of an overplan or two, it
> would make no sense to execute a particular one unless you were reasonably
> confident of executing the best one. The steps of *this* overplan are
> designed to make sure of that."
> 
> 
> *should become something like*
> "This abstract overplan <http://metagovernment.org/wiki/Overplan> tells how
> to do *selective overplanning AND **CAN BE SUPPORTED BY YOU* . An overplan
> is a step-wise, comprehensive plan *YOU TO MAKE UP AND/OR SUPPORT*
> for moving forward in the field of participatory democracy, or more
> generally a comprehensive plan for the future of humanity. Today, most of
> us know little or nothing about overplans. We're almost completely in the
> dark about the steps required to move forward, and therefore unable to make
> much of a contribution. Even if you already know of an overplan or two, it
> would make no sense to execute a particular one unless you were reasonably
> confident of executing the best one. The steps of *this* overplan are *ONE
> PROPOSAL TO BE SUPPORTED BY YOU *designed to make sure of that."
> 
> AND
> 
> 
> http://metagovernment.org/wiki/Overplan/c/NxRO
> "This overplan <http://metagovernment.org/wiki/Overplan> tells how to
> *eliminate
> existential risk* (NxR) by means of *overguidance* (O). It builds on
> Overplan/a/SoP <http://metagovernment.org/wiki/Overplan/a/SoP> and /NxR
> <http://metagovernment.org/wiki/Overplan/a/NxR> by adding overguidance
> steps to facilitate the execution of the existential plans. The underlying
> theory is described elsewhere,1
> <http://metagovernment.org/wiki/Overplan/c/NxRO#cite_note-0> but the main
> principles are easy enough to summarize. You do two things simultaneously:
> *1*) you promote a maximum of personal freedom compatible with equal
> freedoms for all; and *2*) you relate personal action to a universally
> collective end. You do the first by using a vote mirroring inter-network (
> DvI <http://metagovernment.org/wiki/Overplan/c/NxRO#DvI>) to free
> individuals to express themselves in collective contexts by whatever means
> each prefers. You do the second by mythopoeic overguidance (O
> <http://metagovernment.org/wiki/Overplan/c/NxRO#O>) of the enabling norms
> (laws, co-plans, budgets) behind the execution of the existential plans. So
> you help humanity to achieve the ultimate in utopian ends collectively,
> even while maximizing our personal freedoms individually."
>

> you help humanity to achieve the ultimate in utopian ends collectively,
