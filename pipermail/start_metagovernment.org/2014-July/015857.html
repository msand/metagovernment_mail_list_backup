My apologies: LPA is Late Pleistocene Appropriate, which is those
remaining (very few) cultures that live much like our ancestors in the
Era of Evolutionary Adaptation lived, which is the time from 250,000
to about 50,000 years ago when human cultures and language and social
customs evolved (although some of the substrate behavior, probably
including language, evolved somewhat earlier than that).  The last
50,000 years is not sufficient time for evolution to have "tuned" us
much, so we're basically living with minds and bodies that were
adapted to live in bands of 20-30 individuals in an environment of
scarce resources. Which I believe explains a lot about why our
societies work so poorly.

A good survey of the field can be found in Christopher Boehm's "Moral
Origins: The Evolution of Virtue, Altruism, and Shame" which I cite
several times in my book.  This research is the basis of my claim that
direct democracy is actually the natural and instinctive political
organization for human beings.
