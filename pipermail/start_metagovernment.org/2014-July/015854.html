Again, we don't have to agree on the details, just on the basic
principles.  I've seen this issue brought up several places on the MG
site yet no one has actually taken the trouble to even do a poll to
enable ranking of these principles by the number of people who support
them.  Perhaps this is something that we could actually use OpaVote
for: Generate a list of facts/principles, then have everyone rank
them.  Sort of an abuse of that system, but it could prove to be a way
to finally solve this problem.  Another thing it would do: Since
OpaVote works differently from the other systems (i.e., you don't
create an account) it would seem to me that we could turn over the
entire list of email addresses to OpaVote and see if some of the
lurkers would participate in that vote who don't participate here or
on the other voting systems.

I've posted my list of principles several times, but here's an even
more abbreviated version:
1) Representative government is inherently corrupt
2) Direct democracy is our natural and instinctive preference
3) Direct democracy works, but only with technological assistance for
large groups
4) Dynamic delegation is a required component of the system
5) Authoritarian behavior needs to be constrained by the system
6) Radical transparency (including open voting) is necessary to
prevent corruption
7) Radical transparency (including open voting) is required to
constrain authoritarians

Things that I see to be secondary, not important, or impossible that I
know some people came here for that I therefore think should also be
on the list:
8) Consensus in an important goal
9) Deep collaboration among all the electorate is an important goal
10) "mob rule" and "tyranny of the majority" are significant problems
in democratic systems
11) Flapping/cycling is a serious problem in democratic systems
12) Vote secrecy is necessary
13) Different voting systems can be used at different levels
14) Voting data and apps can be distributed rather than centralized
15) Interoperability among voting systems is necessary

I think we should stay away from these because they are not central to
the role of the MG site, but I think they represent core divisions in
our ranks:
16) The new government will gradually replace the existing one by
rendering it irrelevant vs. complete replacement when the new system
is ready
17) One must work within the system to improve it vs. simply replacing
it in a single vote
