In this thread, we're discussing overplans (step-wise ways forward)
and the idea of comparing them.  So basically this thread is about:

  (1) Overplans and comparative overplanning

Mark, you bring up two additional topics:

  (2) qKonsens, a new tool your team is developing
  (3) How qKonsens might be used in overplanning

Could we discuss those in separate threads?  We should probably wait
till someone has a clear understanding of both (1) and (2) before we
open thread (3), in which we discuss the combination.

> > In your work with AG Meinungsfindungstool and the German Pirates,
> > for instance, you hope to achieve an outcome that's helpful to
> > humanity.  If you were to name that outcome and list the steps to
> > it, then that would be an overplan.
> 
> Okay...

So now I guess you roughly understand what overplans are.

> ... So with qK (in its final version) we - as metagovernment - could
> define those stations necessary to compare overplans and then
> everyone is invited to create her own overplan within qK. ...

Technically we might do that.  I assume qKonsens can host a catalogue
of texts, because overplans are texts.  But politically I think it
would fail.  We'd never get competitors (like you and me) to work from
an overplan catalogue that's hosted by a rival competitor.  Even if
Metagov deploys its own instance of qKonsens independently of the
German Pirate Party and AG Meinungsfindungstool, it would still be
tied to those two.  It wouldn't be neutral.

The Metagov wiki, however, is feasible on both technical and political
grounds.  Ideally qKonsens could still be used in the context of the
wiki by whoever chooses to use it, and I hope we can work together to
make that choice possible.  We've already installed a device in the
wiki for this purpose: http://metagovernment.org/wiki/Toolset_switch

Please let's discuss these technical topics in separate threads.  This
thread I hope to reserve for basic discussion of overplans, if anyone
still has questions, etc, about what they are.

Mike
