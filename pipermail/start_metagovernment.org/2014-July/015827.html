By bylaws it is a standard super major to call a minister in the usual
local polity.  Everyone knows she expects about a 95%  approval from the
local polity.

The reason we  expect to do that at the local polity is we manage to do it
at a national level.  Calling a minister is about at best a year long
process.  Big national changes take 20 or 30 years.  And just because you
can win now does not mean that you should not spend another ten years
convincing more electors.

Look around for this quaker about 1750 in sort of new jersey who was
reading his bible and decided God did not approve of keeping slaves.  He
went from no support to unanimous in 20 years.  Quakers require unanimous
last I heard.  They will also listen carefully and thoughtfully for 20
years apparently.  Find read and enjoy.

This is long enough ago that this is not red hot and current.

My denomination is putting big effort into improving their polity and in a
way I think they might want to get closer to that aspect of the quakers but
in a modern era where not everything is face to face.
