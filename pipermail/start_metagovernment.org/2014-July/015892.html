That is good, still it means that everybody will agree with Condorcet. I 
agree. Still I would like to propose something.

We may create groups, every with (approximately) five people. Five 
people should be the ideal size of group that should reach some good 
conclusion. These groups may use some variation of Delphi method or just 
talk. As soon as some group reaches some conclusion, they will post it 
back to this mailing list. There is not limit on conclusion, they may 
just agree that the want to vote and how they want to vote or that they 
want to talk in bigger group.
After that people may start leaving their original groups and may join 
groups that have reached some conclusion and collaborate with them.
If you agree, answer this email: "Groups, yes" and you may also add your 
ideal size of group, time when you can participate in universal time (UT 
or UTC or GMT), you may also list somebody, who you want to be in your 
group. You may also indicate how active you will be and what 
specialization you can provide on what level. You may also list any 
additional information that will be helpful for grouping you. You may 
also tell me, that you will group yourself or how many members you need 
to add to your group.

Regards
Michael
