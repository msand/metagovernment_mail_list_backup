@Michael Allen

I think it is impossible to "UNITE" all people upfront, thus i vote against
the proposal to have a majority of 65% or 80% or something else.
The whole approach is flawed.
My belief is, that oppinions are many and the best which is possible is, to
come up with a proposal and find someone to support that proposal forever,
until he decided to "withdraw" his support it is legit forever.

To give everyone the possibility to maintain an overview over all the
"supported proposals", one maintains his own profile page, which can be
hosted by METAGOVERNMENT, but also adheres to a certain "data format" which
can be CRAWLED, so everyone is free to close their metagovernment hosted
profile and instead upload their proposals in that "data format" (I propose
a GIT REPOSITORY), to their website or github and afterwards to ADD that
new URL to the CRAWLER.

Therefore consensus can be reached among all supporters of a proposal and
its legit only amongst them.

This process will build many "islands of local consensus" which can then be
grown into bigger islands through discussion, eventually growing into a
planet of consensus.
