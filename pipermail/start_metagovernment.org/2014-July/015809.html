> This is exactly my point: A process that involves endless debate is
> IMHO simply unworkable because the whole *point* of government is
> making decisions.  The fact that you don't have time for the debate on
> the Home page (or the MG decision rule) is just a microcosm of what
> it's like for real people out in the real world.  By requiring massive
> time investment, these consensus-seeking systems just either fail to
> deliver a decision, or at best delegate the decision to those who
> simply have time to debate, regardless of their skill in whatever
> domain the decision lies in.  It's time to give up on this fantasy
> that a real government can operate on consensus and start using Loomio
> or other simpler tools to make a decision because only they can
> *guarantee* that some decision will be made.  Until you and Ed (and
> whoever else wants to be in the "process over results" camp) realize
> (and admit) that, I'm thinking we should at least leave the Home page
> blank if not replace it with statement that this project has failed
> and that people should look elsewhere for information on alternative
> forms of government.
