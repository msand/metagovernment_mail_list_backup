My denomination is not unique in being non-creedal.  It is unusual I
suppose.  It takes 20 or 30 years to change the non-creed which is probably
better than science axioms or the US constitution.  Creedal religions seem
to take longer and involve large social movements.

We have congregational polity as do many very conservative groups with very
traditional creeds. Creeds may be hard to change but congregational polity
means the local church get to change theirs.  This may be painful for
everyone and often ends in schism but as in most things being sure about  a
generalization is a sure place to make improvements in your sureity.
