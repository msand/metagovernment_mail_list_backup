Ed Pastore wrote:
> I have been doing some research on bulletin boards to see what would  
> fit our needs best, and at the moment I am leaning toward myBB, in  
> part because it is well liked for its administrative capabilities  
> (something which phpBB is really bad at).
> 
> One native feature of myBB is that users can subscribe to entire  
> categories of the forum and be notified when there is a new topic  
> posted to the forum. Thus we could set up this list server as a user  
> on the forum and have it subscribe to one or more categories. That  
> would mean that every time someone starts a new topic in such  
> categories, this list would be notified of it. The discussion would  
> then occur on the BB... this list would just be more of a notification  
> list. Does that sound good to people?

Some prefer Web forums.  Others will be less inclined to participate
if they cannot use email to exchange messages.  Email is still the
premiere discussion medium on the net.  Each of us has his favourite
mail client that works across all forums, excepting Web forums.  The
typical Web forum UI is bolted on, take it or leave it; if you reject
the UI, then you reject the discussion.  (Thomas invented vote
mirroring to solve to the problem of split consensus.  If he tackled
the problem of split discussion, then he'd invent email. ;-)

Best of all would be software that has both mail and Web interfaces,
as we (I think with Alexander) mooted earlier.  All that comes to mind
is Google Groups (horrid) and what Steven Clift uses (peculiar stuff):
http://groups.dowire.org/groups/exchange/

I'm short on time, otherwise I'd look around.  Although I must say, I
am happy with the status quo.
