I actually hope the opposite: There's a lot on this issue on
matchism.org, but to summarize: "Public Policy" is actually a synonym
(or perhaps even a euphemism) for "Social Engineering", yet the PP
institutions (the JFK school being among the worst in this respect)
don't teach engineering *or* anything scientific about social
interaction. Indeed, you can get a degree from these places without
ever having to take even one class in social psychology or
engineering. Which means they're actually promoting incompetent, or at
least amateurish, designs. Given that the primary goal of PP
institutions seems to be to bolster leadership-based (i.e. SDAP-based)
government, I'd recommend being highly skeptical of anything they have
to say on the subject of social engineering or government design in
general. The examples discussed in that talk I think demonstrate this
clearly: They don't want direct democracy, they're merely working out
ways to keep SDAP-leaders from screwing up without requiring that they
actually give up any of their power.
