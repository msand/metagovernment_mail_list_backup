It was not criticism!
Consider that participatory budgeting  have a 30 year history. Now is
moving!
On line participatory budgeting? 3 years ago i tested the first platform
(italian) e-part and recently they get, with my full congratulations,
european funding.
So is moving and it will get better definitely. Is a different approach
into the same direction.

What I criticize is the drive. When money are at stake then people seem to
be more motivated. I dislike that. Learning/voting  for my own sake or my
neighbourhood  vs learning 4 voting for common good .

I say only that on line have few big advantages, is more economical, allow
bigger crowd to join, in a shorter time.  But I say that should go hand on
hand until digital divide rule.

On another hand usually the decisions are local, fixmystreet or similar
approaches  are diverting the crowd from the big picture and the important
matters, and i see a dangerous trend for that reason.

For the record, I am short of data and i did not listen to the soundcloud
you sent.

Regarding Airesis, we metagovernment group, never even get a single vote on
an issue, i am thinking to propose to close the group!
I am sure that, as is, Airesis could be used for PB very effectively.
First the decision to where and in which % to allocate the money,  first
proposal with different % and different areas (ie education, health,
tourism development, etc),  then decide on practical projects to use them
(build or improve school, hospital, fixing infrastructure, help the
indigent etc).
And i go even further than general PB, once decided the project the
community  should decide who to give the contracts, how and when to give
the money in total transparency and plenty of audit.  The experience of PB
show that less than 60 % of the decision taken during the PB actually is
developed. 40% of the money disappear on the way.

Cheers j

So not criticism but healthy  realism.
