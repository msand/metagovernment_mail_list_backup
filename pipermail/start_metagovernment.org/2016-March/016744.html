>> In Israel participatory democracy became more and more legitimate. The
>> government ordered every ministry to make citizens more involve in their
>> process of governmental decision. Several municipalities actively try to
>> let citizens participate. It is only in an experimental stage, and not
>> widely used, but it is in process.  The bureaucracy understood that in the
>> long run participatory decision making will help smooth things up.
>>
>> I was an adviser for such municipality in the years 2012-2013. In 2007
>> when I started like Michal to advance participatory democracy, the mayor
>> was threaten. Several years later, he paid a company to facilitate a
>> participatory process.
>>
>> After long years of facilitating face-to-face and online deliberation, we
>> are starting to build a tool for deliberation, which is called delib.org.
>> It is an open source software. It is in it’s early stages, but it was able
>> to dramatically shorten time of deliberation and to be more inclusive then
>> regular methods. We devised a problem with a group of 24 participants in
>> less than half an hour. On 7th of April, there is going to be a large
>> party of the Israeli direct democracy movement, where we hope to deliberate
>> simultaneously with 70 participants.
>>
>> I can introduce you to people that may be able to help translate Airesis
>> to Hebrew, and even to Arabic, please contact me privately (
