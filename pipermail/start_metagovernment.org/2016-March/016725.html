Not a big fan of this: Like the term "representative democracy", it seems
to me the problem is a much with the "representative" part as the
"democracy" part. Certainly the plutocrats (well, exclusive of those
composing the Military Industrial Complex) weren't in favor of the Iraq
war, nor do they usually have strong opinions on moral issues (legality of
drugs, abortion, gay rights, etc.) That is, the "representatives" don't
represent them either! Maybe "plutocracy by proxy" would be better, since
it allows for the fact that proxies, unlike representatives, frequently
make decisions based on their own biased outlooks rather than being held to
any standard of "representativeness".
