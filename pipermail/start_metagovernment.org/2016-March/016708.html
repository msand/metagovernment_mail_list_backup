Ok. that sounds decent to me. :-)
I believe, that currently, power is put too far away from the local people.
Whoever is at close distance in the current spacetime should be able to
decide together how they want to co-exist.
In context of people moving around and interacting with people who they
might live together at close distance in a future spacetime, one might
decide to take that into account and also apply some conventions in the
current spacetime that can only be explained in light of the
inter-dependency of different "local groups"...

Personally, for example, I like the idea of the world moving closer
together - and some agreements like TTIP or CETA are as far removed from
local people as possible. The way they are formed is kind of the
anti-thesis of how something like that should have been created if at
all... so definitely a lot of things have to change fundamentally - but in
order to make that happen, i believe, we (as metagovernment) and similar
groups that work for the same general cause - need to succeed to *digitally
network local communities* in a way, that allows their self governance
without the risk of being played out by actors who act on
inter-group-levels.

Even if not all people will become literate in the near future (meaining
the skill to program), then at least some people in every "local community"
need to be able to connect the community into the digital inter-community
dialog at any time to prevent harm.

I listend to your soundcloud podcast - and how they talk about digital
natives and how they might or should participate :-)
...but honestly - *using tools *will not give power to people. They need to
start to being the producers of their tools to have a political dialog and
to make decisions.
This talk or process will happen online between literate people who create
their tools and make all the micro decisions necessary in code.
If they leave that to a few - then they will always be observers and take
the tools and act within their limits (CODE IS (WILL BE) LAW) - what code
allows can be done, what it doesnt cannot be done. Non-Literate people can
demand certain features, but if they cannot speak to machines to make them
happen, they need to wait for those who can.... which might sometimes
listen, but sometimes they wont.

In order to participate and be empowered, people need to learn programming
themselves - even if it's only to being able to judge what code others
propose to them.
(It was a revolution when people could for the first time read the bible
themselves instead of relying on some elite to translate and interpret it
to them)
