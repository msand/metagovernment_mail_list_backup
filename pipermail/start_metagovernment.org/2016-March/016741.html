Dear Jacopo

In Israel participatory democracy became more and more legitimate. The
government ordered every ministry to make citizens more involve in their
process of governmental decision. Several municipalities actively try to
let citizens participate. It is only in an experimental stage, and not
widely used, but it is in process.  The bureaucracy understood that in the
long run participatory decision making will help smooth things up.

I was an adviser for such municipality in the years 2012-2013. In 2007 when
I started like Michal to advance participatory democracy, the mayor was
threaten. Several years later, he paid a company to facilitate a
participatory process.

After long years of facilitating face-to-face and online deliberation, we
are starting to build a tool for deliberation, which is called delib.org.
It is an open source software. It is in it’s early stages, but it was able
to dramatically shorten time of deliberation and to be more inclusive then
regular methods. We devised a problem with a group of 24 participants in
less than half an hour. On 7th of April, there is going to be a large party
of the Israeli direct democracy movement, where we hope to deliberate
simultaneously with 70 participants.

I can introduce you to people that may be able to help translate Airesis to
Hebrew, and even to Arabic, please contact me privately (tal.yaron at gmail.com).
Yet I so believe that Palestinians -Israelis deliberation is a very
difficult problem to solve. I have tried it before, and many of my
collogues tried it too. Israelis and Arabs have very different world views,
and they are entrenched in them. Every side feels very threatens by the
other side story, and our differences in culture are huge. Israelis tend to
be very direct, and Arabs very polite. Israelis tend to adopt Western norm,
while Arabs tend to adopt tribble norms. So although large amounts of money
where put into the subject, it didn’t show positive meaningful results so
far.
