On Jul 7, 2009, at 12:26 PM, Owen Ambur wrote:

> It sounds like we may be debating the distinction between a vision  
> statement versus a mission statement.

I'm not sure I see that confusion. It looks to me like we're mostly  
focused on mission. I don't think I agree with the definition of  
vision as "A concise and inspirational statement implicitly relating  
an organization's purpose to its values." I was under the impression  
that a vision statement was precisely that: our statement of what our  
vision for the future is. In other words: what we envision the world  
will be like if we are successful. If that is the definition, then I  
think we are pretty close if we just use something like the current  
vision: http://metagovernment.org/wiki/Mission_and_vision#Vision

> It would be good to specify our objectives and stakeholders as well  
> as our mission, vision, values, and goal(s).

Given how much debate we have over just the mission statement, I  
thought it would be better to focus on them sequentially. But you're  
welcome to take the lead here if you want to take a stab at all four...
