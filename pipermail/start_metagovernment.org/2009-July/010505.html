Owen Ambur wrote:
> I also still think the concept of "governance" has been vastly
> oversold, tends to deflect attention away from that which we can and
> should do for ourselves in favor of trying to tell others what they
> should do...

I personally share Owen's distaste for the word "government" in this
context.  I believe that we (everyday people) ought to be controlling
the administrative apparatus that goes by the name of "government",
and not ourselves becoming that apparatus.  Elsewhere, and on a
related note, I have suggested that the name "Metagovernment" ought to
be changed (at least informally) to "Metagov", which seems more
friendly.

My own work is (to state it as a mission) to develop the tools of a
direct democracy.  While I don't suggest adopting that as your mission
statement, it's not incompatible with anything else that was put
forward.

These are just personal thoughts, from the sidelines,
