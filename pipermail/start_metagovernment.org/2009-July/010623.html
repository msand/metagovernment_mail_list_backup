>>> On Jul 15, 2009, at 2:15 PM, Jacki Buros wrote:

>>> If we keep this on as a stated goal, then I would additionally  
>>> note that 'every institution of representative democracy' may be a  
>>> bit extreme. Do we as a group feel that there are *no* scenarios  
>>> in which a representative democracy might be better than open  
>>> source governance? I can imagine a hybrid government like the US  
>>> senate & house of reps, where the house of reps is replaced by OS  
>>> democracy but the senate remains. Isn't it a bit soon to say that  
>>> such a strategy isn't ideal?
>>>
>>> Softwares like Votorola use a delegation system, where people can  
>>> delegate a representative but can take that delegation away at any  
>>> time. Projects like DemoEx use a direct-representation mix, where  
>>> there is a representative, but that rep is bound to act in  
>>> accordance with the will of the collective.
>>>
>>> Both of these are more advanced than traditional representation,  
>>> so yes I would say that we want to overturn the traditional model  
>>> altogether. But that also agrees with Jim's statement that my  
>>> wording seems to radical. There should be a way to incorporate  
>>> these forms of permutation into the verbiage. Unfortunately I  
>>> don't have time to work on it now as am preparing for a meeting in  
>>> half an hour. :)
>>>
