On Jul 26, 2009, at 8:34 PM, Owen Ambur wrote:

> 1)      With reference to the vision statement -- “… a world where  
> every person, without exception, is able to substantively  
> participate in any governance structure in which they have an  
> interest” – I wonder if it might not be appropriate to insert the  
> word “valid” before “interest” (i.e., “a valid interest”).  However,  
> that may raise needless complications at this point.

That raises the question of what is "valid." It seems likely to be  
open to interpretation, and that sounds like the imposition of an  
arbitrary authority on a community. I have no doubt that many projects  
will look for some sort of validity standards, but I wouldn't bet that  
all would.

At least in my mind, Metascore does not have any imposed validity  
standard... the idea being that anyone who cares enough to take part  
in a discussion must ipso facto have a valid stake in that discussion.  
(An understood result of this is that, for example, an American  
environmentalist may have a stake in China's Three Gorges project. And  
also that a Chinese factory worker may have a stake in American  
regulations on Walmart.)

> 2)      I thought one of the goals was to seek the ability to share  
> a core set of information across the various projects.  http://www.metagovernment.org/wiki/Main_Page/2009-07#Participating_projects 
>   Is that not the case?

Good point. I have just added a sentence to that effect, though it  
could probably use further editing or expansion.
