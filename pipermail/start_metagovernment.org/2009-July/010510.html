Dear Ed and others: 

 

I am very excited by the ground-breaking work you are all doing. I am
working on producing a major feature film that will help inject the idea of
popular self government both at a local and global level. 

 

It is crucially important that you continue to evolve something that moves
way beyond archaic voting into a system that can draw out the highest and
best wisdom of every individual.  In the old archaic system, a person who
votes based on a 30 second ad has the same weight as a person who has
studied an issue for years or who is intimately effected by it. 

 

You can see more about our movie project at www.onefilms.com .

 

See also www.Earth-os.net <http://www.earth-os.net/>  for my initial
proposal on designing a new wiki-based Earth Operating System. 

 

I have a fuller draft paper on this available if you'd like me to send a pdf
copy for your review, critique and collaboration.

 

Warmly,

 

Arthur Kanegis

PS  You might also enjoy my article at www.E-arth.com
<http://www.e-arth.com/>  on thinking outside the box in evolving a system
beyond violence.

Owen Ambur wrote:

> I also still think the concept of "governance" has been vastly

> oversold, tends to deflect attention away from that which we can and

> should do for ourselves in favor of trying to tell others what they

> should do...

 

I personally share Owen's distaste for the word "government" in this

context.  I believe that we (everyday people) ought to be controlling

the administrative apparatus that goes by the name of "government",

and not ourselves becoming that apparatus.  Elsewhere, and on a

related note, I have suggested that the name "Metagovernment" ought to

be changed (at least informally) to "Metagov", which seems more

friendly.

 

My own work is (to state it as a mission) to develop the tools of a

direct democracy.  While I don't suggest adopting that as your mission

statement, it's not incompatible with anything else that was put

forward.

 

These are just personal thoughts, from the sidelines,
