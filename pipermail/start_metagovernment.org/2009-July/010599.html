Yes, changes to the wiki draft home page are of course highly  
encouraged:
http://metagovernment.org/wiki/Main_Page/2009-07

At some point soon, we should make this the new home page of  
metagovernment.org. It is getting awkward these days when I tell  
people about the project and have to say that the homepage is out of  
date, and that a new draft page is what the project is actually about  
these days. It would also be easier for other projects to understand  
(when we approach them) if we have this on our homepage instead of a  
sub-page.

So, please make changes to this page as you see fit. When there are no  
more changes for a few days and no dissent to changing it, I (or  
someone else) will assume there is a consensus and will change the  
homepage to use the content on this page.

Thanks.

> On Wed, Jul 15, 2009 at 2:27 PM, Ed Pastore <epastore at metagovernment.org

> On Jul 15, 2009, at 2:15 PM, Jacki Buros wrote:
>
> If we keep this on as a stated goal, then I would additionally note  
> that 'every institution of representative democracy' may be a bit  
> extreme. Do we as a group feel that there are *no* scenarios in  
> which a representative democracy might be better than open source  
> governance? I can imagine a hybrid government like the US senate &  
> house of reps, where the house of reps is replaced by OS democracy  
> but the senate remains. Isn't it a bit soon to say that such a  
> strategy isn't ideal?
>
> Softwares like Votorola use a delegation system, where people can  
> delegate a representative but can take that delegation away at any  
> time. Projects like DemoEx use a direct-representation mix, where  
> there is a representative, but that rep is bound to act in  
> accordance with the will of the collective.
>
> Both of these are more advanced than traditional representation, so  
> yes I would say that we want to overturn the traditional model  
> altogether. But that also agrees with Jim's statement that my  
> wording seems to radical. There should be a way to incorporate these  
> forms of permutation into the verbiage. Unfortunately I don't have  
> time to work on it now as am preparing for a meeting in half an  
> hour. :)
>
