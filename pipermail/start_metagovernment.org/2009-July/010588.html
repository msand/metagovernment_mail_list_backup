On Jul 18, 2009, at 8:56 AM, Thomas von der Elbe wrote:

> How should step 6 of the transition steps, the outreach to other  
> projects take place?
>
> I suggest to have an "official" invitation-letter, which could then  
> ideally be attached to a personal notification.
>
> We could then start a new Thread "Invited Projects" where each  
> invited project gets added to a list, so we keep the overview.
> Needless to say, that we invite only those projects, which meet the  
> new Metagovernment mission and vision statement.
>
> Here is a rough draft for an invitation-letter (please excuse my  
> strange german english):
>
>
> "Dear members of Project xy,
>
> we are the members of the 5(?) different e-dem-projects: a, b, c, d,  
> and e.
>
> We all came to consensus that for several reasons an intensified  
> communication and collaboration among certain e-democracy projects  
> around the world would be desirable.
>
> The reasons we see for this are:
>
> 1. to profit from possible synergetic effects.
>
> 2. to avoid competition for users (voters) among us. Because this  
> would unnecessarily slow down the e-democracy-movement as a whole.  
> Therefore we think it would be desirable to define an "open source  
> governance standard", in order to enable the exchange of user-data  
> between all of our projects.
>
> 3. we see the existence of multiple projects as the existence of  
> different approaches to find the yet unknown solution to the  
> 'creation of an open source governance system' for a healthier  
> society. The mere competitive and/or cooperative interaction between  
> projects with the same goal seems the best strategy for answering  
> this problem. To let the interaction happen, we think about settling  
> the projects in a common ground —the standard—, so they can  
> communicate to each other.
>
> 4. ?
> 5. ?
> ...
>
> We think a metaorganization would serve as a step towards this goal.
>
> We would like to invite you and your project to join our group to  
> found such a metaorganization.
>
> Because Metagovernment has long been the platform for our  
> communication already and was therefore on it´s way to becoming the  
> coordinating group for our 5 projects anyway and because it´s  
> software-developing-project Metascore is easy to detach,  
> Metagovernment has offered it´s name and it´s wiki-pages for the  
> founding of the new Metaorganization.
>
>
> In hope for our working together,
> With best regards, ...."
>
>
> What do you think?
