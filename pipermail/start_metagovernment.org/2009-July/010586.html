I have changed this statement on the wiki ever so slightly, as follows:

We expect new forms of governance software to be adopted in small
communities and to spread outward with the potential to replace every
institution of [[representative democracy]] with [[open source governance]].


Instead of :

We expect new forms of governance software to be adopted in small
communities and to spread outward until they have replaced every institution
of [[representative democracy]] with [[open source governance]].

Since this is a draft, perhaps we should all be editing the draft to come up
with more acceptable phrasing? This, after all, is the wiki-way :)

> Softwares like Votorola use a delegation system, where people can delegate
> a representative but can take that delegation away at any time. Projects

> the collective.
>
> Both of these are more advanced than traditional representation, so yes I
> would say that we want to overturn the traditional model altogether. But
> that also agrees with Jim's statement that my wording seems to radical.
> There should be a way to incorporate these forms of permutation into the
