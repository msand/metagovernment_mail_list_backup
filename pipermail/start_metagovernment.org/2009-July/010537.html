These are some great points, Jacki. I think you may have hit on a key  
phrase with "development of tools for direct democracy." And I do  
think that the internet is an essential function of it, because it is  
the new mechanism which solves the most critical flaw of direct  
democracy: the inability of everyone to easily get together all the  
time.

The only thing I see lacking from your notes is the concept of "any  
community." When people hear "direct democracy" they tend to  
immediately think of large governments.

So... how about something like this?

"The mission of the Metagovernment project is to support the  
development of internet tools for direct democracy in any community."

It still sounds a little clunky to me, but I like the direction it is  
going.

> earmarks : ) though I agree with Pietro's remarks.
>
> Development in the larger sense (ie developing capabilities), rather  
> than specifically of software could easily cover these three.
>
> I am thinking something along the lines of the following (in part,  
> inspired by Michael's mission, below):
>
> [Support|Promote] development of [software|tools|potential] for  
> direct democracy via the internet
>
> Or, simply, Support initiatives promoting or enabling direct democracy
>
>
> This brings up a few questions that may need discussion:
> do we really want to focus on software development rather than  
> include algorithms / interface design / hardware-technology / ???
> is our mission to promote, coordinate, foster, ?? engage in   
> participate in the development of these things? I personally like  
> "support" because it encompasses all of these.
> do we want to limit the project to web-based initiatives? It would  
> certainly help clarify the 'elevator pitch'
> At the same time, if there were a better way that didn't use the  
> internet, would we not include that since it's not via the internet?
> Thoughts from the group?
>
> We would then follow this by saying something like:
>
> Specifically, :
> work towards open standards and foster collaboration between projects
> brainstorm about implementation and methods
> promote adoption of direct democracy by communities
> participate in development of tools for direct democracy
> engage existing open source communities with potential for  
> collaboration (read: mediawiki presentation)
>
> On Fri, Jul 3, 2009 at 10:30 PM, Ed Pastore <epastore at metagovernment.org 
> > wrote:
> One comment I recently learned in the exercise of developing a  
> mission statement: imagine you are in an elevator with someone. You  
> both get on on the first floor, and they ask what your project does.  
> You have until you get off to explain it to them. This is referred  
> to as the "elevator pitch." The point is that you have to be  
> extremely brief and also extremely clear. So that said, try to limit  
> your phrases to something you could say to someone in one sentence  
> and have them immediately understand you. The more complexity to the  
> sentence, the more likely you are to lose them.
>
> That said... integrating all of the responses so far, I am seeing  
> this:
>
> "The mission of the Metagovernment project is to foster the study,  
> development, and use of open source software which allows the  
> members any community to govern themselves."
>
> I'm still not liking the nuance of the last section, starting with  
> "which allows..." I think there should be a clearer way to say this  
> and still have it be very concise.
