Maybe the proposal you have written have been rejected,
but at least one of the proposal you have endorsed is always included in a
Pareto Front.
(please re read the last phrase, that is half of the core of Vilfredo. If
you get this you get the selection process)
In other words:
By definition the Pareto Front ALWAYS include a proposal that have been
endorsed by each user.
I even think (but I am not mathematically sure, yet), that a Pareto Front is
the smallest set that satisfy this requirement.

If you are so attached to your own proposal, then you just need to endorse
only your own proposals.
If everybody does this, the pareto front becomes the whole set of proposals.
And society unexistant :).

Somehow the system works on the assumption that although everybody writes,
and everybody endorses, people are not so attached to their own proposal in
advance. In other words, it is ok, and accepted to vote for your own
proposals, if you genuinely think they are better. But it is bad manners to
do it, just to remain in the pareto front. We had some cases in the past.
Now the history clearly shows who writes what, and whoe endorses what, and
it is pretty easy to spot this kind of behaviour.

What is the suggested action against this behaviour?

nothing. Keep on doing your thing. Keep on endorsing only what you agree
with.

Eventually the whole system will still generate its own best proposal. And
the grandious kid (sorry, no better way to define such behaviour), will
remain with its own unedited proposals, only endorse by himself.

Maybe at the end of the whole process, when all have stabilised, the number
of people that propose things can be said, and maybe even a small filter on
proposals written  by a single user, and endorsed only by that user. But at
the end. It should not be added before, or...

the brainstorm would not be neutral
:)



The situation of course is more complex, but let's leave it as that for now
