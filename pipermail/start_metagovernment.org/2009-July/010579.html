I like the

" ...(a project) to support the development of internet tools which enable
the members of any community to fully participate in the governance of that
community."

I am wondering if we shouldn't push a bit more the "fully participate" part.

When metagovernment started it was very clear that the idea was to generate
a system that could let the members govern themselves. Maybe it is splitting
a hair in four, but I think there is a difference between participating in
the governance of a community, and being the governing body of the
communtiy. I mean, many eGov project will have reached completion if the
average member participate in a representative democracy.

But the aim of metagov was broader, deeper. We wanted to substitute the
government, reaching a point where no person was in charge, but we all were.


" ...(a project) to support the development of internet tools which enable
the members of any community to co-govern a community."

On the other hand this might push away some more mild projects.
Any co-thoughts?

meaning in the phrase.
