Hmm,
I have to say that I am not happy about the "direct democracy" reference.

Although I like the term (and concept) of democracy, and I totally dig the
idea that we all should be involved, my feeling is that the term "direct
democracy" by now has taken a life of its own. It does not just mean a
democracy where we all are part of it, but a democracy where on every issue
everybody votes, and the votes are equal. This is what the average person
understand
and is not that different from what is presented on wikipedia
http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Direct_democracy
now this system has a lot of drawbacks (and many positive sides too).

But the effect of this is that many people when they hear about "direct
democracy" immediatly they freeze, and don't want to have anything to do
with that. We cannot risk something like that.

It's better to use something new, and then empower it of a new meaning
ourselves, than having to explain to everybody that our vision will be
qualitatively different from offline direct democracy because...

Cheers,
Pietro
