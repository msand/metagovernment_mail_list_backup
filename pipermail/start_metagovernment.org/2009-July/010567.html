Ed Pastore wrote:
> OK, so... I'm just sort of playing referee here, trying to integrate  
> all of the suggestions since my last attempt. How about a return to  
> some of the verbiage about community self-governance instead of direct  
> democracy, like:
> 
> "The mission of the Metagovernment project is to support the  
> development of internet tools which enable the members of any  
> community to govern themselves."
> 
> or maybe
> 
> "The mission of the Metagovernment project is to support the  
> development of internet tools which enable the members of any  
> community to fully participate in the governance of that community."
> 
> This still doesn't fully integrate Phil Diver's comments that it  
> should emphasize that we intend this sort of governance to be more  
> effective than existing methods. But it is very concise and seems to  
> get across most of what we are trying to say.

It goes without saying that we think it will be more effective. However 
it is possible that the human race is not capable of fully governing 
themselves once the community scales beyond a couple of hundred people 
(the size of the villages we evolved in). It may well be less effective.

It would be good to also mention that it our goal for it to be scalable 
to the point where it can govern a country.
