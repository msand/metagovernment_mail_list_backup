hi all,
My proposal was a little bit to spontaneous. I looked at the pages (a worked 
a lot on them in the past) and I would like to rephrase the chalenge.
On the fractional reserve page the explanation now is wrong (and they know 
it).
The "Money creation process"  and the following explanations are the old 
theoretical explanations of what the financial sector likes us to believe.
Nevertheless, some "classical" economists have empirically proven that the 
system of money creation and reserves is not working like that in reality 
(Kydland & Prescott, Business Cycles: Real Facts and a Monetary Myth, 
Federal Reserve Bank of Minneapolis Quarterly Review, Spring 1990, both of 
them are Nobel price winners in economy). 
http://www.debtdeflation.com/blogs/2009/01/31/therovingcavaliersofcredit/#_ftn3 
Their finding are confirmed by numerous senior bank officials, Public 
servants on top positions and so on. Easy to find.

The task is to give their findings (again) more attention in the original 
article "fractional reserve" page in Wikipedia and maintain it there for a 
couple of months.
Now it is moved to http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Money_multiplier without any 
emphasis on the importance of those findings and references. That is their 
strategy. It is not removed completely but just hard to find for anyone new 
in the subject and with as less as possible references.

Good luck

Paul
