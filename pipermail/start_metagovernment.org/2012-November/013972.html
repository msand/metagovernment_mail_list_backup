ok Thomas, thanks, I give it a try.  The system in Switzerland (indirect - 
direct democracy) is also a mixture of direct democracy and representative 
democracy, this in contrast to the "direct - direct democracy" system for 
example in California.
But the ultimate question is not the system used (it can be by raising hand 
on the central marketplace) but in the end , are the people sovereign and in 
what degree.  In Switzerland people are absolute sovereign because if they 
want they even can change their federal constitution themselves (by double 
majority). http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sovereignty and 
http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Popular_sovereignty
