I agree with Ed that the introductory banner and homepage ought to
work together.  They have complimentary strengths.  The banner is
everywhere, while the homepage has more room for explanatory content.
I also like Ed's proposed content for the homepage.

Here's a rough, first proposal for the content of the banner:


                      Metagovernment

        0                  0                    0            0     
    '       V          '       V            '       V    '       V 

   '    +--> I        '    +    I          '    +    I  '    +    I

    '       '          '       X <--+       '       X    '       X 
        '                  '        |           '            '
                                    |
      Choose             Offer -----+         Extend  --->
      ------             -----                ------


This is visible to newcomers on all Metagov pages.  The text at top is
a link to the home page.  The primary message of the graphical story
beneath is directed at toolset developers.  It says, "Put your toolset
here or you're gonna be a loser."

The first graphic (left) is a live toolset switch, initially set on
the introductory toolset (i.e. the banner).  When the mouse is over
the left third of the banner, the text at top transitions to:

   Choose any toolset you want

(middle) The graphic is faded except for the X and the "insertion"
arrow, which are lit as links.  They lead to instructions for
developers.  When the mouse is over the middle third of the banner,
the text at top transitions to:

   Offer your own toolset.

(right) The graphic is faded except for the "extension" arrow and the
rightmost switch, which are lit as links.  They lead to instructions
for administrators.  When the mouse is over the right third of the
banner, the text at top transitions to:

   Extend these choices to your own community.

What do you think?  Does anyone want to draw a proper mockup?  If not,
I can draw one (after I fix my bugs).

window) is dismissed, it could dramatically collapse to the top right
corner, cueing the user to the location of the popper.
