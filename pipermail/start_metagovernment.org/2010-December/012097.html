The debate in question was over the term used to describe the concept,  
not the group. So for the moment, Metagovernment (or Metagov) is still  
the name of this group. In fact, I think there was a separate Vilfredo  
proposal about that.

This was a discussion of whether or not we could use a better term for  
the overarching idea, which this project first called Open Source  
Governance and then Collaborative Governance:
http://www.metagovernment.org/wiki/Collaborative_governance

If you disagree, then we haven't reached consensus. Same with Michael.  
And so presumably we stick with the status quo. Collaborative  
Governance isn't a bad term; it was just felt by some that it is not  
easily understood by others and that it doesn't convey much sense of  
democracy.

The idea behind using the term Open Democracy is that it does pretty  
well bring up an image of what we are talking about. And while all  
democracy is supposedly "open," the fact that we would describe this  
brand as "open" hopefully makes the listener/reader question what  
makes it different from all other brands of democracy.

Here in the U.S., I certainly don't feel that democracy is open when  
Congress has secret sessions, the executive branch has entire secret  
agencies and vast troves of secret information, and much of the  
legislative process is done without any *chance* for me to have any  
meaningful input. Much of our legislative process at every level of  
government is carried out in "informal" meetings between lobbyists and  
politicians: not something that is open or democratic in any way.

So given all of the above, can you get on board with the term, or  
would you like to propose another (or stick with what we have)?
