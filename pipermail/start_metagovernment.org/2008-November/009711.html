- voting on alternatives, including alternatives to existing norms.

To Pietro Speroni di Fenizio:

1) Limiting time of access to the system will not alleviate the differences arising from the differences in time different people have to devotу to analysis of the alternatives. It can be done off-line.

2) Political systems and society structures are based on believes, which are subjective. There were times when people were already talking about freedom and democracy, but it was perfectly OK to deny slaves and/or blacks of such freedoms. Some consider the private property on the means of production as "sacred" while others are convinced it is a legalized robbery.

My belief with respect to the issue you raised is that every citizen should have equal rights to a) vote on legislature, b) judge performance of executive powers (bureaucrats) and c) assess the mass media.

3) Also on the issue of free time. The fight for 8 hours work days has started about 100 years ago and it has been in place for dozens of years. Since then the productivity has risen by about 2 orders of magnitude, yet many people still struggling to have ends meet (or simply die from hanger). How is that possible?
