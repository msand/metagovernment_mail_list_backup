I've included your original text, because you mailed to me directly,
we like to keep as much as possible on in the open and on the
mailinglist.

> So, while I don't think that the wikipedia approach is altogether bad for now, I will
> certainly attempt to take on the task of evaluating alternatives and seeking a more
> facilitative solution.

That sounds great. So far we've just organized the layout around the
relations as they are represented in the code, because that seemed the
most straight forward way. There have been a few interface ideas for
specific areas such as tagging and navigating discussions, but the
overall concept of the site is still ill defined. I'm looking forward
to what you come up with.

For reference here are the previous discussions I could find:
http://thread.gmane.org/gmane.politics.activism.metagovernment/589
http://thread.gmane.org/gmane.politics.activism.metagovernment/590

The only things in terms of interface I've given any thought to so
far, are to keep the layout flexible. That is, the content scales
across a wide range of resolutions and is not fixed width. I also hate
this notion of a "modern" 8pt gray on slightly lighter gray layout.
I've also taken care to separate xhtml, css and javascript so
hopefully we can swap styles and enable access with mobile
devices/screen readers/printers etc.

> I strongly support the concept of visibility of the topics and a logical system for
> forums, etc., but it will take some thought as to what type of software is best for
> groupthink/consensus-forming.

I think, and am sure pietro would agree, that we should see if we can
organize the content in a more dynamic fashion using tags, relations
between resolutions and scores to filter and rank content, rather than
imposing an organization or taxonomy on the content.

Christoph D. Ritter mentioned that he has some experience with user
interfaces, maybe you two can find a way to work together?

p.s:
> Thank you for allowing me to participate

It's not as if I could "allow" anything in this project. I do feel
ownership for the code mainly because I wrote a good portion of it,
but there's nothing stopping you or anybody from just taking it and
running with it. Certainly I would merge anything you do for the user
interface, if you just pointed me to your branch, even if I hadn't
asked you to contribute.
