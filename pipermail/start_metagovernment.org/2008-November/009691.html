One of the most exciting aspects of the presidential elections for me was
the unprecedented use of Internet technology. Digg, YouTube, and Twitter all
set their own personal records, while Social and Mobile Networking all
played a vital role -- at least on the Democratic side of the isles.
The obvious impact will necessitate the use of similar technologies in
future campaigns, however, given the rate of technological advancement, I
believe that future elections will indeed use the Internet in ways
unimagined by the recent election, such as the inclusion of the popular vote
on specific bills, in the vein of GovIt.com

The idea which has plagued me for some time is the need to record the
landscape of an Open Government -- not so much the technical details, but
more along the lines of a platform if you will -- an Open Source (a la Wiki)
Manifesto of what I refer to as the Open Party. Such a thing will be of
great importance once we begin to challenge the "Closed Source"
establishment.
