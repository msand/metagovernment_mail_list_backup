I have heard the argument that 'marriage' is internationally recognised. No matter where you are in the world, there are rights granted to married couples that are not necessarily granted to civil unions or rainbowUnions, irreapective of where or how the marriage was termed valid.

Having marriages performed by churches and civil unions by the state limits the ability of those couples not affiliated with a particular church to get married.
 
In this light, a synthesis or compromise on terminology may be less than perfect.

There may not always an easy solution, even when one thinks outside the box. 

I don't think it's realistic to require consensus for a bill to become law -- more important is to facilitate consensus. If the requirement to achieve consensus were present in the current US constitution, we would be caught up in endless supreme court cases debating whether or not consensus was achieved.
