On Nov 17, 2008, at 1:25 PM, Ed Pastore wrote:

> First, it doesn't allow for any particular empowered president (or  
> other ruler), so people's level of power is always a direct result  
> of their community dedication, not some artificial empowerment  
> mechanism.
>
> Second, everything is transparent and archived. So if only one  
> person is doing all the work, everyone else can still see what they  
> have been doing, even at a later date.
>
> Third, anyone else can empower themselves at any time by simply  
> spending more time in the community. And likewise, someone who is  
> currently empowered can quickly lose that empowerment if they start  
> ignoring the community.

I just noticed that the first and third points above are the same thing.

Basically 1 and 2 above correspond to the first and second basic  
principles. Regardless of our different approaches, I would venture  
that all open governance projects share those two principles  
(inclusion and openness).
