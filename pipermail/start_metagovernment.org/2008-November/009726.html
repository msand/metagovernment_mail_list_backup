The one I am most familiar with is roughly the same as the above, but  
uses more common terminology:
http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Image:Nolan-chart.svg

In other words:
Left-wingers want economic control and personal freedom.
Right-wingers want economic freedom and personal control.
Totalitarians want economic and personal control.
Libertarians want economic and personal freedom.

The personal freedom/control issue is a matter of how one view's human  
nature. The Left thinks people are naturally good and need to be freed  
to express that; the Right thinks people are naturally bad and need to  
be controlled to prevent that expression.

The economic freedom/control issue is roughly the same scale applied  
to one's view of businesses and markets.

I would view Metagovernment as beyond this spectrum, since ideally our  
software will enable all of these views to be accommodated. Even  
totalitarianism could theoretically be achieved through open  
governance, if the consensus agreed that things were getting out of  
hand and everyone needed to be placed under rigid controls.
