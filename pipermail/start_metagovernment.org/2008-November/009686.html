>From CNN last night:
http://caffertyfile.blogs.cnn.com/2008/11/04/change-the-way-we-elect-the-president/

Holly writes:
"I can bank online, day trade online, file my taxes online, shop
online–all securely– but people have to wait hours in line to cast a
ballot in a federal election. Give me a break! If we can't even get
our act together and set up a secure and functional voting method 4
years after the last election fiasco, then I have no faith that we
will be able to turn the economy around."

And a rough count of the other comments suggests around 3.9% of the
respondents (31/795) were independently thinking along similar lines.
It's not Gallup, and it doesn't speak to what percentage of people
would like or be comfortable to see forms of governance online, but it
is somewhat encouraging nonetheless. Not that I detect any organised
pushback - but for what once seemed like a pretty far-out idea - we
are winning the meme war.

In the spirit of good things _not_ being lost in the noise - if anyone
is harbouring some far-out ideas which they've kept to themselves due
to fear of ridicule, or because they are not fully developed - let's
hear them :-)
