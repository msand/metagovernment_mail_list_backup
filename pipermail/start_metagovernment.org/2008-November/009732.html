I have a rather large problem with the "anarchist" section of the video.
None of those people identified as anarchists, often they outright attacked
anarchist groups, and they did not advocate anarchism, they advocated
violence of an organized nature, which is different than anarchism despite
the fact that anarchism has and does sometimes advocate violence.  The
primary difference is that anarchists who advocate violence advocate it
against a specific group of people and only those people, the ruling class.
They do so not as a method of getting people to follow them, but as a method
of getting people to not follow the ruling class.

It is certainly arguable whether or not a society without a ruling class is
sustainable, I'd say it is, but if I didn't think so I would be an
anarchist.

Oh, one last thing.  Any person that tells you "the U.S. isn't a democracy,
it's a republic" doesn't know what they're talking about.  The U.S. is a
republic, and a democracy, not a direct democracy, but "democracy" in
political terms means more than "everyone votes for stuff and the majority
rules."  There are a great many aspects that make up a democracy, not the
least of which is everyone voting.
