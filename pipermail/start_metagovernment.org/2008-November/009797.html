On Nov 28, 2008, at 12:36 AM, Michael Ross wrote:

> Regarding the rating scale, I audit contract programs regularly.  I  
> used to use a 1-10 scale for each audit question but what I found  
> was that the contractor and the auditor could never agree on the  
> degree of compliance and they always ended up with a "split the  
> difference" compromise.
>
> For my last round of audits, I gave three options, 1) in compliance,  
> 2) out of compliance, and 3) not applicable.  The audit questions  
> are divided finely enough that I don't run into areas where we need  
> a range.  If this were the case, I would slice the questions more  
> finely until I could get by with the yes/no response.

I suppose that it could be said that nuanced scoring is redundant with  
synthesis scoring. That is, if people have the option to move to a  
synthesizing proposal, then they can vote down the original and vote  
up the synthesis. That may have the same net result as nuanced scoring  
for each proposal, but be simpler to comprehend.
