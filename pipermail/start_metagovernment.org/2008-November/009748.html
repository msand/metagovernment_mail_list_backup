I now think you're right; identifying interested parties is a dubious venture, and likely useless for the majority of legislative items. All parties are interested parties, in this particular example as in most. 

I guess the reliance is not so much on requiring consensus as it is in facilitating it through intelligent and accessible interface design. 

A corollary to this is a belief in the inherent empathy of most citizens who might consider such a proposal and whether it does or does not limit the  rights/freedoms of others, provided that all interested parties have an equal opportunity to voice their perspectives and that each voter's decision does not acquire a 'mob mentality'.

Nonetheless, a metagovernment-like system could not exist in a vacuum. Mass and/or corporate media would inevitably play a role in advertising opinions, and would continue to sway voters who are otherwise uninformed, but there is only so much one can control, I guess.
