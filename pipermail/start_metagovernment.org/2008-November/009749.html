My name is Mike Ross and I'm a federal government employee.

I'm not going to speak up too much until I've become more familiar with the
group but there is one comment I'd like to make regarding software
platforms.

Based on the discussion related to software, I believe that the platform
should be as simple as possible.  The group tends to be weighted toward
tech-involved people, a number of whom have coding and development skills.
I believe that the platform should be developed using the most simple
technology available in order to make it accessible to the end-user with the
most basic skills.  The vast majority of internet users do nothing more with
the internet than check email and the e-gov platform should be just as
accessible to them as it is to any of us.

I believe, therefore, that one of the most basic discussions should be about
identifying the platform.  My tendency would be toward "cloud" computing ala
Google that would lead us toward a decent suite of tools and at the same
time eliminate the need for a server contract to host the project (and who
has time to administer those sorts of things in a volunteer organization?).

Taken a step further, since Google is a leader in the open source computing
movement, perhaps a collaboration with Google on developing the software
platform would be fruitful.

Best,

MR

> that laws can only actualize if they can attain consensus. (The point
> of the fourth principle -- and fifth, if it comes to pass -- is to
> greatly soften that radicalism by making consensus much more likely to
> happen.)

> http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/California_Proposition_8_(2008)#Results<http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/California_Proposition_8_%282008%29#Results>

> than that. I'm guessing even for something as huge as the U.S., it

> geographically static as the U.S. might just be obsolete.)

> Hi Ed,
>
> Thanks for putting this list together.  I'm Roger Eaton from Los Angeles.
> Mark Frischmuth clued me in and I have been dipping into the email stream
> now and then.  I've been working with a couple non-profits in the area to
> establish a system of "group dialogs" online.
>
> Our idea here is to vote on "candidate messages" written by group members
> to
> elect a message to represent the group.  Using an iterative process of
> voting where a percentage of messages survive each voting round until only
> one message survives, even large groups can elect a message to represent
> the
> group.  The software is called InterMix Group Dialog Software and is being
> developed by a single programmer in Uruguay, Marcelo Delgado, using specs
> that I co-develop in gtalk consultation with the programmer.
>
> Mark's system and mine both use a dual rating system - for interest and for
> approval.  InterMix enables an alternation between "unity" rounds and
> "diversity" rounds, where in unity rounds, all participants together elect
> a
> message to represent their common humanity, and in diversity rounds, each
> group elects its own message.  The system is meant for consensus and
> community building, and is probably good for setting of community
> priorities, but it is not meant for law-making and I doubt it would work
> for
> that.  A somewhat outdated and imperfect description from a semi-technical
> point of view is available at http://udcworld.org/gatech.htm.
>
> The latest push is to add a forum to the system, so that candidate messages
> can be discussed before being voted on.  This forum system should be
> deployed by the end of the year.  Assuming all goes well we will be opening
> or re-opening a couple dialogs at the beginning of 2009.  See
> http://globalassembly.net and
> http://raoulwallenberginstitute.org/mep/index.html.
>
> If the [Start] list is interested to try the technology, I would be happy
> to
> set up a simple dialog for the list with only one group.  The software
> allows preset topics (which can be ignored by the participante), so if we
> try a dialog, we might want to specify a couple of topics, such as
> "What are the sticking points in getting online democracy working?" and "Is
> there a way to develop closer cooperation between [Start] list member
> projects?".  Any other ideas for preset topics?
>
