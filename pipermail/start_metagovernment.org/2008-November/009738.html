Dmitri wrote:
>
> Is  it  technically  feasible  to  develop  a  system  to  parse local
> legislature  (which  comes  in  various formats), into a system, which
> would then allow:
> - voting on existing norms
> - voting on alternatives, including alternatives to existing norms. ?

My own suggestion (to summarize my previous answer) is to use Web
links to point to the documents in their original location and format.
There is no need to parse them by machine.  Provided the documents are
humanly intelligible, people will be able to vote on them.  Or propose
alternatives, and vote on those.

What's more, people need not wait for City Hall to make proposals.
They can initiate their own (in whatever format), and start voting on
them.

This approach (of logical linking) is more scaleable than that of
physically copying, reformatting, and centrally storing all of the
different proposals, and the countless variants of each.

(But I'm interested in what others might suggest.)
