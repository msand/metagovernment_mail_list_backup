Chris's 26 NOV comment about complexity is dead on.  I would like to add to
that comment the need for moving discussions or proposals along quickly.
The keys to selling this concept to the masses are technical elegance and
simplicity, cost (lower the better), and actually producing results.  If
there is a high learning curve and people don't understand how it works they
won't trust it (think black box voting machines).  If they can't conclude a
discussion quickly, they'll see no point in using it.

What we're really talking about here is Government 2.0.  Gotta be faster,
smarter, more elegant, transparent, and ubiquitous.  We should be thinking
along the lines of Twitter.  Our product has to offer something that simply
smokes the current system right out of the gate.
