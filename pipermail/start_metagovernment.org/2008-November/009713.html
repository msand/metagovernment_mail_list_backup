Government is increasingly putting much of its public records online, creating
opportunities <http://mashable.com/2008/10/14/crowdsourced-beltway-pandits/>for
developers to build useful applications for citizens. From being
alerted
to neighborhood crime to finding the best mass transit routes, these mashups
are helping solve everyday problems.

Recently, the Washington DC government launched the Apps for
Democracy<http://www.appsfordemocracy.org/>contest to encourage
developers to build applications using its data. Today,
the winners were announced, and below, we take a closer look at 17 of them.
