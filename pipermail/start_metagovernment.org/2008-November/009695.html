Hello,

I am responding to the following statement by Jacki Buros

"In massachusetts, the initiatives (bill proposals) generated more news and media attention locally than the presidential election did. It would be interesting to put together an initiative to greatly expand local governments, putting most if not all bills up for a (non-binding) popular consensus vote."

I fully agree with this idea. Informal (non-binding) popular vote is the best, practical, near term action that may bring public attention to the idea of alternative forms of government and governance. While many of us have different ideas on what these alternatives are (direct democracy by majority vote, voting with weights according to the degree of information voters posses, consensus building etc.)

Voting on existing laws or bill proposals eliminates the need to have a mechanism for their development (this will come later) and easily understood by the public. The primary purpose would be stimulate public interest.

My equivalent (working protoype) of such a system http://mexnap.org/infusions/vote/ Many of you have similar projects, for example GovIt.

I propose we develop an international system (social network) around informal voting on local laws\bill proposals. It seems that software can be developed. The biggest problem is probably the promotion of such a system. Here I think it would be valuable to enable voting using mobile phones.

If someone is interested, let's discuss
