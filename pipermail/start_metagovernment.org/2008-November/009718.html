Pietro Speroni di Fenizio wrote:
> I am wondering if it wouldn't make sense to have a max time that
> anyone can pass connected to the System. SOmething like: everybody can
> join, but no one (no ONE!!) can participate more than x time per day
> (like half an hour). Enough to vote, and to propose something. Every
> concerned citizen can spend half an hour for the community. But in
> this way no citizen would become boss simply because he has more time
> to devote to it...

Hi Pietro,

You are concerned with the problem of time barriers.  You suggest adding
new barriers (in the form of rules), so everyone is equally
restricted.

An alternative is to bypass the barriers.  Time barriers can be
bypassed by delegation.  Participants in a hurry can temporarilly
delegate their votes to people they know and trust.  So they can
participate by proxy.  Later, if time permits, they can follow up,
shift their votes, and participate more directly.

This is purely a technical solution.  If it's done properly, the
time-rich will end up working for the time-poor.  (In Votorola, we do
it with cascade voting.)
