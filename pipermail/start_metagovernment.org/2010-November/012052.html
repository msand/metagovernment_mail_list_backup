My only criticism of the process is that mediawiki isn't the best
platform to fork and merge upon.  If I make a small (but crucial) edit
to an existing block of text - say the transition section - then
that's not readily obvious from someone glancing at the section that
the tiny edit is there.  So it can get lost, or - perhaps worse -
they'll incorporable my edit into their copy without noticing.
There's no audit trail with copy+paste.

Distributed versioning systems (e.g. git/mercurial) handle this case
really well, but as far as I know there's no user friendly front-end
like mediawiki that we could use.

In terms of scaling though, without the audit-trail, I don't think it
would work very well in larger groups.
