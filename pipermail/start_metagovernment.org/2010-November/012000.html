I got excited, but I don't see how this system addresses the issue of trust.

Each ballot paper has randomised lists of candidates and you separate,
scan and take home the other half of the paper which contains your
selections and a unique barcode.  The barcode contains the encrypted
data needed to recreate the randomised list of candidates.  So you can
then log on to the voting server and make sure that your paper copy of
the voting record matches the one which is online.

But you can't verify that your selections are being counted.  The
voting server could read in your barcode, decrypt it, figure out who
you voted for and add it to the overall tallies.  Or it could just
make up its own numbers.

The database of scanned results could be public domain, but the
algorithm to decode and collate the results has to be black box,
otherwise you lose anonymity.  Distributing it doesn't solve that
problem.

Richard
