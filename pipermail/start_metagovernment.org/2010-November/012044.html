I suggest to not to pick the MediaWiki integrated solutions if the
idea is to augment participants and participation: the first reason is
the lack of a seamless incorporated WYSIWYG editor, which is friendly
and straightforward to those who haven't yet learned to write using
the wiki markup; furthermore (and second reason), possibly who can do
this also could deal with a mailing list, invalidating the motive of
changing the communication medium. Then we should seek the platform
among aesthetically nice solutions, with simple layouts and where the
parts containing strange symbols are hidden to the user (although this
might not be possible, WYSIWYG editor isn't a common feature between
forums too).

It's a matter of image (a minor one, really) to choose a software that
can be run in our server.
