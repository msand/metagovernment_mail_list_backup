No, doesnt make any sense. I read your words, but i dont understand what
you are saying.
I dont want a "macro mechanism", I would only like to have a solution for a
small potentially growing group of people.
A firm sells goods and services and buys input from suppliers.
The firm should not have workers but only equal partners, so its more like
a network of freelancers grouped together in some kind of collective.
>From the outside, it functions like a firm, but as soon as a supplier or a
customer joins the collective, it will have access to the mechanism that
organizes the collective.
So the mechanism has to be scalable, but its ok if it only works for the
collective.
All members of the economy who are not part of the collective, because they
choose to not join, will interact with the collective like with any other
company.

So how do you allocate earnings between members of the collective of
freelancers.
in the beginning there might be only a single freelancer, but soon there
might be 3 and then maybe 5 and then 10.
...eventually, it grows bigger and then it will have 50 freelancers in a
network collective and it might grow to 100 or 500 or even more.
The bigger it grows, the more formalized it has to be.

So it will not only need mechanisms for distributing the income of the
collective, but also organize the work and it should not grow into a
classic hierarchy.
Its ok if hierarchies build ad hoc in order to accomplish a project, but it
should stay flexible and be able to reaorganize at any time.
The collective should be able to help its members to learn and share
knowledge and skills.

If some members of the collective invest in capital goods and rent them to
the collective, that might be ok. But its important, that the collective is
able to buy back the capital good from the part of the collective that owns
it, so that its not possible to gain power from it beyond what would have
been in a perfect market.
