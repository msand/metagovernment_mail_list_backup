> *[Problem experience]*
> I'm back from a short internship at "Premium Cola", which is a network 
> of freelancers and companys that have a consensus based approach to 
> coordinate the whole supplier network.
> They have open sourced their method and they have open sourced the 
> ingredients and process of the beer they are offering. Currently they 
> try to solve the problem about how to pay network members, but its a 
> hard problem.
>
>
> *[Problem]*
> 1. So what about work like all the things that are done in the context 
> of metagovernment.org <http://metagovernment.org>?
> Obviously it cannot pay for shelter, food, clothes and other things 
> you need in every day life.
> There are projects like "Open Source Ecology" which also have their 
> problems and to my knowledge are not completely autarkic.
> So, assume you have a group of people and something (not neccessarily 
> money) to be distributed, what principles could you use to find a fair 
> solution that everyone will be happy with?
> I'm searching for an allocation formula. Sometimes it is easy, for 
> example when a task is given an one could pay a task wage. But when it 
> comes to open source, it starts to become very difficult. The same is 
> true for scientist or any other kind of work or performance which is 
> hard to measure.
>
> 2. As long as no one is payed there is no problem, but people have to 
> live of something.
> As soon as you introduce some kind of payment system to enable people 
> to live from what they are doing, you need a very good mechanism 
> instead of a hierarchy which decided who gets how much, in order to 
> avoid potential corruption of the whole "ecosystem".
>
> 3. I think I do not understand the core problem and thats why i have 
> such a hard time imagining what direction to search for a potential 
> solution. Currently the problem is solved by hierarchies with a 
> top-down-approach, where each layer measures and decided about tha 
> salary of the layer below.
>
>
> *[My thoughts about the problem]*
> 1. In pure market situations, that problem does not exist, but it 
> starts to manifest as soon as markets evolve into oligopol or monopol 
> markets. Then again, the problem with pure markets is, that it only 
> works for generic assets, and becomes more and more difficult when 
> assets (goods and services) become more specific.
> So in theory, a good mechanism, that would be acceptable by all people 
> involved in some kind of "virtual economy" where participants could 
> provide all the necessary ingredients which are important ot create a 
> self sustainable open economy, would enable it, right?
>
> 2. How could a mechanism be able to encourage or at least enable 
> people to do some kind of general work (for example metagovernment.org 
> <http://metagovernment.org>) without depending on the current system 
> (e.g. beeing paid by a government or privatly funded university). This 
> kind of work is hard to measure.
> Even learning is some kind of work and society or parents or someone 
> has to pay for it to create the infrastructure (learning an scientific 
> reasearch are very similar)
>
> 3. So maybe you could use some kind of crowdfunding mechanisms and 
> explain to people why the work is important and should be funded and 
> eventually they will donate.
> BUT: Even if you succeed with this task, the problem arrises, who of 
> the involved people gets what share of the Donation for what kind of 
> work ... and things start to become complicated.
>
>
> *[What about other thoughts?]*
> I wonder if there has been a discussion in the past about a good 
> method to distribute any kind of income or returns or earnings or how 
> you'd like to call it. What about price negotiation? What about a 
> market place for jobs? What about voting? What about exploiting karma 
> points? What about a mix of all of the above? What about equal "wages" 
> for everyone, but then what about free riders?
> Could you elaborate on that and point me to a books/papers/other 
> sources which cover this topic.
> And I would really like to learn about you opinions regarding this issue.
