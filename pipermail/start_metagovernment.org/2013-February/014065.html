Hi everyone,
  I thought I might contribute my two cents here. Legitimacy is something
I've found to contain two completely differing objectives for two roughly
different bodies in society: there's legal legitimacy, and there's
legitimacy among people. If we want to change the world, in a way where the
starving are fed and today's status quo is disrupted, remember that they
system always responds to this by first not only NOT giving you any credit,
but actually, once they're done ignoring you, next is their attempts to
discredit you. Obviously we want to not give them any excess ammunition but
even abiding by the law, I believe they will do everything possible to deny
you legitimacy in their terms. However, them and their system is the
disease and what we really need is to build up our own system, in which
case the only 'legitimacy' we're really concerned with is the kind which
comes with people, common people. Craiglist is useful and people in my life
consider it a 'legit' way to get a new housemate or other things, it's
functional legitimacy. My point? A) in the big picture, the gov is here to
drain do-good-ers energy into an endless and ineffective coil of wasted
will for real change. and in the smaller picture, B) language more
complicated than the average person understands, is the bane of your most
vital target audience. Wikipedia pages that are too wordy are useless to
the developing minds young and old, and Open Source Governance will need a
massive portion of the population to peacefully replace existing, corrupt
forms of governance. I'm a total nerd and even I'm wishing the language
around metagov could get more simplistic/straightforward, but I also see
myself as some 'random person' checking up on your good work so everything
with a grain of salt eh? Oh, and C) After a group's made a decision, you
want as little resentment as possible, which is a complicated thing. Did I
tell anyone here about my idea for open debate tokens?

ALso, I really wanted to check out this Blinap project, but I can't find it
online. Can I get a link? Thoughts?

Thanks all,
Tony
