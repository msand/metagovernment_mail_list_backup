Scott Raney <scott at metacard.com> wrote:
> This all sounds good to me too and the system ought to facilitate dynamic organization like this as much as possible (working groups, etc.).  Where we run into problems I think are with more permanent structures (like that nightmare degenerate of this process, "representative government" ;-)

I imagine keeping the system flexible and responsive enough to deal with potential “degeneration” is possible through “dynamic” self-organization. Programming into the system from the beginning…..the ability for a quick and creative response to this kind of *noise* is key. 

My sense is that developing a successful (tiered) scaling model is the biggest hurdle.  What is the maximum group size we can gather and still make timely and intelligent decisions together? Then scaling becomes simply a numbers game. Population / ideal group size = # of tiers.

In terms of actual ‘representation’….what I’m imagining is a completely different animal than the beast we have before us today.

> Yeah, but it's hard to anticipate all the ways people might try to game the system.  Better to build a robust counter to this kind of chicanery into the decision rule itself (IMHO this means ensuring the broad middle of the distribution curve are actually running the show).

Yeah, but it’s hard to imagine all the creative ways neurotypicals would come up with to *game the gamers*. A "robust counter” might simply be the advantage inherent in the percent difference in size between the 2 populations. Authoritarians aren’t any smarter, nor would they be any more committed to their “chicanery” than neurotypicals would be to keeping the system free of these isolated perturbations.

> No amount of technology or resources will be sufficient if authoritarians can find a way to restrict access to it by whatever out-group they're targeting any particular week.  It's not their fault, it's just what they are genetically programmed to do.  

Agreed. And this authoritarian programming lives in all of us…..to varying degrees, depending on the situation. It’s an archetypal construct that served us well in the past - to protect the tribe from invaders or other forms of danger.  In Jungian terms one could say it’s a 'warrior' archetype. If someone  endangers my family in some immediate and apparent manner, my ‘warrior' is going to come online real fast.  It just needs to be held in check by the other archetypes - (the ‘sovereign’, the ‘magician’, and the ‘lover’).  It’s not evil in and of itself….just out of balance. 

In my mind, it’s simply a question of how to channel this ‘energy’ (or those individuals with a lot of it showing up in their personal psyche) towards support of ‘the tribe’. This of course will need to run parallel with…...the tribe includes *everyone*.

> So instead of great leaps in the technology of *things* what we really need is a leap forward in the technology of human organization to compensate for these instinctive behaviors.

Agreed. That is the preponderant task at hand. What would you say is the next best step to take?
