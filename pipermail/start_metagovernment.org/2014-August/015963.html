> potential member and says "Hey, this is something I could support" or
> at least "Hmm, this sounds sounds promising, I'll hang around long
> enough to see if they get anywhere or maybe it goes down in flames".
> This new page is unlikely to do that IMHO because it simply reeks of
> compromise.  Which brings me to my second criticism of approaches that
> emphasize collaboration and especially consensus (the first being that
> for any real-world problem facing a heterogeneous population the most
> common result will be "no decision"): The resulting laws/policies will
> most likely simply be ineffective because they don't make any
> significant change to the status quo.
>
> The process of governing involves meeting two potentially incompatible
> requirements:
> 1) The laws have to be effective (or why make them?)
> 2) The laws have to be fair and enforceable
>
> "Consensus" greatly helps with #2 (if they voted for it, they're a lot
> more likely to follow it), but reduces the odds #1 will be met.  A
> simple majority rule, or at least the exploitation of that rule to
> produce laws that are maximally invasive, greatly improve the odds of
> #1 at the expense of #2.  I don't know how we incorporate this dynamic
> in our systems, but do know that "simple majority" and "consensus" are
> out as decision rules.
