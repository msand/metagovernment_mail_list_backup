I was involved with the Anti Nuke movement in the early 80’s (Diablo Canyon). I was a part of an affinity group called the “guides collective". We kept watch on the plant for several months prior to the Blockade and then during the action we led *affinity groups* through the canyon to the back gate of the nuke. We would send a representative to the Spokes Council where decisions were made for the *Abalone Alliance* (the group at large).  Very similar to #Occupy…..though the group dynamics were a little less refined back then. :)

In the 90’s I was with a group from Salt Lake that was protesting nuke testing in Nevada. One of the actions I attended began early on a Saturday afternoon in a large conference hall in Vegas. Around 250 people gathered in the room early in the afternoon to come up with an action plan for the next day. It was a fairly typical cross-section of *anywhere* America (doctors, lawyers, housewives, hippies, ranchers, children, and so on). The conflict at times was a bit intense. One group wanted to burn the flag as a symbolic protest. Another wanted to be able to hop the fence and run amok on the test site. It was a little crazy for awhile. The facilitation was amazing though….and by the end of the afternoon, all 250 people walked out of the room all-lined-up for the next days action. It was one of the coolest things I’ve experienced. 

They used the same model as was mentioned in the first group.…(I want to call it *tiered scaling*, though I suppose you and others would probably call it *representative democracy*). Either way, it worked really well for a group that size. I would like to see a much larger group and another tier added to the scaling process sometime.

In my mind, the group in Nevada was deeply *synergized*. And that kind of empowerment has created many significant changes in the past. We arrived committed to something that held deep meaning….and left in a deep *coherence*. It was powerful.

 And that can go either way…..  That's why we need to have a check on the influence of the *authoritarian* complex (as you’re good at reminding us about).
