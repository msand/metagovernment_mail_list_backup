Utilitarianism does have a big problem with altruism, both in
requiring it and predicting and accounting for it when it occurs
naturally.  It also has a lot of trouble incorporating other
instinctive behavior.  The standard handwaving response is that these
things are all to be incorporated into the utility function, but
without any explicit instructions how to get the data or how to
incorporate it into the decision-making process. These are issues I'm
currently trying to reconcile in my Plan.

> I did not read the full text of the American Constitution but the fact that

There are actually two documents I recommend reading, the Declaration
of Independence (announcing the break from our English overlords) and
the actual Constitution.  They're both relatively short and inform not
only because there are some good ideas in them, but also because you
can see a lot of ways they failed to deliver on their promises.  I
also recommend reading the USSR constitution(s) which superficially
closely resemble the US constitution but if you can read carefully
you'll see how they turned the whole thing upside down by putting in
just a few sentences about how the Party is the ultimate authority on
*everything* (and of course there is no carefully laid out description
of how the Party actually works).
