I'm very happy to see government agencies attempting to open up to  
public input more, but Ideascale is a poor way to do it. My objections  
are:

1. It is closed-source and privately controlled, so there is no way to  
verifiably audit the results it produces. One could claim that it's  
not being used for actual elections, so no big deal. But... if  
agencies are acting on the results it produces, then there certainly  
could be motivation for corruption of those results. I don't see why  
governments should ever use closed/private source for anything that  
might affect decision-making. They're inviting a scandal.

2. It makes the far-too-common assumption that something that gets the  
most votes must be the best, when history often shows otherwise. It  
seems downright archaic to use such a primitive concept of voting when  
we have the power of modern information technology at our disposal.  
They could learn something from our "Consensus through synthesis"  
principle. And from some, heck most, of our member projects. They all  
seem to have a more sophisticated vision of voting than a popularity  
contest.

Separately, I very much agree with your assessment of the misguided  
approach in doing this top-down. But what can you expect from  
politicians? If they were actually trying to do it right, they would  
be looking at how to open up the executive and legislative decision- 
making processes; an nobody is even talking about that. That is, after  
all, my motivation in being involved in this project... politicians  
aren't inclined to defer to citizens on a regular basis, and there's  
no reason to expect that to ever change.

> Dear Metagov Colleagues, a number of agencies are using http://ideascale.com 
>  to enable input and feedback on their open gov plans, and while  
> that’s good, it would be better if:
>
> a)      The “ideas” were documented on the Web as proposed goals and  
> objectives in StratML format, and
> b)      Any system conforming to the applicable standard(s) could be  
> used to provide input and feedback on those proposed goals and  
> objectives.
>
> Presently, there does not seem to be any way for users to conduct a  
> queries in their own terms and find “ideas” of interest to them  
> regardless of where they are posted (i.e., on which agency’s  
> feedback system), much less to use any interface preferred by the  
> user to provide input and feedback.
>
> Documenting ideas as goals and objectives in StratML format would  
> enable the creation of indices to address the former problem.   
> Hopefully, Metgov can help address the second half of the problem.
>
> BTW, “citizen-centricity” was supposed to be a hallmark of President  
> Bush’s management agenda: http://xml.gov/stratml/crane/PMAwithoutotherinfo.xml 
>   However, his Administration never demonstrated an understanding of  
> what that means, operationally speaking, and to the degree that the  
> Obama Administration may be satisfied with input and feedback  
> systems based upon the structure of the bureaucracy rather than the  
> objectives (and attributes) of citizens, there will be cause to  
> wonder whether they understand or support that concept either.
