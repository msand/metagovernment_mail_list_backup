You wrote earlier:
> The break point is what you refer to below as "official  
> consideration." How can we know what that is if there is no  
> authoritative mechanism for defining it?
>
> I am all for removing limitations wherever possible, but at some  
> point a community needs to decide that they have reached agreement  
> enough such that they can act. That decision needs to be made either  
> by a person or by a vote of the community.
>
> Many of us don't want to see decision-making concentrated in anyone  
> less than everyone who wants to participate. So that leaves an  
> authoritative vote. Where does that vote occur? More precisely: How  
> does the community know when a proposal has turned into a policy?

In this regard, I think there are two types of decision:

   i) Executive - what *is* to be done

  ii) Normative - what *ought* to be done

Which type do you mean?  These could be examples of the "tiers" you
mentioned earlier.  They are very different and they must be kept
separate, though they may enter into relations.
