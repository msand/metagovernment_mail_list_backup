Thanks Giovani,
that was a very interesting and sobering article.
I have to say for as much as I would love to go to a TED conference, I could
never ever convince myself to pay 6000 $... and then seeing myself refused
the possibility to speak with the people I need to speak.

Also I have participated in numerous conferences over the years and one
thing that I have learned is that conferences are always better if you
present your stuff, and if you present your stuff right at the beginning.
Because then everybody knows what you do, and people who have interests in
common with you will come and talk to you. If not it is you who have to look
for them. And you always will find less of them. So going to a TED
conference and not presenting, right now, would be really shooting yourself
in the foot. You don't get to present, no one comes to talk to you, the
people who present are generally in another place where you have no access,
and you are around with a lot of other really cool people... who you don't
know why they are so cool. Which means that with great probability you will
miss the people who are doing stuff similar to you.

So, no, I would say TED is good if you present, and if they invite you, and
if they pay (The attendance, at least, the hotel and trip maybe).  Then it
is good.

Personally I would like to see two TED talks coming out of our group. One
about metagovernment in general. And another on the idea (which is at the
base of Vilfredo) of using a genetic algorithm to decide among people, and
not with majority voting, but using a pareto front.

I think each of those would deserve its own 10-18 minutes talk. How to make
it happen? Boh! Maybe we should all suggest each other as possible invited
speaker. But before we need to have talks out there.

Speaking of which...
does anybody know when is the OReilly conference will return the reviews? I
would like to know if I got accepted. Because if not I might recycle the
ideas for other papers...

Cheers,
