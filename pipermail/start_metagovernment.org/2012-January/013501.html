A hammer can't do much except pound nails.  The promlugation rules of
G/p/govn (b) are similarly restricted, but in their own way; they can
only guide the passage and enforcement of other rules.  Now, if we
develop (d) network operation rules, then they'd likely be restricted
to the administration of vote-mirroring networks.  (The two rulesets
(c and d) would ordinarily be the issues of separate polls.)

We the working group (d) can do much more.  Although we inherited 3
specific aims, we can always change them.  Ultimately we're restricted
only by our imagination.

No, I think you're right.  I just misundersood you.  I think the
working group should *bottom-line* the administration of the mirroring
networks.  Those networks have components (pollwiki and streetwiki)
that would be costly to replace if they got into the wrong hands.
http://wiki.occupy.net/wiki/Open_Voting_Network#Technical_architecture

They should be in the hands of the people who are ultimately at risk,
which means the population at large.  I think the working group should
take responsibility for ensuring that happens.
