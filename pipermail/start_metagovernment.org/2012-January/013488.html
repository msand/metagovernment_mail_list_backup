Yes, that's maybe the simplest way to get started.

There are two other reasons why I decided to nominate you, aside from
your suitability: (1) To save you time.  The candidate's role needn't
always be active.  Often the voters can talk amongst themselves and
reach consensus without anyone's help.  In that case, the candidate
need only register the final fact of consensus by patching it into his
own draft of the text.

(2) I think it would help the OVN gain traction if someone other than
me took a prominent and responsible role.

It's interesting how this relates to the organizational structure
being debated for Metagov.  Although it's OK to enforce a single
voting method for the ratification of group decisions, it's *not* OK
to enforce a single method for the primary consensus making that leads
up to ratification.  For that, I think we need something like Outcast
that's open to any number of voting methods.  (I guess the OVN is just
the group that says so, and tries to make it happen.)

Mike
