On a related note, i would like to share with you my findings from a
meeting i just had with Prodromos Tsiavos<http://www.tsiavos.org/?page_id=2>,
a lawyer who specializes in open data/source projects, to whom i mentioned
the Metagovernment case. The take-away points from our discussion were:

   - Since at the moment it seems there are only individuals and not legal
   entities (e.g. companies/universities), this simplifies things a bit in the
   sense that there are more options and less hussle
   - The UK or Ireland are good choices for setting up a legal entity, as
   the requirements and upkeep there are rather minimal compared to other
   countries
   - A non-profit seems like a natural  match for this project's principles
   and goals, however the implications and requirements should be well
   understood by all participants before settling on any option
   - Some options for a suitable legal entity are a Community Interest
   Company <http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Community_interest_company>, a
   Charity <http://www.charity-commission.gov.uk/> and a Limited Liability
   Partnership <http://www.companieshouse.gov.uk/infoAndGuide/llp.shtml>
   - Prodromos is also interested in the project and could contribute by
   providing some consulting

talk to you soon
