I drafted a proposal: http://zelea.com/w/User:Mike-ZeleaCom/G/p/govn
This is a small-group governance issue.  It's similar to the one we
worked on for Metagov last year.  One difference is the context, of
course.  This is done in Occupy, where the OVN is just one working
group among many.  So if the practice catches on, then we can
immediately expand it.

Another difference is the clean separation of primary and ratification
processes.  Actual ratification is beyond the scope of the mirroring
network (OVN).  It belongs to the governance mechanism itself, which
is more in the baliwick of other projects in Occupy (like the FGA).
So this is another opportunity for cross-project cooperation.

I nominated you as candidate.  I think you're ideal in that role:

We would do the same kind of thing we did with the Metagov rules.
We'd try and reach agreement.  So we'd mostly draft, paste diff URLs,
and talk.  We'd critique the software too, because half the job is to
pinpoint usability problems and fix them.

There are several forums we could use, but maybe one of these is best:
http://www.nycga.net/groups/ovn/forum
https://lists.takethesquare.net/mailman/pipermail/lists.takethesquare.net/occupy-dev/
 
It's an experiment and I don't know where it'll go.  But I'm pretty
sure the design we're developing (both tools and practices) can scale
to the level of Congress.  We couldn't have an impact on the 2012 US
elections (too little time).  But we could easily make the news in the
runup.  Primaries are crucial, of course, and the OVN can do both
electoral and legislative primaries in ways that cut across all
political parties. *

> > a different project can still contribute to OVN without "hurting" their own

> 
> Yes. Every part of Votorola (Outcast Ui, drafting media, difference bridge, 
> vote counting, voter registry) can be ripped apart and reassembled to any 
> needs. Even the vote counting algorithm can be customized with Accounts inside 
> the current default architecture (1). This means any process that can be 
> expressed in delegate terms is implementable in its framework (including no 
> delegation as a trivial case). Also the difference bridge is not elementary to 
> the technical process (we think it is communicatively), so you can leave it 
> out or offer a better way of people to compare and synchronize their drafts 
> (including copy and paste/none). It also is an interesting problem in itself 
> to compare documents meaningfully.
> You can also leave out drafting and just use voting for predefined positions 
> (a bit like liquidfeedback, imagine a CMS like drupal predetermining the 
> drafts by an organisation). All you need to do is to make the Wiki read-only 
> and implement the necessary draft parsing for your CMS.
> 
> Just wanted to point that out, because (new/outside) people lately tend to 
> think that OVN is only a concept and we have little to offer as implementation 
> on metagovernment/votorola questions. We endorse any reuse (including 
> commercial) of our software and we don't see it as our baby, but as a 
> collective asset. This might help a bit to separate us from the buzz.
