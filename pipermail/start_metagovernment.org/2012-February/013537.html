hi Amir,

I am a member of democracy international  http://www.democracy-international.org/  wich may be of interest to you. (e-book for free download on http://www.democracy-international.org/book-direct-democracy.html in 8 languages)
On the other hand we have bad experiences with political parties and their involvement in direct democracy. They are interested in so far that it not endangers their position to decide and at best they want to launch referenda when it suits them (this system of government induced referenda is prohibited by law in Switzerland). 
That is why we were very interested in the Demoex experiment in Vallentuna, a suburb of Stockholm (Sweden ) who got an elected representative in 2002.
They forced entry in a representative system with a direct democratic initiative.
All over the world similar initiatives are starting and recently they start to work together on E2D. http://e2d-international.org/
In very short:

-in public office elected members of  Parties, members of E2D, will vote 
accordingly to the decision of the citizens in the circumscription involved (on a dedicated website).
- The party will not have or develop a "program". Citizens can induce 
initiatives who result in a proposal of law submitted by the elected 
representative(s) or vote in referendums on laws or propositions, proposed 
by politicians, if they want to do so.

Options: (not limited) - Liquid Democracy, secret voting, use of Electronic 
Identity card, end to end auditable voting system (Helios) , forum discussions, 
possiblity to propose amendments, counter proposals,...

There are websites who provide the possibility for citizens to express their views but it is very frustrating if they are only consultative at best. Make it possible to let decide the people themselves if they want to (if the people want they can  work with liquid democracy that allows delegation, but delegation can be redrawn at any moment ), it can be done now.

We (from E2D) gave a workshop on the ENA conference in Freiburg last year, ppt presentations, preparations and report are available on http://www.e2d-i.net/ (forum is closed and transferred to the E2D forum)

Anyway, I try to make time to read your plans, they may be of help to all of us.


Kind regards and success with the organisation.
