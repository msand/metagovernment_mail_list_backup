> And what does that mean, anyway? That is, what do G, p, and mho stand  
> for? Is that Global, plan, and (arbitrary)?

Ordinary users won't need to worry about this, but here's some
details:

  G

    The name Thomas gave to the global area (for global issues)
    http://u.zelea.com/w/G

  G/p

    Area G defines its polls (aka issues) as sub-pages named G/p/*.
    The "p" doesn't really stand for anything (maybe poll), and it's
    only a convention.  The specific naming pattern may vary from area
    to area.

  G/p/mho

    The local, in-wiki name that I gave to this particular poll.  The
    final term "mho" is just an identifier.  It's best if it's short,
    and also neutral in regards to the issue.  (I got "mho" from
    "Metagovernment HOme page".)

More on poll names here: http://u.zelea.com/w/Category:Poll
