Pietro Speroni di Fenizio wrote:
> The idea of using evolution to develop the next version of a page is
> by no means unique or new. It is in at the base of Vilfredo (which
> is in fact called a human based genetic algorithm), but also at the
> base of mixedink, whitehouse 2, and I suspect votorola, even though
> I haven't explored votorola enough to say for sure.

Votorola uses the drafting model of the common pollwiki (diverse
population of variant documents undergoing continuous mutation and
recombination).  It is close to that of recombinant text.  MixedInk's
is even closer.^[1]  Recombinant text was invented in 2001.^[2]  It's
an evolutionary genetic system that differs from (but is related to)
human-based genetic algorithms (HBGA).  The relations among all
evolutionary genetic systems are outlined here:^[3]

  http://zelea.com/project/textbender/d/overview.xht#Biological 

Whitehouse 2 is (I think) evolutionary only in the sense that it
undergoes incremental changes (like almost everything), but with no
relation to the genetic basis of natural populations.  Vilfredo has no
genetics either (right?).  Can it properly be described as an HBGA?
