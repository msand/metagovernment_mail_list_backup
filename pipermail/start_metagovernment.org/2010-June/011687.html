Sr. Alvaro,

Buenos Dias.

Your basic premise that public voting means coercing and private voting
means non-coercing itself may be wrong !!

In India we follow secret voting and still there is coercing.

In a truly open system (for convenience I call it democratic system, which
may be a misnomer as the so called current day democratic processes are
autocratic), the individuals should have a right to vote or not vote, vote
in public or private, publish their voting or keep it secret... but yet all
actions must be transparent for accountability without any coercion.

The issues to be voted must be open and debatable in public. The invitation
to vote must be open (public). The voting process must be open. The counting
and announcement must be open.

Open means, fool proof and fail proof; with audit trail of all actions for
accountability.

Am I right in my presumptions!!
