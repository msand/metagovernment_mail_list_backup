Thomas and I spoke off list, but I'll add my voice here.

Thomas von der Elbe wrote:
> as far as I can see, we have 4 open questions ATM concerning the free 
> range voting project:
> 
> 1. What name should it have?
> 2. In what structure (organization) should it be embedded?
> 3. What are the conditions of ownership for its domain?
> 4. How should its homepage look like?
> 
> Is there something missing? Is this the right approach? (I have never 
> done anything like this before.)

For the most part, I think these doors are all closed (details further
below).  It was always your initiative to bring projects/organizations
together, and I don't regret having followed you.  It's given us an
open architecture, for example.  But now we're stalled.  Wherever we
go we meet with closed doors.  Developers and organizers are too
intent on protecting their turf (naturally), so they can't open up.

I think it's time to change tack.  Instead of focusing on the
meta-stuff - building tools and organizations for e-democracy - I
think we should focus on doing e-democracy itself.  On this path, we
are far from the leaders.  E-democracy is already out there and
happening.  For example, here's a community of ordinary people who are
already engaged with the political issues that matter to them:
http://forums.e-democracy.org/groups/mpls/messages/topics.html

Where else do you see anything like that?  That's the question I'm
asking in these threads:

  Good quality online deliberation.
  http://lists.thataway.org/scripts/wa-THATAWAY.exe?A1=ind1006C&L=NCDD-DISCUSSION#5

  Where are local issues discussed? 
  http://groups.google.com/group/torcamp/t/599f081d93ab19f2

  Where can e-democracy start?
  http://groups.dowire.org/r/topic/29jMfcqD5sp6ibfjEWwY3M

Online discussions are largely Steven Clift's initiative.  I'm keen to
follow his lead.

Neither of these has gone anywhere.  Only you and I have active

If we can reach consensus on this, then we can host the common
pollwiki/streetwiki (and what not) under the new domain name.  For my
part, I can't see letting anybody but the public decide the ultimate
ownership and name (with only an interim caretaker, till the public
"comes of age").  So this issue wouldn't depend on 1 and 2.  We
(caretakers) could reach a separate consensus on this, alone.
