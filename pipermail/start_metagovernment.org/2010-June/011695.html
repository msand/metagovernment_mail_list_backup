Thinking about this from a technological standpoint, I would guess  
that a workable solution might be to have each vote tied to its user  
via a public/private key pair (as in public-key cryptography).
http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Public_key_cryptography

A user who wished for any particular vote to be public would unlock  
the key on that vote, exposing its underlying voter information. A  
user who wishes to maintain anonymity would keep their info locked,  
but nonetheless could still verify that their vote is being counted by  
checking it with their private key.

 From the end-user experience, however, this would simply look like a  
checkbox that they check or leave unchecked to have their vote shown  
as public or private.
