> Would it be correct to say that Votorola is the focus of activity, in
> terms of working on tools, of the people on this list? What about
> Candiwi?

The technical focus is supposed to be on the common infrastructure,
which in turn is centered on the pollwiki/streetwiki.  You won't see
Votorola in there.  Votorola is just a provider of plug-in services
(pollserver, trustserver, difference bridge) at the periphery.
Candiwi is invited to plug into the periphery too, as an alternative
pollserver.  We could then cross-mirror the votes, enabling the users
to free-range between the two pollservers.

Is Candiwi capable of building consensus on the issue of a home page?
Even if the developers don't want to plug into the commons (for
whatever reason), we could still compare the two architectures side by
side.

Otherwise, you could vote temporarilly with Votorola, while at the
same time helping us to locate other voting tools/projects that are
ripe for plugging into the commons.  We want the voters to have a wide
choice of facilities.
