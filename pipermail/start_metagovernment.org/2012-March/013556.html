means!

On the one hand it sounds like my own attempts at describing the "Limits
of Perception" (Chapters 3/4 of my book) and, on the other it sounds like
quasi-quantum mechanics. Specifically I can't grasp it's connection with
the relatively straightforward requirement, within the decision making
process, to ask participants, from time to time, to declare a preference
(which is all "voting" is about). Examples of what you're trying to
describe might help.

More importantly, I'm focussed (in this context) on the principle of
appropriate use of the "scientific method" and it's role in the decision
making process. The point is to ensure that the debating process (which is
vastly more important than the voting process) is "forced" to include all
intelligent aspects of the debate, including all shades and sources of
dissent, (even the irrational) before decisions are made, optimally by
consensus. (Voting is "only" necessary where we fail to achieve consensus
and thus represents a "failure" of the debating process)

I haven't read Curtis' essay, but I have got (and watched) his 3 part TV
series of the same name. Does his essay say anything different?
