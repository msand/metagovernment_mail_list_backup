It sounds like we are basically agreeing: at least I agree with most of what you say below. Earlier in your and Michael's writing, I thought I saw references to consensus as if it were a society-wide thing. And I just want to make sure that you acknowledge that that's not really possible for any but the largest issues.

In most cases, consensus is mostly about consent of the passive majority and consensus of the active minority. Even within a small group (I see this even in a 5-member group I participate in), it is often the case that on many issues, most of the group members are willing to go along with the thought-leader. We both agree (right?) that there's no problem with that, as long as that leader is not empowered to rule over the others, but is only empowered by their temporary and easily-withdrawable consent.

In any event, I would not agree with the characterization of non-participants as "idiots." Some people are passionate about economics... many more are passionate about football. I may not understand the motivations of others, but if they want to spend their social participation capital on arguing over which team is the best, that's their right. If all we did as a society was make society perfect and efficient, we'd not have much to live for.
