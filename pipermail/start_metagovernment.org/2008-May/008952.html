> I don't disagree with simple -- as long as the system is customizable
> (whether internally or externally), then users can do whatever they want
> with it -- provided they have the skills.
>
> I would just suggest that we keep the participants' needs and wants in
> mind (and allow their needs/wants to be decided by consensus) rather
> than impose our vision of government on them.

 The role of a designer is to make better decisions than consensus would
have made.

We cannot impose our vision - whatever tools we give, they will bend them to
implement the kind of government they want. But we shouldn't implement
features to help this or that kind of government "just to give them an easy
option".
