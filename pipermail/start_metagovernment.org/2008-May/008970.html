The reason I want ratings (I have stopped calling them votes, see the
thread on my design) to be 1:1 with real identities, is that I want an
automated means of measuring consensus. Otherwise, you'd need admins
that go over the discussion, notice when there is consensus and move
it to "prerelease" state and later to "legislation" state - and I do
NOT at all trust people with this decision.

If we already have non-anon ratings, I thought non-anon discussion
would also be a good idea as a way to fight spam - but that should be
thought out, balancing it against the advantages of having a way to
post anonymously (if there really are any, in a world where a
metagovernment rules).

>  >  As I said elsewhere, the second step is to issue
>  >  encryption-smart-cards that never expose their private key, to
>  >  mitigate the risk of someone hacking into PCs, stealing the keys and
>  >  building a voters-army.
>
>  I think of PGP encryption as a big hurdle, that might prevent the adoption
>  of this software, but I can't think of any other way to ensure that messages
>  aren't forged. If the encryption itself is a danger, then smart-cards are a
>  sure fire way to bury the project in its tracks.

If our software handles the encryption well, then all the user will
have to do, depending on the level of security, is:
 * filling a form and paying a few dollars to the local
signature-providing agency (later on, keys will be distributed with ID
cards) and later sticking that key in the computer whenever he wants
to post
or
 * waving the mouse around the screen for a minute and then forgetting
all about it

It is NOT scary and will not prevent adoption.

Encryption isn't such a  hurdle to users if it is dealt with 100% backstage...
