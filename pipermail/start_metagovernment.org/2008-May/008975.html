On May 4, 2008, at 11:32 AM, Aur Saraf wrote:

> On Sun, May 4, 2008 at 6:23 PM, Jacki Buros <jburos at gmail.com> wrote:
>> I would also be interested to know the list's response to the idea of
>> allowing users to approve/disapprove of parts of legislation and
>> comments by highlighting the particular text they wish to rate.
>
> This will be complicated to implement and won't add much - you can
> always comment or edit if you agree with something but not the rest.

There may be a simple way to do it. Instead of letting people comment  
on any sentence, we could just allow and encourage people to make  
sections in their resolutions. For example, "Funding" would always be  
a separate section of a resolution.

At that point, it shouldn't be too difficult to let people comment on  
each section. Or perhaps let people comment on the whole resolution,  
but at least they could easily reference any section in their  
comments. That sort of function is used in MediaWiki (the Wikipedia  
engine) by simply generating an HTML anchor for every section.
