That's more or less my idea, except that I don't believe the security of a
key saved on a PC is enough for something so sensitive and therefore
advocate using smart cards that can sign and encrypt (but that is a decision
that could even be left to the admin, or to the user, or to the organization
that signs certificates...)

> The issue I see here, is that messages don't really need to be under

 You don't revision control messages. You revision control THREADS. See
wikipedia's discussion system. I don't see any reason to not have discussion
as PART of non-final branches of legislations.

>
> One that comes to mind, would be something implemented on top of
> freenet. This is very experimental atm. and usability would be a big issue
> here, but it may be an option. All data would be "on the net", so to
> speak, including the data for the scoring system. When a client connects
> to the system, it would download the scoring data, and then retrieve other
> data, such as messages, according to the users priorities and their
> scores.
>
> I would like to emphasize here, that the biggest issue would be usability.
> Wrapping your head around all these concepts (PGP, DVCS, Freenet,
> Scoring) is a rather big burden for a new user, which may not seem
> justified
> until you've come to know why they have been put in place. It may not be
> necessary for the user to know all this, but since trust in the system is
> essential, it would be good, to keep the system as simple as possible.

> On that note, trust in this kind of system (once you understand it) is
