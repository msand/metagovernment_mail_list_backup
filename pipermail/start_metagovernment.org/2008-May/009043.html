On Sat, May 10, 2008 at 6:00 PM, Aur Saraf <sonoflilit at gmail.com> wrote:
> Hi all.
>
> I want to clarify something, because of an email I received from Manuel.
>
>
> This thread, as well as two others I opened about my design, deal with
> a design proposal that *I* am writing, that I will want to suggest to
> the community once it is ready, and I hope that there will be other,
> radically different, design proposals written by other people.
>
> Why? Because I do not believe in design-by-committee. It is a
> well-known meme in programming circles that design-by-committee
> doesn't work. I think the only solution would be for each of us to
> fork his own design document, where he is the sole decider, he is the
> one who has a vision and knows where he is taking the design, and
> others give him feedback and suggestions that he chooses to
> incorporate if they fit with his vision... And when we're all done, we
> all propose our versions as resolutions and try to reach consensus
> about which is best or how to synthesize them.
>
> When I ask definitive questions, set definitive answers or generally
> act like a know-it-all, I am talking about MY design, for which I have
> a vision that will only be fully expressed in words once I'm done. I
> thought that was apparent because at the beginning of the discussion
> of my design I stated that, and these threads were stated to be about
> it... In other threads I try to be much less benevolent-dictator-like.
> I saw that Manuel has a different design document in the works in his
> repo, and I invite anyone to fork my design and change and finish it
> as he sees fit (and I wouldn't state anything absolute about it, just
> give him friendly suggestions), and would love that to happen as it
> would test my ideas about non-linear discussion/editing that are
> relevant to the final result.
>
> I'm sorry if I sounded like I'm taking control of the whole Metascore
> design - in a community that has a goal to be leaderless I don't thing
> that anyone should, and I am definitely not qualified.
>
