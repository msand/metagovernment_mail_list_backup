I'd say you need about 10-20 min to get up and running, no need to set
up a server.

"Mercurial or Hg for short (the chemical symbol for mercury) is a
Distributed Version Control System. Instead of using a single
repository per project, each developer can have their own repository.

freeHg.org makes it quick and easy to share your repositories."

Tutorial including installation guide and various translations:
http://www.selenic.com/mercurial/wiki/index.cgi/Tutorial

Further reading:
http://hgbook.red-bean.com/hgbook.html

Once you have it installed, rather than going through the ordeal of
publishing patches, set up your own repo at freeHg.org (4-5min)
and push your local repo to it. You can just point there and anybody
can pull your changes.

I won't reproduce how to do that here, there's enough documentation on
the web.
