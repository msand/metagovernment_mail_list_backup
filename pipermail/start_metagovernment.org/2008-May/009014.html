> Seeing that there is much community support for a binary system and
> thus the advantage of Ed's suggestion being the only supported one is
> lost,let me formalize and propose a binary system:
>
> *** RESOLUTION 1 ***
>
> A resolution is proposed to the mailing list by enclosing it in a
> block delimited by "*** RESOLUTION <serial number> ***" and
> "********************".
>
> The serial number is a number that no resolution in a thread already
> has, and is used when rating to indicate which resolution you are
> rating.
>
> A resolution is rated by including a line with "R<s/n>+" or "R<s/n>-"
> in an email in the thread. An explanation is preferred in the + case
> and MANDATORY in the - case. Only the latest rating of the same
> resolution by a user is taken into account.
>
> If, at any point in time,
>  * 72 hours have already passed since a resolution was posted
>  * 90% of the users active in the mailing list in the past month have
> rated that resolution +
>  * no user active in the mailing list in the past month had rated it -
>  * an email is sent to the mailing list with the subject "[resolution]
> <an appropriate title for the resolution>" and the resolution quoted
> in the message body
>  * no other resolution to the same issue was accepted
> then the resolution is accepted.
>
> For convenience, proposed resolutions must be complete - they cannot
> reference other resolutions by name but must instead quote anything
> that they wish to incorporate.
