On Sun, May 11, 2008 at 1:05 AM, Jacki Buros <jburos at gmail.com> wrote:
> I also think a user should be able to summarize the legislation for
>  the community, and debate whether the summaries accurately represent
>  the entire law -- ie, in case there is some objectionable earmark
>  buried in the legalese that the summary neglected to mention.
>
>  Ideally, the summary makes reference to the main document,
>  facilitating verification.
>
>  That way people can vote without having to read the whole darn thing.
>  And, those who do bother to read all or part of the legalese can
>  summarize usefully for the rest.
>
>  Thoughts on this?

In my guess of how the system will work out in practice (that is, what
kind of community will be built around it) I see columnists and
bloggers as taking up this role, *outside* the repository, linking
into it, and about credibility of the summaries - their reputation
will be built on it.

So I do not think this needs to be taken care of, as the ecosystem
will take care of it outside the software.
