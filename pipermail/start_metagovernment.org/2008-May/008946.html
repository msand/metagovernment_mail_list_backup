On Fri, May 2, 2008 at 4:59 AM, Jacqueline Buros <jburos at gmail.com> wrote:

>
> I think it's important to separate the vision for the software from the
> various visions for implementation .. whenever I've spoken about the
> project, I approach it from the software .. while discussing the various
> ways it could be usefully implemented. Obviously the software
> development needs to keep the implementations in mind, but in this area
> I would like to see the software impose as few limitations on possible
> implementations as possible. A community should be able to define itself
> and implement the software in the way it sees most fit for
> self-governance.
>
> Is this consistent with what others are thinking?
>

The way I see it, the only tangible output of this project is the software,
and that is the only output that will matter.

First of all, because your average joe WON'T read the manual. Second,
because patterns of usage will emerge when there is usage, and they WILL
surprise us, as patterns of usage of every piece of social software have
surprised its creators to date.

I see our role in this project as platform developers. When a platform will
exist, a new project can start to spread its use to certain places - like
the world government or Apache Foundation or wherever. It might even be
comprised of the same people. But until there is a platform, there's nothing
to talk about in that front.
