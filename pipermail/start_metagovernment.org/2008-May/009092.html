There seems already to be a lot of support for opening the project up. It is
already pretty open de facto (anyone who wants to join, can) but that's
still a barrier and transparency has its merits in proving that we are (or
at least want to be) what we say we are. Thus, I'm willing to get behind it.
R1+

Resolution 2 strikes me as more difficult to get behind right away, though,
for a couple reasons.
1) The resolution speaks of these things as "customs" when they really
haven't been established as such in the community *yet*. (Pedantic I know,
but we don't want any disagreement between the history and what we claim the
history to be.)
2) What qualifies as "something to show" and being ready for "official
endorsement"? This leaves the idea that I should already have a complete,
"ready to pass into law" resolution before proposing it, which would mean
fudging some of the variables that I know will need more community
discussion before submitting it. Is the correct course of action merely to
do this and then explicitly provide a neutral vote for my own proposal?
Feels kludgy.
3) If there are philosophical discussions going on at all (implied by your
use of "before" and not "rather than"), I think everyone should have a
voice. Of course, there is the astroturfing angle... but I think notability
of a person within the community will be taken into account *by* the
community without needing us to put it into code like this.

- Chris

P.S. For clarity's sake, should we rename the resolution on making
resolutions (the current R1, R1.1,  to being 0, 0.1, &c to keep them from
clashing with this R1 and R2? Having two Resolution 1s confuses things a
tad.
