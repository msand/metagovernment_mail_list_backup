On 5/5/08, Ed Pastore <epastore at metagovernment.org> wrote:
> I'm trying, but at first glance Mercurial doesn't seem very intuitive.
> Where is the actual content? All I see is changelogs, but no view of
> the final content (as it currently exists). And does one have to do a
> self-install of Mercurial just to participate?

This is a tech experiment. It is not supposed to be intuitive. What my
idea of metascore is is exactly *an interface on top of mercurial that
would make it intuitive for this use (which isn't mercurial's intended
use)* (well, actually on top of git, but the differences are
minor...). But we will learn from it a lot of things about how to make
Metascore intuitive (and whether this concept for Ms is indeed a good
idea or not).

Yes, you need to install mercurial to participate. You can find the
files in the website I linked to (go to the changeset, click on "file"
near the html file's name, find the link called "raw" and click
that... not very intuitive, took me 8 minutes to find it)... But
please don't. Please make a little effort and participate in my
experiment.

Once you install mercurial, it's a matter of typing one command and
then you just have the files on your computer and can read them and
edit them like any other files.
