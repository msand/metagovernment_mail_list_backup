I don't always agree with Conseo about the priority of economic reform
over political reform, but I do agree the two are interconnected and
interwoven in places.  And thanks to Conseo's persistence, we now have
some economics in the design of Votorola/Outcast.  We have a resource
accounting mechanism in which votes and pledges are bound together:
http://zelea.com/w/Category:Account

Comparing the benefits and shortcomings of Liquid Feedback and
Votorola is less important, I think, than evaluating the opportunities
we have to cooperate.  Resource accounting may be another example of
that.  We take Thomas's idea of vote mirroring and we extend it to
pledges.  This gives us not only a consensus that stretches across
multiple platforms, but also a pool of resources to act on it.

Eventually our projects will cooperate in such ways if they survive.
There must be a degree of cooperation.  We don't say that because we
think it is preferable to the alternative, but because we know there
is no alternative.  We know the reasons and we don't keep them to
ourselves, but broadcast them everywhere we go.  And we've yet to find
the person who can refute them.  But it's worth the effort, because if
we didn't have this constant confirmation of eventual success, then
we'd probably have lost courage long ago and quit, like so many other
projects have.
