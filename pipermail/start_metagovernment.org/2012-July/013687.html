> Hey guys,
> 
> I would like to use an open source alternative to Facebook. Mainly 
> because I don't want a huge company to mess with my data. But since I 
> can't convince my friends it doesn't work. I guess, there is a 
> "network-effect" where people go where the others already are and then 
> they all stay there. If this is true, open source alternatives will 
> never have a chance.
> 
> So I was thinking, how can we open source facebook itself then!?
> 
> Since all of us here are into e-democracy, I don't have to convince 
> anybody of the power of coordinated collaborative online-action. Now if 
> we would ask the facebook-users if they want their network to be owned 
> by themselves, a non-profit-organization or a big company ... I'm shure 
> 90% would vote against the company.
> 
> So then it's just a question of how to get our will. I propose to buy 
> facebook and then make it open source. In order to buy it we ask the 
> users something like:
> 
> "Join this facebook-group, if you want facebook to become open source! 
> The more members/momentum this group gains, the more people will be 
> willing to donate some money to the non-profit-organization "XY" to buy 
> facebook.
> 
> If the money doesn't rise as fast as the wishes for an open source 
> network, and the members of this group make already 50% of all 
> facebook-users, we will collectivly move over to an open-source 
> alternative, like Diaspora, ... "
> 
> The nice side-effect of this would be, that as the numbers rise, the 
> price of facebook will fall ... until we can afford it.
> 
> What do you think? Do you foresee any problems? I'm not so familiar with 
> facebook, so would a group and a call for membership in it, be the way 
> to go or are there better ways for this within facebook?
