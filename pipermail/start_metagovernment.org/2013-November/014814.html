> No, it can't.
> It can be done "for fun" or "symbolically" for a short period of time to
> "inspire" or to do a "proof of concept",
> but otherwise it will not work.
> Think of it in terms of "path of least resistance".
> People have
> 1. a life
> 2. there is many people on this mailinglist
> 3. manual processes are error-prone
>
> thus
> 1. will cause people to increase their time spent on metagov and reduce
> activity in other domains
> 2. will need ALL participants to do so, OR else it WONT WORK
> 3. if not all of us who do it, do it in the exact same way, the
> inaccuracies will cause more missunderstanding and further discussion about
> correting the manual process all the time
>
>
> I have real experience with this over 12 months of daily activity in a
> pirate party working group called "AG GOuFP".
> They have a wiki and everyone has its own profile page and they have a
> wiki where they post their summaries and they have a mailinglist which is
> automatically SYNCED to a forum.
>
> It just does not work if you do not have the tools to "reduce resistance"
> to a degree, so that the "path of least resistance" changes.
