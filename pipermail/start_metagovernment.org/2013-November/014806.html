Hi, folks.

I have been continuing to mull over the details of how to form a U.S. 
nonprofit to serve the interests of this global group. I am ironically stuck 
at the concept of governance, since the expectations here are so 
antithetical to our objectives.

The bylaws for a typical American nonprofit define a board of directors, and 
those directors typically are elected by the society membership. In other 
words -- representative democracy.

Can some of you help me brainstorm/research some other ways that bylaws 
could be defined? I don't think there is any hard requirement for a board of 
directors; but as far as I know, there also is no other way it is done.

I think the question I am asking (in order to conform to the norms here) is: 
how can I define a "board of directors" to be the group of whomever wishes 
to participate, and further how can that "board" be constructed such that it 
acts responsibly in controlling the company and disbursing funds?
