> The bylaws for a typical American nonprofit define a board of directors, and those directors typically are elected by the society membership. In other words -- representative democracy.

> Can some of you help me brainstorm/research some other ways that bylaws could be defined? I don't think there is any hard requirement for a board of directors; but as far as I know, there also is no other way it is done.
> 
> I think the question I am asking (in order to conform to the norms here) is: how can I define a "board of directors" to be the group of whomever wishes to participate, and further how can that "board" be constructed such that it acts responsibly in controlling the company and disbursing funds?
