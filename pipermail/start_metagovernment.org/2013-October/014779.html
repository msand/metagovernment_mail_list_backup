Max! I am delighted that you are choosing to continue this!

------------------------------------------------------------------------

    Max> Blinap structure is difficult to understand. Good for wonks,
    maybe big not for a techno idiot.

Ned> I heartily agree with your assessment: I have been working (for 
years) to make that better, but still (as you say) have a long way to 
go. Working out the design took years, and now trying to 
learn/understand how to present the design looks like it will take at 
least as long again. (The current state of the presentation is a good 
example of a "partial incremental solution" ...)

Given your talents and interests (and the current abysmal state of the 
presentation), I would suggest that we not start there. Instead (since 
we already have), we might start by trying to develop a [comprehensive] 
set of criteria that could be used to evaluate and compare different 
decision system designs. That would be potentially of considerable value 
across all of the projects of the Metagovernment community. (Pietro and 
Bentley and Mike among others have already been doing that sort of work 
recently, so that our efforts would add to theirs.)

I am genuinely interested to hear what your objections are to the two 
criteria that were introduced earlier in this thread:

    (C#1) The decision system design must give everyone, equally,
    opportunity to participate.

    (C#2) The decision system design must reliably produce "good"
    decisions (from the perspective of outcomes produced by the decisions).

------------------------------------------------------------------------

(This seems enough for one email; we can tackle the proxy issue, the 
interdependence issue, and the transitivity issue in other emails. In 
fact, at your discretion, you might address each of these two criteria 
in its own email, or even its own thread.)
