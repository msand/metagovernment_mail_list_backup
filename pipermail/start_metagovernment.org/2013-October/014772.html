Hi Max,

------------------------------------------------------------------------

    Max> There a number of functions that require compensation.

Ned> I agree. And, different functions will often require different 
models for determining compensation. If you have a list of such distinct 
functions in mind, perhaps (on its own separate thread) you could post 
the list?

------------------------------------------------------------------------

    Max> This will often involve money.

Ned> If we so choose (and I hope that we do), yes. And within the 
"compensation through money" model, there is the distinction between 
"compensation from direct donations" versus "compensation from 'public' 
funds". These two sources of compensation will need to be distributed 
through two very different systems.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

    Max> So maybe not after we all have abundance.

Ned> Yes. I agree that if we play our cards well (at the metagovernance 
level and at the global governance level), we can potentially render 
money mostly obsolete through creating enough "abundance for all" to 
drop essential commodity prices to near zero.

(But even then, there might still be a role for money in distributing 
scarce non-essential non-commodities and such -- everybody can have 
pineapple, but not everybody can have fresh Hawaiian pineapple.)

------------------------------------------------------------------------

    Max> This coming year I will want to look at a micropay donation
    system for funding.

Ned> Great! And also, micropay donation systems distribute direct 
donations. We will also need a system for distributing "public" funds 
(e.g., from the foundation that Ed is aiming to set up).

------------------------------------------------------------------------

    Max> Figure that discussion moderation can have a strong money
    component.

Ned> This appears to me to be a very "packed" sentence. Are you 
proposing that we adopt a moderated discussion system of some sort? (If 
so, maybe you could start a new thread with the proposal?)

------------------------------------------------------------------------

    Max> Assume this is what will work in a transitive representation
    scheme and try to figure out how to tune it to deal with the shills
    and trolls.

Ned> Having implicitly proposed that we have a moderated discussion 
system, you are now further proposing that we have some sort of 
transitive representation scheme? (If so, maybe you could start a new 
thread with the proposal?)

------------------------------------------------------------------------

    Max> Easy enough, but always temporary.

Ned> I am not sure what you intend as the referent for "easy enough" and 
"always temporary" -- dealing with shills and trolls?

------------------------------------------------------------------------
