1. someone else, maybe your former self, already mentioned, that people
around here are "anti-cartesian".
I never heared that word before and have absolutely no clue what you are
refering to.
What is it?

2. Imagine a world where every person on the planet has an informed
oppinion about government and could express them in a "machine readable"
way. So basically, the machine (his personal digital assistant) could apply
his assessment to every decision that takes place somewhere. If i were in
that situation, i would not need a "human" to vote on my behalf.
All i need is some "high level expression" of my will that could be
accessed by my "machine agent" and applied to every decision which is made
somewhere. I could even specify my will on all kinds of details, but could
also express my will in a way, that leaves room for "implementation
details" within the boundaries of my expressed will.
My will will then be applied through my machine agent, as long as i am
allowed to "vote" on something.
A decision that only is valid between 2 persons (Person A wants to marry
Person B) is probably a decision, that i am not allowed to decide upon as
long as I am neither A nor B, according to other "aspects of my will",
which will probably have led to a "rule" that says exactly that.

So, yes, we are far from an "imaginary world" which is mentioned in the
introdoctuion of the above section, but there are lots of things that needs
improvement in order to make that "imaginary world" a reality and i do not
see how making "delegation of my right to vote" will improve anything.


Reminder: WTF is "anti-cartesian"? :-)
