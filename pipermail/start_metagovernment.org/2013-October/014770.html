    )
    Paul> a small remark about “democratic methods”. Democracy is about
    the legislative rights of citizens. Using some tools who can be
    about “participation”, having a say” , “decision making” or even
    voting and voting systems in any other proces has nothing to do with
    democracy and serves only them who wishes to hold on to legislation
    by interest groups.

Ned> Alex, I have to agree with Paul: your "issuing shares" system for 
compensating work done for a for-profit business enterprise cannot and 
should not be used (as you seem to say above) to compensate work done 
for a not-for-profit "what's best for humanity, nature, and future 
generations" metagovernance/governance system. Do you agree?

> 1. those who own shares own part of the subject which the share 
> represents, so they become owners and therefor can decide about 
> everything related to the subject. Because normally,there are many 
> owners sharing a subject, they need some tools and democratic methods 
> to decide about the subject.
> 2. There will be revenue in some form (probably USD or EUR, but maybe 
> Bitcoins or even something completely different). The shares will 
> waste away with the average rate of depreciation and replacement 
> investments have to be made in order to get new shares. BUT the 
> current amount of shares each individual holds at any moment, divided 
> by the total amount of shares in circulation, will decide how much 
> revenue each individual gets. So the shares will be a source of income.
>
> A share is a token which generates monetary income if u own it. The 
> token will depreciate and the rate of depreciation will depend on the 
> subject to which the "token" (which is the share), refers to and how 
> that subject will evolve or be developed...
>
> So i guess, the value of a share for someone who might BUY it, stems 
> from the fact, that it generates income and grants "decision rights" 
> when it comes to the subject it represents and those "decision rights" 
> might be of value to someone who thinks, that he can influence the 
> subject in a way, so that the income stream generated by the token 
> will increase :-)
