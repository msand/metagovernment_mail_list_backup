On Oct 15, 2014 12:12 PM, "Steve Coffman" <steve at stevecoffman.com> wrote:

> Scott Raney <scott at metacard.com> wrote:
>
> Don't get me wrong, I'm an environmentalist too, but IMHO the solution
> to a degrading environment isn't the government (as it exists) and
> especially isn't the economic system.  It's primarily a religious
> issue: The People have to *believe* that dealing it with is the right
> thing to do.  Once that is in place, it pretty much doesn't matter
> which government or economic system you have in place as the necessary
> changes will be made.  But this is why it's not something I think it's
> necessary or productive to discuss here even though allowance for it
> should be part of all of our system designs: Metagovernment for me is
> not a religious or marketing organization and so I'd prefer to
> concentrate on more nuts-and-bolts kinds of things here.
>  Regards,
>    Scott
>
>
> When I consider what needs to happen for us to avoid the myriad of looming
> catastrophes, and the timing within which it needs to happen, I think that
> developing a platform *outside* the constraints of contemporary political,
> religious, and economic structures...one that can skillfully focus on very
> specific solutions...is our only realistic hope.
>
> Creating renewable energy technologies, replanting forests, educating
> children in third world countries, (and so on), isn't dependent on regional
> or global politics and economics so much as it is on the creative focus of
> individuals, and smalls groups like us.
>
> What Elon Musk and his team have done for humanity and the environment, in
> just a few brief years, is way beyond what any polity has been able to
> accomplish through elected representation. The initiative the X-Prize
> foundation is launching for the education of illiterate children around the
> world will have a far greater cascade (in so many different domains) than
> any initiative the U.N. is capable of forwarding toward that end.
>
> In my mind, the only way we will bring about real *transformation*,
> requisite to both the timing and scale of urgency we are facing, is by
> developing an entirely new model/structure/process/platform for human
> evolution.
>
> I think we must begin building something that can empower a very large
> number of people, (and organizations), in a very short period of time.  If
> we involve contemporary political, religious, and economic institutions, we
> are just asking for (more of the same) delay and dysfunction.....too much
> entrenched polarity, negative momentum, greed, distrust, and apathy.  The
> current system is a dinosaur.  In my mind, the sooner we let go the better.
>
> I believe we could begin this effort by looking at what *really* needs to
> happen and when. If we ask, 'what are our fundamental priorities?', I don't
> think creating better systems of governance is tops on the list. Would be
> nice...but how long will *that* take?  I'd rather focus on advancing
> permaculture as an alternative to commercial agribusiness in order to save
> the bees, or towards developing technologies for cleaning up the mess that
> BP has left us with in the Gulf of Mexico, or providing water filtration
> systems for the billion or so people that live without potable water on a
> daily basis, ......and so on.  Those are issues that really matter....to all of
> us.
>
> In my mind, the *only* thing that will get the job done, from where we
> are currently positioned, is a very large segment of the population
> empowered to work synergistically towards solutions that connect us and
> provide for the whole of life. We *don't* need a *democracy* to do these
> things.  We just need to create a platform that's crowdsourced and funded,
> horizontal, and self-organizing.  One that is set it up to evolve and
> sustain it's own process without a hierarchy determining it's orientation
> and administration.
>
> In the not to distant future the smart phone will be our ballot box and
> we'll be making decisions globally through a hybridized social network.
> Corporations will be member owned and operate with a charter that has a
> heart and soul.  They will manage the infrastructure of our society.
> Politicians will fade into the dark history of our troubled adolescence.
>
> The singularity is near.  I say, let's create something (together) that
> has the potential to move us to the stars....without leaving a wake of
> destruction on this lovely, beautiful planet we call home.  :)
