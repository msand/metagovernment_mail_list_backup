There will certainly be issues where authoritarians in different
Localities will cancel each other out, but that doesn't mean we can
ignore their influence.  Firstly because this general dissent will
mean smaller majorities will be seen for proposals that affect
multiple Localities differently (i.e., there goes that consensus thing
again).  Secondly, especially during early part of the transition,
there will be cases where all the SDAs in all the Localities will vote
as a block (trade policy being a likely area).  To prevent the
adoption of irrational (tribalistic) policies it's still going to
require massive participation from the rest of the population to
overwhelm SDA biases.

An interesting proposal.  It would be nice to have the flexibility to
designate different proxies/delegates for proposals in different
domains.  One thing I'm definitely planning is to provide the option
of a weighted list of proxies.  This will handle the situation where a
single proxy doesn't vote on a particular proposal and when multiple
proxies vote in opposition to each other (it'll be your own little
"instant runoff" system).  I'm also trying to figure out how to
prioritize proposals by an individual's interests.  Sort of like what
FaceBook does, but the categories would be explicitly specified in a
user's "Goals" section (e.g., "I want to see everything concerning
land use policy in my Locality" or "I want to see any animal-rights
proposals at the top of my feed" or "don't bother me with anything
related to taxes as I let my proxies handle all that stuff").

A lot of this type of stuff we'll just have to establish by
prototyping.  Flexibility is nice, but not when it makes the system so
complicated that the majority of people (who will want to minimize
their time investment) can't figure out how to use it effectively.
