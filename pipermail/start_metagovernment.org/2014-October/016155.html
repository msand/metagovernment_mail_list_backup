> wiki. And that we have monthly meetings not only to discuss topics
> intensively, but also to review what has gone on on the list in the past
> month.
>
>  But this thread did catch my eye, and I have submitted an alternate
> proposal. Mine lists some objections to the standing one, and I'll
> reiterate them here:
>
>  There are a couple problems with "democratization of global governance."
>
>  "Democracy" is a totally ambiguous term these days. The term
> "democratization of global governance" could be taken to mean that we want
> the government of China to be more like the government of the United States.
>
>  And "global governance" sounds like world government. While we are
> aiming for global, decentralized, and/or non-specific systems and
> communities; I don't want people to think we're trying to make one global
> government of the world. Something along those lines is more of a vision
> thing anyway. And our vision statement implies that.
>
>
>
>  There are a bunch of other proposals listed in the standing proposal.
> They certainly should be addressed too, but not as bits buried in this
> proposal. Especially the project to overhaul the list of Active Projects
> and Related Projects.
>
>  Michael had a great idea for how to approach it, in this thread starting
> here:
>
> http://metagovernment.org/pipermail/start_metagovernment.org/2013-April/005464.html
> but he required some upgrades to the wiki for it to get done. I can try to
> install those if someone wants to take it on that way. Or we could take a
> less technological approach and try to sort manually. But using semantic
> wiki does have some very nice long-term implications for a sophisticated
> list.
>
>  Note Michael’s idea was revisited somewhat in this thread:
>
> http://metagovernment.org/pipermail/start_metagovernment.org/2013-September/005798.html
