Hi Pietro (Good Question!),

Your question has led me to notice that indeed I was not clear. (That 
happens when I hurry ...) So now I will start over.

I see a community Vision quest as potentially a very worthwhile project. 
The question then becomes, how to proceed?

We could start by hammering out a single vision statement through 
Vilfredo, as we did the Home Page text. But, in order for a brief vision 
statement to actually mean anything useful, it must be backed by an 
actual vision of something. If the something is a social system or 
social sub-system, it is going to be a very complex thingy, with myriad 
moving parts all interconnected and interacting. (Just a brief overview 
description of Scott's "ThePlan" is 85 pages long. The brief overview 
description of Isonomea that was on wiser.org had 160 pages. The 
Votorola material is similarly lengthy, as are some of the Sociocracy 
texts.) Vilfredo cannot handle such lengthy and complex proposals.(Yes?)

My thought is that we need to have already reached a common vision in 
order for it to be worthwhile for us to try to produce a brief vision 
statement backed by that common vision.

So then the question becomes, how might we best begin the project of 
reaching a common vision?

My (here proposed) answer to this question is that it might be good to 
start by proposing fundamental "filter principles", that any proposed 
vision must be in accord with (in order to be considered as a 
candidate). Coming up with a list of such filter-principles to use in 
vetting candidate visions would be very metagovernmenty work, and if 
done well would produce a list that would potentially have lasting and 
general usefulness. (Scott will want to have "effective" and "efficient" 
and maybe "Paleolithic" on that list.)

Once we have the list of filter-principles in hand, we would then have a 
very powerful tool to apply in trying to select/synthesize a common 
vision. Once we had a settled vision, we would then be ready to hammer 
out a brief vision statement backed by that vision.

The foregoing is (now) my proposed plan for executing "The 
Metagovernment Vision Project".

The right way to use Vilfredo at the beginning of this plan (if Vilfredo 
is to be our tool) would be to propose each individual filter-principle 
separately to Vilfredo. Then the list of filter-principles would be 
populated by the winning result of each Vilfredo thread.

In the interest of grounding this discussion with an example: were we to 
decide to undertake "The Metagovernment Vision Project", and to execute 
the plan here proposed, and to use Vilfredo as our synthesis/decision 
tool, my first filter-principle proposal (to Vilfredo) would be THE 
PRIME DIRECTIVE:

    To be acceptable, a candidate vision (social system or sub-system)
    must be explicitly, intentionally designed to eliminate Power Over
    Other People in every way possible. By design, there must be no
    concentrations of power entailed, caused, or permitted within or by
    the envisioned social (sub-)system.

    The reason for this is simple. It does not matter through what means
    power is initially concentrated (through political, criminal,
    police, military, economic, financial, religious, or whatever other
    means) -- the end result will always be the same because power is
    fungible: any form of concentrated power can be used to acquire more
    power of any kind. As soon as there is any concentration of power of
    any kind produced by any social sub-system, the overall social
    system will commence to morph toward further concentration of power:
    the existence of concentrations of power sets up positive feedback
    loops that lead to further concentration of power.

(Given that Vilfredo always wants to start with a question, I guess we 
would have to play Jeopardy to come up with a question to which this 
proposal would appropriately respond ...)
