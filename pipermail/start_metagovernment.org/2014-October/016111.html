> Unfortunately, I think the answer is yes.  But you'll get a chance to
> fix this by adding your own proposal in the next round.
>
> But this brings up what I think is a design flaw in the current
> Vilfredo: The lack of a measure of *effectiveness* of the proposal.
> All it seems to currently do is have people assess the *accuracy* of
> the proposals with the result being that there is a strong force
> applied to weaken them as much possible to avoid setting off anyone's
> BS detector.  The result is like the new MG Home page, something
> that's technically accurate but completely ineffective for its
> (assumed) purpose.  In a Chinese philosophy sense, it's all "yin" and
> no "yang".  There needs to be an opposing (or complementary, if you're
> a Taoist) force that would work to make the statements stronger and
> therefore more effective.
>
> I've been trying to think of how this would work and have a couple of
> suggestions:
> 1) There needs to be some "goal" specified along with the original
> question: How are we going to assess whether the output of the process
> is a success or not?  In the case of the Home page, I would have
> assumed it is primarily supposed to be a recruiting tool, to get
> people to read the rest of the site and join the mailing list.  In one
> sense the "real world" can be counted on to provide this feedback, but
> that's too inefficient: By the time that feedback arrives the
> organization may be dead (e.g., if some company used Vilfredo to
> determine a feature list for their product or a marketing plan, they
> need to know it will work *before* they launch because that is a
> bet-the-company decision).
>
> 2) There needs to be two types of feedback, one on accuracy one on
> effectiveness.  While I'm still more a "ratings" rather than "yes/no"
> kind of guy, I think you can do it with a simple measure of whether
> the participant believes the proposal would meet the goal or not.  Not
> sure how to integrate these two measures (e.g., combine at each phase,
> run parallel Pareto fronts, or wait until the end when the integration
> and final vote occurs), but fortunately it's not my project so I don't
> have to run the experiments to see which works best ;-)
