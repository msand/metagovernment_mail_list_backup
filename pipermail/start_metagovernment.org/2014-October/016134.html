I see a community Vision quest as a great idea!

But, (in keeping with the "many visions" Home Page sentiment) we might 
find it productive to begin by gathering a list of the visions that 
already exist among those in the community. Or maybe even by gathering 
discrete elements of those visions (for example, discrete functional 
specifications that the envisioned global social system is intended to 
satisfy). My thought is that we need to side-by-side articulate our 
views (and our differences) as a precursor to tackling the amazingly 
difficult endeavor to try to arrive at a single common vision. (This 
work done well would be in itself be of general value.)

If we try the straight-to-it approach, then Vilfredo might be an 
appropriate tool. If we try the collect-elements approach, then we are 
going to need some other tool. (Among those potentially available, 
SettleIt->AllAngles would be my first choice, but it is still not 
operational enough...)

>     Speaking for myself (and too briefly, because these listserv email
>     exchanges have not delivered enough "bang for the buck" over the

>     strategy to travel there) that I am working from.
>     ( http://metagovernment.org/wiki/Vision#Vision )
>
>     The first sentence expresses one particular design specification
>     that I heartily embrace (for the new global social system that is
>     my destination-vision).
>
>     The second sentence does not at all express my vision of how to
>     get there.
>
>         First, "spreading out" is not the important part; "spreading
>         up" is the important part. For example, the CIA has spent the
>         last hundred years in South and Central America systematically
>         crushing every community that appeared to be on the way to
>         implementing a better/successful social system. The Powers
>         That Be cannot afford to have better examples existing, so
>         they crush such examples as they appear. (Read "Confessions of
>         an Economic Hitman" and "War is a Racket" for evidence.) The
>         "how to get there" strategy that the sentence suggests fails
>         to take this reality into account. (Just because the Hobbits
>         in the Shire choose to ignore Mordor does not mean that Mordor
>         will ignore the Hobbits in the Shire ...)
>
>         Second, the notion of "collaborative governance" is very much
>         in dispute within the community, and has been for a long time.
>         That term cannot be used in the vision statement unless/until
>         its meaning has been clarified and agreed upon.  (See for example:
>         http://www.metagovernment.org/wiki/Collaborative_Governance_v2.0 )
>
>
>     The third/last sentence of the vision statement again expresses
>     several laudable functional specifications, but too vaguely to be
>     of much use. (It is the sort of might-mean-anything sentence that
>     politicians use in campaign speeches.)
