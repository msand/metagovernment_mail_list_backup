Scott Raney <scott at metacard.com> wrote:
> Umm, I'm thinking this is what democracy *is*, or at least should be.
> Choosing to not call that thing "government" is just perpetuating the
> misconception that "the government" is somehow something different
> from "us".  The problems with our governments not looking/working like
> that is our own damn fault, not the fault of "the government" or "the
> corporations" or even "the authoritarians".  Which I guess means I'd
> like to correct my previous statement: There is a religious purpose
> for Metagovernment and that is to instill faith in people that we can

> better spent helping us than in grousing about our current systems or
> trying to fix them (or game them).

Right. Though I don’t see the point in dwelling on what democracy and government should be...or could be.
The *system* (as we know it) is no longer valid or useful as a tool for managing our affairs. We've used 'democracy’ as a front to proselytize our imperial ambitions, and ‘government' is the behemoth running the machine that’s taken us to the edge. I no longer see the value in associating what’s emerging with those words (or concepts). Too much baggage!  But, call it what you want….I think the most important thing is that we begin designing and developing the next iteration. And that we do it soon….like now.
