> I thought that it may be easier to give potential decision-makers an exam
> before they may decide.
> E.g. everyone creates one multiple choice question and publishes it with
> correct answer. 200 questions are randomly taken for the exam for potential
> decision-makers. The best 5 decision-makers than decide the thing that
> needs to be decided.

> and studying. It may take specialist into decision making too. There are no
> official boundaries and it is still localized. No age limit.  The question
> do not need to be translated. If we select randomly the decision makers
> from top 10% in the exam, the motivation may rise. It can be tuned or mixed
> with existing structures for better results and easier transition.
> Still I should e.g. think more how make public sector respect the
> decisions and how to allocate resources for the decisions.
