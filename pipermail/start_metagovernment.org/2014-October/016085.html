Hi James,
the old version of Vilfredo (which you are currently using) did not use any
external packages that I remember, except for the edit box. The new one is
written in python and uses indeed bootstrap. It also has a strict division
between back end and front end. Something which the original did not have.

The reasoning behind the limitations has to do with what I call "fair
participation" which basically boils down to evaluating proposals only on
the base of their content, and not on who proposed them, and when were they
proposed. People with more friends can usually propose things earlier and
have more friends supporting their idea. I do not have many videos on
Vilfredo, and we are not preparing them as we are busy producing the new
version, and as such any video would be outdated as soon as it comes out.

You can see a talk I gave some years ago on fair participation as it
relates to fair division (a mathematical sub field) here:
https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=o8Ty-E9xlDA
