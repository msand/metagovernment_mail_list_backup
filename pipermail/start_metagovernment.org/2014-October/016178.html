On Wed, Oct 15, 2014 at 10:12 AM, Steve Coffman <steve at stevecoffman.com> wrote:

> In my mind, the only thing that will get the job done, from where we are
> currently positioned, is a very large segment of the population empowered to
> work synergistically towards solutions that connect us and provide for the
> whole of life. We don’t need a *democracy* to do these things.  We just need
> to create a platform that’s crowdsourced and funded, horizontal, and
> self-organizing.  One that is set it up to evolve and sustain it’s own
> process without a hierarchy determining it’s orientation and administration.

Umm, I'm thinking this is what democracy *is*, or at least should be.
Choosing to not call that thing "government" is just perpetuating the
misconception that "the government" is somehow something different
from "us".  The problems with our governments not looking/working like
that is our own damn fault, not the fault of "the government" or "the
corporations" or even "the authoritarians".  Which I guess means I'd
like to correct my previous statement: There is a religious purpose
for Metagovernment and that is to instill faith in people that we can
just create a new system and then switch over to using it whenever we
think it's ready.  Their time and energy (and money) would be much
better spent helping us than in grousing about our current systems or
trying to fix them (or game them).
