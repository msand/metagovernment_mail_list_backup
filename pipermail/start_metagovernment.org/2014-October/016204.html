> power, their innate prejudices and obsolete moral codes lead them to

> the representatives are unskilled or uneducated, it's because they're
> easily corruptible and the system is designed in such a way to allow
> special interests to exploit this characteristic).
>
> Speaking of which, as part of my utopias/dystopias survey I recently
> finished HG Wells' "A Modern Utopia".  The leaders in that system are
> called "Samurai" and they are self-selected: Nobody votes them into
> (or out of) office, they just have to follow "The Rule" to demonstrate
> that they're qualified.  "The Rule" being a very odd set of criteria
> including being required to take cold showers, wear special clothes,
> be teetotalers, and avoid any occupation that involves buying or
> selling things.  It wasn't exactly clear to me what any of these
> things had to do with being good at government, and worse, these are
> exactly the kind of arbitrary rules that Authoritarians would be happy
> to follow but the neurotypicals would not (i.e., it's exactly the
> opposite of what *I* claim is the way to ensure good government).  It
> also didn't seem very "Utopian" to me: There are no pets, there seemed
