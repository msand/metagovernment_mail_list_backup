Michael was clearly onto something when he blanked the home page: it gave us a strong incentive to work on a solution. Now that I took the intermediate step of publishing text on the page, the urgency has declined quite a bit hasn’t it?

So I suggest we take an even bolder next step: gather together a pool of money and decide what we’re going to do with it. A large enough pot of money should provide a strong incentive (ideally, I’d like to see on the order of US$20,000, though even a few thousand would probably be a big enough carrot to get us to fight over it with some good fervor). I’m not really in a place to put up that kind of prize by myself, but I was thinking we could crowdfund the prize. 

What do you think?

And if you like the idea, do you have ideas on how to amass a crowdfunded prize? Or another way to gather a pile of cash for this purpose? I was looking at Kickstarter, and they seem to be very finite-project-oriented and may not accept something so open-ended as: “Create a cash prize and let an open community use open governance software to decide how to spend it.”

Regardless, note that crowdfunding sites don’t do much to publicize projects. So if we start one, we need to also have a publicity plan to get it recognized and to incentivize people to contribute.
