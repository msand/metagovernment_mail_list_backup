One piece of feedback I had for Vilfredo was to be able to alter (or mutate) a proposal without having to submit an entirely new proposal.  Having to scroll back and forth between several similar proposals while trying to figure out what was different in each one of them was kind of frustrating for me. 

This was just posted on the Loomio - Feature Ideas group. I think this combined with Vilfredo would be a really useful set up. 

These first 2 screenshots are from quip.com




> Loomio Community - Feature Ideas
> 
> Realtime Collaboration
> 
> by Jacob Gadikian
> 
> My two bits in terms of feature requests is most certainly realtime collaboration on nearly any document that loomio can serve. Realtime collaboration is something I have seen used via my organization's quip account to really positive effect countless times.
> 
> I have a gut feeling it's going to work out quite well here, too.
> 
> Are there plans in the works for this?
> 
> Personally, I think it is something that will make loomio more fun and really help it to attract users to its deliberative process.
>

> Proposal: Integrate Realtime collaboration wherever possible
> 
> by Jacob Gadikian
> 
> Because of its positive effect on user engagement, Loomio should integrate realtime collaboration features wherever possible.
>

> I'm really happy to do that. I'm going to need about a week or so to get to know loomio to make some recommendations. I'd like to let you know about my use-case.
