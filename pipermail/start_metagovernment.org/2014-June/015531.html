Here's a draft plan for the home page.  Would anyone reasonably be
unable to accept it?  http://zelea.com/w/User:Pipe-1139-ZeleaCom  *

That's how I think we'd introduce Votorola into the discussion of an
issue: a carefully worded question about a carefully worded draft.
The answer should be, "No, there's no reason why this draft cannot be
accepted."

The aim is to get consensus right from the beginning, no matter how
large the scale (e.g. whole world).  Other methods begin with lots of
text, and then sweat the consensus.  We throw the text out the window,
leaving holes or "abstract hotspots", and take the consensus instead.
Then we grow the text on top of that consensus without ever losing it
(later we'll see how it's kept pretty safe).  But first, do we have an
initial consensus in this case?  Or did I botch the seed draft?
