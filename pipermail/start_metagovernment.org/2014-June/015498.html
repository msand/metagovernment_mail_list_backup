> An end run?  So that's your idea...  Now I see your thinking.
> Vilfredo is similar to Blinap in that both require unanimity.  You
> like unanimity (and dislike majority and plurality), so you thought
> you might use Vilfredo to introduce a unanimous decision process.
>
> But that wouldn't be right.  Vilfredo's a fine tool for brainstorming
> or small-scale decision making, but we haven't agreed to use it as our
> decision platform, any more than we've agreed to Loomio.  In fairness,
> we must open up *all* of our decisions to people in the list, even to
> non-users of Vilfredo or Loomio.  And the decision rule here is rough
> consensus, not unanimity.
>
> I put you down as a 'no', Ned, but I think your reasons are invalid.
> I hope you change your mind.  We can continue to brainstorm and build
> consensus with the Vilfredo process even while we add content to the
> home page with our decision process.  The two processes are not
> mutually exclusive, as you imply.  They can run in parallel without
> blocking each other.  Both are useful and valid and neither can mount
> an "end-run" around the other.
