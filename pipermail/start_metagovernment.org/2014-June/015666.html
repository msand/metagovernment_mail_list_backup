Hi Mike,

I think both "decision without deliberation" and "deliberation without
decision" are real problems.  The former plunges us into wars, crisis and
messes of all sorts, the latter is fiddling while Rome burns.  If your
point is that decision without deliberation is worse, I'll accept it.

An enormous topic you mention in your comment is that of authority.  The
"lifeless machine" and its decision cycle have authority over us.  You
claim that the system will take its cues from the public, which I'd like to
believe.  But not all systems are democratic, and not all democratic
systems are honest.  We don't have to look beyond newspaper headlines to
see examples of authorities enforcing their will at the point of a gun over
the public.  I think our chances of success are higher working in
cooperation with existing authority systems rather than seeking to dominate
them.

Something about our conversation reminded me of this old (2000) article
from David Brin imagining online tools for deliberation and decision.
Still insightful 14 years later: http://www.davidbrin.com/disputation.html

I need to run but will chime in with additional thoughts as they
percolate...

Mark
