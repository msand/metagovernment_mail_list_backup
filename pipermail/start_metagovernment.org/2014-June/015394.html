Hi Bentley,
if you are a simple participant (not the person who started the question)
Vilfredo will contact you in the following situations (I hope I remember
well :-) ):

1) someone started a question and have invited you. People can only invite
you if you have worked in a room together before, so if you just joined
this will not happen.

2) you are participating in a question and the question moved from one
phase to the other (hasn't happened yet). That is, from writing to voting.
>From voting to writing, or from voting to consensus found.

3) you just moved to the writing phase from a voting and you are a "key
person". In which case Vilfredo will specifically ask you to rewrite a
specific proposal. (more about it as soon as it happens)

There are other situations where Vilfredo will comunicate to you, but
mostly through the website. I have not implemented yet all the messages
Vilfredo should tell the users because I am scared to make them feel
Vilfredo might be spamming them (I might have coded a couple extra, like
when you write a proposal and people ask a question about it).

As we are moving from Vilfredo to Vilfredo 2.0 which is changing the core
algorithm inside, we are also not putting any effort in designing the
communication protocol better, as the situations where Vilfredo will need
to communicate with the participants will change.

So for now,  just wait. As soon as Ned moves the question on, you should
receive an email.

Cheers,
