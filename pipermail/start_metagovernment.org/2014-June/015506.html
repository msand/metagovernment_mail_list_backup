I am unsure how the rules allow change in rules and am not very interested
in the details.

But I do have an interest in wondering about the mailing list for this
site. That interest might be satisfied as a byproduct of the following rule:

After we have gone through all the approval steps we might wish on a
proposal we then do an email vote for the whole mailing list on the
surviving proposal.

Unless there is cheating the quorum must be okay.

Plurality wins the last yes/no.

Notice the new question of who is an elector is raised here.

But for me I am curious how many real addresses there are and if there
would be any unexpected participation.  And lastly I have a suspicion that
multiple voting schemes yielding the same result gives an improved result.
This of course assumes a no decision is liveable and the value of  decision
is not fully located in the decision but also in the process.

I am here just saying.
