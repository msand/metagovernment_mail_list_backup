Dear All,
The Vilfredo question has now ended. It has not "formally" ended because
the system recognises only consensus as a possible outcome. But basically a
whole "max time" (2 days) has passed without anyone feeling the need to add
anything. So whatever we have reached, this should be seen as representing
the community.
You can see the whole history of what happened here:
http://vilfredo.org/vhq.php?q=164 also if you are not logged in.
And what we have now are 4 alternative, mutually exclusive proposals:

In order of appearance they are:

------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Proposal 1:

Countless public decisions are made each year by communities, cities,
nations, and many other kinds of organizations. Those decisions affect the
quality of our lives, the health of our biosphere, and the welfare of
future generations.

Metagovernment is the community of people who are working:

   - to make those decisions be of the highest quality.
   - to give everyone the option of being directly involved in making those
   decisions.
   - to use technology to work on issues together when it is convenient for
   us.
   - to explore new ways of organizing society and working together.

We discuss and work on ideas and technologies for new governance systems
that will achieve these goals.

Join our community <http://metagovernment.org/wiki/Participate> and start
to claim control of the important decisions affecting your future.
------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Proposal 2

Better Lives Through Better Decisions
Globally, at least tens of millions of public-matter decisions are made
each year. Those decisions affect the quality of our lives, the health of
our biosphere, and the welfare of future generations.

The mission that brings the Metagovernment group together is to find/create
better tools and systems through which to make those decisions.


   - We wish the decisions to be of the highest quality.
   - We wish to have the option of being directly involved in making them.
   - We wish to use technology to work on issues together when it is
   convenient for us.
   - We wish to explore new ways of organizing society.


Visions
Metagovernment is uniquely neutral ground. Most other groups, tool makers,
projects, companies and organizations are more-or-less locked into
particular visions. We are not: we enjoy an abundance of visions. There are
almost as many different visions represented in the group as there are
individuals in the group. (That often makes for very lively discussions
...)

Issues
In addition to directly discussing software, individuals of the group also
frequently discuss fundamental "metagovernment" issues that are ultimately
deeply relevant to software specification and design. A few such issues
have been settled (at least for now). Numerous other such issues have not
yet been settled.

Settled Issues
Here is a short list of settled issues:

*Empirical Approach*

We share a common understanding that empirical questions need empirical
answers.

For example, the question "Does majority rule produce 'better' outcomes
than super-majority rule?" is an empirical question. As such, the question
cannot be answered simply through discussion, nor can it be answered by
polling the relative popularity of the two concepts prior to actually
measuring and comparing the results produced by using the two rules.

We frequently experiment with new ways to make group-matter decisions.

Designs and discussions are empirically driven rather than (for example)
ideologically driven.


*Radical Transparency*

We are all agreed that all aspects of public-matter decision making should
be as open as possible. In particular, all software should be free and open
source, and all systems data should be freely available online. [*Are we
all agreed on that? To my knowledge, it has never been proposed ...* ]


Unsettled Issues
There are many more unsettled issues than there are settled issues. This
list is just a sampling:


   - Power Over Others: Concentrated or Distributed?
   - What Metrics Measure Power Distribution?
   - Democracy: Direct or Representative?
   - What Metrics Measure Degree of Democracy?
   - Decision Matters: Personal, Private, Public?
   - What criteria distinguish among decision matters?
   - Decision Methods: Count Votes or Resolve Objections?
   - What Metrics Measure Public-Matter Decision Quality?
   - Harnessing Creativity: Prizes or Profits?
   - What Metrics Measure Harnessed Creativity?
   - Strategies for Change: Work Within or Render Obsolete?
   - What Metrics Measure Change-Strategy Success?
   - Jurisdictions: Nested or Global Only?
   - Property Ownership: Private or Public?
   - Workforce Organization: Partnerships or Employers/Employees?
   - Organization of Work: Vested Hierarchies or Contracted Projects?


Finding/creating full solutions requires being open to considering all
potentially relevant factors and perspectives. We try to live that here.

We welcome you to join us!
The menu along the left side of this page contains links to a variety of
site resources, including a guide to participation.

I think proposal 3 can be seen as the "none of the above Michael asked for
(is it?). And in which case I would suggest to go forward with a voting
among those 4 proposals.

Now, it is important to vote in  a way that similar proposals are not
disadvantaged.
So I would suggest that we vote using Majority Voting. This means each of
us will rate each proposal with a value:
terrible, bad, ok, good, great.

And then we assign to each proposal the mediane of the votes. And we
compare those medianes. The good thing about this way of voting is that it
is quite strong against strategic voting.

What do you think?

(Michael, can we consider proposal 3 as the "none of the above" proposal?
Is that ok for you?)
