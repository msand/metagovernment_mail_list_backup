On Jun 2, 2014, at 4:25 AM, Ed Pastore <epastore at metagovernment.org> wrote:
> I think Alexander’s point was not specifically to get answers to those questions, but to point out that Loomio has downsides as well. 

Seems like you’re making a bit of an assumption here Ed. I’d like to hear from Alex what his point was….'specifically'.

> I seek not to lock us into one platform

Except maybe e-mail and the wiki?  I’m merely suggesting that we try out each available platform for a given period of time. Move around a bit….test, suggest, collaborate. Perhaps you missed that in my other e-mails.

> (which is not even a participant in our group),

Does it really matter whether *they* are a participant in *our* group. Their group is open to everyone….as is ours. We are all in this together. Lot’s of room for cross pollination I would say.

> but to experiment, try out different platforms, explore flexibility.

Exactly!

> Loomio has a great-looking interface, but it is rigid and inflexible in some of its underpinnings. 

I believe they would wholeheartedly agree with you. They are just getting started….you of all people should know these things take time. If there is any inflexibility on this issue, I would say it has found a very comfortable home on this list.    ;-)

> Other platforms have strengths that it does not.

And vice versa. Let’s try it out and help them to make *their* platform better.  Really though, at some point we must examine the glaring irony of a group that's all about designing systems for global democracy….but is mostly unwilling to consider collaborating with *another* group.  Aren’t we all in this together?  It seems to me that the people on *our* [MG] list would have a lot to offer *those* people developing Loomio.   The resistance to doing so is highly suspect….in my mind.

> For this group to know when a proposal is being presented, we need a broadcast technology (i.e., this email list), not a destination where people must check periodically. My primary objective is to give us some uniformity so that the casual observer can tell when a new proposal is presented, and when people are replying to it.

I get an email every time a new discussion thread begins in any Loomio group I am a part of….or when a proposal has been made….or when a proposal is about to close.  You would too if you signed up.  ;-)

> The wiki is not perfect, but we have to sacrifice some perfection for flexibility.

Indeed (on the wiki not being perfect).  In my mind, being flexible would look like being open to trying something new…..maybe even helping *them* out a bit as well.

I don’t get the resistance on this list to using Loomio for a while (or Airesis, or Vilfredo, or Blinap, or whatever). Is it an ego thing (“my system is different, better, or more uniquely special”), or a proprietary thing (“I can’t make any money helping someone else with their platform”)? 

The planet is on it’s way to becoming a piece of toast. E democracy is perhaps the only hope for creating the kind of culture that can turn that around. E democracy is all about developing means of collaboration….coming up with solutions *together*. And we’re still talking about us and them….”ours" and “theirs”.   I suppose one might find this humorous, if it wasn’t so fucking perplexing.
