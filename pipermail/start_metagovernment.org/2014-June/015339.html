Steve, I think Alexander’s point was not specifically to get answers to those questions, but to point out that Loomio has downsides as well. 

I seek not to lock us into one platform (which is not even a participant in our group), but to experiment, try out different platforms, explore flexibility. Loomio has a great-looking interface, but it is rigid and inflexible in some of its underpinnings. Other platforms have strengths that it does not.

For this group to know when a proposal is being presented, we need a broadcast technology (i.e., this email list), not a destination where people must check periodically. My primary objective is to give us some uniformity so that the casual observer can tell when a new proposal is presented, and when people are replying to it.

The wiki is not perfect, but we have to sacrifice some perfection for flexibility. I have added some additional instructions to the wiki article.
http://metagovernment.org/wiki/Proposals
It really isn’t hard. And it’s a wiki… the beautiful thing about wikis is that you don’t have to do a good job. Just do anything, and others can improve it. In fact, if you want to add a proposal and can’t figure out how to add it to the wiki, just follow the format “PROPOSAL 2: topic of proposal” and ask someone else to add it to the wiki.
