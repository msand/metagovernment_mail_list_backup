Pietro Speroni di Fenizio said:
> Hi Michael, in those years we have tried several strategies to try to
> promote Vilfredo. And for most of it it was quite transparent and possible
> to even write and vote without being registered (Vilfredo would then record
> it as an Anonymous vote). Now we closed that off, as there were several
> problems and it was quite common that people would write a long answer just
> to see it deleted because they forgot to login. So we wanted an easy way to
> make sure that would not happen. At least while we wrote Vilfredo2.0
> 
> But regardless of this, there is a deeper lesson we got. We do NOT want
> random people coming and voting. Vilfredo is not a poll system. It is a
> recurrent process where the same people go through a process to find a
> consensus between them. Which is not directly representative of what
> happens outside. While it is very rare but possible that a person might
> just stumble upon a question and participate to the end, it is much more
> common that someone that no one knows who is, votes in a random way, and
> changes the Pareto Front slowing down the process for everybody.
> 
> So the next Vilfredo will not be organised in rooms, but in a more
> traditional way. And it will be possible to set up questions where only
> some people are allowed to vote or to propose, or to do both.
> 
> But we are thinking of ways to make an automatic animation out of a
> question. So everybody can get up to speed on what has happened. And this
> could also be used to promote it.
