On Jun 8, 2014, at 11:21 AM, Scott Raney <scott at metacard.com> wrote:

> I voted against the "proposal" but couldn't find any way to actually
> edit it.  Neither Airesis nor Loomio is ready (or maybe even designed)
> to do what some of you seem to be expecting them to do, which is to
> support collaboration on *generating* proposals.  This is what wikis
> are for, albeit IMHO they are solving this problem in a way that is
> simply inappropriate for political projects (i.e., any project where
> what is being debated are peoples *opinions* on things, not merely
> facts, which is the reason Wikipedia works but most other wikis,
> including the metagovernment site,  don't).

The way Votorola works (or at least the way it has been used here) is in conjunction with a wiki. People use wiki articles to create proposals, then those can be compared to each other, sections can easily be copied from one to another, and votes can hop between competing proposals as they develop. It is exactly built for proposal generation and consensus-building, and it works now and it does not rely on any authority (no single person moderates the proposal). Vilfredo works completely differently, has no wiki, and yet also is extremely adept at proposal generation combined with consensus building. It too runs right now and requires no authority. SettleIt looks like it too, working in yet another completely different way, is built for consensus-building and proposal generation. Loomio is so primitive in comparison, that I don't know why it is even being discussed here.
