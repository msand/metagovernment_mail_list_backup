> Hi Ed,
> I also am loving how the list has recently come back to life.
>
> And we should remember that those discussion software (mailing lists,
> discussion groups, I am not sure about wiki), tend to self manage respect
> to the size. When too many people are using a channel it makes it easier
> for everybody to hear, and as a result some people walk out.
>
> This could probably be a good reason why we could move to a discussion
> group. Where at least when some people want to discuss something, they can
> open another space.
>
> I can for sure mirror my explanations and the questions I receive about
> Vilfredo in the wiki.
> Vilfredo does not have a good tutorial in English (we have it in Italian),
> so
> In fact I think I should even go back in the list, and recover my past
> explanations from this month.
>
> *Speaking about Vilfredo Timing,*
>
> I have to admit I am really impressed how Ned is managing the question.
>
> My philosophy of 1 week per phase is absolutely unable to keep so many
> people participating. I usually had to send several emails to some people
> asking them to participate. What I have observed is that people tend to
> participate *en mass* on the first day. There are some, but very very
> few, that participate in the next days. On the other side, when you wait
> for them, you pay a huge price from a social point of view: people tend to
> forget. And as a result the participation drops.
>
> It is basically better (I found) to have a faster turnaround, with some
> people that miss a turn and regret it, then to wait for everybody and bore
> the people who are actually not missing a turn, and participating actively.
>
> And part of the problem is also that Vilfredo is trying to really plug
> each other brain together into a superbrain. And this simply cannot be done
> if some people are really eager to participate, and want to do it fast,
> while others prefer or can only do a very slow speed participation.  Either
> you warm up some or cool down the others. It's like playing chess online.
> You need to find a speed that fits all.
>
> If some people are on vacation, then it is probably not the right time to
> ask a Vilfredo question. Or we must have a much better mobile version.
>
> *I wonder if we could design a system where it is not the person who
> started the question that moves it on. But the community itself.* So it
> could cool down at times, and speed up at others.
>
> For example we could start by letting people say how they feel respect to
> moving on.
> For example you could chose between:
> [ ] wait for me
> [ ] do not wait for me
> [ ] I voted but I can wait
> [ ] I voted but I want to see how others vote and maybe change my vote
> [ ] ok you can move on
> [ ] wait for ...
> [ ] MOVE ON. It has been ages now!
>
> This would not move on the question automatically. But it would give some
> good information to Ned or whoever is asking the question. And to move from
> this to an AI that moves the question on should not be that hard :-).
>
> Each person could start a question by signing up to it and say if they are
> aiming to participate at every turn (wait for me), or they will just be
> participate if they can (do not wait for me). And maybe give their favorite
> speed. Then the system could take the mediane of the speed, and use that as
> a first guideline.
>
> I'll add it to the list of things that should be considered on Trello.
>
> Pietro
