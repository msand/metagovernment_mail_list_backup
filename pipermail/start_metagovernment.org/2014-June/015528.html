The earlier projects allowed for resubmission of ranks until the official close of voting, and the planned systems will as well. I presume that's what you mean by continuous. 

Nothing is up at sygnol.com (or its planned sister sites) just yet. I'm early in the development process, building a body of explanatory content for potential partners.  I'm putting minimal energy into maintaining the older sites, other than converting the datasets and using what's there for the occasional demo and video project.

The big gamble for Sygnol is to attract an audience. If just a small but measurable percentage of voters check it out prior to an election, candidates are likely to be attentive to it... especially during a fractured multicandidate election in a relatively small state where 100 vote margins could be make or break for a candidate's viability going into the next primary or caucus.  I haven't said much yet about my approach for convening useful candidate/voter discussions, but I'm certainly not ignorant of the natural fears and antagonisms in that space  For now I'll cite Politics 101... Politics is managed competition.
