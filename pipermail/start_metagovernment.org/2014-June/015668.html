Hi Mark, thanks for elaborating.

I don't know whether we have a real dispute here, or just a difference
in our diagramming techniques.  I draw diagrams with deliberation and
decision processes, too.  I draw them as independent processes with no
direct interconnection.  I think that's how they really are.

> I think both "decision without deliberation" and "deliberation
> without decision" are real problems. ...

To solve that problem on paper, I insert a non-binding "guideway".
The guideway indirectly and loosely interconnects the two process:
(see figure PGD)  http://zelea.com/w/Stuff:Votorola/a/M1

> ... I think our chances of success are higher working in cooperation
> with existing authority systems rather than seeking to dominate
> them.  ...

I agree on condition that *both* processes (public deliberation and
authoritative decision) remain independent of each other, such that
neither can dominate the other.

Maybe it's just the way your diagram is drawn, e.g. from the decision
perspective, that makes public deliberation seem a harnessed process
no longer independently running according to its own priorities.
http://democracylab.org/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/ProcessDiag_Behavior_0.png

But even if you and I have a dispute, I don't think it's reflected in
the larger societies we live in.  The decision authorities in those
societies (modern democracies) already have a deep respect for the
independence of public deliberation and opinion forming.  And public
opinion, for its part, has no power over those authorities aside from
the respect they already freely grant it.  Maybe we can agree that
this arrangement of mutual independence and respect should not change.

Mike
