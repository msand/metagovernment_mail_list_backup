Thanks Alex, these suggestions are very helpful.

> Why is all this what you are write (the steps/phases in the given
> order) necessary? It looks like a lot of complexity.

Yes, it's definitely complex.  But it's built up from several layers.
Just now I wrote up the base layer as a separate draft, keeping your
critique in mind.  http://metagovernment.org/wiki/Overplan/d/SoP

   CoP    (CoP) Compare the overplans
    |
   SoP    (SoP) Select the best overplan
    |
   ToP    (ToP) Track discussion of the overplan
    |
   XoP    (XoP) Execute the overplan
    .
    .

This is a kind of overplan too (an abstract one), but much simpler.
So let's re-apply your critique *here* first of all:

> What particular problems do they solved?  How are these problems
> currently, without that "new process" solved?  Why is the above
> approach better ...?

I agree that's important to explain.  Probably we should explain it
right at the top of each overplan.  So here's the top of this one:

   This abstract overplan tells how to do selective overplanning.  An
   overplan is a comprehensive plan for the future of humanity.
   Today, most of us know little or nothing about overplans.  We're
   almost completely in the dark about the possibilities for the
   future, and therefore unable to make much of a contribution.  Even
   if you already know of an overplan or two, it would make no sense
   to execute a particular one unless you were reasonably confident of
   executing the best one.  The following steps are designed to make
   sure of that.

> ... who executes...? is it one person? all the people?  how exactly?

I think all overplans must be for the reader to execute.  There'll
usually be steps that require cooperation from others, but the reader
should be able to secure that by following the steps of the overplan.
Rather than say this outright, however, I'm trying (a little harder
now) to make it obvious from the wording of the overplan.

So what do you think of this base layer, as far as it goes?  Is it
good enough, or does it still need improving?

Mike
