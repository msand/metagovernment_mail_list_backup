Hi, Scott. I won't answer you inline because you are repeating yourself: I have already answered all of your questions and you are ignoring my answers.

I have answered you as to how a government can be replaced: by making the old one irrelevant. I also invited you to make this group irrelevant. But you cannot assert a minority decision-rule on a community that has never used that and which does not generally accept that.

Deciding what's on our home page is NOT a minor decision: it is a defining moment of this community. Michael has brought us to a point where we must examine who we are and how we present ourselves to the world. Coming up with a fast-but-wrong answer to this question would be the worst possible outcome. Just like most minority decisions. They get the job done... but they do it wrong. What value is that?

If instead we accept your premise that the decision-rule can be changed in the same moment that it is proposed, then remember that I proposed a one-vote-rule which makes me absolute dictator of this group: whatever I say goes. So if your claim is valid, then so is mine and I thus revert us back to our original decision rule.

And thus again, I demonstrate the failure of fast-but-wrong decisions. I would much rather that we take our time and come up with a great homepage than that we put up some junk that doesn't represent the community.
