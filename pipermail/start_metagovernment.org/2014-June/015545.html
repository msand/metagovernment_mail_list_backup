Well, speaking for myself, I'll keep using Vilfredo for as long as
it's marginally useful for its purpose.  I wouldn't want to switch
unless I found something better.  Currently I'm doubtful there's
anything better.  But Vilfredo still needs work before the rest of the
world is likely to see its potential.  By using it whevenever it's
useful, I help to keep Pietro and Derek on their toes, and therefore
help (in a small way) to make something that really works.

The stack approach doesn't work well in my case, and doesn't feel
right.  My competitors (the engineers who design the tools on the
stack) generally aren't helping me by critiquing *my* designs, so why
should I help *them* by critiquing theirs?
