Driving ideas...  I've been involved in attempts to drive the idea of
something or other into someone or other in this field for 7 years
with little to show for it.  We made progress as a small team.  We
spawned several good inventions in tight discussions by Skype or
email.  But we never made much progress communicating with rival
teams, or with a general audience, or uncommitted individuals (as in
lists), despite huge efforts.  Often the idea we wanted to communicate
was nothing but, "Hey, let's cooperate on something (anything) at a
minimal level, and mature this field a little." *

So... One thing to like about this approach of comparing overplans (or
whatever we call them) is it seems to be unstoppable.  We don't really
need to convince anyone of the approach up front, or even explain it
(though probably we should try).  Instead it'll be enough to start
doing it.  Others will get sucked in willee nillee, because there's
just no reasonable alternative.  Or am I wrong?  Is there a reasonable
alternative to the steps below?

   CoP    Compare the overplans (or whatever we call them)
    |
   SoP    Select the best overplan
    |
   ToP    Track discussion of the overplan
    |
   XoP    Execute the overplan


Another thing to like about this approach is that you can never get
seriously stuck or lost.  You just keep following the steps of your
chosen overplan.  At worst, you'll have to cycle back to earlier steps
(like SoP) more often than you hoped.  But eventually you'll succeed,
guaranteed.  Or again, am I wrong?
