> catalog them as Spam.
>
> Vilfredo explains step by step what you are supposed to do.
>
> Generally in Vilfredo we have a writing phase and a voting phase, followed
> by another writing phase, and another voting phase... and so on. (In the
> next version of Vilfredo we might have found a way to have only one phase,
> with great happiness of some of the older members who have asked it about 4
> years ago. Sorry I needed to clarify the math). The person who started it
> decides how long each phase lasts (also this is non democratic, I know).
> Ned has chosen that each phase will take a minimum of 24 hours and a
> maximum of 48 hours. This means that he will not be allowed to move on the
> question before the first day. But after 2 days the question will move on
> automatically if he still has not done it (and if there are at least 2
> proposals/2 voters).
>
> Even if you think you know how Vilfredo works, you should pay attention as
> there are many changes that have been made (and even more we are planning
> and testing and designing right now). Differences respect to previous
> versions: now you can say if you do not understand a proposal. Now if a
> proposal cannot be deleted because of you (and only of you) you will get
> alerted, and a request to consider changing your mind. And we have a nice
> video explaining how graph work, and what is represented there.
>
> I am very happy people are using Vilfredo again.
