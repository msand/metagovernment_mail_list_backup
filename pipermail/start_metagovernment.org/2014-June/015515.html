I don't think this is a group decision, Jacopo; it's an individual
decision.  Anyone who wishes to use Airesis is immediately free to,
and anyone who doesn't can't be forced.  A group decision (one way or
the other) won't change that.

Likewise each person is free to use Vilfredo, or not.  There was no
group decision to use it.  Some started using it, while others didn't.
It's an experimental tool with a unique design for consensus making,
which makes it interesting.  I think that's why people are trying it.

Unless I'm wrong (please correct me), Airesis isn't so much a tool as
a place to live.  It's a group-hosting platform with built-in tools.
Most of us aren't looking for that because we already have the best
mailing list software (arguably better than your forums, though some
may disagree), the best wiki software (almost certainly better than
your document storage), plus whatever other tools we choose to add to
the mix in the future.  For a tech-savvy group like us, we already
have pretty much the best living space that's available.  I think that
explains why we're less interested in Airesis.

(To be clear, I'm not voting in this proposal.)
