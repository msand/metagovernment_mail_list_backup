I'm starting some work in the wiki, Ed, which might turn into a large
investment.  Could we arrange to publish backups of the content?  I'm
looking for something similar to what I publish for the pollwiki:
http://zelea.com/system/host/obsidian/var/cache/pollwiki/backup-partial/

I create those files by running this custom script:
http://zelea.com/system/host/obsidian/usr/local/bin/vox-backup-wiki

The specific files I need are 'wiki-dump-full.xml' and
'wiki-images.tar.gz'.  The relevant backup commands are in the
following sections of the script (at the bottom):

   * Lock the wiki
   * MediaWiki content
   * Filebase
   * Unlock the wiki

Sometime in the next few weeks, would it be possible to arrange this?

the resulting files being posted somewhere on the wiki's web server,
or the Tux server.  I imagine they'd be less than 100 megabytes.

Then anyone could download and secure the backup.  It's enough (with
those two files) to recreate all the content and revision history,
e.g. to a separate wiki.
