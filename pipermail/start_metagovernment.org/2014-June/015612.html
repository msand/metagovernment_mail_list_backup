On Jun 21, 2014 11:51 AM, "Ned Conner" <npconner at earthlink.net> wrote:

> Unless someone asks for delay before the 24-hour timer times out, I am
> going to roll us over to the vote phase of Generation 5 as soon as it does.
> We now have 10 proposals to look at (including 5 new ones), and I am very
> curious to see what's in the new ones.
>
> I will then leave the vote-window open for the full two days, hoping that
> even the very busy will have a chance to vote.
>
> ------------------------------------------------------------------------
>
> In hindsight, when Pietro suggested that we try to reduce the size of the
> Pareto Front, I see that he put me into a "be more selective" mindset. That
> then led me to think this:
>
>    If you are going to be proposing a new version of an existing
>    proposal in the next generation, it would be appropriate to vote
>    against the existing version (since you no longer prefer it,
>    compared to your new version). If that removes the existing version
>    from the Pareto Front, so much the better, since the old version
>    will not then show up in the next generation to compete against your
>    new version. (I am trusting Pietro to correct me if I am wrong,
>    again ...)
>
> Pietro then did correct me:
>
>    Maybe Ned.
>
>    Vilfredo works by asking everybody to accept everything as long as
>    it is slightly acceptable. It will never reach a consensus if people
>    all only accept their best and only. Basically the idea is: if
>    everybody is as open as they can be (but not more) then a consensus
>    can be found.
>
>    Notice in this how the comments are becoming more precise. Before we
>    would vote down something for big things. Now a wrong word can be
>    enough to strike down a proposal.
>
>    I wonder how Vilfredo would vote if we require people to vote up at
>    least n proposals. With n that is 1 at the first generation. 2 at
>    the second generation, ...
>
>    If you strike down a proposal because you want to rewrite it, you
>    are being very selective. Also in theory people should use the
>    proposals in the Pareto Front as guidelines. So everybody knows what
>    are the ingredients, and the Pareto Front represents the Community.
>    So personally I would try to get the proposal I want to rewrite in
>    the Pareto Front, and then rewrite it. If not someone could just
>    vote down my proposal because it is a rewrite of something we
>    already decided as a community to vote against.
>
>    I think we should accept that minor correction, like small grammar
>    correction can be done after a consensus is found.
>
>    In fact, as I explained here:
>    http://blog.pietrosperoni.it/2013/04/29/one-document-
> multiple-point-of-views/
>    it would be interesting to integrate Vilfredo with a wiki. So you
>    use the normal wiki procedures in general. And Vilfredo when a flame
>    war raises.
>
> I am beginning to appreciate that in Vilfredo, a vote does not mean what
> it means in an ordinary count-votes system. (Steve in an earlier email
> mentioned that he saw only 1 proposal in the final Pareto Front of
> Generation 4, when in fact there are 5. I suspect that he was noticing that
> 2712 had more votes than the others, and took that to mean that it was
> therefore the single winner.)
