Hi Alexander,
thanks for your vote.

Signup via Facebook is available (I think - unless facebook changes in the
API broke it), and we are surely interested to add google+ and twitter.

Regarding the way Vilfredo handles anonymous users, indeed past decisions
are already available for anonymous users. If you go to the history of a
question page, and you are not logged in, you can read what is going on.

For example the actual question 's history is available at:

http://vilfredo.org/vhq.php?q=164

with the graphs and everything. When we are voting, the graph of the actual
voting that is going on is not, and cannot be available. This is part of a
design decision to make sure that at least the first vote that you do, you
do it only basing yourself on your own conscience. You can then change it
as much as you want, but at least you know what you are giving up, in
exchange for what.

What we can do to make it easier for external users, is that if someone is
not logged in, and tries to open a question he is not redirected to the
login page, but to the history page, where they can see what is going on.

Cheers,
Pietro
