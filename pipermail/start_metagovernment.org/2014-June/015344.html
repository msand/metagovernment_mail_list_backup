Email and Wiki do not have a "Lock In" effect, because the wiki is open and
the data can be used elsewhere.
The Email List ist archived and it is a standard format, so no problem here.

The Pirate Party in Germany has a system called: "Sync Forum", which
synchronizes their mailinglists with a forum.
People can either use the forum or the mailinglist and the content is
mirrored to the other medium automatically.

What we might need is a plattform that has an IMPORT feature, which accepts
"discussions" or "profiles" or whatever other data items we might have.
Once that is done, everything cna be discussed in that new tool instead of
the old. Ideally, its still SYNCHRONIZED with the old tool, so that those
people, which do not transition to the new tool are NOT EXCLUDED from the
discussion.

The "AG Meinungsfindungstool" of the german pirate party is working on some
kind of ontology to make that happen, but i do not know whether their
approach is the right one.
http://disco.codeplex.com/

I know that those things take time. And i've seen hundreds or more
approaches of tools that try to somehow solve all kinds or problems when it
comes to groups or communities and their problems that should be solved
through discussion and some form of democracy...

Whenever there is a new tool, if it would adhere to certain "standards" or
bring up such standards in regards to:
1. protocols (technically)
2. API's (to import/export all data + remote control their tool)
3. data formats
4. open source client, which allows hosting of their software on "my own
hardware" but connection to their existing network

... then it would be relatively easy, to create a tool, that complies with
those "standards" and is thus compatible with it.

THINK: http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/IBM_Personal_Computer
