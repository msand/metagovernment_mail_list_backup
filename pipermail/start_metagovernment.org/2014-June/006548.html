> new format, I called it PROPOSAL 1. If you agree with this simple, helpful
> format, please re-introduce any active proposals using this format so that

> Hi Ed,
> Below are a couple of screenshots of [MG] on Loomio. The format is *so*
> much more user friendly and well organized than the wiki. Every time I go
> to the wiki I get lost and spend way more time than I want to looking
> around for pertinent info. Case in point: I followed the link you suggested
> to the proposal page you created. I read it (backwards and forwards)
> clicked on your proposal…and nothing happened. Where is your proposal? I
> clicked on “add yours here”…and nothing happened. The wiki has always been
> very frustrating for me. I think it’s a bit shortsighted to expect that
> newcomers (or most anyone for that matter) will sift and sort through all
> of the various pages, and links to other pages, etc.  Maybe I’m a complete
> dolt, but I have to say the wiki really doesn’t work that well...for me.
>
> Whereas Loomio is a walk in the park. It’s simple, straight forward, easy
> to use….and keeps track of all basic info for a group on the groups home
> page. I don’t understand the resistance to using it. Sure it doesn’t have
> all of the bell and whistles that everyone would like….but it’s being
> developed fairly quickly. The Loomio people are very open to feedback and
> suggestions...and I imagine a developer could create anyone of the bits and
> pieces people on this list have suggested are missing….(but then again, I’m
> not a developer so I can’t say for sure.)

> Anyway, I’m really trying to figure out what is actually going to work
