I guess I'm more of a bottom-up kind of a guy:  Other than a desire
for "good government" I don't any of us are in a position to specify
what it should even look like or how to achieve it at the level of an
"overplan".  Although I do give a basic structural outline of how the
government itself should be organized in my book (i.e., mostly that
the executive branch are all *employees* of the government rather than
elected officials) and some key points about the structure of The
System (i.e., that the secret ballot is not only not necessary, but
actually undesirable), I'd rather do without an "overplan" in favor of
designing a working decision-making system first and then seeing where
that leads us.
