Max said:
> Different people, different overplans.  A reasonable way to proceed
> into the future is to $X it into existence.  Is that not always how
> it works underneath the axioms?

Yes, roughly speaking.  The future is the product of a top-level
program that we run.  I agree.

An "overplan" is a top-level program, then.  I guess "over-program"
(or "over-macro") would be more correct.  But "overplan" sounds
better.  Maybe it's good enough.

> This $X in some places is a convention for a macro that gets
> instantiated.  I ran across this in the form $DEITY and liked it.

Then someone ran $modernity and $deity became a meaningless lifestyle
choice, no longer a viable overplan at all.  But $deity was just one
variation on $immortality.  I'll open a separate thread on a different
variation called "eliminating existential risk".  That one runs in the
context of modernity.  I claim there's no sane alternative to running
it, and we'll see if anyone can refute that claim.
