On Jun 8, 2014, at 11:44 AM, Ed Pastore <epastore at metagovernment.org> wrote:
>  Loomio is so primitive in comparison, that I don't know why it is even being discussed here.

One metric you’re leaving out is *usability*. I don’t know much about Vilfredo or SettleIt because I’ve never had the opportunity to use them, but Votorola was beyond my ability to apprehend a couple of years ago when I was working with Michael to introduce it to #Occupy. (Maybe it’s better now…..hopefully so.)  I say this with all due respect to Michael as think a lot of him and I really enjoyed working with him during that period of time.

In spite of, or maybe because of, it’s “primitive” simplicity, Loomio is actually being used by a large number of people.  But I will say it again, let’s use Votorola, and  Vilfredo, and SettleIt, and Blinap, and Discourse, etc..  Let’s use them to discuss *The Plan*. Or let’s use them to reframe the homepage to better suit the current [MG] community,. (It seems like there is some interest in doing that.) The effort could be worthwhile on a number of different fronts.

>  Have you looked at Vilfredo? It is fantastic at building consensus, and lightyears ahead of loomio in architecture.

Then let’s use it.

> Again, why Loomio? …...it has barriers to participation,

I think those barriers exist only in your own mind. You can set a group up various ways (see below). It can be public or private.  I don’t know why it was set up the way it was. In that moment I imagine it made sense to the person who took the time to set it up for us.  Let’s use it to vote on whether to make it public. 

> and it has no representation in this community.

What would that look like...and why does that even matter? I’m a member of the *Loomio Community* and I’m a member of [MG]. I would like for this group, (at some point in time) to give it a go. That’s why it’s on the table. Is there some *rule of representation* that would prevent us from testing it out for a bit? And then maybe offering them some constructive feedback?

> It should be the last choice for any decision, not some sort of gold standard, which I continue to fail to comprehend.


I think you’re being a bit defensive. No one has suggested that it be held up as "some sort of gold standard”.  Certainly not me.  (Though I do like the UI ).  ;-)

Your resistance to using Loomio is loud and clear. What’s not so clear to me is why you want to continue using our current platform. I mean, what could be more "primitive" than e-mail?
