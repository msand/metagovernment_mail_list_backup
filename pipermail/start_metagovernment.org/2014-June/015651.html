Hello everybody. So Ned have moved the system to the next generation.

You can see the result of this generation here:
http://vilfredo.org/vg.php?q=164&g=5

This page will open also if you are not logged in.

This time Ned and Michael are key players. And should probably write their
own version of the proposal they did not like trying to use the information
from the comments.

On the other side I want to clarify one thing, we are going near the point
where people are and will start to get tired with the question. We have
made a lot of progress respect to the first generation, and if you look at
the table (on the right side of the page linked) you can see how proposals
slowly changed and slowly got more integrated and more similar.

Vilfredo does not always reach a consensus. Sometimes (in fact most of the
time), it just reaches a very clear, unsolvable, polarity. Or a three poles
polarity (does that have a specific name?).

At that point it makes sense to vote among the options.

Vilfredo 2.0 will basically let people move on as long as there are new
ideas, and new proposals being inserted. But as soon as the threashold of
time is reached and no new proposal has been written, the system will
assume that the community creativity on that topic has exhausted. Now in
that version people will vote on a sliding scale, so Vilfredo will have
enough information to directly extract a complete list of all the
proposals, thus giving a clear winner.

If we do not reach a consensus on the next proposal I will suggest to just
pick whatever are the proposal in the Pareto front and Vote with Majority
Judgment on them.

Majority Judgment works by letting everybody express what they think of
each proposal on 5 adjectives:
Terrible, Weak, so-so, good, Brilliant.
Then it extracts the mediane of the votes for each proposal and uses that
as the result.

Using the mediane instead of the average (like you do in range voting)
makes the system robust against strategic voting (where people do not vote
what they honestly think to manipulate the result).

Also there is a theorem that states that the mediane on a (single peak) 1d
evaluation, is the condorcet winner of the various options. In other words
more people will prefer that value respect to any other.
