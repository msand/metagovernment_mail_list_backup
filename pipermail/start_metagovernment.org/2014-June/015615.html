> Like Metagovernment, DemocracyLab is in the midst of reworking its home
> page, and an interactive version of the diagram will be the central
> storytelling feature.  The basic message is that civic tech applications
> are those that address at least one phase of the problem lifecycle
> described.  Visitors will then self-identify into one of three roles -
> developer, participant or supporter, and click through to a page presenting
> information on civic tech applications tailored to their perspective.  The
> goal is to create a sustainable ecosystem, where collaboration among
> developers is facilitated, civic tech users can find applications
> addressing issues that are important to them, and potential funders can
> easily research and perform due diligence on projects of interest.  As some
> of you may remember, DemocracyLab is a 501(c)(3) nonprofit organization
> based in the U.S. (Seattle, more precisely).  Like many of you, this isn't
> my full-time job, so progress is slower than I would like, but I'll keep
> the group informed as we move forward.
> 
> Concerning my critique of this discussion and process, I'm in the same boat
> as many in that I'm not able to effectively keep up with all facets of the
> discussion.  I was initially a bit taken aback by blanking the homepage,
> but in hindsight it appears to have been a good decision because it has
> spurred the group into more meaningful discussion than at any point in my
> memory.  I suppose what feedback I have is that the "deliberation" about
> what should go on the home page, and the "decision" to approve it might be
> thought of as distinct steps.  Collaborative writing tools might be
> effective for deliberation, while an app designed to make it easy to decide
> among well-drafted discrete choices could work for decision. I think a
> small number of group members are going to be able to participate fully in
> the writing and deliberation, but that a much larger group would be willing
> to participate in a decision process that was well defined and relatively
> easy.  Having more group members participate in a final decision will add
> legitimacy to the outcome and cause more members feel invested in the
> organization going forward.
>
