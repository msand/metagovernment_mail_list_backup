Mark said:
> I think deliberation is ongoing, but that when it's set against a
> time-based decision cycle it will take on a character rather like
> breathing.  Increasing in inputs and interest in a lead-up to a
> decision, and exhaling actions (or inactions) afterwards, then
> learning from the results, then reframing, leading up to the next
> deliberation, and moment of decision. ...  To me, deliberation
> without decision is best done at the pub.

You employ strong images here, Mark, but I disagree with your message.
The problem we face is not deliberation without decision, but decision
without deliberation.  Society is ruled by a lifeless machine whose
decisions make no sense.  You propose to hook living, breathing people
up to that machine as though it were a benefit to them, like an
artificial respirator.

I feel certain that the public will be strong enough to deliberate the
issues independently, taking no cue from the decision system or any
other authority.  The system will instead take *its cues* from the
public.  When public deliberations result in a consensus, then the
decision system will obey that consensus and decide accordingly.

Society's enforcement of decisions (laws and executive orders) is
legitimate only so long as freedom of speech is respected.  It follows
that the decision machine must keep its scaly tentacles out of our
free deliberations.  It must not attempt to rule them in any way.

Mike
