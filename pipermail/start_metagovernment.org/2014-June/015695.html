Here I think I can explain to folks why these four steps are needed.
That's really all I've been hoping to do in *this* thread, lately.

Here's my explanation: given that an overplan is "a comprehensive plan
for the future of humanity", there is no reasonable way to proceed
into that future other than by following these steps:

   CoP    (CoP) Compare the overplans
    |
   SoP    (SoP) Select the best overplan
    |
   ToP    (ToP) Track discussion of the overplan
    |
   XoP    (XoP) Execute the overplan
    .                               
    .

So, for any rational person who's stuck and feels confused about to do
in order to help humanity along (which I think means nearly everyone),
then it's because he's *not* following these steps.  (So I claim.)
Because if he *were* following them, then he'd always know *exactly*
what to do: he'd follow them until he got stuck, then he'd cycle back.

If these claims are correct, then we could press the argument more
widely and "force" humanity onto a utopian track.  Reason has that
power over people *in general* (at least in modern societies, where
free speech is respected) that it cannot be resisted.  So there's
endless possibilities, and lots of depth, in just these four steps.
