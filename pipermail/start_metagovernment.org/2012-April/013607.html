Wow, the bots are getting past the captcha and the e-mail challenge!
I heard they can solve captchas by re-showing them to people at high
traffic sites under their own control.  I guess the spam pulls in the
traffic and the traffic solves the captchas.

I'm surprised they answer the e-mail challenge.  I imagine that's
difficult without exposing themselves.  Possible remedies:

  * A subscription-based blacklisting extension, preferably one that
    includes a blacklist of email addresses.

  * A contextual captcha that asks a question about Metagov, one that
    people seeking Viagra, penis enlargement or free pornography can't
    easily answer.

What a nuisance, though!  If the breakthroughs are only occaisional,
maybe it's better to hold off.

Ed said:

> $wgGroupPermissions['autoconfirmed']['skipcaptcha'] = true; //different from default

I was wrong about this.  Autoconfirmation has nothing to do with email
confirmation.  The bots are autoconfirmed (thumbs up) immediately
after account creation.  So I have this in my config:

  $wgGroupPermissions['user'         ]['skipcaptcha'] = true;
  $wgGroupPermissions['autoconfirmed']['skipcaptcha'] = false; # that would be everyone, new users are currently autoconfirmed immediately

Autoconfirmation can be delayed, I think.  But that might not affect
the bots much, as apparently they can solve the captcha.

I noticed that.  The account creation message links to the welcome
message in the user's talk page, which is therefore automatically
created.  There's an option to hide the creation from the change log:
http://www.mediawiki.org/wiki/Extension_talk:NewUserMessage#Bot_in_Recent_Changes

But still, it seems an overkill to create all those pages.  What about
putting the welcome message directly in the account creation message?
