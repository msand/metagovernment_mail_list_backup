Some of them expose themselves right in the spam. Most of these are SEO-spam, where they are trying to build links to sites in order to get higher search engine ranking (a practice which often has the opposite effect, since the search engine managers understand this practice and try to work against it... yet people still do it anyway).

For example this page:
http://metagovernment.org/w/index.php5?title=Special:Undelete&target=User%3ABookm12345&timestamp=20111215110727
(link only available to sysop wiki users, sorry)
links to this website link-building-serviceMUNGED.info/ (munged, since I certainly don't want to link to them.

That is a link builder! They are the people actually running this particular batch of spams. What to do? They have a contact page, with their phone numbers. But what can I do?

All I have thought of so far is changing the terms of use to the site, but I haven't figured it out entirely. Something that makes contributors liable to pay (who?) US$100,000 if they post links to sites which are not directly relevant to the realm of democracy, governance, and related software development? But even if I do that, the only way to enforce it would be to hire a lawyer and go after them. Do you think it's worth it?
