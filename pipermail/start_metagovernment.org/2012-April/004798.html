There is an AP article going around about how Al Gore spoke at the SWSX conference.
http://www.politico.com/news/stories/0312/73912.html

A notable highlight:
To fix what he called a no-longer functional U.S. government, Gore urged the audience to begin a new “Occupy Democracy” movement. He pushed for the creation and implementation of digital tools and social media to “change the democratic conversation.”
Gore talked of a “Wiki-democracy” of “digital flash mobs calling out the truth” and “a government square that holds people accountable.”

However, there is what claims to be a full transcript of Gore's only appearance there, and it contains no such statements:
http://www.kernelmag.com/features/report/1575/al-gore-vs-sean-parker-the-full-transcript/

I was just wondering what others' take is on it. Regardless, it is interesting to note that there is some increased awareness of the potential of the internet to really fundamentally transform democracy.
