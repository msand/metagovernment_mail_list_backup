Hi Max,

I did not and do not hold the view that you "hijacked the thread". I do 
hold the view that you asked me a question that belonged on its own 
different thread. So, I created a new thread for that question.

The examples of your short-term goals that you provide (improve mailing 
format, provide ambitious mediathread, update spreadsheets) suggest that 
you are a "code first, think about things later" kind of guy. I used to 
be that way, but got tired of throwing away code. Now I much prefer to 
think things through, and try to reach agreement-in-principle with as 
many others as possible, before plunging into coding.

So, my short-term goal in the "Corporate Governance Structure" thread is 
for us (the participants) to reach agreement (actually settle the 
matter, so that we can then build on the settlement) concerning whether 
to vest decision-making power in the global group, or in national 
corporate entities. (This is one way to frame the issue; there are also 
other ways to frame it.)

That short-term goal does not involve coding; it involves reaching 
agreement-in-principle, through discourse (with or without occasional 
group hugs).

In this thread, I guess you want me to pick and present one or more 
short-term goals that involve coding or some such. If so, then my 
response is that I don't think that we (the community) are far enough 
along yet in our thinking things through and agreeing in principle to be 
ready to start substantive development projects that would serve the 
purposes that I value. (Over on the Isonomea wiki, there are lots of 
development projects sitting on the runway. So, on this list, my work is 
-- through discussions -- to eventually make those development projects 
of interest.)
