We can create formal rules of governance for the global organization, but creating a formal company is something I'd like to avoid. Some of the reasons are detailed on that page linked below. Any sort of formal company would be organized under a host country, and would thus make us subject to that country's laws: a sort of antithesis of what were driving toward.

That's why I came up with the US company (and ones in other countries) as a servant of the global org to get around some of those issues. The companies are subservient to the global org, which itself is not tied to any country.

I have tried looking at global organizations Red Cross/Crescent, Amnesty International, Greenpeace) to see how they resolve it, but for the most part they still have to pick a country. The closest I have seen to a truly global organization is Amnesty International, which has a global secretariat. But that still is based in the UK. (I also just noticed Friends of the Earth has a similar structure, but their secretariat is based in the Netherlands.) It has to be based somewhere because it employs people. In my suggested structure, our global org would have no income and no employees, so no nation could claim that we owe them anything.
