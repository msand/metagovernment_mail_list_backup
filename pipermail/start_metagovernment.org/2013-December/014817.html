> Hi Ed, Paul, Stephen, Max, Pete, Alex,

> This is how global decisions were being made in 1974:
> http://www.ingoldwetrust.ch/minutes-of-kissinger-meeting-on-gold-1974
>
> It is still how global decisions are being made now: "informally", in
> secrecy, by a handful of mostly old white guys that are well-placed and
> well-connected and utterly ruthless in their commitment to narrow zero-sum
> self interest. Currently, the Trans-Pacific Partnership is being negotiated
> and decided in just this way.
>
> I for one am interested in humanity having a better system for making
> global decisions.
>
> Creating a more formal organization for the Metagovernment global group is
> indeed much more complex than continuing with our informal no-decisions
> "business as usual" at the global scale. And we (members of the community)
> are indeed not all on the same page at this point regarding decision rules
> and such.
>
> Therefore, ... we should set up yet another network of national corporate
> entities (corresponding to the existing network of nations) that will make
> global decisions basically the same way that such decisions are being made
> now? I suspect that potential donors would probably not find that approach
> very inspiring: "Where's the beef?"
>
> If we wish a global decision system that reliably makes good-for-all
> decisions, and in which there is no distinction between governors and
> governed, we are going to need to create that decision system ourselves,
> and be the first users of it, ourselves. I do not see any way around that,
> however complex and difficult the undertaking may be.
>
