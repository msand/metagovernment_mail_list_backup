Daniel, to those only have a hammer, everything looks like a nail.  So I
certainly don't want to convey the impression that I believe StratML is the
answer to everything.  

On the other hand, based upon the first definition of the term "policy" at
http://www.bing.com/Dictionary/search?q=define+policy&FORM=DTPDIA&qpvt=defin
ition+policy, StratML does seem to me to be a fairly good structure for
expressing it:  "course of action: a program of actions adopted by a person,
group, or government, or the set of principles on which they are based"

Indeed, according to that source, "strategy" and "plan" are synonyms for
"policy".

However, rather than theorize about it or simply accept someone else's
perspective, how about: 

a) using Joe Carmel's XForms form --
http://xmldatasets.net/XF2/stratmlxform3.xml -- to see how ITIF's policy
statements "fit" the schema for StratML Part 1, and to the degree that new
or different elements may be required,

b) working with an ANSI-accredited standards development organization like
AIIM to specify a standard for "policy" statements that is tightly
integrated with the kinds of (strategic and performance) "plans" required to
translate policy into effective action?  (In my view, we have far too much
"policy" and far too little effective action.  In other words, many policy
statements are under-specified and thus ineffective, as if we naturally
prefer to talk about problems rather than solve them.)

Note: AIIM's StratML Committee is now working on StratML Part 2, Performance
Plans and Reports: http://xml.gov/stratml/index.htm#Part2  It is already
complicated enough that I fear trying to address still more complexity right
now might unduly raise the risk of failure.  However, if and, hopefully,
when we get to Part 3, the concept of "authorities" (e.g., "policies") is on
our radar: http://xml.gov/stratml/index.htm#Part3 

The degree to which the value of the StratML standard is recognized and
acted upon by others, like ITIF, will have significant bearing on whether
further effort on the StratML standard will be worthwhile (and might be
accelerated with additional participation and expertise).

Owen

Kevin, seeing on LinkedIn that you joined the ITIF group prompted me to
convert ITIF's mission statement to StratML format for inclusion in our
collection at http://xml.gov/stratml/drybridge/index.htm#ITIF

Robert, I encourage ITIF to consider the potential benefits of widespread
usage of the StratML standard (ANSI/AIIM 21:2009), whose purposes are
outlined at http://xml.gov/stratml/index.htm#DefinitionPurposes

I have converted to StratML format all of the agency open gov plans of which
I am aware, at http://xml.gov/stratml/drybridge/index.htm#OpenGovDirective

I am also promoting the notion that political parties and candidates for
elective office should publish their platforms on the Web in StratML format.

In support of your policy proposals
goal<http://xml.gov/stratml/carmel/ITIFwStyle.xml#_b2385d12-64ec-11df-8661-e
e507a64ea2a>, it would be good if ITIF could:


a)      participate in promoting that notion,

b)      publish some "model" objectives for potential incorporation into
such "plans" and

c)       report to your stakeholders which candidates have and have not
included them in their campaign platforms.
