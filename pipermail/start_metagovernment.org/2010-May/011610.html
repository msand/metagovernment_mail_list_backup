There is no shortage of opportunities.  For example, the term "community"
occurs 46 times in NASA's open gov plan.  Relevant objectives in their plan
include these (and many others):

Ideation -
http://xml.gov/stratml/carmel/NASAOpenwStyle.xml#_467d8cda-61fb-11df-9cc1-d2
6b7a64ea2a 
Open Source Software Process -
http://xml.gov/stratml/carmel/NASAOpenwStyle.xml#_467c35ba-61fb-11df-9cc1-d2
6b7a64ea2a  

With reference to GSA's plan, Metagov could establish a goal to persuade
them, in the spirit of "openness," to enable the use of other tools besides
Ideascale to support online "conversation".
http://xml.gov/stratml/carmel/GSAOpenwStyle.xml#_a5123bb8-5fd7-11df-b2a2-a95
97a64ea2a 

>From my perspective, it would be great if Votorola and other Metagov tools
and services could enable folks to provide feedback on any and all of the
goals and objectives set forth in all of the U.S. federal agency open gov
plans -- http://xml.gov/stratml/drybridge/index.htm#OpenGovDirective -- as
well as the plans of any other organizations, worldwide, rendered in StratML
format.

Note, the E-mail addresses for most of the agency open gov contacts are
provided as mailto links to submit comments at
http://xml.gov/stratml/drybridge/index.htm#OpenGovDirective
