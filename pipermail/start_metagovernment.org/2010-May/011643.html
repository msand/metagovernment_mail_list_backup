Matías Battocchia wrote:
> One active project of Metagovernment, Votorola, is a functional
> application for liquid democracy. It may provide PdI much of what
> you are willing to develop. Talk to Michael and Thomas here, they
> are the developers.

Also Adhocracy (talk to Friedrich).  But private voting (as David
requires) is not the main method of Votorola (nor Adhocracy).  We
don't think we can build an unforced consensus that is based (as it
must be) on widespread participation *and* rational discourse, if the
voters are hiding behind veils of anonymity.  That would take you back
to the dynamic mass voting, mass media and mass manipulation (status
quo).  So it's undesirable in principle.  (And we foresee other
problems that would make it infeasible in practice.)  Public voting
OTOH has none of these drawbacks.

Still, Thomas and I have discussed the possibility of adding a proxy
blind to the voter registry.  It would provide a degree of personal
privacy for those who cannot/will not participate in public.  We'll
probably have to add something like that, at some point, as a kind of
safety valve (I mean because there are sometimes legitimate reasons to
vote privately).  Let us know if you need to see the details.
http://metagovernment.org/wiki/Streetwiki#Proxy_blind
