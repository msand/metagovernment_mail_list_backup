Im sure you have valid points, but my concern, at this point, is not the
merits of public voting vs private voting per se, but rather the perception
that the general public and subject matter experts have of them.

In other words, if the general public expects private voting, and if the
general public is skeptical or regards a public voting system as
insecure/suspect/unreliable, then we have no option in our quest for
credibility as an organization to create a secure system. Furthermore, I
have consulted some experts on the matter, and they let me know that they
would not hesitate to condemn a non-secure voting system outright as
unreliable/fringe/non-serious/suspect, and derail our entire project from
their position of authority as opinion makers.

Hence, as I said before, I am convinced that for us to be a credible party,
we must cover this requirement that the public associates (correctly or not)
with a "serious" and reliable initiative.

Ofcourse, from an engineering standpoint, a public voting system is much
much easier. In fact, as I have said before, creating a practical secure
system would be something of an achievement, as right now these systems are
largely just prototypes for academic research.

Our system would be open to anyone (eligible in "normal" elections), not
just party members or people who voted for PdI in "normal" elections. People
are surprised when they hear this, but our objective is to create a channel
for _everyone_, and that is what a vote for PdI stands for, even if it does
transfer some of your voting power to others.
