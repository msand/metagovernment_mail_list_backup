In the early, prototyping stage, alphabetical listing may be the best that
can be done.  However, if we aim for large-scale usage, a query-by-example
(QBE) interface will eventually be needed to enable users to discover
communities of interest to them based upon their own attributes and
interests, expressed in their own terms (rather than any that we may try to
pre-determine for them).

Edit wars and bad feelings.  I may misunderstand, but if you allow
editable content on a page like that, it'll be the source of endless
disputes.  Who has the right to edit it?  What kind of organization
does it impose on the areas?  These are political questions, but the
facilities of the central pollwiki (outside of areas) ought to be
neutral with regard to political questions.  Do you agree?

> ... Then it could become much nicer than just in alphabetical order

> chaotic. ...

  i) When it becomes a problem, how will people solve it?

 ii) When there is no central pollwiki, but rather a universe of
     independent pollwikis, how will people solve it then?

It would be ideal if (i) and (ii) were the same solution, then we'd
feel like we were on the right track.  In any case, no harm could come
from a neutral, alphabetic listing.  We could at least start with that
(and revisit later), if you agree.
