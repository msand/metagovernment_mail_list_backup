> 1.  Your chosen system of 'Direct Democracy' seems very similar to a form
> of governance I've become interested in called Sociocracy (
> http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sociocracy).  [...] Have
> you considered Sociocracy?
>

I think that at least some members have read about this form of government
in the past. What should be taken into account first is that it was
conceived before Internet; I would not use it "as is" but get some
inspiration from it instead. Circles are like interrelated communities. When
the thing gets territorial a community cannot ignore their neighbors, for
instance; intercommunity-communication should then exist. Here you will not
find much attraction to the concept of 'representative', so the
communication may take place as collaborative-written letters exchange or
just inhabitants of one community also discussing and voting in the other.

It is worthwhile reading Sociocracy Wikipedia article.


> 2.  I can find no reference to a proposed economic model.

An economic model is far beyond our scope for the moment. Please take a look
to
http://metagovernment.org/wiki/Related_projects#About_money.
Also to this book (
http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Down_and_Out_in_the_Magic_Kingdom) from Cory
Doctorow.

My epiphany came while reading George Orwell's 'Animal Farm' (
http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Animal_farm): corruption will make any the
economical model fail. Corruption can be overcome with transparency.
Fortunately, as computers can register with ease lots amounts of information
and explore those registers, decisions taken 'online' will be transparent.
On a second thought, Metagovernment *is* doing something to improve economy.
