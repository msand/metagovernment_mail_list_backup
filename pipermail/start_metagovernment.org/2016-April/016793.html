> I was trying to apply the mechanism know from you to Neighbourhoods's
> Messengers' Network and I came to an interesting paradox.
> Let us assume the decision (labelled as global) of the neighbourhood was
> "neurotipical" and that it was some standardised neighbourhood. With an
> average number and quality of SDO, RWA, etc...
>
> Now, should I expect all neighbourhoods to be standardised and simple say
> that they will probably accept it as well (except they express the
> opposite)?
>

This would be an example of "sampling", a design that Ned's Blinap relies
on. Given your premise, it actually should work fine in practice *but* it
requires a lot of faith in statistics that most people currently don't
have. Widespread adoption would therefore be very difficult to achieve.

What about local conditions? Climate? Available technology?
>

In practice I'd guess that it'd be very unlikely that two "neighborhoods"
would have the same psychological makeup, even if most measurable factors
were similar.  I therefore would think it unlikely that one neighborhood
could serve as a proxy for another. Fortunately for my proposal this isn't
necessary because it's actually a lot easier to measure each individual and
provide proxies at that level than it would be to try to find a way to
combine them (into tribes, neighborhoods, whatever) and work the
voting/proxies at that level.
