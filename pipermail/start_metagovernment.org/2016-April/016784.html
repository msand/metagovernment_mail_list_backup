> Delegating their vote to psychologically matched proxies.

That is to assume that their psychological matched “proxies” are genuine – which in reality is not as most political campaign strategists will manipulate  along the lines of what a specific niche want to hear – psychologically. If you are going to “anchor” to a proxy, I believe you need to do that on something more than just a “feeling” of  right or wrong, as that, as history proved over and over again, is prone to manipulation.

The "proxies" will be matched the same way that eHarmony and match.com work: You answer a bunch of questions and get matched with a selection of other people who would likely vote the same way as you do, but for whatever reason have more time to research the issues or who have had some specialized education or work experience in a particular field. The only way to manipulate people under this scenario is if you simply lie on the questionaires. But there's a built-in fix for this: With open voting it'll be trivial to detect someone like this, either before you pick your proxies, or perhaps even systematically (people's profiles might get an asterisk if their voting record does not match their profile).

 

And, manipulation is not a quality of a limited group of “authoritarians” is part of our own nature, so even if you are going to dilute that top influence you are still going to get an unwanted bias from with the system. In my opinion, whatever system you implement you will need something to validated /score how genuine that vote is.

But we've got to work with the raw material we've got. Another key tenet of matchism is that morality and progress in public policy is a progressive thing: It is vastly more difficult to change people than to change systems, so we change the systems now, and the morality of the people will follow along some decades later.

 

>They therefore don't have to become experts on a subject

But unfortunately this would take decades to achieve and we may not have that long, so we need to put the corrections in the system design, not require that the people change first.
