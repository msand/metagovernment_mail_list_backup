Agree: This is "moral progressivism" as described in the matchism
manifesto. A more blunt example I use there: A million years ago, prior to
the availability of the resources to create and maintain prisons and mental
health facilities, it was perfectly moral to banish (to certain death) or
even murder problematic individuals in your band. As we learn more about
ourselves and our technology improves our morality must change to
accommodate these things (albeit with a generations-long lag time). This
philosophy can accommodate small differences in morality that result from
discrepancies in knowledge and technology (e.g., between countries,
although probably not between adjacent neighborhoods), but with the advent
of cell phones and the internet the differences at this point IMHO are
vanishingly small.
