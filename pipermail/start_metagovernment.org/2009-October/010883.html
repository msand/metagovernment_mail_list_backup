Someone recently posted StackOverflow under the Good Examples page
http://www.metagovernment.org/wiki/Good_examples

I just had a look, and found their reputation system to be intriguing.  
Read the "What is reputation?" section on:
http://stackoverflow.com/faq

One notable thing is they say: "Reputation is never given, it is  
earned" (which reminds me of what Jim Gilliam says of WhiteHouse2,  
that reputation can't be gained for anything you do, only for what  
others do in relation to you).

Also, they have an interesting reputation-privilege system, where one  
can't even comment on posts until accumulating 50 points, and where  
one is granted administrator-like control at the highest reputation  
levels.
