Sounds like a good objective to me, Matías.

>From my perspective, it would be good to use AIIM's emerging StratML standard.  http://xml.gov/stratml/index.htm  

I have already documented the missions, visions, values, goals, objectives, and stakeholders of several of the Metagov projects in StratML format, at http://xml.gov/stratml/index.htm#Other 

If anyone would like to add to the collection or edit any of the descriptions (plans) already provided, he or she could use the PDF Fillable or the Xopus form at http://xml.gov/stratml/index.htm#Forms

Of course, too, it would be great if any of the Metagov applications could be used to help the group "govern" its own activities (by committing resources and achieving results) in pursuit of its own objectives (to the degree that consensus, much less commitment may actually exist on any of them).

BTW, this very same issue (lack of commitment and thus resources) has plagued the U.S. federal CIO Council's ET.gov process.  See http://et.gov/stage2.htm & http://xml.gov/draft/ETLifeCycleStage2.htm 

See also Project Management XML Schema (PMXML):  http://xml.coverpages.org/projectManageSchema.html
