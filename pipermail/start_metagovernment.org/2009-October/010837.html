The Huffington Post is certainly on the cutting edge of social media --
ahead of Mashable, who claims to be THE news source for social media.

I'm curious to know what the interest of such tools would be to this list?

My inability to participate for the last several weeks has been due to my
new position as a social media experience planner in Ohio. I honestly had no
idea what the job entailed when I applied, but it has been a wonderful ride
through the world of social media. It is strange to make a living designing
user experience for the likes of Twitter and Facebook.

While the workload has been heavy, I have been searching for ways to bring
my new skills back into the Metagovernment fold.

My assumptions are that social media is of little interest to the developers
who are working on their own solutions for internet governance. These
assumptions were drawn from comments I have read in respect to the security
concerns on Facebook and other social networks. However, social networks are
where the people are gathering. Shouldn't governance exist alongside the
people?
