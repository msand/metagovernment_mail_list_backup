Governance is broader than Democracy. If I had to choose between them, I'd
keep the first. Note that adopting Democracy limits the software voting
feature in the following way: it should always stick to "one man, one vote".
While the tendency of scoring is to create a very balanced kind of
Meritocracy.
Collaborative Governance sounds fine to me. It reflexs exactly the spirit of
our philosophy and is easy to understand for everybody.

I can make changes in the wiki next week, so there are at least 7 days to
tell me "I think you shouldn't replace Open Source Governance by
Collaborative Governance."
