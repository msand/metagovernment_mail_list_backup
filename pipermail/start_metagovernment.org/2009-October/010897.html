Owen Ambur wrote:
> I have already documented the missions, visions, values, goals,
> objectives, and stakeholders of several of the Metagov projects in
> StratML format, at http://xml.gov/stratml/index.htm#Other

The OpenKollab crew was also thinking along these lines.  They figured
that self-expressive info was needed for inter-project collaboration.
But there's another point of view (almost opposite), that depends on a
different kind of info:

  a) Visibility of projects.  Show which ones actually exist.  Show
     all of them, together.

  b) Facts about projects.  Show not just the self-expressed hopes,
     aims and interests of the projects, but verifiable facts.

  c) Actual collaborations.  The past can be a guide to the future, so
     show past and current collaborations.

So in contrast to a *subjective* StratML, we may need an *objective*
CollabML.  Here's one take on it, in the form of a semantic wiki:

  http://openkollab.referata.com/wiki/E-democracy

Please fill in a fact or two (anybody who's interested), or add a
project.  It's just an experiment, but it might lead to something.
