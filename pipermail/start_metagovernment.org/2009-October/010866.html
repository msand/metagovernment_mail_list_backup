Not sure what's all about Facebook for e-democracy here... maybe we have
different thoughts.
I agree that FB is a very nice platform with very well done features.
I would also agree with the statement that it could be a good place for
e-democracy movements *IF* started by Civil Society *BUT NOT* if
promoted by Governments as a way to take seriously into account
citizen's input (at maximum it could be used for institutional
propaganda, and communication of results).

But if governments start using FB to create their e-participation tools,
well then we are far away from real e-democracy. The only and simple
fact that FB is "closed-source" and governments can't be sure of how FB
treats their and citizen's data is alone a non-go. Let alone talking
about EU governments, as FB is a US-based company.

The EUGAGER proposal we submitted was thought to be supported by
governments as an alternative to facebook (it does not exclude
integrations for non-secure apps), but it would provide citizen's and
govs with "secure" apps that can really be trusted (not only in terms of
identification, but also data integrity and checking). And of course be
fully-auditable open-source.  (soon news about that... :-)

Maybe you guys were suggesting FB for grassroot apps created by citizens
and for the citizens... I just wanted to be sure that when we talk about
real governments supporting open and transparent governance it does not
mean using facebook to interact 2-way with citizens for serious stuff...

my 2 cents ;-)
giovani
