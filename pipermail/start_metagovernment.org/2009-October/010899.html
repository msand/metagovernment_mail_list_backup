Well,
dear Owen, dear Michael,
the reason why I never joined in in Owen efforts, is because

a) I don't believe in top down efforts in those domains (it is a pragmatic
position, not a political one. I think they don't work).
b) I think semantic web is essentially a subjective view point based on the
subjective perception of the wrld of the people defining the onthology
c) I think the only solution is an intersubjective solution that is based of
getting the results from many many people.

What do I mean? Look at any popular page, as it is represented in delicious.
What tags do those people use.

You want a list of related projects? Just go to:
http://delicious.com/popular/edemocracy
And this is being really fast and dirty. By analysing the whole set of tags
that people use you could do much better.
This weighted list of tags (also known as a tag cloud) will represent the
best description of the project. And this description is devoided of any
structural content, it is cultural dependent, time dependent (the tag cloud
of today is different from the tag cloud of last year). But this very
information is enough to make you aware, given a page (which can point to a
project) of what are the similar projects that are around.
Yes it can be done better. No, it cannot be imposed, nor can it be done in a
top down way. Not unless you want to coerce people into following you, and
are willing to lose a huge amount of data.


This is why I have never responded to any StraML or CollabML message.

Regards,
Pietro
