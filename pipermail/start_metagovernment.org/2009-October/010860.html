The reason that many of us have resisted joining Facebook, Linked In, and
other "social networking" services is that we resent having to re-create our
digital personas and turn them over to every Tom, Dick, and Harry working on
their own "solutions".  The paper I wrote on that topic a decade ago is
available at http://ambur.net/PushShove.html

Fortunately, progress is being made, as evidenced, for example, by the
conference on OpenID conducted at NIST last week.  See
http://xml.gov/documents/completed.asp and, more specifically,
http://events.oasis-open.org/home/forum/2009/schedule 

Hopefully, in the not-too-distant future, it will become socially
unacceptable to continue to try to "capture" and hold customers hostage (by
making the switching cost unduly high) -- rather than attracting customers
by providing superior service (for each logically separable transaction)
without regard to where they choose to maintain their digital personas.
