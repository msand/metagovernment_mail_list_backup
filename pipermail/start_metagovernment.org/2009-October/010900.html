Owen Ambur wrote:
> Of course, too, it would be great if any of the Metagov applications
> could be used to help the group "govern" its own activities (by
> committing resources and achieving results) in pursuit of its own
> objectives (to the degree that consensus, much less commitment may
> actually exist on any of them).

We've been doing this in a small way with Votorola.  When your hands
are involved, you see things that are otherwise hidden.  Usually it's
little things, but sometimes it's bigger.  Just in the last couple of
weeks, for example, we saw precisely (we think) how voting and
drafting will interface with discussion.  We saw the concrete objects
that people will be talking about, and we realized they couldn't be
talking about anything else - not productively.  It's hard to guess,
but obvious in retrospect:

In an ideal democracy, every political discussion is exclusively
focused on a difference of (a) ___________________.  In an actual
democracy, every such difference may be made concrete and visible as a
difference of (b) ___________________.  The concrete form (b) may be
electronically stored and addressed by a standard URL, embeddable in
support of the relevant discussions (a).

So the actual may support the ideal.  What are (a) and (b)?
