On 09/23/2016 09:50 PM, Flash Cards wrote:
>> no clear boundaries of who should be considered a participant
>> with voting rights and who shouldn't. Creating sockpuppets
>> would be trivial. For any meaningful e-dem action to take
>> place you need to have a defined meaningful group that uses
>> the results of the votings in an agreed upon way.
> 
> Would not some simple identity verification system be sufficient
> initially? Like verification via text message (to a phone number) or a
> mailed letter (conventional postal service).

Maybe. Who checks on the SMS service? Who checks on the bureaucracy
of servicing accounts? What if the attacker is a nation state that
can fake phone numbers and postal addresses ad lib? One practical
way to tackle this is to have people become members of an association
or political party, so you collectively define procedures for legal
registration, elect officials to run them and judges to check them.
