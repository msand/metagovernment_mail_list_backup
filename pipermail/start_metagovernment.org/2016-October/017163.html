The second reference is especially useful, because it can tell us something about the predictive power of the model you are proposing. Based on it, I believe, we can estimate answer to the following question. Imagine we have a group of people (similar to one in the paper). We split it in two equal parts, self-reported liberals and conservatives. (You can eliminate some people from the overrepresented subgroup if you need). Now take their big-5 profiles and use them as best as you can to predict their affiliation (lib or con).


Question: based on the paper, what success rate of your prediction you can hope to achieve? (It is anywhere from 50% to 100%; the former is a fully random prediction, the latter is the perfect prediction.)


And a related question: what minimum success rate (in the previous question) you would like to have to consider your method useful? Give a specific number please.
