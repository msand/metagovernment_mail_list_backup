May be beating a dead horse, but I'd still like one of the
consensusists on the list (i.e., Ned) to explain to me how it would
work in a real-world example. You could pick abortion or gun control
or welfare or immigration or minority rights/treatment, but I'd prefer
you show how that most fundamental of government practices, taxation,
would work:

Say I'm a radical libertarian and believe to my very core that
taxation is a form of coercion and is therefore an unacceptable
infringement on my liberty. A proposal comes up that requires taxation
to fund. How does this issue get resolved while adhering to the "no
unresolved objections" requirement:

1) All proposals that require taxation fail.

2) All proposals have an exclusion such that libertarians do not have
to pay taxes.

3) The libertarians are all declared "unreasonable" and their
objection is overruled.

If you pick #3, you also have the onus to describe who decides what is
unreasonable and what procedure they use to do that.

I eagerly await enlightenment.
