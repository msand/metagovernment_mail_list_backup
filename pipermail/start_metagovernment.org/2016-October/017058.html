Restarting the list someplace else is an hour or two kind of project
and so is not a reason for not doing it. I'd still recommend moving it

democracy system, no? ;-)

> On Oct 4, 2016, at 9:44 AM, carlo von lynX <lynx at time.to.get.psyced.org>
> wrote:
>
> So far, sociology has not figured out any other solution than
> moderation. If we also want justice on top of moderation we
> have to elect a court of appeals that watches over the moderator.

IMHO this is just because there hasn't been a proper incentive to come
up with an alternative to moderation: Remember, just about every other
list/forum/etc. has an *incentive* to allow or even facilitate chaos
because more controversy = more eyeballs = more revenue. I've run
mailing lists before and am always baffled by these calls for
moderation or censorship because it seems to me they're massive
overreactions to what are relatively minor incidents. This list, for
example, is fine and I'd recommend the whiners just work on getting
their story straight rather than crying to list mom or playing
passive-aggressive games when their ideas are attacked.

> Welcome to actual Internet democracy. To anyone who may not

>
> I am open to suggestion or to directives from a group consensus, but I agree
> with Pietro that moderation violates the spirit of this group. I will not
> exercise authority over other members of the list, and I do not agree with
> any proposal to give such power to anyone in this group.

Agreed. If the list were a little bigger I'd recommend just signing up
additional managers (google groups, for example, makes this very
easy), but I'm not even sure that'd be addressing the real problem,
which is the hosting service not the list management.
