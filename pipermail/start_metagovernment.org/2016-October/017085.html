Agreed Ed. But our dogfood are eDemocracy platforms. No one among us
suggests that a mailing list could be a workable form of government or no
government was workable at all. So basically the dogfood this mailing list
has would be:
Vilfredo (not good for discussion), Airesis (adopted as it is not coming
from here, but workable as it has discussion board I think), candiwi
(pre-alpha), whitehouse 2 (we never managed to install it with Derek) or
liquid feedback (also not good for discussion). Another option would be to
use github.

I wonder if github could be used as a tool for discussion. Just a wild idea.

SO basically we have no dogfood available except maybe Airesis. But Airesis
is basically only implementing a discussion package, so the philosophy is
the standard philosophy from any discussion board (Jacopo might fill us up
on this).

Maybe making a self moderating discussion that scales to any size board
might be a workable aim for this community. Using everything we have
learned from Vilfredo, liquid feedback, airesis (especially the blind
commenting system), candiwi and whitehouse 2 and github.

In the meantime we have no dogfood that supports our philosophy. And the
unmoderated mailing list is not better than the moderated one. They both
don't work.

> This is basically a question of eating our own dogfood. Not in terms of

>
> I do (hypothetically) moderate already, in that if someone starts sending
> outright commercial spam to the list, I will boot them. But if I take
> action against someone based on much fuzzier judgements of the content and
> motivation of their posts, it is very difficult to determine where to draw
> the line. If I do it based solely only on my own judgement, I am acting as
> an authoritarian, which I will not do. I am more than willing to moderate
> (and/or to enable others to moderate) if we have a ruleset that clearly
