Here are some of my counter-principles to Scott's "Axioms of Government":

 1. (D) A rule of action or choice is justified, and thus valid, only if
    all those affected by the rule or choice could accept it in a
    reasonable discourse. (This is the Habermas Discourse Principle.)
 2. By extension, a population-scale public-matter decision system is
    legitimate to the extent that it produces decisions that are in
    accord with (D).
 3. Anyone can propose anything, on equal footing. (Some must not be
    more equal than others, either with respect to opportunity to
    propose or with respect to treatment of proposals proposed.)
 4. A proposal succeeds at the point that it reaches the status of
    facing no unresolved objections.
 5. Vote-counts do not decide. ("Might makes right" is not a principle
    that I buy into. It ignores/overrides (D).)
 6. Guesses about what will or will not work are just guesses. Show me
    your test results, and I will show you mine.
