> People have the right to self-governance, but not if
> it virtually guarantees political or economic instability (or election
> of Hitler, which IMHO in itself should have been considered an act of
> war by the nations he had already disclosed were in his sights even
> before he became Chancellor (i.e., "Lebensraum")).

You are naive.  ANY government of humans (compared to say, robots), is
guaranteed to have social and/or economic instability.  THAT IS NOT
THE PROBLEM.

The problem is when there is no recourse or means to self-correct
(like allowing others to win an election).  The best any government
(cf anarchy) can accomplish and WILL EVER ACCOMPLISH is fairness and
balance *over time*.  Fortunately, governments have that (or can
create it easily).

> Any government

In some ways you are right.  The failure is that nothing gets done.
You can compare the success of the metagovernment effort, for example
(very little has been accomplished, but it has hosted a good forum.)
