I call BS here too: You can't base a society, or even a decisionmaking
system, on "facts". All these "facts" are subject to interpretation
and bias, most especially in determining which ones are relevant and
which are not. To claim otherwise is just naive.

> Not trying to be mean to anyone. 99% of Internet activists thinks

Again, BS: I'm no expert on this, but the HyperCard mailing list I'm
on has functioned without moderation (not counting a few unsubscribing
of spammers) for almost 30 years now (it started on AOL before there
even was a public internet). Sure there have been a few bruised egos
and some hasty departures, but it endures exactly *because* it lacks
all the drama that those types tend to inflict on any rational
discussion.

useful information in that entire field. Now if you want to talk real
science, there is a bit of work in social psychology that might be
