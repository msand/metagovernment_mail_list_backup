Again, how is that working out for the Syrians? The freedom to leave
is worthless without a guarantee of admittance someplace else. Same
with calling for help: What if no one answers? What if ISIS and Russia
are the only ones who answer?

But all of this is a side issue: The real problem is that you're still
considering moral standards as something that stops at your chosen
border. It's a common mistake, and in fact is a consequence of our
genetically programmed tribalism. But once you get past that (and it
isn't *too* difficult to socially engineer conditioning to ensure that
everyone does), you'll see that the whole concept of "if you don't
like it, leave" is morally and intellectually bankrupt. For example,
given what we now know about its origins and consequences FGM (and to
be even-handed, probably circumcision) is immoral *everywhere*. The
People (of Earth) are just unwilling to try to enforce a ban on it in
central Africa because most people don't consider the residents there
as "people" (in this case it's even harder to justify because of the
racism issue piled on top of the usual tribalism). Again, if someone
is living under a dysfunctional government, it's not just a problem
for them, it's a problem for *all* of us.
