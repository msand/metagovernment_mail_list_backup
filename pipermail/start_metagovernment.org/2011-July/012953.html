      Decision Contexts

To be fully functional, a global trans-community social system needs 
these parts:

    * decision making system(s)
    * charters & contracts (legal instruments)
    * financial system
    * economic system
    * learning systems
    * systems to support research, invention, design
    * systems to support arts & entertainment
    * systems to support health
    * communication system(s)
    * transportation system(s)
    * emergency response system(s)
    * landparcel infrastructure systems
    * manufacturing/production systems

So are these examples of what you mean by "decision contexts"? (If your 
answer is "no," my feelings will not be hurt. I actually like Pietro's 
suggestion better that "what" questions and "how" questions are two 
different decision contexts.)


On 7/13/2011 1:38 AM, Pietro Speroni di Fenizio wrote:
> Hi Ed,
> I am not really sure how to insert this.
>
> One of the things we noticed in Vilfredo is a difference between 
> "what" and "how" questions. Or more in general between questions that 
> are looking for a single answer, and questions where multiple 
> concurring answers can be accepted. For example if we are speaking 
> about, what shall we do about global warming, we have several 
> strategies that needs to be followed in parallel. While if we have 
> decided that we are going to build a solar panel, then we ask: "how" 
> shall we build this solar panel? we are essentially asking a question 
> where we want a single, complex answer.
>
> The division is not strict and there are how question that needs 
> several answers and what questions that have a single answer. And 
> often you can rephrase a how question as a what question, and 
> viceversa. But generally there is a difference.
>
> This is important in Vilfredo because we have found how Vilfredo is a 
> great tool for a limited group of people to tackle a how question. But 
> it does not seem to work so well with what questions or if the number 
> of people is too big.  And it is for this that we are developing the 
> bubble system as part of Vilfredo.
>
> Now I think this division should be expressed in the page, but I am 
> unsure on how to add it, after all here we are speaking about type of 
> decisions, and my discourse is about type of questions.
>
> Cheers,
> Pietro
