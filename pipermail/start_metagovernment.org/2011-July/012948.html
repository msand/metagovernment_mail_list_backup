I would add this:

  4. The need for action.

Ideally the meeting is centered on action.  Not only is it prompted by
a problem of action - we were trying to do A but we ran into problem P
- but it also results in action - we decided on plan B instead, so now
we're doing it.  Otherwise what is the purpose of meetings?  Why
bother with any kind of organizational effort at all if nothing real
ever comes of it?

On the other hand, action itself is often impromptu.  It is often
motivated by a need or opportunity that just happens to arise.  If it
does not arise here - if we ourselves do not feel a need, or see an
opportunity - then probably it has arisen elsewhere.  We ought
therefore to go looking for it.  Looking is itself a valid form of
action.

What do you think?  Could we do something about this?
