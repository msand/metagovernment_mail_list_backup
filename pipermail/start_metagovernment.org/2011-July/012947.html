Ed Pastore wrote:
> I would like to suggest we try to have monthly voice meetings. What do  
> you think?

One alternative to consider is impromptu meetings.  Each meeting would
start immediately as the need for it arose.  More details below.

> Issues I'd like to resolve for future meetings include:
> 
> 1. Being more accessible to a global audience. (Matteo was excluded  
> from that meeting because he resides in Japan, where the start time  
> was local midnight.)

I could easily have an impromptu meeting with Matteo.  I am often in
his time zone, so to speak, because I keep odd hours.

> 2. More reliable hosting. We might want to consider asking if  
> individuals would like to make ad hoc payments to a commercial  
> audioconferencing service. There are ones which work with both Skype  
> and phone, and which are not really very expensive at all.

Impromptu meetings are likely to be smaller, so hardware limitations
would be less of a factor.

> 3. Transcribing the meeting better.

An impromptu meeting will usually be preceded by discussion in some
other medium (list or IRC) that has prompted it.  If it is also
followed by a sentence or two of summary, then that would suffice to
plant the seed of transcription.  Anyone who wanted more information
could simply ask, "Can you elaborate?"

All of this might happen more naturally if meetings were focused on
some kind of action.  I feel that action is important, so I'll start a
separate subthread on the topic.
