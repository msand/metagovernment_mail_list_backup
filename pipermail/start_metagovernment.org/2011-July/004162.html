At the recent Skype meeting, we touched on a topic which I believe  
needs more attention in most governance projects: clearly defining  
different kinds of decisions.

Some of our mechanisms may make sense for one kind of decision, but  
not for another. For example, coordinating the construction of a  
community service such as a homeless shelter may be a matter of just  
getting the material and labor necessary to make it happen. But making  
gambling illegal or legal is an societal ethical decision of a  
completely different context.

Previously I had started this page on the wiki:
http://www.metagovernment.org/wiki/Decision
It is just a basic outline at this time. I encourage others to expand  
it and/or to respond on this thread.
