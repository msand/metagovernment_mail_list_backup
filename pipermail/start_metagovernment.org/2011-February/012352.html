I am not so enthusiastic about all those means that make it easier
immediate reaction and short-term "collaboration", because I am really
not a fan of street movements. Almost every revolution crashes into a
despotic ruling, more despotic even than what you've had before - and
you know why? because well, you have overthrown your king or your
government, but have you prepared your next morning? do you know
perfectly well in how long time there will be free elections, and who
will be managing them, according to which rules etc.? maybe tomorrow
you'll be engaged in street fighting over what is better, Condorcet or
Pareto or...?

Sit at your houses instead, and do some tedious long work, that of
elaborating basic rules (Constitution, and first of all, what kind of
elections, how and when). Do it openly (not only transparently, but
open to any new participant, however numerous, stupid and even
ill-intentioned they might be). If you are not ready with your work
when some revolution happens on the street down your windows - it's
not for you this time, this one, you should just quietly continue the
same job. YOUR revolution will be not for overthrowing the next
monarch or next bolsheviks or Jacobeans (French jacobins), but for
installing something positive that you have elaborated meantime.

Cyril, the leader of a sit-at-home revolution...
