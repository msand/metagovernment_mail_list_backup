Am Friday 11 February 2011 schrieb Thomas von der Elbe:
> Jonas, maybe we should work together.
> 
> It looks to me, as if you are working on an aspect of e-dem, which we
> still have to with Votorola and vice versa.
> 
> You are about delegation which could maybe be called "range delegation",
> where a single delegation covers a whole range of polls:
> 
> "I delegate to one party for healthcare, another party for school, a third
> party for nature preservation, and so on."
> 
> And this range can include at maximum all existing polls (like in the
> current political system) and at minimum one poll.
> 
> Votorola only covers the last case so far: I can delegate my vote in each
> single poll only. I am really keen on this type of delegation, so more
> developement is necessary here.
> 
> On the other side, another really important question, which needs to be
> answered is: Once thousands of these "range-delegates" come together in a
> single poll, how do they find consensus?  A simple list of their
> positions/ideas is not good, because it forces them to vote strategic. It
> suppresses good ideas, and therefore favoures compromises over synthesis.

Yep. I think this is far more than a simple new feature. The problem you 
describe is imo rooted in the question of how do you classify e.g. health-care 
issues? The group you describe might not even know about the polls they are 
automatically assigned to and will not be able to follow all the discussions.

The problem of categorizing might not go undemocratically, e.g. when defining a 
poll, so if issues are summarized under a category, this should imo happen by 
drafting and be part of the content being discussed. This kind of 
metainformation can then be advertised by your delegates who will show you 
what similar issues they vote upon and you can then over time push your 
position not on every political issue, but on those you are interested in or 
in where you trust your delegates. Grouping all polls in a category will 
simply be overwhelming and very unspecific. It is also not good to simply 
delegate (a.k.a. vote) on categories, because as you have put it Thomas, this 
avoids synthesis and creates blind mass action imo. If you have a split 
consensus which differentiates over too many polls than what will happen 
(hopefully) in Votorola's case is, that people will start to talk to each 
other. If they try to outrun a poll with an oppositional consensus, then the 
oppositional poll can simply join the polls of each other to change the 
consensus to their liking which will result in a single poll over time. So you 
will be able to create polls for a bunch of similiar problems and even 
systematical questions and positions and not only for single issues during the 
collective learning process.
