Decisions will be made like breaths will be drawn, an inhalation of
deliberation and an exhalation of action.

I think the more difficult question is how online communities gain the
authority to make decisions.  Right now all of that authority rests in the
real world.

I also think it's important to note that there will be no one right answer
concerning how to make decisions.  Marcos, your solution sounds sensible,
but would not be the right choice for every decision context.

For example, I just took place in a group process that worked under a
consensus model - 90% of the group needed to be at "yes" before the proposal
moved forward, and the commitment was to take as long as necessary to get to
90%, or if that wasn't possible, to walk away.

Another example might be decision environments where consent isn't needed,
only critical mass for action.  For instance, my homeowners association
decides to have a work party on Saturday when 5 people commit to showing up
at 10:00 AM.

There would be many other decision environments that could be imagined.
Perhaps it would be a good idea to take a stab at cataloguing them?  Perhaps
someone's already done this?
