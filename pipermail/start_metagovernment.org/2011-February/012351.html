There is one idea floating around (I forget the author), which is to
use smartphones to run ad hoc mesh networks. Each phone connects to
nearby phones using wifi, and they form a grid amongst the mob on the
street.  Once this network is in place, a layer of encryption and
anonymisation works on top, and then on top of that the applications.
The original suggestion was that each phone would act as a Diaspora
node, but they could equally be running Vilfredo or something else.

As the crowd moves through the streets, it carries its own cloud
network, its own means of sharing ideas, photos and video, and of
making decisions using deliberation software. And of course, it is
more difficult to shut down than the internet proper.

Immediately, it could carry information on where the riot cops, medic
stations and water cannons are, but could be used to discuss and
decide where to go, what to do or what should happen once the riots
are over.
