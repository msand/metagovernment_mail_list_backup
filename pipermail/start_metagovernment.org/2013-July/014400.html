Maybe the most important clothing for a vote is the identity of the
person who casts it.  Votes are public [1] and a personal vote (cast
by a person) is tied to (clothed by) the person himself/herself.  See
for instance the arrows (votes) and filled circles (persons) here:
http://zelea.com/w/Stuff:Votorola/p/assembly_election/multi-winner

A second form of clothing is a position draft, which is a textual
contribution to a position [2].  It's similar to what you call a
proposal.  See for instance the pinkish/magenta draft symbols here:
http://zelea.com/w/Stuff:Votorola/p/legislative_action

I mention these two forms of clothing because they're probably the
most important overall.  But we have others, too.  Maybe the best
index to the docs is the glossary, which tries to define the terms:
http://havoc.zelea.com/w/Stuff:Votorola/glossary


 [1] http://havoc.zelea.com/w/Stuff:Votorola/glossary#vote
     This differs from the votes cast in secret ballot decision
     systems, for example, which are truly naked.
 [2] A position is a vote plus its clothing, roughly speaking.
     http://havoc.zelea.com/w/Stuff:Votorola/glossary#position
