> A "naked vote" responds to a proposal without itself being a
> proposal.  It has no "rational discourse clothes" on. It expresses a
> preference without presenting or promising reasons for that
> preference. It does not submit itself to criticism and systematic
> examination. It cannot effectively be challenged through rational
> discourse, since it is but a raw, naked, inarticulate "grunt" of
> preference.

Okay, that's a good description.  I might not contrast "naked vote"
with "proposal vote".  Proposals are just one possible form of
clothing for a vote; maybe insufficient on some occaisions; maybe
unecessary on others.  Consider instead the category "clothed vote".

But again that's no stick to beat Votorola with, as I think you'll
learn if you continue reading the documentation.

> A proposal submits itself to criticism and systematic examination. A 
> proposal presents good reasons to implement the proposal, or at least 
> promises that good reasons exist and could be requested. Even in the 
> case that the proposal springs from a subjective preference, the 
> subjective preference is publicly revealed, and may through discourse be 
> counter-balanced, or balanced in relation to wider consequences.
> 
> A "naked vote" responds to a proposal without itself being a proposal. 
> It has no "rational discourse clothes" on. It expresses a preference 
> without presenting or promising reasons for that preference. It does not 
> submit itself to criticism and systematic examination. It cannot 
> effectively be challenged through rational discourse, since it is but a 
> raw, naked, inarticulate "grunt" of preference.
> 
> "Communicative rationality" is communication that is "oriented to 
> achieving, sustaining and reviewing consensus -- and indeed a consensus 
> that rests on the intersubjective recognition of criticisable validity 
> claims". A proposal constitutes such communication. A "naked vote" does 
> not constitute such communication.
