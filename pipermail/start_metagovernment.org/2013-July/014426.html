> Long time lurker here.
> 
> I couldn't agree more that the rules - as you call them - of money are broken.
> 
> It's not about rules though in my opinion. Rules are enforced but their is nothing in the way we use money that needs enforcement.
> 
> In my opinion the problems lay with the design of our national currencies. Compared to the designs of the 14th century the designs of our contemporary currencies have not changed at all.
> 
> Some theoretical ways in which the design could change.
> 
> - individual currency units could be taggable so that as a currency unit passes through various hands tags could be added but never removed.
> 
> - the complete history of individual currency units could be made available for consultation at will.
> 
> These are just two suggestions that would radically change our world beyond recognition.
> 
> Throughout the years I've developed a potential method through via which the transition between our current national currencies and a newly designed currency could happen without any government intervention.
