Ned Conner said:
> ... the deciders cannot have knowledge of what the decision ought to
> be at the start. The knowledge of what the decision ought to be only
> comes into existence at the moment that the decision is made. ...

This is your design choice, as I say.  Changing the last phrase to "at
the moment the knowledge is acquired" expresses the general case.
Generally a knowledge of the "ought" may be acquired without forcing
the "is".  This allows for another design choice in which we try to
acquire that knowledge outside of any authoritative decision system.

Regardless of how it's acquired, the acquisition is what I term
primary guidance.  I infer that Blinap/Freetimea is a primary guidance
system (among other things) and I have no problem with that. ;^)

> 
>     *Given* that the knowledge of what the decision ought to be *does*

> case, the question is a "Have you stopped beating your wife?" question:

> assumption/assertion that the knowledge of what the decision ought to be 
> exists prior to the beginning of decider deliberations. In the system 
> that I have been describing, that knowledge *cannot* be known at the 
> start, because it only comes into existence at the end, at the point 
> that the decision is made.
>
