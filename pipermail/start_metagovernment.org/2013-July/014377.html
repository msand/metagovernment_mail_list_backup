Hi Mike,

Ok then: by your definition of "to vote", proposing is formal enough to 
constitute voting. Therefore discourse can be decisive, if it proceeds 
through successive, recursive proposing. (Yes?)

------------------------------------------------------------------------

So do you have any problems with the distinction drawn between "naked 
voting" and "discourse voting"? (Votorola uses naked voting. Blinap uses 
discourse voting.)

------------------------------------------------------------------------

    Ned> My contention is that we would be better off to use only
    discourse voting in our (decisive) public decision systems [and in
    our "guidance systems"], in order to bring to bear upon democratic
    decision making the values of discourse that Habermas talks about.
    Naked voting does not bring those values to bear. 

Shall I take your silence concerning my contention as a vote of 
agreement? :-)
