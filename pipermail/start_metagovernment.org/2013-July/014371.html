It occured to me there are many examples of discursive baselines that
wouldn't be useful (discussions on abortion, and so forth).  So I'm
adding the qualifer "potentially valid" to discussion property (b):

  (b) focused on the issue of an action norm that is potentially valid

      Validity is tested by the discourse principle: "Just those
      action norms are valid to which all possibly affected persons
      could agree as participants in rational discourses." *

Many long running discussions are fueled by the threat of an invalid
norm being forced on its dissenters, and these cannot qualify as
discursive baselines.  Only discussions (or parts of discussions) that
instead focus on mutually agreeable outcomes may qualify.  Anyway,
that's what I figure.

Mike


  * Jürgen Habermas.  1992.  Between facts and norms: contributions to
    a discourse theory of law and democracy.  Translated by William
    Rehg, 1996.  MIT Press, Cambridge, Massachusetts.  p. 107.
