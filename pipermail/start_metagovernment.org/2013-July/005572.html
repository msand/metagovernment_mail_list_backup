Hi Mike,

You have asserted that voting is a form of decision making, and that 
discourse is not a form of decision making, because it cannot be decisive.

Consider the following sequence of events:

   1. A proposer proposes a proposal.
   2. Notification of the proposal is sent to millions of members.
   3. The proposal successfully passes through all existing and
      applicable "proposal criteria filters".
   4. No one proposes any objections to implementing the proposal.
   5. The proposal is implemented.

This sequence of events has been decisive. If, as you say, discourse 
cannot be decisive, then voting (or sortition) must have been involved. 
So, please: show me the votes.
