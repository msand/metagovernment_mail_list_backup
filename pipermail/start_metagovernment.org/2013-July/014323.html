On 7/2/2013 4:55 PM, Ed Pastore wrote:
> Greetings, folks.
>
> I have repeatedly brought up the idea of formalizing this group with a 
> nonprofit organization, and have also repeatedly brought up numerous 
> objections to the idea. After more reflection, I would like your input 
> on a different approach to the same idea.
>
> Basically, I think I have been approaching it backwards. I have been 
> thinking of formalizing this group into a corporate entity. But when I 
> look at the way international organizations such as the Red Cross and 
> Amnesty International are governed, the corporate entities are more 
> like servants of a less well-defined global group. That sounds like a 
> great model for us.
>
> So I am proposing creating an American nonprofit organization. This 
> organization would not be *in control* of the Metagovernment project, 
> but rather would be (loosely) *controlled by* it. This would allow the 
> organization to form within the strictures of the American tax code 
> (which can be burdensome), but still fully maintain the collaborative 
> governance of this group.
>
> The big benefit of an American nonprofit is that it is the most 
> eligible entity for applying for a large and diverse array of grants. 
> Monies donated to the nonprofit might be earmarked by the grantors, 
> but generally I would see funds being directed in three ways:
> 1. To distribute funds to member projects
> 2. To advance Metagov-related projects such as free-range voting
> 3. To publicize and strengthen the project
>
> Performance of those tasks would be left to the nonprofit 
> organization. But the big decisions (i.e., how to distribute funds, 
> which Metagov projects to advance, how to characterize the project 
> when publicizing it, etc.) would be controlled by this group using 
> real collaborative governance.
>
> If I go ahead with this and it works well, we could form more 
> nonprofits in other countries and even more American ones. There's no 
> limit, when we look at it this way.
>
> Thoughts, objections, variants?
>
>
> P.S. I'll point out a semi-personal motivation in this as well. I have 
> very little time to commit to this group because of the demands of my 
> employment and business venture. If I could (eventually) be employed 
> by this nonprofit organization, I would end up with vastly more time 
> and energy to devote to this group.
>
