Hi Ned, (and Ed)

> ... if a proposal constitutes a vote, is it also true that pretty
> much any speech act of rational discourse constitutes a vote of some
> kind?

By definition, it must be formalized.  Speech alone is not enough.

> If we add Ed's (to me) clearly valid suggestion (that the spectators
> of a discourse, if they are free to participate, are voting when
> they do not participate) ...

Far from voting, they do nothing.  That's all we know and all we can
rightly claim.  Any further interpretation of their inaction belongs
to us alone; we cannot rightly attribute it to them.

Mike
