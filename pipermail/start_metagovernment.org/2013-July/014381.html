Ned Conner said:
> Ok then: by your definition of "to vote", proposing is formal enough to 
> constitute voting. Therefore discourse can be decisive, if it proceeds 
> through successive, recursive proposing. (Yes?)

Yes, votes can be decisive.
 
> So do you have any problems with the distinction drawn between
> "naked voting" and "discourse voting"? (Votorola uses naked voting.
> Blinap uses discourse voting.)

I'm afraid neither of these terms is meaningful to me.  Votorola and
Blinap serve different purposes.  I welcome critique, but I think you
need to become more familiar with Votorola's design before comparing
it to others.  It's not designed for decision.  The votes serve
intermediate structural and informational functions that are hard to
summarize.  You'd have to work pretty hard yourself to understand the
design; reading documentation and stuff like that.
