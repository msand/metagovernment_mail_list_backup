Hi Mike,

Attaching the voter's name to a vote does not clothe the vote from the 
perspective of rational discourse. The vote -- with or without the 
voter's name attached -- cannot be challenged through discourse unless 
the reasons for the vote are given, or at least promised. A "position" 
is indeed a fully clothed vote.

All of the votes that are called votes in the Votorola documentation 
appear to me to be naked votes. If all participation occurred through 
proposing positions, then all Votorola votes would be clothed. (I guess 
I should ask directly: Do you agree that Votorola does use naked voting?)

My contention (my proposal, my position) is that we will be better off 
if we entirely abandon the use of naked voting, and instead arrive at 
decisions (including the un-decisions of "guidance") through successive 
and recursive proposing (that is, through discourse).

Throughout history, all sustained attempts to effect democracy through 
the use of naked voting have gone not so well. What leads you to think 
that the use of naked voting in Votorola will buck that trend?
