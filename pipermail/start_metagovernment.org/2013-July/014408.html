On Jul 20, 2013, at 12:20 PM, Ned Conner wrote:

Hi Ned,
Thanks for responding. I appreciate your feedback. 

>   Stephen> *Appraisers* would be elected and trained by the Community
>   Network.

> Here is my counter-proposal.
> 
> Any member can register to be put into the pool of available appraisers. The individual can register as a general appraiser, or can specify particular categories of interest.

OK. They would still need to be trained though....to some degree.
Maybe it would just be 'a requirement for them to read the *Appraisal Training Manual*...with a brief test following'?

> When a new proposal is received, it is appraised (tagged, categorized, judged) by the proposer (as part of the submission process).

Yes, this would perhaps make the appraisers job a little easier and would help to familiarize *proposers* with the appraisal process. 
Also, by "judged" do you mean, 'as to whether a proposal fits the proposal *criteria*'?

> The proposal is then distributed (by a set of individuals pretending to be a software distribution program) to appraisers in the pool, using the proposer's categorization.

Ok....who would those individuals be...and what would they be called...*proposal distributers*? Or would this be done by an initial *organizing committee*....until the software could be developed?

> Upon receiving a new proposal, each appraiser can choose to independently appraise it (or not). 

It seems to me it would be good to have (at least) 2 appraisers working independently on each proposal. If there are discrepancies between them and/or the proposers *submission appraisal*, there would follow an *investigation* as you suggest below.

> All appraisals that are received are then compared. [I]n the beginning, an investigator would be selected from the pool of investigators to do the comparisons manually, and then investigate any discrepancies among the appraisals.

> Any member can register to be put into the pool of available investigators. Again, the individual can register as a general investigator, or can specify particular categories of interest.

> The investigation (if there is one) culminates with the investigator making a proposal of findings and recommendations. (These proposals are primary drivers of the evolution of the decision system.)
> 
> The investigation process is the alternative to having a training process. Everybody is continually learning together, "on the job", as things move along.

Ok..I think I'm liking all of that. We have pools of *appraisers, distributors, and investigators*. Would there be any reason not to combine (any of) these into the same pool?

> Instead of paying salaries and such to vested positions, appraising and investigating are project work, with payment for service rendered.
> 
> How much would be paid would be determined through discourse-through-proposing.

I'm thinking (some) people would volunteer for the positions mentioned above....at least initially. I suppose it would depend (to some degree) on how involved or lengthly a proposal is....or becomes as it is developed further. Which brings up the question as to whether proposals would need to be re-appraised at some point before they were put to a *call for objections*.

Also, what would the requisite disconnect/disclosure be between *appraisers, distributors, and investigators* and the *proposers*?
Ashland is a small town and it's very likely that one could know the other.
