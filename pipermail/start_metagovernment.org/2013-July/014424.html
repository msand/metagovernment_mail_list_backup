The beauty of wiki is that you don't have to do a good job. You just do what you can, and others can come along and improve it.

Here's all you need to do.

1. Log in to the Metagov wiki (to reduce spam, our wiki requires login to create pages).
http://metagovernment.org/w/index.php5?title=Special:UserLogin
On that page, you can create an account if you don't have one.

2. Think of the term you want to write a page about. Try to make it a simple term, not a long thought. For example, the Metagov wiki has a page on "Representative Democracy," not on "The Pros and Cons of Representative Democracy."

3. Do a search for that term. Let's say your term is "widget." So you get this:
http://metagovernment.org/w/index.php5?title=Special%3ASearch&search=widget&go=Go
If you're logged in, near the top you'll see:
    Create the page "Widget" on this wiki!
and "Widget" will be in red. Click it.

4. Type away in the text box. You're creating a new page. Click save. You don't have to be done, just save whenever you want. If you want to edit more, click the edit tab at the top.

5. Edit until you don't feel like it anymore, then tell the list that you've created the page and invite others to improve it.
