Then three is "Money laundering laws" and lots of stuff that prevents those
who dig into the matter to start an alternative system.



> In my opinion the problems lay with the design of our national currencies.
> Compared to the designs of the 14th century the designs of our contemporary
> currencies have not changed at all.
>

I dont want to discuss money, because there cant anything be done about the
money system right now. I've been through years of endless discussions and
lots of inspirational ideas and precise criticism of the issues, but its
not possible to act on it in a way that would change anything. In order to
change the money system, you first would have to fix democracy... ...but
there might be a second way and that is through changing how people use
"equity" :-) ..which also has a lot to do with democracy on a corporate
level, because it decides who gets to vote and who not.



> Some theoretical ways in which the design could change.
>
> - individual currency units could be taggable so that as a currency unit
> passes through various hands tags could be added but never removed.
>

bitcoin has that mechanism, but it doesnt say a thing about how money is
created and how it might vanish... but really, this discussion will lead to
nowhere...


> - the complete history of individual currency units could be made
> available for consultation at will.
>

people dont care. It's already online and if you want to learn about it,
there is an endless stream of videos and texts and the like available.


> These are just two suggestions that would radically change our world
> beyond recognition.
>
> Throughout the years I've developed a potential method through via which
> the transition between our current national currencies and a newly designed
> currency could happen without any government intervention.
>

Many people have, small local currencies exist, but they will never replace
the big currencies. As soon as one might grow to a point where it will look
promising, the law's kick in and its brought down one way or another.
Happened many times in the past.

What do you think about the way we treat equity?
