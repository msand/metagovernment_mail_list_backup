On Sep 3, 2008, at 10:53 AM, Richard Franks wrote:

> I like the phrase "Open Government" too. My only criticism of it (as a
> title) is that it perhaps doesn't differentiate itself enough from
> what we have now. I can imagine the following conversation:
>
> "Have you heard of Open Government? It's a form of Government where
> anyone can participate."
> "Like what we have now? I could run for office tomorrow!"

If we are leaning toward this phraseology, I would prefer "open  
governance" to "open government." The latter, as you say, implies  
openness in existing governments. In fact, that is what  
opengovernment.org seems to be about:
http://opengovernment.org/
While, as noted previously, "governance" more broadly encompasses any  
sort of organizational management.

And also, to be clear, I think the term Metagovernment is still  
central to the project, and may become a sort of brand name in itself.  
Though really, the intention of Metagovernment is to be the government  
of governments: i.e., the regulating body of Metascore. The philosophy  
itself still needs a definite name.

> RMS's idea of working the concept of mass participation into the title
> somehow I think is quite insightful.

Then I'll repeat my suggestions of "participatory governance" and  
"collaborative governance." They do seem to very clearly get across  
what we are getting at. Though I think "open governance" does as well.

Since this is such a diverse international group, can I ask the  
speakers of other languages how these terms feel when translated from  
English? Unfortunately, I don't think we have any Asian-language  
speakers, but I'd be interested to see what you speakers of romance,  
germanic, and slavic languages have to say...
