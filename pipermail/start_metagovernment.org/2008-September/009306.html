The tag study is very interesting. I have two concerns about using it
to form canonical opinion though:
1) It is not a very large sample
2) The very first version of metagovernment.org in the wayback machine
( http://web.archive.org/web/20070519063610/http://www.metagovernment.org/
) does mention "open source" twice.

So we shouldn't assume that people are automatically associating "open
source" with Metagovernment of their own volition. Although I do
imagine it helps geeks "grok" the collaborative nature of the project,
it probably does nothing for non-geeks.

I just asked my half-geek wife - "What do you think of when I say:
open source governance?" - response: "anarchy!" then after some
thought "because that means that _anyone_ could have an input!!?!".
Something then perhaps to avert fear?

Grassroots Governance? - Sounds so unlikely that it almost prompts the
"how the heck would that ever work?" question, at which point examples
could be cited.
