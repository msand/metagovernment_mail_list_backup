On Sep 27, 2008, at 7:17 PM, Scott Lewis wrote:

>> I understand that it's nice now, but what is Google's motivation?  
>> They are owned by their shareholders, and those people are solely  
>> motivated by profit.
>
> This seems a little harsh to me.  It is indeed true that Google is  
> owned by shareholders, and that many of those folks are motivated by  
> profit.  OTOH, it's overstating the case, I believe, to assert that  
> that means that Google's motivation for *everything* they do as a  
> company is profit.  For example, Google allows it's employees to  
> spend 20% of their time working on projects of their choice...for  
> several reasons...but with one reason being that it tends to keep  
> their most talented, smart, and creative people from getting bored  
> with the company and leaving.  And it's far from clear that in the  
> case of Google Moderator (which was done by Google employees in this  
> 20% time), that their motivation is to make money (that wasn't the  
> stated motivation from Taliver as described here
>
> http://googleappengine.blogspot.com/2008/09/introducing-google-moderator-on-app.html

As they say in the world of mutual funds... Past performance is not an  
indicator of future performance. At any time, the board of directors  
can change the course of the company, especially if for any reason  
Google suddenly starts to experience financial hardship. The fact that  
they can change the terms of agreement at will and without notice  
means you are at the mercy of the shareholders, and those people's  
motivation is -- there's no better word for it -- all about greed.

Things are good with Google now, but I see no reason why that means  
they will always be so. People can earn my trust, but a corporation  
does not behave like a person.
