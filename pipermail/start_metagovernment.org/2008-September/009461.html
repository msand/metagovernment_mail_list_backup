In response to Ed.-------------

Ed: "The more involved you get in a community,  the more people there 
will respect you and the more they trust you to  make changes: you can't 
just jump in and immediately change everything."

This is a double edged sword.  If respect, success and trust were 
obtained based on honesty, integrity and intelligence then this would be 
a wonderful world.  Unfortunately most people make decisions based on 
arbitrary inferences.  Let's look to advertising as an example.  A key 
element of advertising is the "affirmation of a lfestyle", basically 
this means you show a particular way of life as being "normal" or 
"positive".  This is designed to make people living that lifestyle 
relate to the characters portrayed in the ad, and feel good about 
themselves based on this positive reinforcement.  Through transference 
the viewers apply and associate these positive feelings with the 
company.  Now apply this to a discussion.  People will let bits and 
pieces of information about their lives, slip through when commenting in 
forums etc.  This can be used to infer the lifestyle of that person.  
i.e. a comment like this;  "my partner and I have decided to stop buying 
dog food from china because we'd never want to hurt our toy terrier 
Princess Henrietta", divulges a great deal of information about the 
commenter.  We can determine that A) they are homosexual based on the 
"partner" reference. B) They probably anthropomorphize their pet based 
on it's name and the fact that it's a smaller animal.  C) The previous 
observations can draw the assumption that the commenter is male as, 
stereotypically (and we all think in stereotypes) homosexual males are 
more likely to have a small anthropomorphized dog than are homosexual 
females.  Now if you were a homosexual male with a small dog, you could 
then potentially start applying assumed attributes to this commenter 
based on your own beliefs/observations about yourself.  You may think 
religious thinking is a boon to society since you are oppressed by it, 
you may then apply this thought to them as well, you may think southern 
states are a bunch of bigots because of other stereotypes and you may 
then transfer these thoughts to the commenter. 

It seems insane that mankind works this way, but almost all evidence 
shows that we do.  The major problem resulting from this is that ideas 
are then judged by the person suggesting them rather than the validity 
of their idea.  People who are known to be "different" will have their 
ideas ignored while people who are deemed "popular" will be regarded 
with great esteem.
