> It seems insane that mankind works this way, but almost all evidence

I really don't know how this will play out, but in some sense I think  
a lot of "different" people will end up in different communities. But  
in the long run, again, I think we can't get very far here until we  
try some real-world experiments. As long as we keep an open mind,  
hopefully we will recognize when we're "suppressing" people. And if we  
have a strong consensus model, that too should do a lot of correction.  
Reference the third basic principle of this project.

 From what I understand, the Quakers' fight against slavery started  
with one person. The rest of the quakers totally disagreed with him,  
but instead of ignoring him, they *supported* his quest to convince  
them that they were wrong. They actually provided him a means of  
living in order for him to go around trying to convince them to  
abandon slavery. And after a mere 70 years, they did come to a  
complete consensus against slavery... 70 years before the rest of the  
United States.
