At the time of writing, the IRC log isn't up yet, but I imagine it
will be shortly:
http://okfn.org/wiki/meetings/2008-09-24

It was a very interesting discussion, and pretty much all of the
Metagovernment-centric content was covered first, to summarise:

* The Open Knowledge Foundation seem to be advocates for all instances
of "Open Knowledge" and express an interest with working with 3rd
parties as far as it is compliant with their agenda -
http://opendefinition.org/1.0/  - or 'OKD'
* The "non-commercial" clause from Creative Commons is not compatible
with the OKD
* Because of this, they suggested CC-by or CC-by-sa
* Also suggested was CCZero ( still in draft -
http://wiki.creativecommons.org/CCZero ), which appears to be an
attempt to create an internationally accepted definition of 'public
domain'
* In addition, they also maintain a license compatibility list:
http://opendefinition.org/licenses

Overall, it sounds as if an alignment could be mutually beneficial..
and would be another incentive to drop the non-commercial clause.
