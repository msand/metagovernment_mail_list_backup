I'm starting to glimpse the light at the end of the tunnel now that I have relocated my family to Barcelona. I should be in a position to make a fuller contribution again in another few weeks once I've found work and hit a new routine. Meantime I'm trying to keep up to date with the emails.

I think Richard's article is very good. I was not aware of the history, and I understand and support his position. But it seems he is slightly living in a time-warp. I am one of the people that came to the movement well after "open" and "open-source" had been seized on by the media and popularised. But personally I had always assumed the open source movement to be a social force for freedom as well as a pragmatic improvement in terms of software development and support models. In any case I'm not against the term "open" so long as we all know that there is an ethical imperative for freedom assumed and incorporated. 

So "open governance" probably does it for me. It is not something we need to stick to forever if we decide it needs to evolve further.

BTW - it strikes me that Richard should be calling free software " freedom software" which is what he really means.

Paul
