This article is relevant to the scoring system:
http://tech.slashdot.org/article.pl?sid=08/09/16/1239213

The main problem is when doing ratings, who is the arbiter of the truth?
The scoring system should filter out the truth, but it can only ever be as
good as the people scoring, which is demonstrated by the storys that make
it on digg in comparison to slashdot.

There should be an incentive in the system, to make people give scores
objectively and with a certain degree of critical thinking. Taking advogato
as inspiration, the score of a resource should be relative to the people
that you trust. The idea being, that if you want people to trust you,
you shouldn't blindly score something on a passing feeling. Not sure it
will work, but it's the best approach I can think of.
