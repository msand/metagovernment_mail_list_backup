I tentatively agree on both major points. Our copyright probably  
should be as open as possible. The U.S. government considers its works  
to be public domain:
http://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/Commons:Licensing#Works_by_the_US_Government

And what would we do if a commercial entity wanted to quote us...  
charge them money? We don't even take money as of now.

And I certainly wouldn't want to take action against the Progressive  
Initiative for quoting us:
http://www.progressiveinitiative.org/Initiative3
If anything, I think it's great that they're modeling after us. That  
should be promoted, not restricted.
