On Sep 24, 2008, at 8:16 AM, Nicholas Church wrote:

> I do believe a non-profit corporation is governed by certain
> laws, i.e. you have to have board members who meet at predefined times
> etc.  Based on the link you showed me, you would rather have everyone
> make the decisions rather than a select few.  You could make it a
> requirement that all board members sign a document agreeing that they
> will vote in accordance with the will of the majority.  Make it a
> misconduct clause whereby they are stripped of their power if they  
> fail
> to vote with the metascore results.

I really don't like having our group be "governed by certain laws." We  
shouldn't have to bow to any particular government. It just seems  
antithetical to the spirit of the organization.

Also, personally I am opposed to a board of directors no matter how  
constrained because again, I think it goes against the spirit of open  
source governance. A board is at best a form of representative  
democracy, and that's precisely what we are trying to replace. Yes, I  
had actively promoted a board of directors in the initial formation of  
this group, but that was just because I figured that was the way these  
things are done... I love the idea of there being no single individual  
who can be pinned down as our "leader," no single decision-maker or  
authority who, even nominally, has power over others.
