On Tue, Sep 16, 2008 at 3:08 PM, Ed Pastore <epastore at metagovernment.org> wrote:
> I think the problem Roustam is getting to is that there is no such
> thing as truth; at least in most modern philosophical systems.

Agreed - but one doesn't need to know (absolutely) that one is heading
towards absolute (unattainable) "truth" in the classical sense --
rather one can be content (logically) knowing that the probability of
one heading towards absolute (unattainable) "truth" is greater than
that of them not.

It's just a reiteration of the scientific method I guess -- "I don't
know 100% that that ball will come down when I throw it up, but the
probability is judged to be beyond a threshold, so we'll build upon
that, and change our assumption if need be"
