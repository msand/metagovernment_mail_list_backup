As a non-developer in the IT world I interpret the word "Source" to mean
more than simply "Code".

>From the religious standpoint, the "Source" of our reality has gone by many
names. However, from an "Open" perspective, one individual or a single
religion has no valid claim to the "Source". So it goes with Government,
Education, or anything which emanates from a specific point of reference --
aka, the "Source".

Furthermore, I interpret the word "Code" to equally apply to the above
examples, as religion has a Code of Law, as does Education and Government.
In the end, all "Code" is merely words written to perform a specific
function within an "Operating System" -- which, of course, can be
interpreted to mean more than a "Computer".

So goes the philosophy once it leaves the explicit domain of Software
Development.
