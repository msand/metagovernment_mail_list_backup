While I personally refer to "Linux" rather than "GNU/Linux", I
recognise that to RMS it is about a social movement he is campaigning
for, and that while I'm not a member of it, I'm glad it exists for it
has brought about great positive change. I'd move to be supportive of
(or at least not work against) RMS's aims where possible, and where it
doesn't directly conflict with the aims of Metagovernment.

Looking at the Wiki page:
http://www.metagovernment.org/wiki/Direct_democracy

I agree with Pietro that DD over the net is potentially a different
beast than a crippled form of DD pre-internet - the algorithmic
application of synthesis voting should (I think) overcome the "issue
overload" argument.

If words/phrased can be easily co-opted, then this would seem a good
choice to me. Direct = positive, and alliteration has been in the
arsenal of marketers since they were born, for good reason.
