On Sat, Sep 27, 2008 at 12:09 PM, Rob Peters
<thewinterlion at googlemail.com> wrote:
> However, I don't think App Engine is worth it for 2 reasons: 1. You can't
> upload files.

http://stackoverflow.com/questions/81451/upload-files-in-google-app-engine

It isn't as clean as it might be, but it's not a showstopper if there
is a requirement for users to upload files.


> 2. App Engine's greatest features (load-balancing and
> unlimited scaling) are problems we don't have yet.

It is also free until you get (according to 'typical' estimates)
around 5million hits/month, so that's one less thing to worry about -
in addition to the ability to scale - which, as you mention, is less
of a concern when using GAE anyway.


> I really think App Engine appeals to people in the same way that people
> dream of being a rock star.  Musicians worry about getting signed to a
> record label and then they figure they will make millions.  But in truth,
> most musicians will never make any money from their work.  But the dream
> drives them on.  Google says to people, "when you hit it big, we'll be there
> to make sure your web site scales."  In truth,  most people's websites will
> never hit it big, most sites will not get slashdotted, and I'm willing to
> bet that 99% of all App Engine sites will never require any scaling beyond
> what's available with the most basic of web hosting.

Agreed. I don't think that's a bad thing though. It allows for really
quick development and exploration of ideas. Countless times I've
configured and tweaked a LAMP environment just to test something out
quickly and spent more time in pointless drudgery than fun hackery -
no more!


> We have some mountains
> to climb with Metagovernment, but scaling issues of our web application is
> not one of them.  And it may never be one of them.

Metagovernment is not a project of small ideas - if I didn't think
scaling was going to be an issue in the future then I would be
investing time into a project which I thought would.


> Why sacrifice essential privacies and freedoms for a benefit we don't know we need?

Which privacies or freedoms are being potentially sacrificed?

I'm not advocating that GAE be used as a Metascore implementation
platform - it fails the "single point of failure" and "distributed"
tests. Both those arguments would fall the moment the source to GAE
was opened though (assuming no atypical hardware requirements), or if
a GAE compatible backend was developed - perhaps from the fully
functioning SDK version.

>From what I've seen though, it'd be harder to retrofit an existing
application onto GAE.. so for the prototypes in development it doesn't
seem relevant.
