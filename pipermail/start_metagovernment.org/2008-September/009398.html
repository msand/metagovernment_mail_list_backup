> how do you define "100% truth" and, more interestingly, "51% truth"?

The nice thing, is that you don't have to - all you have to assert is
that a system has a greater probability of being true, than false.
Over time, such a system will trend towards "absolute truth" -- even
if the initial conditions give the system a very slight advantage --
if such a system uses the "truth" it generates to improve its own
truth-determining capabilities.

Of course, the quicker initial value of truthiness you have, the less
time the process will take.

It's a classic phenomena seen with Artificial Life simulations and
'fitness' - maybe Pietro can help me out here?
