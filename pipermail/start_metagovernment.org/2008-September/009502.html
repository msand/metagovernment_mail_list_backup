I think the critical point is that the application could be moved easily off
of Google's platform if the agreement for any reason became undesirable.  In
the mean time, it's providing valuable resources and is generating a great
deal of interest in the open source community.  It's been interesting to see
the eyes of developers light up when they start to understand the tools and
technologies the App Engine makes available.  My experience so far is that
in the eyes of developers, having a technically interesting project is
almost as important as having a noble purpose.
 
Plus, Google's motto is "Don't be Evil".  That's got to count for
something... :-)
