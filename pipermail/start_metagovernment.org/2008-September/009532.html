"Starting Thursday, voters who are registered in Arizona but live overseas
will be able to vote online through a unique Web-based system. The Secretary
of State's Military and Overseas Voting system will allow registered voters
to apply for early ballots online and then submit their ballots
electronically using a document scanner."
http://ap.google.com/article/ALeqM5gyJXyREMROriqcjKMjGxgEx_n0zgD93GT9HG0

Furthermore, if you Google "Arizona Online Voting" you'll notice a couple
links to FCW.com that mention two different websites for online voting --
both of which seem invalid: Votation.com and Election.com. I'm unable to
find exactly what Arizona has to do with these two websites, but I
did discover that Election.com was sold to a Saudi group of investors for an
incredible amount of money.

http://www.thelandesreport.com/Election.com.htm

Votation.com, "the first global Internet election company," has its own
unique history that goes back to 1999 and involves VeriSign as a primary
shareholder.

http://findarticles.com/p/articles/mi_m0EIN/is_1999_Dec_16/ai_58270932

http://findarticles.com/p/articles/mi_m0EIN/is_1999_Dec_16/ai_58487613
