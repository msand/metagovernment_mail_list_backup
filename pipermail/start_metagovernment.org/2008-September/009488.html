>From experience, I've seen that when you start letting a ton of people in, you end up having to administrate everyone because of their lack of tact and their own personal agendas creating arguments and opinions.  Also, more avenues have to be created to house all of the opinions, due to cluttering up the list or group.  Pretty soon, the information becomes bogged down with clutter and nothing gets done until the medium is cleaned up or a new one is created.  It basically just creates more talking and less production.  

Lastly, it can still be "open" and provide a slow trickle of people over time, where it can be easily managed and adaptable. Unfortunately we live in times of competition and wanting to be the creators and early adopters before someone else markets the idea all over the place.

It seems like everyone on the list so far are good level headed people with good suggestions.  I agree that getting the first steps and format completed is a more sustainable and focused route.

Just my "opinion" to add to the slow cluttering.  :P
