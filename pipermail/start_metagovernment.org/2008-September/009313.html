Agreed on the second. A deeper study would be necessary to understand
how many people are just blindly scanning a page for keywords, and how
many read the whole damn thing, and then find out the keywords from
their understanding on the topic.

Still it 'seem' to me that if people did not like the word open in
this context it would have been used far less. But I admit it is a
sensation.

Regarding the first:
Experience with tagclouds showed me that even such a small sample is
quite representative of the population. I never have seen the first
tags be totally changed after 50 or 60 people. When it happened, it
was mostly because a new tag appeared, as a new concept was coined,
and was percolating through society.
For an example of this, please look at the entry on my blog:
http://blog.pietrosperoni.it/2005/05/28/tagclouds-and-cultural-changes/
