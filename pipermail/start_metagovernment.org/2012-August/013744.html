> Hello Alex,
> 
> thanks for your response. Yes, the concept is not visionary and it was 
> never meant to be.  I'm an old man (51) and I don't want to spend the 
> rest of my life within a lousy political system, while dreaming of a 
> better world.
> 
> My concept is meant to be a practical approach, which can be applied 
> tomorrow. It is based on the experiences I made so far with conventional 
> parties. It is the attempt to correct the existing concepts for the 
> better, it's an evolution, not a revolution.
> 
> In my eyes VOTOROLA has one big mistake: it assumes that people are 
> willing to participate in the political process. This is not the case. 
> Of more than 60 million voters only 5 million are organized in political 
> parties in Germany. And even among those 5 million only a minority is 
> actually actively involved in the political process. I therefore come to 
> the conclusion that the majority of voters are not willing to contribute 
> the required effort of time and energy, to participate in the political 
> process. But if only a minority is driving the political process, a 
> system like VOTOROLA only leads to a dictatorship of the active, just 
> like we currently have with our conventional parties. (The concept of 
> VOTOROLA has, in my opinion, a couple more flaws, which I will not 
> explain here).
> 
> This is also the key to the understanding of my concept: the voters are 
> not dissatisfied with the political system as a whole. They are in 
> principal content with the concept of "politics as a service". The 
> voters are discontent with the outcome of our politics in detail and 
> that again is due to the lack of communication between politician and 
> voters. My concept tries to overcome this communicational gap.
