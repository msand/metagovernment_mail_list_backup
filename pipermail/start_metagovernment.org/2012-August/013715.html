Am Sonntag, 5. August 2012, 12:30:28 schrieb Peter Zbornik:
> This is a reasonable condition, and thus, unless we have a wold-wide public
> party, there needs to be some voter qualifications (except for being human,
> above 16/18/21 years of age, not seriously mentally impaired etc. 

Why? How can we assume that this selection is neutral and forms a common body 
to build consensus or even only a working democratical process on top of it? 
Sociology will tell us that the nation is ideology and that there are very 
different social levels involved. But even more: How do we know that this 
selection is even democratical itself? Have we voted upon it?

If I partition a contradictory society in different ways I will have different 
outcomes out of the same "democratical" processes even for a 50% majority rule 
(not for consent though!). The only real strength of the democratical process, 
a strength overseen in status-quo misdesigned processes, is its totality. If 
you are in a really democratical process, you trust that this process is good 
for you in general (total). Only few people really trust in "democracy" right 
now, how can we assume that the idea itself is appealing without showing 
participants how it is so? Why is breaking it down in the current form of 
segments a neutral first step?
