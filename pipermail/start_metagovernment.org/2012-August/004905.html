Thomas said:
> In theory we should be able to press down the price to almost zero,
> shouldn't we. But ofc we would need a number of probably 90% of all
> users to agree to move away. But then, that should just be a
> question of time, right?

Yes, I think so.  The agreement will attract resources as it grows.
Those resources will open a path to action.  There's no telling what
path will open first, but the nearer it gets to opening the lower the
share price will fall, till purchasing Facebook itself becomes a path!
All of Facebook's capital is tied to the immobility of its users.

> Ed wrote:
> > I think a Facebook group makes sense, as it is just too beautiful
> > to run the campaign from within Facebook.  However, Facebook has
> > the right to close down groups whenever it wants.
> 
> Ok, that is a real danger!

We might have anti-trust laws on our side.  Your initial post is clear
that the aim is to get around Facebook's monopoly.  As long as that
remains true, Facebook cannot arbitrarily shut down the group without
breaking the law.  They're allowed to have a monopoly, but not to
abuse it.  It would be a clear abuse to deprive the group of the use
of Facebook for the purpose of promotion and publicity, because it is
precisely here that Facebook has its monopoly.
