Hi Jacopo, I checked it out.
1) what I told you about the motorizzazione is not universal. The
Motorizzazione of Rome bias the speedometer to show a higher speed. But
others do not insert any bias, or even insert a negative bias showing a
lower speed. There is a place where people made a class action against a
company that was working with the government (and getting also a percentage
of the tickets) and biasing all the speedometer making people think they
were travelling slower.

2) the limit has a 5% of tollerance, with a minimum of 5 km/h. Which means
that for every speed below 100km/h, the actual speed limit is 5km/h more.
And for every speed beyond 100 the actual speed limit is 5% more. So if the
speed limit is 130 km/h the real speed limit is 136.5 km/h (as measured
from your very precise GPS).

http://www.asaps.it/82-Quesiti/298-Limiti_di_Velocit%C3%A0
