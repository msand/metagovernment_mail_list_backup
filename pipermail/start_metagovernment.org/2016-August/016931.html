>I wouldn't go for "good decision index", because nobody can know if decision is good. You may make a decision that will earn you 1 million dolars, but after that, you'll lose all your friends. was the first decision was a good decision?

 

I second that, further more decisions have a time dimension as well – a decision might bring in benefits in short term, however in medium-long term could prove very bad. And the other way around, sometimes taking a hard (presumably bad decision in sort term) could provide stability, make things better, in the long run.

In my opinion attempting to evaluate a decision is same as trying to predict the future which even if it were possible it will generate a paradox by itself. 

The only thing you could really measure is likelihood/probability of given system to generate good/bad decisions. Knowing the ingredients and the interaction rules of the setup you could calculate an efficiency index and  probability of developing problems such as corruption.

Hi Mchal

I wouldn't go for "good decision index", because nobody can know if decision is good. You may make a decision that will earn you 1 million dolars, but after that, you'll lose all your friends. was the first decision was a good decision?

 

There is well knowen index for deliberation: deliberation qualety index (DQI): http://www.delib-democracy.org/wiki/index.php?title=DQI

But this index is about the process, not the decision.

 

I would suggest two main ways to try to evalute a decsion: 

1) that it answerd most of the needs of the group members, with minimal burden (high ROI).

2) that it passed John Rawl's Veil of ignorance <https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Veil_of_ignorance>  (a just decision).
