The basic design is laid out on matchism.org, but to clarify a few
(new) points of emphasis:
1) It's a voting system, not a deliberative system or a
consensus-building system. Nearly all of the proposals will be
produced by experts who know how to write for The People rather than
for other experts (or in the case of most of our laws, apparently not
for anyone). Eventually this may broaden to allow user-submitted
proposals, but IMHO that is not even an important feature, let alone a
necessary one.

2) My current thinking is to tie into social media and smart phones to
piggyback on their success (e.g., instead of just making a smart-assed
comment on Reddit or Twitter, you might actually use your phone to
*vote* on whether we should add some additional hurdles to immigration
policy).

3) A heavy-duty proxy-finding and utilization system: Most of the time
people's votes will come from the autopilot (i.e., computed using the
votes of their proxies). They'll then get a text or email specifying
how their proxies voted and why (i.e., when you cast a vote you'll
also be asked for a quote specifying *why* you voted that way), at
which point you'll have the option of letting that computed vote be
cast for you, or to override it.

1) People are naturally interested in politics

2) People are naturally efficient in their resource allocation (i.e.,
they're "lazy", or more properly, too busy to invest a lot of time in
something that will have no lasting effect). This is particularly true
of Neurotypicals because they don't crave power, unlike our current
SDAP leadership which is highly motivated by it.

Find a way to combine those two Clinations and the new system will
take off like FB and Twitter and the other popular social media
services that offer something that somehow managed to hit the sweet
spot in their niche. But I also believe a key factor in the success of
a new system is merely believing that it can be: People (as are common
on this list) who are afraid to use the word "revolution" or to use
their systems to make a decision about abortion or gun control IMHO
have merely guaranteed their own irrelevance.
