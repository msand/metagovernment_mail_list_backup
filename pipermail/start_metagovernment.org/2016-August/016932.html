> metagovernment systems decision index

Probably the most obvious case to examine in terms of likelihood to generate good decisions or any decision at all. Say,

-         Ingredients: smart people, from various backgrounds, all interested in making things better – so from that perspective it gets a good score

-         Interaction/ground rules and setup systems – there is just the basic debating of personal point of views with no general consensus on say  find a common ground of action and define a general framework of values and properties universally accepted regardless of any proposed system – so that has a low score 

So you can say that even if here you have smart people, well intended and ready to put some effort into it, there is some major “interaction/setup” problem which prevents them in generating any good decision as a group – rather than just individual – which defies the point of the existence this group.  

So rather than looking say at decision to make the metagoverment.org website looking one way or another you should probably ask yourselves if you actually collaborate/organize/work together well? If not the likelihood for the outcome of the website is either to never have any (like now) or to have something which only a small number will people will use, which given the scope of the group.
