Non-sequitur: The People have nothing against inventors or explorers
or even leaders even though most of them are not those things. They
*do* have a problem with these self-selected elites dictating how
things are going to be with no regard for what The People want. In the
EEA they could just opt out when these conflicts occurred (or, take
action to end the wannabe dictator, solving the problem permanently).
Unfortunately SDAP technology (i.e., armies wielded by kings, lords,
slave owners, misrepresentative government, etc.) makes that much more
difficult to do now. But the SDAP need the control of the
neurotypicals to keep them from recklessly starting wars or
discriminating against some segment of the population just as much as
the neurotypicals need them to explore/engineer/organize/operate the
systems we all rely on now.

> Now, that you mentioned brexit – they casted a vote without even questioning what is the plan once they get out, and how will that be better, not even bother to ask the basic questions.

Agreed, it was a bad decision. But not because the decision was left
to The People, but because the people that made the initial proposal
did a half-assed job and the people that explained it to the people
essentially manipulated them into voting against their own interests.
But this is just a design flaw in the system the people were operating
in, and IMHO you therefore can't use it to reject the idea that The
People should get to make the decisions that are required to run a
government. We can use technology to reduce the incidence of bad
decisions like this, but, as with my gay-marriage example, sometimes
The People will lag the moral pioneers more than some of the pioneers
(probably including most of us) would like.
