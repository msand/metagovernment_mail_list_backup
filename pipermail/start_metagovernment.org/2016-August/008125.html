I'm working up a letter to the editor for the local paper, which currently
features a non-stop stream of letters about Trump that I believe for
the most part are missing the point. What do you think: Should being a
psychopath disqualify one from being president?

Certainly the best-known psychopaths in leadership positions (Stalin
and Mao) had a negative impact on the people they governed, but should
the fact that Donald Trump meets the clinical criteria for being a
psychopath/sociopath disqualify him from being president?

Although the popular conception of psychopaths is that they're all
criminals (they're only 1 or 2 percent of the population, yet make up
over a quarter of the prison population) the vast majority of them are
living successful, if not driven, lives all around us. They're highly
over-represented among the ranks of CEOs, surgeons, and lawyers, for
example. We seem be strangely fascinated by their skills and abilities
to "get things done" (Frank Underwood in the Netflix series "House Of
Cards" being another textbook example).

I've seen a lot of comparisons between Trump and Hitler, but although
there is some overlap in campaigning technique (fearmongering,
demagoguery, etc.) Hitler wasn't a psychopath. He was instead in
Altemeyer's classification a Double High (high SDO and high RWA). The
appropriate comparison would therefore be between George W Bush and
Hitler (besides the two having similar psychological profiles, the
justifications for the invasions of Poland and Iraq had significant
similarities and I believe are the completely predictable result of
putting Double Highs in positions of power).

So the question we need to ask ourselves (and Trump supporters) is:
Are the predictable downsides of putting a psychopath in power
(aggregation of power, ruthlessness, discrimination against the weak,
etc.) a worthwhile trade for their higher levels of success in
achieving the goals they have set for themselves? And: Given how
predictive these psychological profiles have proven to be, shouldn't
we examine *all* of our candidates using these tools? My prediction
for what will happen if we start doing this is that we'll realize that
we need a new political system (perhaps a direct democracy with
delegation) because the people serving in our current representative
democracies can't adequately represent us because psychologically they
are very different from the rest of us...
