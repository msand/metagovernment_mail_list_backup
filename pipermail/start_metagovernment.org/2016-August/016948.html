Or by blatant censorship of their posts. Check it out yourself: Any
topic that is even the least bit controversial will have as many
deleted posts (indeed entire subthreads) deleted as you're able to
actually see. Which is why sites like https://uneddit.com and

I hadn't looked at Reddit since last year, but see that's it's gotten
significantly worse in that time. I still do read Slashdot regularly,
though, and agree with the general concensus on Slashdot that there's
something seriously off about Reddit (of course not anything nearly as
bad as what they have to say about even more poorly regulated sites
like 4chan). But my main criticism of *all* of these sites is that
they're designed to attract eyeballs, not to facilitate mutual
understanding of a complex topic. The posts that get upvoted sometimes
contain some insight or useful information, but for the most part
they're merely clever trolls posting snappy one-sentence comebacks. I
don't find any of that interesting or useful, which is why I don't
believe this group should move, nor should we use any of these sites
as a model for how to built a discussion/rating system.

Sure, this is a common idea. And I think completely bogus and

Are we talking about Japan? Their debt ratio is several times higher
than even the USs. But I don't think any of that is a useful criticism
of neurotypical rationality as my claim is that the people that put
the government in debt (in *all* countries) simply aren't
neurotypical. Take the corruption out of politics (e.g., by getting
rid of misrepresentative democracy) and regular people will be able to
make far more rational decisions. Hopefully more rationally than even
they're able to do with their own personal finances (e.g., unlike
SDAPs that shiny new weapons system won't have anywhere near the same
appeal to them that a new HDTV does).
