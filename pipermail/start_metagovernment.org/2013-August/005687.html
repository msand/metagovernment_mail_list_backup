Actually, we have been talking about *two* organizations. One of them 
is, as you say, an American non-profit corporation. The other one is a 
*global* "society" that will drive all of various the national 
non-profit corporations (including the American one). The global society 
may or may not itself be nationally incorporated anywhere.

Since the American non-profit corporation will be driven by the global 
society organization, the bylaws of the subsidiary corporation will have 
to refer to the global society (as the driver), and the workings of the 
global society will need to be formally described, in its own bylaws or 
some such.

Thus, I named that other thread "Society Bylaws" to allow for another 
thread to be created, entitled "American non-profit corporation bylaws". 
We will need both. (And now we have both.)

There will actually not be "an organization to serve us". There will be 
multiple organizations to serve us, because the individual nations will 
have it no other way. So, to prevent recurrence of this confusion, maybe 
henceforth we should speak of "a set of organizations to serve us".
