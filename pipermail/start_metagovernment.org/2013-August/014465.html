Hi Alex,

    Ned> Once we have the money in hand, my proposal is that
    compensation for Metagovernance work be determined democratically,
    through proposing and agreeing/deciding.

    Alex> I feel that there has to be a predictable mechanism which
    prevents dictatorship of majority. It should be about process and
    tasks and not about people. I'm not sure how to do it, but its
    important to put a lot of thought into this.

Alex, I could not agree more. YES!

And also, should I take your response as a "yes" to the proposal that 
(in principle) compensation for Metagovernance work be determined 
democratically?

------------------------------------------------------------------------

    Alex> Do you know "Scrum's Planning Poker" or the "Wideband Delphi
    Estimation Method"? What do you think of this approach?

Scrum's planning poker can be useful in planning and prioritizing and 
such. But it seems to me that the best basis for determining 
COMPENSATION for people is measuring and appraising what has happened in 
the past (work accomplished). It would be better to base compensation on 
what has happened, not upon guesses about what may happen. For example, 
when predictions turn out to be bad, the compensation to the predictors 
should reflect that. Yes? (Maybe I am not understanding how you wish to 
apply scrum's poker to determining compensation?)

------------------------------------------------------------------------

    Alex> And last but probably not least, principal-agent-conflicts
    should be avoided, so people do not try assign points to tasks for
    other political reasons ...

Again, I could not agree more. These are the sorts of problems that our 
direct democratic decision system design must forestall and mitigate. 
Designing good systems is hard work.
