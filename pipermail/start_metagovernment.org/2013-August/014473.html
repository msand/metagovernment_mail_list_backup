Here's my elevator pitch then, for InterMix Voices of Humanity Social
Media<http://voicesofhumanity.org>
.

InterMix Voices of Humanity (VoH) is an exciting new form of social media
designed to leverage global consensus for peace and sustainability into
concerted action. InterMix VoH software works by creating voices for groups
in online forums by having highly rated messages float to the top - nothing
new there, but InterMix also provides a discussion facility where multiple
groups can participate together on a particular (or general) topic. Within
a discussion each group has its own voice and in addition the participants
are recombined into six voices of humanity: Voice of Women, Voice of Men,
Voice of Youth, of Experience (middle-age), of Wisdom (seniors) and the
Voice of Humanity as One.

The InterMix process marginalizes religious and nationalist hard-liners,
giving control to those who want to cooperate. With the best in humanity
shining forth, a heart-felt community will grow and with it the desire to
cooperate in action. Best of all, there is nothing stopping all this from
happening massively on a global scale creating a global consciousness to
underpin our efforts to handle the upcoming population peak.

You can sign up for the beta test here: voh.steering.intermix.org.

>> When people ask me what our group is, I often fumble: trying to boil down
>> our huge number of radical concepts into something that is to them a brand
>> new idea.
>>
>> So I realized I need to develop an "elevator pitch<http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Elevator_pitch>."
>> In case you're unfamiliar with the idea, it's the scenario where you're in
>> an elevator with someone and you have until the elevator reaches their stop
>> to convince them your idea is worthwhile. The elevator pitch is, by
>> necessity, going to gloss over a lot of detail. It's a sales pitch, and it
>> should be ~30 seconds long.
>>
>> So, I've made a few drafts and come up with the following (which I think
>> still needs to be shorter). Let me know what you think, and/or how it could
>> be improved and/or what your elevator pitch is.
>>
>>
>> *METAGOV ELEVATOR PITCH:*
>>
>> Every time we vote someone into power, we lose all control over the
>> decisions they make until the next time we get to vote. Metagovernment is a
>> global group of people creating internet tools that let you delegate your
>> vote to whomever you want, withdraw it whenever you want, or keep it for
>> yourself and vote directly on the issues that matter most to you.
>>
>> By their nature, these tools don't work with a majority rule system.
>> Instead, they use a sophisticated consensus model. Computers and internet
>> connectivity really help with that. The amazing thing is, when people are
>> forced to work together to create a solution that works for everyone...
>> they do! And these solutions are much stronger, better, and longer-lasting
>> than the decisions made by small groups of representatives who inevitably
>> make political compromises, if not purely selfish decisions.
>>
>> We're not expecting our tools to take hold in national governments at
>> first. But voting for representatives happens in many different communities
>> we're involved in: social clubs, nonprofit organizations, homeowner
>> associations, community groups, school boards, and so on. We expect our
>> tools to take foot in the smallest communities, and expand from there as
>> people get used to the idea of taking control of -- and thus responsibility
>> for -- their democracies.
