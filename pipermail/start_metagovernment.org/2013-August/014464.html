i know prof. joseph huber.
i believe having seigniorage distributed as a basic income is not a bad
idea, but it lacks a "philosophical basis".
what is money? its a service granted by all those who accept money. It is
VITAL to understand this and its not easy.

*1. *Imagine you have a tangible asset, like a house or a car or a fork.
Imagine now all people on earth die over night, your house is still able to
provide shelter, your car is still able to move, your fork is still able to
serve as a tool with which you eat, but what about money?
*You could have all the money in the world, but without other people, its
rendered useless.*

*2.* If there were a fixed amount of money at some point in time (maybe
because it was decided that the upper limit on debt was reached and no new
debt shall be created. Now imagine that a few persons for some reason have
nearly all the money which exists.
Those people without money are specialised to sell there services and
products via money and even if 2 persons want each others goods and
services and could provide those goods and services, they are NOT ABLE to
do the necessary transactions, because they lack money. So the few persons
who have all the money indirectly prevent those who have no money from
doing business. Those who do not have money have to WAIT until those who
have it actually BUY something, so the money again circulates, right??? :-)
So in a strict view, those who can provide goods and services and have no
money to do business amongst each other are put into "standby mode",* they
have to wait and automatically offer some kind of "standby duty"* or
"on-call service" for those who have money, even if they dont want to, they
DO offer this kind of service and the reason is, that they use the same
money and only this kind of money which is held by the few people...


*1.+ 2.
*Without other people, money is useless.
If you use money, thus you are offering an "economic API" or "econmic
interface" to the economy or all the people who do business, you
automatically put yourself into a position, where it might happen, that you
have to offer "standby duty" (we have this situation all around the world
and because those who have the money do not buy, debt increases)
Debt increases, because the "standby duty" is not paid for by those who use
it by "holding money" and so those who offer the "standby duty" have to
pre-finance their costs through more debt.... ever increasing debt...
around the world.


*Solution?*
Accept, that money is a service granted by all who use it to all who use it.
If you hold money, you have to pay for it, you pay all others who use money.
If you use money, you are being paid by all others who use money.
So if you are a net receiver or not depends on how much money you hold.
THIS WILL REVERSE THE "GREAT DIVIDE (between poor and rich)" :-)
Because if money would be like this, those who want to be "richer than
others" have to work hard for it and the bigger the distance between
poorest and richest person, the harder it gets to get richer (that would be
the opposite of today).
It would also create INCENTIVE for "rich people" to help the poor ones
(they will rather do that then loosing all their money through the "money
service cost" they have to pay)
