Hi Alex, (Great response!)

In just this little exchange between the two of us, we have now opened 
up several fundamental matters to be decided (between the two of us, as 
precursor to having others agree as well). As you say, agreements must 
start small -- between individuals.

Because the matters that have been opened are so fundamental (even 
partly below awareness), it may take some work to articulate them 
clearly and agree on the articulation. Also, it will probably be best to 
tackle them one at a time. Let us start with: "What is our business?"

------------------------------------------------------------------------

    Alex> I dont know your definition of corporation, but democracy
    mainly happens on the street through spending money or not on
    certain things, and it happens every day during work time.

The Metagovernment community is now on the way to becoming a non-profit 
startup. The startup will be in the business of attracting donations and 
then spending that money to design, develop and deploy democratic 
decision systems.

You and I and the other members of the Metagovernment community are 
already part of that startup. We have equity in the startup by virtue of 
our participation -- which is just the way you want it to be.

If we ourselves (Metagovernment) are unable to agree upon a full direct 
democratic decision system design through which we ourselves can then 
make decisions in operating our own startup, then "they" (anyone outside 
of our own community) would be silly to suppose that we have any special 
expertise that would help "them" to make decisions democratically.

We need to "dine on our own dogfood" as proof-of-concept that we know 
what we are talking about, so that when we approach others outside of 
our community for donations or as prospective users of our systems, they 
won't just laugh at us (e.g., "let me know when they finish playing in 
the sandbox").

    You say: "a job is where democracy happens". 

I say, right here right now is where democracy happens, between you and 
I, in this discourse. (This is our work environment -- yours and mine.) 
In each instance that we (you and I) succeed in reaching agreement, we 
have taken one more small step toward full global direct democracy.

    You say: "The work people do every day is what they have insight
    into and what they are familiar with."

The work that you and I do every day has to do with conceiving and 
designing and discussing democratic decision systems. That is what we 
(you and I) have insight into and what we are familiar with. We need to 
help *ourselves* argue about it and make *ourselves* owners of our 
personal work environment. Then maybe our own self esteem will increase, 
and we may feel comfortable enough to tackle the next layer in the 
hierarchy in a democratic fashion. Our own work together (you and I) 
will then grow bottom up and so will the tools THAT WE OURSELVES USE, in 
doing our own work together.

    You say: "Its easier to create a tool and make people use it, when
    you start with a startup or a project team and make it work".

We are a startup. We (self-selected members of the Metagovernment 
community) have equity in that startup. We can start with us. We can 
start with us NOW.

We (you and I and other community members) begin by reaching agreements, 
one after another after another, as quickly and efficiently as possible. 
(The aim is not to aimlessly, endlessly chatter. The aim is to reach 
agreements, one after another after another, as quickly and efficiently 
as possible. Our progress is measured by the quality and quantity of 
that growing corpus of agreements.)

At this point, we don't have any special tools. We have only listserv 
emails and wiki-pages. But even just those tools are sufficient to 
enable us to work toward growing our corpus of agreements. We just need 
to focus on our business: making proposals and reaching agreements. That 
is the business of our startup. That is what we produce, and what we 
will be helping others produce.

Do you agree? (Shall we start making proposals with the aim of reaching 
agreements?)
