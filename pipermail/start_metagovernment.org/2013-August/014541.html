> Participants would then have to choose
> what content to put in them.  But those who naively used them to
> express reasons would be roundly defeated by those who instead used
> them to express votes and to force the decision one way or another.

> into a system of brute force decision.  Do you see what I mean?
>

+1


>
> However we choose to define consensus, we want it to arise from the
> "unforced force of the better argument" (Habermas).  That won't be
> possible if we get it mixed up with decision systems, which are
> necessarily media of brute force.  Why not drop the function of
> decision making entirely and fall back to rational discourse?  That's
> really all that's needed.  The world has plenty of systems for forcing
> decisions on people, but few supports for reasoned discussion.
>
> And if we don't attempt to glue reasoned discussion (open and free) to
> decision making (centralized and forced), then we needn't jam everyone
> into the same discussion medium.  Harry and friends can continue using
> Web forums, while Alex and friends continue using email, and so forth.
> No need to argue (as in the other thread) which is "better".
>

Sure, but this is not so much about what i would like to use or what others
would like to use, but more about what "mainstream people would use" if it
