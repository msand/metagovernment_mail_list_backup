I covered a potential algorithm for managing Dissent in one of my forum
posts back in 2007
https://fullmoon.nu/forum2013-import/viewtopic.php?p=868#p868

couple of things to note. First, if you choose to visit, please ignore
the "invalid certificate" warnings and add an exception. I've instituted
https to increase privacy (makes it harder for the snoops to learn what
you're visiting online) but I can't be arsed to pay for a "legitimate"
certificate.

Second, it's a long post, so if you're not particularly interested in
the debate I was having with "the Prof" over the merits of democracy,
search for
"5 Prioritise Dissent". That'll take you straight to my proposed
algorithm.

Essentially, in my view, it is impractical, in a democractic debate to
aim for genuine consensus. (the complete absence of dissent) That is a
recipe for decision making paralysis.

What is practical is to afford priveleged status to dissent and to
insist that dissenting arguments are a) given full coverage and b) given
full and proper answers, preferably to the satisfaction of the dissenter
but at least to the satisfaction of the vast majority of those taking
part in the debate. (my preferred "majority" is at least 80, preferably
90%) and only when a dissenting objection is agreed by that majority to
have been satisfactorily answered, can the debated move on.

Another extremely important part of the solution to the consensus issue
is the target of "Pareto Efficiency" - the notion that no decision is
valid unless we can ensure that there are no losers and that at least
one person gains. In the case of dissent based on prospective loss, this
implies that the majority are obliged to compensate the dissenting
minority for any losses they incur as a result of the decision.

If Pareto Efficiency is not possible (loss is unavoidable, for example,
because the global economy has collapsed!) then the rule is modified so
that all losses are shared proportionately to the ability to sustain
loss.

Hope this is on topic and helps the debate.

Cheers

Harry

>>>>>>  1. *MEMBERSHIP: UNCOERCED*
>>>>>>
>>>>>>     Any sentient being (globally) may freely choose to become a

>>>>>>  2. *DECISION MAKING: DIRECT DEMOCRACY*

>>>>>>     decision that the society makes. Each member participates in
>>>>>>     decision making on equal footing.
>>>>>>
>>>>>>     (Emergency decisions are made through a subordinate emergency
>>>>>>     response system. Any member may participate in the post-emergency
>>>>>>     reviews of decisions made during emergencies.)
>>>>>>
>>>>>>  3. *PARTICIPATION: THROUGH PROPOSING*
>>>>>>
>>>>>>     Members participate in decision making exclusively through
>>>>>>     formally proposing. Votes cast apart from proposing (through
>>>>>>     ballots, polling, "like buttons", "dislike buttons" and such)
>>>>>>     provide information only, and have no formal effect on decision
>>>>>>     making.
>>>>>>
>>>>>>  4. *DECISION RULE: NO UNRESOLVED OBJECTIONS
>>>>>>     *
>>>>>>
>>>>>>     The decision rule is that a proposal is cleared to be implemented
>>>>>>     at the point that it faces no unresolved objections. Objections
>>>>>>     are proposed. Objections are resolved through successive,
>>>>>>     recursive proposing.
>>>>>>
>>>>>>  5. *NOTIFICATION: PROPOSAL PROFILES, PROPOSAL MAGNETS*
>>>>>>
>>>>>>     Effecting direct democracy at the scale of the global population
>>>>>>     requires that the decision system have an affordable sub-system
>>>>>>     that reliably notifies each member of new proposals that may be

>>>>>>     practices that will together effect efficient and reliable global
>>>>>>     notification will be co-created and evolved through successive
>>>>>>     successful proposing.
>>>>>>

>>>>>>     filters; each new proposal must successfully "pass through" all
