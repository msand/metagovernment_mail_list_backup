I try to put my personal thoughts in the few following sentences:

1. money is created by adding amount x to all peoples accounts (each person
has only one such primary account (like a social security number)
2. having money ...(including money with which u can pay and money with
which u normally cannot pay, for example BILLS (currency is nothing else
then bills issued by currency holders to the a bank))... in essence means
using a service supplied by all other people, means you have to pay for it
(the rate/costs of that service must be determined in a democratic fashion).
3. using money ... in eassence means all others have to pay you for
offering that "service / economic interface"

=> how much u have to pay and how much u receive depends on how much money
u have compared to how much money others have. This will determine whether
u are a net receipient or payer. (again: money here means any kind of
"financial debt instrument", be it cash or bills or whatever. its any kind
of claim-liability-pair)
