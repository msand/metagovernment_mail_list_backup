On Fri, Aug 30, 2013 at 9:07 AM, Daniel <avatar2004-mg at yahoo.fr> wrote:

> Hey folks,
>
> Name's Daniel. Long time follower on this list though shamingly never
> managed to get involved.
>
> I'd like to propose a plan of action that could potentially cover the
> issues that have surfaced lately regarding the mailing list, overall
> decision-making and actually making progress.
>
> First, I suggest we transition our discussions to a forum-like platform.
> The mailing list would remain active and posts on the forum would all be
> published as individual messages on the ML. The handling of posts to the ML
> is slightly more complicated and depends (initially at least) on the set of
> modules that are available for the forum software. Parsing mail content
> especially when trying to correlate with upstream messages and trying to
> identify and extract user content, quotes, formatting, etc. is not an easy
> feat (I've been there). A solution that is much more affordable to start
> with would be to enhance the messages by embedding links for the various
> actions (view details, reply, +1, etc.). That way we can focus on the
> correctness of a reference implementation as a web platform and later start
> pushing features down other channels (email, mobile, voice, etc.).
>
> With almost no extra effort, the forum can at least allow us to create
> some structure by segmenting the conversations across categories and topics
> and by providing a threaded view in the web interface.
>
> Second, well ... we'd have what I believe would be a minimally viable
> platform for experimentation. A platform that would actually be *in use*
> since our "real" discussions would be conducted on it. Through small
> increments and iterations, we can move towards a rich discourse / debate /
> collaborative reasoning platform :
>
> - Integration with a wiki / publishing platform
> - Argumentation
> - Decision formalization
> - Integration with external e-gov tools and platforms
> - Integration with a ticket-like system for the delegation of tasks
> - Identity management (SSO, reward system, karma, membership certification
> - badges, privacy, etc.)
>
> All of these things could be done separately but in our case, I believe
> two conditions are necessary to actually set things in motion :
>
> - Build and contribute to something that is in *real use*. If we can
> manage to eat our own dog food, not only will it taste sweeter for those
> who produce it but I'm positive we'll have even more success selling it.
>
> - Build a free discussion and informal communication platform and make it
> the foundation for any other system. Collaborative governance ultimately is
> all about building a model for transitioning from INFORMAL to FORMAL, be it
> ideas, statements, arguments, decisions, action plans, etc. It all starts
> with an informal discussion even at the highest levels of formalism (how
> would you go about proposing a change in the constitution if not by first
> expressing your thoughts with your own words in an informal context ?).
>
> Let me know what you think about all this and if we can settle on a course
> of action, I can start working on a migration plan.
>
