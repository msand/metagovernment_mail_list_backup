Hi Marc, (Great Discussion Tagging! A joy to read, indeed! :^)

My understanding was that Daniel was not suggesting that we try to 
formalize informal discussion. One reason for migrating our informal 
discussion to a forum is that the forum platform can provide better 
support for informal discussion (which we would greatly benefit from in 
my view ...).

No one is suggesting that we abandon email. For notification, email is 
arguably the best option that we have available (for now).

Beyond that, it is possible, as you say, to do amazing things in email 
-- it is not impossible. But trying to pack all of the functionality 
into emails would be like trying to build ships in bottles -- which is 
also possible. The question is, what course of design and development 
seems most likely to give us the best return on our investment of time 
and other resources.

(And also, the discussion of whether to try to pack application 
functionality into emails has already been formalized, at 
http://allangles.co/Demo.html?id=14 . To me, it appears that all of the 
arguments to try to use email that way got soundly rebutted over there. 
If you think otherwise, it would be good for you to hop over there and 
rebut the rebuttals, and advance new reasons to try to pack fancy 
functionality into emails.)

------------------------------------------------------------------------

I agree with you that it would be good to have a rule that says: "Keep 
it as short and simple as possible, but not shorter and simpler."

I also agree that it might be useful to formulate a set of such rules to 
apply to discussion messages -- I look forward to reading your proposed 
set of rules.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
