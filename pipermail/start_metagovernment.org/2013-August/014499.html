>     First, for reference, here are the proposed bylaws being discussed:
>
>        * *Bylaw #3: Participation: Through Proposing*
>
>
>          Members participate in decision making exclusively through
>          formally proposing. Votes cast apart from proposing (through
>          ballots, polling, "like buttons", "dislike buttons" and such)
>          provide information only, and have no formal effect on decision
>          making.
>
>        * *Bylaw #4: Decision Rule: No Unresolved Objections*
>
>
>          The decision rule is that a proposal is cleared to be implemented
>          at the point that it faces no unresolved objections. Objections
>          are proposed. Objections are resolved through successive,
>          recursive proposing.
>
>     ------------------------------------------------------------------------
>
>
>          RESOLVING OBJECTIONS
>
>     An immediate question that arises is: How may objections be resolved?
>
>     One way to resolve objections is to "overwhelm" them with votes,
>     using a majority or super-majority version of the "might makes
>     right" principle. This is currently the /status quo/ method used,
>     globally.
>
>     My contention is that we can do better than this, in all cases.
>     There are better ways to resolve objections, and it is worth our
>     while to find and adopt those better ways, because using
>     brute-force voting to "resolve" objections (particularly at the
>     scale of large populations) has historically not produced
>     sustainable social systems.
>
>     As an example, an objection that rests on some sort of
>     identifiable logical fallacy (/ad hominem/ argument or whatever)
>     can -- by prior agreement -- be considered to have been resolved
>     at the point that an objection to the objection is proposed, that
>     clearly reveals the logical fallacy. (The objector may still
>     harbor the objection, but the objection will then no longer be
>     able to hold back implementation of the original proposal.)
>
>     @Paul: An objection need not be a veto, depending on the methods
>     available to resolve it. I agree with you that the veto system
>     design is not good, because it enables blackmail and because it
>     creates "tyranny of the minority", just as you suggest. Two of the
>     criteria that "better" methods must meet are that they do not
>     enable either blackmail or tyranny.
>
>     But is the "prior agreement" regarding methods of resolving
>     objections settled by voting? I propose no. For each individual,
>     that prior agreement happens, first, at the point of joining the
>     society. One must agree to the existing methods as a condition of
>     being accepted as a member. Once accepted as a member, one is then
>     free to propose new methods, to object to existing methods, and so
>     on. Through successful proposals, the methods are free to
>     continually evolve and elaborate.
>
>     As Ed has suggested, if the society starts out using voting as a
>     method, voting would cease to be a method at the point that a
>     proposal to eliminate voting as a method were to successfully pass.
>
>     In the beginning (now), there is nothing to prevent the existence
>     of multiple societies, that use differing sets of methods for
>     resolving objections. Over time, the "better" methods will
>     prevail, through the greater progress made by the societies that
>     use them. (Or not.) Our work is to come up with those "better"
>     methods, in setting up our own society. Thus, the proposed bylaws.
>

>

>
>                                 What follows is a first draft of
>                                 proposed bylaws for "an organization
>                                 to serve us". This would be the global
>                                 metagovernment (I have called it a
>                                 "society") that would drive all of the
>                                 various national foundations and such
>                                 around the world.
>
>                                 The draft also exists as a wiki-page:
>                                 http://www.metagovernment.org/wiki/Society_Bylaws
>
>                                 ------------------------------------------------------------------------
>
>
>                                  1. *MEMBERSHIP: UNCOERCED*
>
>                                     Any sentient being (globally) may
>                                 freely choose to become a member
>                                     of the society. Any member may
>                                 freely choose to cease to be a
>                                     member of the society.
>
>                                  2. *DECISION MAKING: DIRECT DEMOCRACY*
>
>                                     Each member of the society is free
>                                 to participate in making any
>                                     decision that the society makes.
>                                 Each member participates in
>                                     decision making on equal footing.
>
>                                     (Emergency decisions are made
>                                 through a subordinate emergency
>                                     response system. Any member may
>                                 participate in the post-emergency
>                                     reviews of decisions made during
>                                 emergencies.)
>
>                                  3. *PARTICIPATION: THROUGH PROPOSING*
>
>                                     Members participate in decision
>                                 making exclusively through
>                                     formally proposing. Votes cast
>                                 apart from proposing (through
>                                     ballots, polling, "like buttons",
>                                 "dislike buttons" and such)
>                                     provide information only, and have
>                                 no formal effect on decision
>                                     making.
>
>                                  4. *DECISION RULE: NO UNRESOLVED
>                                 OBJECTIONS
>                                     *
>
>                                     The decision rule is that a
>                                 proposal is cleared to be implemented
>                                     at the point that it faces no
>                                 unresolved objections. Objections
>                                     are proposed. Objections are
>                                 resolved through successive,
>                                     recursive proposing.
>
>                                  5. *NOTIFICATION: PROPOSAL PROFILES,
>                                 PROPOSAL MAGNETS*
>
>                                     Effecting direct democracy at the
>                                 scale of the global population
>                                     requires that the decision system
>                                 have an affordable sub-system
>                                     that reliably notifies each member
>                                 of new proposals that may be of
>                                     interest, and that avoids
>                                 inundating individuals with new
>                                     proposals not of interest. The
>                                 details of the software and
>                                     practices that will together
>                                 effect efficient and reliable global
>                                     notification will be co-created
>                                 and evolved through successive
>                                     successful proposing.
>
>                                  6. *APPLYING PAST DECISIONS: APPRAISAL*
>
>                                     In order for the decision system
>                                 to be affordable and reliable at
>                                     the scale of the global
>                                 population, it must have a sub-system that
>                                     efficiently and reliably applies
>                                 all relevant past decisions to
>                                     each new proposal that is
>                                 submitted. (The past decisions serve as
>                                     filters; each new proposal must
>                                 successfully "pass through" all
>                                     such filters in order to be
>                                 cleared for implementation.) The
>                                     details of the software and
>                                 practices that will together effect
>                                     this will be co-created and
>                                 evolved through successive successful
>                                     proposing.
>
>                                  7. *AMENDMENTS: THROUGH PROPOSING*
>
>                                     Additions and amendments to these
>                                 bylaws may be effected through
>                                     successfully proposing.
>
