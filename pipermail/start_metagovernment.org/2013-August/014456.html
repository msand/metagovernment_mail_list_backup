Or to put it more generally in a way that might not be too popular on this
mailinglist:
1. Someone who puts lots of effort into metagovernment for several years
vs.
2. Someone who just joined the mailinglist recently and starts to talk a lot

Will their "votes" both have the same weight? ...like maybe on real issues.
suppose there is already donations to metagovernment or people should
invest their own work/time/ressources into something. why should they? what
about free riders?
There is so many problems which exist because there is no mechanism that
can reward those who do stuff. how to assess what people do? who should
asses it and in which way?
how to share any cake that might get created? will everyone get the same no
matter how much effort he or she has put into something? will there be
shares? will someone decide upon a "wage"? will all decide? will it become
beaurocratic???

i believe we have to discuss this issue and decide upon it in order to make
outcome more predictable. *Assume i put in 40hours a week or 60hours a week
into metagovernment.
i would have to be a very rich person, because otherwise, how would i pay
my bills?*
Even if i believe in the metagovernment vision, how can i ever put in all
my effort into it?
Currently, those who do put effort into it have to "write it off"
immediately and need to be intrinsically motivated all the way and do it
for idealistic reasons and a kind of hope, that one day the effort might
make a difference....

...but only very few people can operate this way, most people (mainstream)
will never do it and some of them (with very egocentric tendencies) might
even frown upon it.

The metagovernment vision is way to important to put it on such a sandily
foundation.
But in order to change that, without corrupting the vision, we have to
decide upon a mechanism of how "the cake is shared".
Manye on this list might avoid this question as good as they can, but this
must be solved if it should ever be successful.

Some startups grow from 0 to 500 workers in under a year and set new trends
that revolutionize a certain domain of life for a certain "target group" of
customers.

Put it differently:
Why can 500 freelancers not "over night" create something that could
"compete" with a company with 500 workers? Its both 500 skilled people, but
the degree of organization (network of processes) is much better within the
company, i believe thats the main reason.

how to enable people to set up complex work processes on the fly to
efficiently produce resulst? people need to pay bills, people only can
spend their time ONCE!

this issue is not tackled by metagovernment, but i feel its vital and needs
to be solved!
Everything else will fall in place if this issue is solved, because its
possible to center all features of a software around this foundation, but
its avoided.
