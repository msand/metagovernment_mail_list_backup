I've started a new thread on this as I think it is different from the
conceptual discussion on consensus into a technology platform discussion.

 Alexander, please correct me if I misunderstood but I believe you are
suggesting that we should use email as the standard for most collaboration.

I disagree:

I was proposing a prototype which has no bearing on the final
technology platform. The criteria for choosing a prototype is ease of
development and flexibility because it would all be re-written for
production. To try and create an email service for a prototype would
increase the difficulty and reduce the speed of exploration.

I have developed email services before but I can not think of any way
this type of structure could be implemented completely in email. If
you would provide a description of what you want I will have a look to
see if I can do it.

I have not used listserv (or similar) software for years.

No one I now in real life uses listserve (or similar) software currently.

My son tells me that none of his peers use email anymore except when forced to.

My work is transitioning to online websites for most of the
collaboration. Email will always be used for some communication where
it serves best.

The majority of information and interaction for the people I know
(regardless of  technical aptitude) is on Facebook, Twitter, Blogs,
search engines, StackExchanges and services. They use email mostly for
one way communication from an organization or periodic two way
communication between small groups or individuals.

most applications today use email at some point and so would all my
proposed designs. Email has it's use and it's place but I disagree
that it should be the main collaboration method.

I do not see the value in limiting our exploration of a new path for
government to a single technology. Thousands of attempts will fail but
if one works then we have an improvement.

I would like to hear the opinions of the others on this list. Is
Alexander's opinion a majority?
