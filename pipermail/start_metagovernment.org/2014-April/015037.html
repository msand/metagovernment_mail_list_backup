Scott Raney,

"The best we can do is to design a system that supports filtering that
> stuff out of the feed"

I wouldn't limit us to that one solution. Your statements read like you are
not open to other options.


"my second issue, which is the use of the term "facts" and "truth" in
> settleit.org and other debate systems."

To my knowledge you won't find the words 'fact' or 'truth' on the
SettleIt.org website. Everything is a statement which can be debated.


"In the abortion debate, one of the "facts" is that the Bible says it's
> wrong."

In your example of the abortion debate, one of the statements is that the
Bible says it's wrong. In SettleIt.org under that statement people would be
debating that the Bible does not say that abortion is wrong and others will
debate the weight of the bible on the abortion debate which includes if
someone should believe in the Bible.

I believe one of the major reasons for constant disagreement and inaction
is the tendency for people to disregard others concerns instead of
addressing and resolving those differences. If you just filter
out religious statements then you are not engaging with a large section of
the group. You can't make a group decision by ignoring the concerns of
others in the group. You can use the religious filter to realize and
express the reasons for your current opinion but it does not serve the
group. Obvious nonsensical and destructive statements should probably be
filtered out but it will take time to determine those standards.


"editing other people's stated opinions just feels 'icky'."

I will work on making this more clear and answering it here is helping me
work through the text.  SettleIt.org is not a place for personal opinions.
Entering a statement is stating that this is a reason to believe or not
believe in another statement. It is assume to be reasonably shared by
multiple people and could be expressed in several ways with some ways being
clearer than others. All statements are an attempt to be a fact but the
extent to which they are true can always be debated.

Regards,
Bentley
