> C) I think I understand the fine distinction you're making about consensus
> vs. majority rule. At the same time, the technology I've been developing is
> largely focused on ranked choice voting tools. So I presume you'd be eager
> to invoke Arrow to say I'm wasting my time on an unreliable approach. My
> answer is that I'm primarily concerned with how communities of people can
> improve the thinking and talking activities that precede choosing. In order

Again, I agree that this is a good component of the process, but in
the end you've simply *got* to have a majority-rule vote.  If your
process works well that vote will merely be a formality and you'll
have a large supermajority decision.  But if not, contentious
government is better than no government (i.e., this is encoded in
business philosophy as "an imperfect decision is always better than no
decision".
