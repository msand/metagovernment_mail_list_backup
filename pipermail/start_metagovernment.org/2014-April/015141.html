If I may present a potential scenario:


- We all assume there will be an increase in activity from the general
public in politics with this new system in place, as individuals would have
a greater say.


-We also have to assume a drop-off in participation as the proposal goes
down the line. That is to say more people will vote on it at its final
stage then will help craft it.


- This creates small groups of advocates that could craft crazy proposals
and gain some interest for their cause. Whatever interest they generate
works against them though as their proposal becomes more popular, good
citizens of the world will begin to notice more and start to investigate
their potential weaknesses. This exposes any hidden agenda or truths as the
debate carries on.


- I surmise that a proposal should only go forward to a voting stage with
at least a 51% participation rate of all active members on the site. This
should be a difficult thing to do and will require sustained interest in
the proposal guaranteeing it remains near the top of the site to ensure it
gets seen.


- Participation does not necessary include making a post, as many users can
not be bothered to research a strong counter argument. Participation can
simply be reading the proposal a bit finding an opinion that reflects their
view on the issue and up voting that post. This up vote increases the
weight of that post in the discussion and makes their opinion a larger part
of the decision, exposing it as well to possible weaknesses.


- This process creates our pros and cons list that can be drafted and
presented in a simplified way to then move on to the voting stage where
still more of the public will be involved. A majority of 2/3 of the votes
take it. Everything is open and transparent the entire time.


- Participation is voluntary but there can be penalties for incorrectly
participating in the discussion. You can never be penalized for up voting
or voting. This limits input without limiting quality.


Any reason why this wouldn't work?
