I would be worried about this problem.

http://xkcd.com/927/
And as far as the plan goes. It would be very hard to iterate quick cycles
and build the thing with public/crowd source funding alone. In the end you
talk about how you could accelerate features faster and innovate at a
quicker pace than existing social networks because you would not be as tied
to the secrecy that those social networks require. They spend billions of
dollars on those things for a reason, It's not easy to innovate. It takes
smart people. and smart people are expensive.  And, yes they have to make
money on them because sustainability is impossible without income(time +
money). Who is going to host the servers? Process the Data? Pay the
janitors of the data centers?

Let's operate on the assumption that your idea is great and survives it's
kick-starter campaign to fruition and people start using it. What is the
plan for sustainability? I agree with the "If you build it. They won't
come" idea mentioned before. It will be a hard grind to get something
working that as you mention will have a lot of kickback from the
authority/power at hand.

I also agree we should focus on finding people who want to use it. In small
communities or organization for example.

I think that a kickstarter to get money to have dedicated resources to find
and help the adoption of current systems in order to find out what works
and doesn't would be really effective. Although since it is not a product
kickstarter might not be the right platform. You get the idea though.

I think it is hard when you are building this for "people" as in for a
undefined group idea, compared to developing it for a Person, or some group
of people that already exist.
