Very interesting thread. Im also of mixed opinions an thoughts having been
a lurker on this list a long time, and having tried myslef and watch other
idealistic projects crash and burn.

My two main observations as the reasons for failure are

A) the herding cats problem. Scott, i am imterested eager even to read what
you have written, yet the problem of advanced democracy not unlike
the climate change problem is oversolved and underimplemented, mainly
becuse the only thing people will do for free is advance their own pet idea
or project. It is dual layered collective action problem to use econ speak.
Even though rverybody benefits there are Too many choices what to do and if
to do it. The only way such a problem gets fixed is massive authoritarian
intervention it massive public groundswell. There are good reasons to
beleive that neither will ever happen in this case. Hard problem.

B) if you build it they dont come. The population of people who desire
advanced democracy is about 3% of the population. The other 97% is i have
to say, just dumb about this issue. As a species we tend to be
authoritarian boneheads. Also hard problem.

System wide change is then so unlikely no one seriously attempts it. Thus
it falls to the hobbists.
The hobbists could achive something meaningful if they boned up on the
utopian communities movement, joined up in gangs of about thirty, evolved a
common bushido for the new century, and started to take over small dying
towns of about a population of 500. Succed at that level and you can level
up from there.
