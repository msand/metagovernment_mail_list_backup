>>
>> The Airesis.EU project will deliver an E-democracy platform beyond state
>> of the art that will set the beginning of a new era in government approach.
>>
>> The final ambition of Airesis.EU is to serve as the open source global
>> standard of deliberative social networks.
>>
>> We have the strongest motivation and the highest ethical reasons, we are
>> the answer to this call in a broader sense rather that to the specific
>> requests. As a matter of fact, most of the core actions are answered by the
>> values we share, more than by the functions of the platform.
>>
>> The vision and the mission of Airesis.EU is to have an open government,
>> the work put by the association Tecnologie Democratiche in to the Airesis
>> platform focus on participation as the only way to realize an open
>> government. To delegate unconditionally a group of people to take all
>> decision is not anymore a viable option because we cannot accept the risk
>> of their mistakes.
>>
>> Our leader is the search for consensus, his force, collective
>> intelligence. We share the same values with the partners of our consortium
>> which ultimate aim is not only to provide evidences of affordable
>> participatory budget proposals but for the practical use of such tools and
>> suggestions on how to implement them. Our French partner Citoyen du Monde
>> will manage  France’s administrations that have signed up for the pilot as
>> commitment to the scope of the call, but as global organization in
>> accordance with the Charter Globalization (1949, Ed.1984-Updated 2004)¹
>>  they will validate Airesis.EU as a legal process in Recim² / People
>> congress and Assembly and legitimate Open Government with UN organisations
>> - People Congress³ and UN Geneva⁴.
>>
>> We came across a number of software platform that are planning to do
>> exactly what Airesis is
>>
>> doing⁵. Most of them are at the drawing board, some are at the
>> programming stage, some are online but are not curated and look abandoned,
>> and we see a number that are addressing single specific issues⁶,  these
>> plaforms are limited in that they don't allow users to create groups and
>> develop organically around issues of concern and interest. There are
>> participatory budgeting software that are developed to be offered to
>> municipalities for a fees but are used for collecting ideas and give
>> general indications, not real decision taking⁷.
>>
>> The most successful examples of real participation is achieved by
>> participatory budgeting projects carried at the local level by ONG  with
>> rare use of web based platform.
>>
>> Our project is unique because start from the well established
>> deliberative model that implement Schulze⁸ algoritm this will be used by
>> the participative budgeting  module, the whole system will interact with a
>> full set of open API with any other platform willing to integrate with the
>> system. We open Airesis.EU to be use by other platform to work in a
>> synergy by consolidating the citizen effort under a single integrated
>> platform.
>>
>> The Airesis.EU project will therefore constitute a harmonic and
>> complementary  investigation into the technical and operative aspects  of
>> ICT tools, from the most advanced collective intelligence theories  as well
>> as  the political, legal and sociological theories and implications  that
>> derive from them, all of which impact  markedly upon  e-democracy and
>> e-government.  The Airesis.EU project aims at  providing a comprehensive
>>  exploration of legal, social and technical factors and, by critically
>> examining  the legal and ethical pros and cons of certain tools and/or
>> technologies, at  assessing the full picture of  their significance and
>> impact.
>>
>> We believe that if is used and promoted as a means of seeking consensus
>> in areas of the world that are under political tensions or as
>> transformative scenario planning tool where major and important decision
>> need to be taken Airesis.EU could give the best results.
