Juan,

We were able to cooperate with the municipal authorities because we use a
cultural principles that we call "Satyagraha", in Hebrew we call it "stick
to the truth". It is roots are in Ghandi civil right struggle, but it is
also have deep roots in Judaism, and if I understand correctly, it is also
one of the main themes of Christianity.
It's main principals are as follow:

   1. the people are the Sovereign of the state or city, the government is
   their servant, and are elected to serve the people. usually the government
   is let to take it course, but if the people are not satisfied, they have the
   right to take things back to their hands again. it could be also in minor
   concerns.
   2. We are all Human-beings. We love each other and appreciate one
   another. we talk with respect to each other, and we collaborate. between the
   community and with the people in government.
   3. The main goal of the community is not politic, but the cultural life
   of the community. of being friends and helping each other.
   4. All are equal in every meeting, and in life
   5. We help to build a culture of friends.
   6. We will try to avoid a fight, and take every nescery actions to get to
   our goles without a fight. only if the government is acting not truly, and
   with deception, that the anger of the community will rise, and hard measures
   will be taken (it happened to us only once, the problem was solved in a
   day).


These ideas make the community  very strong and flourishing. when a
community works on these ideas, it is very strong, the government official
likes to work with the community. they enjoy the friendship of the people.

Of course the we works with other features of direct-democracy, like
meetings, forums, groups of action, general meetings etc.

Tal

>> My name is Tal, and I am from the Israeli direct democracy movement. I
>> would like to introduce to you our projects and area of intrest. I can
>> elaborate more on projects and topics that you will find interesting.
>>
>> We started our group in January 2008. we have done research on the
>> problems of represntetives democracy and tryed to get femiliar with diffrent
>> stiles of direct democracy (In Athen, Switzerland the USA, and anarchists
>> groups) , and we sarched for ways to bring direct democracy.
>>
>> We found out the ways and reasons for the control of unselcted oligarchies
>> on the represntatives democracy. and then we strated to think how can we
>> take from them the power, in install it in the people.
>>
>> we found out that the main reason for oligarchic control is because small
>> groups with allot of resources can coordinate themselves and take collective
>> action much easily then large groups without extra resources.
>>
>> We therefore set several initiative that will enable the people to act
>> together much better.
>> 1. We have one project that is called *Ecllesia <http://bit.ly/ecclesia> (in
>> development)*, which is built by the Django community in Israel. It
>> should be an extended facebook, which will let huge groups to debate, and
>> find out way to cooperate as a hall. it is based on a method of arguing,
>> that will enable large audiences to get in easy way to an agreement.
>> 2. Another project is Called OpenKnesst, which will be much like the
>> TheyWorkForYou project. It's aim is to make the parliamentary work much more
>> transparent. and will let the citizens what their MPs are really doing :-)
>> This we hope will make the MPs more obligate to the public that selected
>> them.
>> 3. WebActivism (webactivism.info) - it is a group of people that created
>> web2.0 based campaigns. it will help new people run there own campaigns. we
>> hope though to make the the public more powerful in meaningful and
>> influential campaigns.
>> 4. a group for advisors for democracy. The aim of the group is to help
>> communities get organize, and work democratically. it advise them how to
>> influence the local government, and how to create a democratic culture. we
>> believe that this will create the cultural base for real grass-root
>> democracy. We have done allot of research in this area, and we are now
>> experimenting in a real communities. so far it is going quite nicely and
>> have a good influence on the local government.
>>
>> We have more projects in our mind, which we think can strengthen the
>> citizens, but some had failed and some we didn't get to them yet.
>>
>> If you have any questions, or disagreement, please let me know.
