FWIW, Metagovernment started as a top-down sort of structure. See for  
example the "Levels of Government" section in this early draft:
http://www.metagovernment.org/archive/2007-03-20/

As more people got involved, it became clear that one person's  
taxonomy was never going to be as robust as a crowdsourced one.  
Further, consensus in developing a rigid taxonomy seemed less  
adaptable to an ever-growing group and an ever-expanding realm of  
control. Much better, it seemed, to let the taxonomy grow organically  
as people chose to make it happen.

The latter can be much more difficult to deal with at first. But in  
the end, IMHO it saves a lot of headaches and makes for a much more  
robust categorization. As an example, look at Wikipedia's category  
system, which does fairly well despite having to deal with nearly  
three million articles in the English version.
