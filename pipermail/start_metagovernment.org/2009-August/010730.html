For a conference, we should provide a significant amount of lead-time.  
Many conferences are planned six months to a year in advance. In part,  
this is to allow for travel plans to be made, which we do not have to  
account for.

But it is still worthwhile to have more than a month of lead time to  
allow people to block off the time in their calendar. Having more than  
a month also gives us more time to promote and publicize it. And for a  
format like I was suggesting (where presentations are prepared in  
advance), we certainly would need to give the presenters more warning.

However, we certainly can have a meeting before then. Note Matias has  
updated the as-yet-unscheduled Meeting 3 page:
http://metagovernment.org/wiki/Startup/Meetings/3
