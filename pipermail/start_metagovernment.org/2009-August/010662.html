inherently flawed. I use to discuss this for a long time. Now I don't
to avoid "wasting my time". ;-)

> We fully concur that the need to complete forms etc to participate in
> processes is not optimal and it is as a result of this that we are in the
> process of working towards the development of a distributed architecture
> that would eliminate this need. Take a quick look through the recent
> discussion threads and you'll see that we are working through the nitty
> gritty. Unfortunately the issues are complex and the solution won't be
> developed without a coordinated effort that will take some time.
> The architectural methodology that is evolving for the platform that we will
> develop will not involve very robust meta structure about organizations and
> projects in the way that you are working on the problem. We are working more
> on creating an open information and interaction space that will be defined
> by certain syntactic structures that facilitate communication and
> interaction, and where the community of practice makes the links.
> As you will see if you take a close look at the documentation, I have been
> quite vigorously defending the need for top-down, hierarchical ontology in
> order to satisfy our the requirements of the OK Community of Practice. My
> argument can be found
> at: http://wiki.openkollab.com/wagn/OK_Ontological_Considerations
> On the flip side, there has been a strong push to think about a completely
> different way of information processing that does not categorize information
> from the top-down in a hierarchical manner. This argument is best summarized
> by Clay Shirky in the following
> piece: http://www.shirky.com/writings/ontology_overrated.html
> The most recent proposal has been advanced by Tav. Details can be found
> at: http://wiki.openkollab.com/wagn/Tent_as_advanced_by_Tav_on_Aug_14_2009
> The discussion continues...
> Given the direction things are going it is unlikely that we will adopt a
> framework with a robust top-down structure similar to the framework that
> StratML is developing.
