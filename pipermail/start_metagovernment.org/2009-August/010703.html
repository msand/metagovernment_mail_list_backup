Pietro, since I live in the area and could attend the Gov 2.0 conference
without travel expense, I requested an "invitation" without knowing about
the substantial registration cost.  I don't begrudge anyone trying to make a
living.  Indeed, one way or another, we all must do so, and better that it
be through direct, open, publicly "advertised" sources than through
indirect, unclear, if not necessarily "secretive" means (e.g., tax
subsidies).  However, I did feel a sense of being baited-and-switched, which
leaves me a little more skeptical of the sponsors than I was before.  I also
tend to share your view of this being sort of a "closed club".  I have
nothing against such clubs.  Indeed, I believe we have the right to form any
associations we wish.  However, as a retired .gov employee, I do believe
those who are on the public payroll (paid with tax revenues extracted by
coercion) have an obligation to share with the public any information
exchanged at such events.  If they don't feel comfortable doing so, they
should not be participating in such events, and if we continue to allow them
to get away with it, we will have no one to blame but ourselves.

Owen
