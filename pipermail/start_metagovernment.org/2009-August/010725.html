On Aug 29, 2009, at 5:06 PM, Owen Ambur wrote:

> While anyone should be free to "gather" in the same place and/or at  
> the same
> time as others if they wish, no one should be precluded from  
> participating
> due to artificial constraints based upon time or space.
>
> Thus, it would be good if the conference could be scheduled over a  
> span of
> time, e.g., at least 24 hours, if not several days, and participants  
> should
> be free to contribute asynchronously, using any means *they* prefer,
> preferably but not necessarily on the Web.

I have been thinking along these lines, and it seems that the only  
solution is to reinvent the standard conference format somewhat.

The traditional conference has a speaker (panel, group, etc.) present  
a topic at a certain time and place, and then there is often a Q&A  
after that.

I suggest that we have presenters prepare a relatively short video in  
advance. We than post all the videos to a website by a certain time.  
Anyone would then have a day or two to review the videos they want to  
watch and write in questions. Presenters would then appear live at two  
different times (about 12 hours apart... or maybe 36 hours apart) to  
reply to those questions and also to live follow-ups and new live  
questions. These additional sessions would also be archived for later  
viewing.

This raises some technological issues (especially getting speakers to  
be able to produce and encode videos) and also potentially financial  
issues, but those should be surmountable.

In addition to the above, we would also want a free-form chat  
venue(s). It would be great if we could do that in audio or video, but  
text might be more realistic.

> In my view, it would be good if such contributions could be more
> specifically focused than on a general title and "theme", i.e., upon  
> a set
> of longer-term goals and nearer-term objectives proposed by the  
> conference
> organizers.  (Participants should be free to propose other goals/ 
> objectives
> if they wish.)

It might be most reasonable and sufficient for us simply to ask each  
member project (along with new member projects, hopefully) to present  
their project. I think it would be beneficial to all of us and to a  
lot of other people to have such expositions of each project in a  
unified venue.
