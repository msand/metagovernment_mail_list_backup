>
>
> I agree. The viewpoint of the Metagovernment project is more of an
> end-state, not of a something to be injected into the status quo right now.
> Our bottom-up approach is a way of gradually transforming governance around
> the world from the smallest forms and spreading outward from there.
>
> None of us are under the illusion that we are a short-term goal for
> large-scale democracy efforts. Until we have built up some credence in small
> communities and built more mindshare as a workable solution for larger
> groups, it is entirely reasonable (and necessary) that others continue to
> work on other approaches to increasing democracy.
>
> The definition of Metagovernment at this page<http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Open_source_governance>
>  is particularly restrictive: "Open governance project aimed at replacing
> all governments with scored websites."
>
>
> Just to clarify, that page is on Wikipedia <
> http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Open_source_governance> and is not

> time. Our own definition of Open Source Governance is here:
> http://metagovernment.org/wiki/Open_source_governance
> and a better description of our intentions is here, in the "Transition"
> section of our home page:
