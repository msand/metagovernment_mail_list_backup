Hi Michael, Welcome to the list.

> I see folks are tangling on the website with the concepts of user ranking
> and scoring.  Perhaps this is out of scope, but has it been suggested or
> discussed of having a "soft security" (human based) such that individuals
> (and groups of individuals) invite and vouch for other individuals (and are
> to some degree accountable). Scoring and karma could then accrue to groups
> rather than individuals. Individuals would desire to be from a "reputable"
> group . ..  .

I'm not sure anything's been discussed in much depth, or with a
critical eye.  There's a bunch of different projects here, and maybe
only one of them has directly tackled the problem of authentication
(I'm thinking of Votorola's neighbourhood trust network).
 
> Sock-puppeting is very hard to counteract but if individuals are required to
> form/join social groups who have a collective reputation, there is a
> reasonable chance for self-organizing governance, self policing and
> distributed ID verification.

The problem would then fall to the social groups, with each group
developing its own solution.  But if a group is larger than a few
dozen people, then whatever method it developed could not be 100%
soft.  It would require the support of an administrative or technical
backbone.  In that case, it would be likely that the best method could
be exported as a universal solution, to be used by everyone.

Do you have a particular method in mind?
