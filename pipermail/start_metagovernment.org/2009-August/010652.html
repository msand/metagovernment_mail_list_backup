> As you know, the OpenKollab project is aiming, among other things,
> to identify specific collaboration opportunities by matching
> projects together on the basis of the information that we are
> collecting. In Phae I of our plan is to post this information in an
> open space: ... http://wiki.openkollab.com/wagn/ProjectInfo. In
> doing so and in actively marketing the the fact that we are doing
> this, we hope to collect data on roughly 100 projects/smaller
> organizations.

BTW, here's another project list being compiled for the domain of
online participation and e-democracy:
http://lists.thataway.org/scripts/wa-THATAWAY.exe?A2=ind0908B&L=NCDD-DISCUSSION&P=15644

> Phase II of the project will then involve manually reviewing the data and
> mapping organizations together to the best of our ability. In this process
> we hope to identify the information that is relevant to actually match
> organizations to each other.

It would be really great if you could focus on e-democracy, because
we'd really like to see the phase II information, as soon as
possible. :)

> In Phase III of the project, we will modify the questionnaire as well as try
> to automate, in the platform, the matching of projects to each other. We
> will also then try to roll the idea out more widely and get a larger number
> of projects on the platform.

I imagine it would be difficult to automate, except by something like
a mechanical Turk.  The mappings will require intelligence and domain
knowledge.  Not only the mappings, but also the integrity of the data
will require human oversight.  If it's just a dumb machine, it will be
incessantly spammed.  (No doubt there's enough interest too, to power
a Turk - so no problem.)

It seems the most immediate requirement is for project and
organization lists that have timely, authenticated data - and a means
of keeping them that way.  A standard toolset and process for doing
that would be a big step forward in itself.  We don't have that yet.
It's a necessary preliminary too, to any kind of mapping effort
(whether automated or manual).

(Maybe my comments are off base.  I don't have the full picture yet.
 I'm on your list, so I'll wait to learn more.)
