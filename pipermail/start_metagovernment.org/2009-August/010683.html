On Aug 17, 2009, at 5:53 AM, James Sadri wrote:

> While I see that as an important area of innovation, with positive  
> implications for direct democracy efforts, I don't think at  
> GlobalDemo.org we could reduce all the global democracy efforts to  
> this level.

I agree. The viewpoint of the Metagovernment project is more of an end- 
state, not of a something to be injected into the status quo right  
now. Our bottom-up approach is a way of gradually transforming  
governance around the world from the smallest forms and spreading  
outward from there.

None of us are under the illusion that we are a short-term goal for  
large-scale democracy efforts. Until we have built up some credence in  
small communities and built more mindshare as a workable solution for  
larger groups, it is entirely reasonable (and necessary) that others  
continue to work on other approaches to increasing democracy.
