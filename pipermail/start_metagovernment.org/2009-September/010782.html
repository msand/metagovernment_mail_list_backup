oh yes, I agree with that (tricking the system should not be permitted).
In our proposed application "draft2gether"
(http://www.telematicsfreedom.org/en/projects/draft2gether-0) we propose
that the user - after a "x" number of comments, ratings and amends done
to one draft text, is mandated to rate "n" random other amends before
getting the right to continue participating. Also, we thought about an
algorithm that gets the length of a given amend/comment and calculates a
number of seconds needed before the user is really allowed to rate it
(ie.: based on configurable average reading speeds - ie. 400wpm for
skimming
http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Reading_%28process%29#Reading_rate). This
could be a better approach in terms of usability. What do you think?
