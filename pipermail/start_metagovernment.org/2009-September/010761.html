Here are a couple items that caught my attention in the early reports coming
out of the Gov 2.0 conference:

 

The Limitations of Gov 2.0.

Could it be that many of the people most likely to be affected by the
suggestions . aren't even participating in the conversation?

http://www.prospect.org/csnc/blogs/tapped_archive?month=09
<http://www.prospect.org/csnc/blogs/tapped_archive?month=09&year=2009&base_n
ame=the_limitations_of_gov_20>
&year=2009&base_name=the_limitations_of_gov_20 

 

That question feeds my bias that the focus should be on what we ourselves
can do together, rather than what we'd like to try to force others to do.

 

                Gov 2.0: Open Government Directive Nearly Ready

. there must be a structured approach to open government, including a
schedule for the public release of government data in machine-readable
formats.

. government agencies should engage the public in developing open government
plans and publish their completed plans.

http://www.informationweek.com/news/government/policy/showArticle.jhtml?arti
cleID=219700334
<http://www.informationweek.com/news/government/policy/showArticle.jhtml?art
icleID=219700334&cid=RSSfeed_IWK_ALL> &cid=RSSfeed_IWK_ALL

 

StratML is a structured approach for releasing the data contained in
strategic and performance plans in machine-readable format  It would be neat
if the applications being developed in alignment with the mission of Metagov
could be used to facilitate engagement and feedback from the public on the
open government plans drafted by agencies (which should be published in
StratML format).
