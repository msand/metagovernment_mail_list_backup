Hi Ed,

>> In those projects they took a statistically representative sample of
>> the population.
>
> If it's a statistically representative sample, then isn't it necessarily
> "representative" democracy? :)

In a different way:
When the population vote they don't vote people similar to them. This
is why the senate, and the camera are not samples of the population.
Women are underrepresented, as are poor people and people from racial
minorities.
Also as working in the senate is a full time job, people soon forget
where they were coming from, as they identify with their new role.

The idea behind representative democracy is to find the best people
that represent everybody.
But if you are going to train those people, then you don't need to
select on knowledge, but can select on the fact that they really
represent the populations statistically.

It is a different system
