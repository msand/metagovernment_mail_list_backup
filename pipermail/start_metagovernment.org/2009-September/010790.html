Hi,

I also like the Votorola system.

Yet when we compered the Switz democracy with  the California democracy, our
group noticed that a more healthy democracy emerge in places in which
citizens are more involved. Therefore we are searching for ways to enhance
participation.




2009/9/21 Ed Pastore <epastore at metagovernment.org>

> On Sep 20, 2009, at 3:22 PM, Michael Pilling wrote:
>
>  But why do so many direct democracy attempts fail? Lack of
>> participation. My main reflection on that is that most humans are not
>> wired for direct democracy, and don't care or have time for all the
>> details. Sure some people swing that way but most people most of the
>> time tend to pick somebody they like and follow.
>>
>
> Votorola is a great solution to this, but the other approaches have their
> value as well. Direct democracy can allow for delegation without necessarily
> having a structured system like Votorola. It can be completely unstructured:
> where people agree to let others make the decisions simply by their choice
> not to participate at this time.
>
> There are benefits and drawbacks to either approach, which is why it's
> great that we have both approaches involved here and we can see how they
> play out in comparison to each other. I sincerely expect (hope?) that
> communities will try several forms of governance software before picking the
> one that works best for their membership.
>
