Good analogy, Michael, and thanks for bringing "barnstars" to our attention.
>From my perspective, the problem with them is that they are proprietary to
Wikipedia and other "stovepipe" systems that use them.   It would be better
if they could be posted anywhere on the Web in standard (XML) format for
aggregation, indexing, analysis and use for the benefit of various
communities of interest.  

 

While I have not taken the time or trouble to try to "institutionalize" that
thought, I did document it quite a long time ago (2001):
http://ambur.net/WildXML.html#XMLkudos  I'd also add that it is better and
more appropriate to commend the content itself, with kudos accruing to the
authors and contributors indirectly as a result of the records they create
and share, rather than through some more mysterious assessment of their
"personalities".  (Not all witches are bad, nor are the bad ones bad all the
time.)
