Hey Philippe, you are surely not alone in this. I'm also favorable to
the idea that e-dem software works only if it is fully open and
transparent (going further, not only the software but the entire
telematic communication - see our battle at
http://www.telematicsfreedom.org/en/projects/user-verifiable-telematics)

Now, regarding metagov and its integration with other, closed projects:

On 27/01/2010 18.44, Ed Pastore wrote:
> .... but I would be leery of including them in free-range voting,
> since their output may be skewed. At the very least, I would encourage
> the FOS projects to put tags on their output, flagging it as
> unverifiable.

Man, that is just awesome! The fact that closed solutions can integrate
with our (future?) standard but be marked as "unverifiable" or
"untrusted" is a fantastic idea. It communicates the fact that
transparency can be made only by using FOSS and at the same time leaves
choice to users that consiously want to (or are obliged to) use closed
solutions.
I'd just like to hear the members of closed projects that are on this
list about what they think of it...
