This is, by now, an old conversation. But, I am just now catching up
on all the recent metagov activity. Sorry for the late response!

I wanted to bring the group's attention to mysociety.org 's source of
funding -- useful because it's a halfway-successful model

http://www.mysociety.org/

http://www.ukcod.org.uk/Structure

They have a few paid developers and many volunteers, supported in part
by a commercial-web-development arm (builds and designs
democracy-related websites for government, BBC, etc).

If, for example, metagov raised funds in this manner, a particular
project such as VGtA might not bring a profit on its own, but the
group learns from it. Those within the group who take on commercial
software development jobs benefit from the collective knowledge of the
group and have access to the developers of sister projects, so each
(such as VGtA) adds to overall sustainability whether it brings in
cash or not.

To clarify, I am certainly not advocating that we adopt this type of
structure, nor that we raise funds by soliciting commercial
development jobs. But the example has given me some ideas.

Jacki
