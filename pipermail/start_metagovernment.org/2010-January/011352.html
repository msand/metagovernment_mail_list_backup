I was going to write basically what Jacki wrote below, so I'll just  
elaborate a bit.

If commercial projects want to participate, we can't/(won't) stop  
them. This list is open to anyone who wishes to subscribe, as is the  
wiki.

However, if they choose to act on behalf of this project under the  
principle of adhocracy, then when doing so they implicitly must uphold  
at least our first two basic principles.

It might be nice to have http://policypitch.com join our community,  
but I would not be willing to say that the product of their engine is  
trustworthy, since the source is decidedly closed. So for example,  
they could participate in our standardization effort (e.g., user  
portability), but I would be leery of including them in free-range  
voting, since their output may be skewed. At the very least, I would  
encourage the FOS projects to put tags on their output, flagging it as  
unverifiable.

But anyway, getting to the heart of the issue at hand; I think Jacki's  
summary is good as-is, and if TuxFamily doesn't like our non- 
commercial wiki despite our doing the blog without the NC, then we  
could just try some of the other sites Philippe listed earlier in the  
thread.
