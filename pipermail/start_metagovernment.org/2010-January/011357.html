Trustworthiness should be evaluated by an “authority” that itself has been
certified (and is routinely audited) as being capable of assessing
trustworthiness and is accepted as such by the community affected.  Any
system or record that has not been certified by such an authority is, by
definition, untrustworthy (except to the degree that we are inclined to
accept it because it reinforces our preconceived, albeit perhaps
ill-conceived, notions).

 

When we developed the first <http://et.gov/stage1.htm>  stage of the ET.gov
site <http://et.gov/> , we recognized that emerging technology components
and specifications should be tested and certified as conforming to the
applicable standards.  Those requirements are documented in the tabulation
at http://et.gov/history/enhancementrequirements.htm  Unfortunately, the
resources to build out the process never materialized.  However, at some
point, volunteers in a community (like Metagov?) could build it out and it
could come to be recognized as having legitimacy
<http://ambur.net/French&Raven.htm>  because it is in fact inherently
trustworthy (as least to the degree that truth can be known in this life, at
the current state of science).

I think we, as an organization, have the goal of furthering FOSS

If we agree on that as a goal, can we also agree that the best way to 
accomplish this would be to *include* non-FOSS projects in our 
discussions? And, in collaborations like 'Standardization', etc? 

Going FOSS to a commercial interest can be threatening; it is our job 
to demonstrate concrete benefits of releasing code. And the threat of 
'non-inclusion' in Metagov is, frankly, not a very big factor in their 
decisions. Much more persuasive will be the interaction with community 
members, where going OS can be beneficial in terms of cooperation, 
exchange of ideas, etc. 

I also think that, given that our second basic principle is to support 
transparency and FOSS, we can gain approval from projects like 
TuxFamily.org even though we may also agree to dialogue with non-FOSS 
projects. I don't think these are inconsistent. 

My two cents, anyways.
