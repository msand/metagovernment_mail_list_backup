We had something similar happening in a forum I participate in.

I don't know if I told the story. The old forum broke down, and we, the old
crowd, decided we wanted now to make a new forum, where we could decide how
it would work. We discussed all the details, and also the name. Among us was
a new person, we did not know very well. Before we finished deciding he
bought the domain name, installed a standard forum, and invited everybody
in. Calling it "our website". But he kept all control. When we discussed
this further, the community has already settled. It was starting to grow. We
tried to set up a concurrent community, but the community would only visit
it in the week-ends. And eventually it died.

It was a great lesson.

After some years this guy lost interest in the topic, so he defined a few
low level administrators to policy the system, went to live in Africa, and
we pretty much lost all tracks of him. The board remained there, with its
google adsense, request for donations when you sign in, and is now the top
forum in its topic.

Forking only makes sense if you have some really good novelty.
