Aren't we limiting ourselves ahead of time.

Yes it's true that transparency is important. But I would not subscribe that
our community will only, and always have only FOSS projects as members.

What if tomorrow MixedInk wants to join us.
Think how important the exchange would be.
Both technically and strategically.
Should we close the door on their face because they are not FOSS?
And WhiteHouse2 was not open source when joined, nor for some time was
Vilfredo.
In fact what if we receive some money from the EU to develop the community
software. We already have found how difficult was to dialog between our
community, and the external, non FOSS, community.

I think we should be inclusive, and not exclusive, so I now question the
choice of TuxFamily.

Are we sure there isn't another alternative.

Maybe the idea of wordpress.com isn't such a bad idea, after all.
