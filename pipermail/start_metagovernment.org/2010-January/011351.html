I think we, as an organization, have the goal of furthering FOSS
software, and encouraging useful tools to release code and license
using a FOSS license.

If we agree on that as a goal, can we also agree that the best way to
accomplish this would be to *include* non-FOSS projects in our
discussions? And, in collaborations like 'Standardization', etc?

Going FOSS to a commercial interest can be threatening; it is our job
to demonstrate concrete benefits of releasing code. And the threat of
'non-inclusion' in Metagov is, frankly, not a very big factor in their
decisions. Much more persuasive will be the interaction with community
members, where going OS can be beneficial in terms of cooperation,
exchange of ideas, etc.

I also think that, given that our second basic principle is to support
transparency and FOSS, we can gain approval from projects like
TuxFamily.org even though we may also agree to dialogue with non-FOSS
projects. I don't think these are inconsistent.

My two cents, anyways.
