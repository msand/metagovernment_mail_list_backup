> * Backtracing of trust in the wiki.  So, if there's a problem, you
>   can navigate toward the root of the network, closer to registrars

I'm still caught up in a business trip and can't dig through all of  
this, but it looks very ambitious and yet very practical. The one part  
I'm most leery of is the above, where precedence is ultimately given  
to admins.

I'd like to reiterate my suggestion that administration (and indeed  
servers) should be distributed in order to prevent authoritarian  
tendencies (ie corruption) in admins.

Additionally, in terms of trust, I'd like to remind you of Manuel &  
Pietro's idea of an internal trust mechanism (sorry; can't dig up the  
reference from my phone). It basically uses spheres of influence to  
determine user authenticity.

As an example, if I set up a hundred sock puppets and have them all  
rate each other as trustworthy, the fact that all of their  
relationships are internal to their group (with no/few external  
relationships) would allow the software to essentially collapse them  
all into one person.
