My objective in applying is to address several of the things you bring  
up below.

It is very difficult for Metagovernment to seek any funding because we  
aren't an official organization. Apart from private donations, we are  
extremely unlikely to receive any other funding. Separately, I have  
had much, much less time to work on the project than I would like  
because of other demands on my time: much of them having to do with  
earning an income.

These particular fellowships are generally for one year. My personal  
aim in applying is to be financially free to work on the project full  
time. As part of my grant proposal, I am also proposing that I would  
spend a significant portion of my time working on finding the proper  
way to form an organization that is neither ruled by empowered  
individuals nor tied to any one nation. I believe from previous  
discussion on this list that Belgium would be the best place to effect  
that, so I am also proposing I spend some time there (there is an OSI  
office in Brussels) to potentially effect that.

If we could form an organization that would be officially organized  
*and* governed by our own "dogfood," we would not only be better  
positioned to progress, but we would also have made the most  
significant step: a real-world instance of institutionalized  
collaborative governance.

That's my aim. I think it would be great for others to make their own  
proposals on whatever grounds work for them. Receiving fellowships  
like this is always a long shot, but I think this one is worth  
considering.
