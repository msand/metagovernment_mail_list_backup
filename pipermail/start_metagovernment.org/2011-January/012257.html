Pietro, I agree completely that transparency and open source implementation
are crucial to the development of the right solution to our shared problem.

However, Spigit has beaten us to market, and now has Fortune 500 companies
and one of the world's premier cities as clients.  The $10m in funding
they've received is going to allow them to continue to rapidly develop new
features and maintain their market lead.  So, what does that mean for us?

I've come to think that the lifecycle of a citizen's engagement in an issue
is (crudely):

Problem >> Solution >> Decision >> Implementation

Spigit's very slick interface is geared towards identifying problems and
brainstorming solutions.  Decision making still rests in the hands of
traditional government or corporate authorities, though it can be informed
by the thinking of the community.  They provide no tools for implementation
of solutions, which presumably happen within the existing organizational
structures.  I think that if our collective work is to compete successfully
with a tool like Spigit, we'll need to deliver value in the areas of
Decision Making and Solution Implementation, which I know some of our
projects are working hard to accomplish.

I'll post more as the brain keeps working...
