I played around with it and even though I didnt manage to create a 
test-proposal, I think I understand the overall concept . It makes a 
very good impression.

I think in principle its similiar to Votorola. The only real difference 
I see  is, that DemoWave (as I understand it) puts all versions of 
proposals (parents, children and others) into a list whereas Votorola 
structures them into a tree (or forest of trees). The benefit of this 
tree-structuring is imo a better overview and division of labour, if the 
number of proposals rises very high. The tree forms through 
"communicative delegation", a combination of voting, delegating and 
drafting.

I made two pictures and a short text to explain this here (Please 
excuse, if the text sounds a bit rough. It emerged out of a very heated 
discussion.):

http://u.zelea.com/w/User:ThomasvonderElbe_GmxDe/Communicative_Delegation

I'm very curious to hear your opinion, especially since you are already 
into delegated voting.
