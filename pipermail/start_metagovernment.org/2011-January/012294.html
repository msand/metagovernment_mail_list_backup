Hello Jonas,

Welcome to our list!

It's always a good feeling, to meet likeminded people!

I read through the manifesto of your party and many things a very 
similiar to what we are doing with Votorola, especially the delegation. 
Now I'm particularily interested in what you call "Document evolution". 
Please foregive me, if I inadvertently missed the explanation, but my 
question is:

How does this evolution happen?

Like this?:

A proposal cannot be changed once it is published. Lets say it gets 
rejected. So someone publishes another proposal, which he feels might 
reach consensus. But it also gets rejected. And so on, until a good 
enough proposal emerges.

Now in a large scale implementation with millions of voters we would 
expect thousends of proposals in parallel for one single question, 
right? Does the evolution of documents just happen by all the people (or 
delegates) looking through and voting for or against all those proposals?

The concept behind Votorola is, that a proposal can change all the time, 
whenever there is a good reason (a large majority wants a change, a 
typo, or a good argument, ...) This way we expect to reduce the 
overwhelming amount of proposals to the necessary minimum.

Do you understand what I mean? Did I miss the point in your manifesto?

Greetings,
Thomas
