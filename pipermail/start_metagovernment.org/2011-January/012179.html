The Open Society Foundations' "about" statement is now in StratML format at
http://xml.gov/stratml/drybridge/index.htm#OSF or, more specifically,
http://xml.gov/stratml/carmel/OSFwStyle.xml

BTW, the tragic shooting of Rep. Gabrielle Giffords has highlighted once
again the threat not only to public officials but also to open lines of
communication with them.  No doubt, it will lead to more caution and greater
restrictions on F2F access to public official.  However, F2F communication
has always and will always be constrained by time and space anyway ... and
such constraints are artificial to the essential aspects of communication.  

Through the enlightened use of technology, greater openness and not merely
more but, more importantly, more efficient and effective communication with
elected officials can be achieved, unfettered by the constraints of time,
space, and the need for personal security.  

Of course, too, information technology can help reduce the need to defer our
own responsibilities to others, thereby granting them disproportionate power
and recognition, which in turn makes them targets for attack, literally as
well as figuratively.

Owen
