> BTW, I have been encouraging IRS to enable tax-exempt charitable
> organizations to document their missions, visions, values, goals,
> objectives, and stakeholders on their own websites in StratML format.  Section
> 10 <http://xml.gov/stratml/references/HR2142.htm#SEC10> of the new GPRA
> Modernization Act (GMA) requires U.S. federal agencies to publish their
> strategic and performance plans and reports in machine-readable formats
> (like StratML).  “Tax-expeditures” (such as deductions attributable to
> donations to qualified charities) are referenced five times in the Act.  So
> it will be necessary for agencies to estimate the amount of tax revenue not
> collected due to donations to charities pursuing objectives that complement
> those of the agencies themselves.  Rendering the plans of .gov agencies and
> tax-exempt charities in StratML format will facilitate compliance with this
> new provision of law.  The data requirements associated with the GMA will be
> prime topics of consideration in StratML Part 3<http://xml.gov/stratml/index.htm#Part3>
> .
