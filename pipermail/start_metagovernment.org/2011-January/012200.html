Thanks for the pointer, Alex.  The request is now in StratML format at
http://xml.gov/stratml/drybridge/index.htm#DOS or, more specifically,
http://xml.gov/stratml/carmel/IFPwStyle.xml 

 

While they are not all 501(c)(3) organizations, perhaps a qualifying
cosponsor might be found among those listed at
http://xml.gov/stratml/drybridge/index.htm#Nonprofits 

 

Among the purposes of the StratML standard is to facilitate the discovery of
potential performance partners:
http://xml.gov/stratml/index.htm#DefinitionPurposes  However, tools,
applications, and services are needed to leverage StratML documents in
support of those purposes.

 

BTW, I have been encouraging IRS to enable tax-exempt charitable
organizations to document their missions, visions, values, goals,
objectives, and stakeholders on their own websites in StratML format.
Section 10 <http://xml.gov/stratml/references/HR2142.htm#SEC10>  of the new
GPRA Modernization Act (GMA) requires U.S. federal agencies to publish their
strategic and performance plans and reports in machine-readable formats
(like StratML).  "Tax-expeditures" (such as deductions attributable to
donations to qualified charities) are referenced five times in the Act.  So
it will be necessary for agencies to estimate the amount of tax revenue not
collected due to donations to charities pursuing objectives that complement
those of the agencies themselves.  Rendering the plans of .gov agencies and
tax-exempt charities in StratML format will facilitate compliance with this
new provision of law.  The data requirements associated with the GMA will be
prime topics of consideration in StratML Part 3
<http://xml.gov/stratml/index.htm#Part3> .
