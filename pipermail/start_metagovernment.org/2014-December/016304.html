> You're with Ned here, accurate description of the problem, completely
> wrong analysis of the cause.  The tendency toward authoritarian
> solutions comes from our *leaders* and the fact that our systems allow
> certain types of people (in Matchism they're SDAPs (Social Dominators,
> Authoritarians, and Psychopaths)) to rise to positions of power.  It
> has nothing to do with our decision rules or whether our governments
> are local or global.  Designing a system, even a consent or consensus
> based system, that doesn't take this fundamental difference in
> individual behavior into account is doomed to failure.  We'll be far
> worse off with a million Hitlers each running their own city/state
> than we are now, and if you're system doesn't specifically constrain
> them, that's exactly what you're proposing (e.g., the "last man
> standing" outcomes in Vilfredo).
>
> > If we want to solve the problem of war this decade, we will fail. If we
> spend this decade training people to work together on problem-solving, war
> will eventually solve itself.
>
> "Local" solutions have been tried.  It's the warring-states period (in
> China), Spartans vs. Athenians, Feudalism, city-states in Italy (i.e.,
> Machiavelli), etc.  I think we can be pretty sure that what you
> propose is a recipe for global instability.  Unless maybe you propose
> to train *all* people the world over on these new systems
> simultaneously.  In which case I think it's a good idea, but then,
> it's a ground-up global government and not a local-oriented system as
> you're claiming it to be.  It also happens to be what Matchism is
> based on (i.e, a fundamental component of that is that we need to find
> a way to ensure that each of us considers every other human being to
