On Mon, Dec 22, 2014 at 10:34 AM, Michal Štěpánek
<michael.stakor at gmail.com> wrote:

> how many people support your Matchism ?

Well, considering it hasn't even been released, let alone promoted,
the number is of course insignificant now.  That's not an argument
against it.  To argue against it, you'd have to read it, understand
it, and find the flaws in it.  Otherwise it's just another case of
saying that human flight is impossible.

> I hope that the answer will give you
> a clue, how much you critical you should be to other movements.

Right, so what you're saying is that the only movements that are
successful are those that "play nice with others".  The clue that I
get here is that you don't understand how revolutions occur.

>  Even genial
> and absolutely right ideas are useless, if there is not enough support for
> them.

Agreed.  But Communism, fascism, democracy, etc. didn't get adopted
because their promoters were afraid to criticize their competition.
So in addition to not understanding revolutions you also need to brush
on the concept of "marketing": Designing your "product" to enable
criticizing the competition not only makes your product better, it
indirectly makes their products better too.

> As much as I know, people like a very limited amount of criticism and their
> support is corresponding to it.

Right, but this isn't about making people feel good about their
existing biases and prejudices, it's about challenging, and then
changing, those things.  If you think we're going to change the world
without ruffling a few feathers, you're one of the people who's biases
and prejudices needs to change.
