Hi Michael,
when Metagov started the idea was to build a software. We started
discussing one that eventually became Candiwi. At about the same time some
of us (ma and Giovani) participated in a EU grant application. We did not
win the grant, but in this process the idea for Vilfredo goes to Athens
where born. So for some time there were two software. Then other software
started joining, and Metagov became a metagroup for various software. Most
of them were either discontinued, or found that the discussion inside here
was not that relevant to them. SO the participation went down.

Also there were some concepts inside metagov that I think pushed some
people away. If you find yourself disagreeing with someone at a fundamental
level, but do not have the time, or the patience, to discuss it, you will
just walk away and do your thing. I think this is what happened in many
instances.

So what remained was mostly people talking (still better than many other
places), and a wiki that no one reads. And the most important: a long list
of relevant softwares.

I am now considering to make a series of pages in the wiki about my
positions in the various topics. Anonimity, consensus. And invite other to
do theirs as well. Something like: Pietro on consensus, and Michael on
consensus, and so on. This mirrors better what happens in science where
people write rarely, and each person tend to present his point of view. I
don't want to have to defent my point every time someone says something.
Instead I think it should be the other way around. If someone says
something that strikes me as really really good, and makes me change my
mind, then (and only then) will I change my position. Less discussion, more
personal research.
