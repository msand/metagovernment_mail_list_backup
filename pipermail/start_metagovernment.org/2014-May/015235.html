I am in the middle of two crises and cannot catch up on my reading in this list right now, but I strongly disagree with this proposal. Defining what function something has at this moment limits what it is able to be. Do we want to be nothing but a discussion group? Should we delete the wiki? Should we stop participating in each others' projects?

At the very least, I would change "discussion group" to "community," as it opens the door for other forms of community. Additionally, this group has performed and continues to perform functions as a testbed for projects to experiment on. Michael, you can say that hasn't worked for you, but we have used Votorola (very successfully!) to come up with the rules for defining the home page, we have also used Vilfredo and Candiwi to come to decisions, as well as some other platforms on occasion.

Also, the wiki does perform functions outside of what you may know. It gets a fair amount of traffic (see the footer of any page), is linked to from ~10 pages in Wikipedia (which is huge), and the lists of active/related projects have potential to evolve into a really great resource (something that I would love to work on, and would encourage others to improve).

Additionally, the purpose of a home page should be to inform people about what the group/project/community/whatever is about: why we exist, what the mission is. If we're going to change the home page, I'd rather it be to something like the elevator pitch:
http://www.metagovernment.org/wiki/Elevator_pitch
(perhaps with some modification to the second sentence.)
