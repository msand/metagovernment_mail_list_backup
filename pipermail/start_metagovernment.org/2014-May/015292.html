Haha,

Here is another one that might sit you better.

Let pretend you are charting a course through dangerous water. You gathered
your plans and decided on a course, but before you leave someone comes up
and says, "hey, I have navigated those waters before. It actually looks to
me like you ship sits deeper than you thought it would. Here, and here
(points to the map) you might want to revisit." The response, "Actually, we
have already decided, so we will go with that." As if somehow the act of
deciding made the path through more safe.

The dangers of the path is independent of decision. But, if you would like
to continue on that course, I'll support you. Just know it has real risks
that exist outside of wether or not we decide that they are there.

Cheers,
James
