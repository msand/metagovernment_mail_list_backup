Knowing Ed, I think he'll answer (as time permits) that people who
wish to vote and can afford to vote (as time permits) will vote.
That's the way people usually do things, of course.

I haven't read the thread (sorry) but jumped in just to underline a
point that Ed seems to be making (which probably you agree with, too).

> > Let the interested volunteers work up proposals, then as those
> > proposals near acceptance their visibility grows, more people jump
> > in, and they [original volunteers] meet resistance. So the
> > proposals need to be tweaked.

Our only assurance that a consensus text is legitimate, or at least
evolving toward legitimacy, is its unconditional openness to critical
outsiders; i.e. their freedom to intervene through voting, drafting,
and other means of expression.  That freedom must apply to everyone
(the biggest possible number) though the number actually taking
advantage is relatively small.

Probably there's no disagreement on this.  Most likely people are just
emphasizing different aspects, so it appears there's disagreement.
