I just want to make an observation, Pietro, rightfully observe that many time in a ML there is repetition of issues with rivers of words spent to describe them and hours of smart people used to do so. It seem to me that intelligence is wasted because there is not a method to use the energy and, here I may be wrong, little or no outcome. Where is the problem? For me the problem is on the ML structure, it is not a smart way of holding a discussion when the participation is more than 3 people. I experienced the same problem in the development group of the participatory democracy I am involved where incredible ideas are found or developed but often get lost in the river sometime we record them in a different environment and then we can tackle them in a more productive way.
Unfortunately we are all used to use ML and reading a list rather than using markup language or a GUI to navigate Ideas, Concepts, Solutions.
I do believe in consolidation of various platforms, driven by mass decision, will be the key to develop the best platform, it must be flexible but must to be one.
Why one? because should connect all the people on issues, and the issues should be so well organized that will be difficult to say something new. I often have ideas and after looking on the web I see other have developed such ideas. To say something new today is becoming very hard, in a ML you waist your time!

Jacopo
 
On Wed, May 14, 2014 at 4:57 AM, Michael Allan <mike at zelea.com> wrote:Pietro and Paul - I think it would take two able-bodied persons to
agree on a purpose before we could suppose that a group even exists
here.  We all know that people *do* agree on goals and active groups
*do* exist, but only outside the context of Metagov.  Metagov itself
is a mailing list of individuals that's often infiltrated by external
groups.  Folks sometimes get the idea that Metagov itself is a group
and will someday accomplish something, but this always turns out to be
an illusion.  The key to dispelling that illusion and turning the
page, I suggest, would be a "consensus" of 2 able bodies on the
purpose of Metagov.  This is really my only point.

I agree with Pietro that a plain old mailing list would also be
useful.  For that to work properly, I think all that's needed is to
stop pretending that it's something else.
