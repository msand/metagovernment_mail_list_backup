Maybe we should "get real" again :-)

We are a community here around metagovernment and we experiment with issues
that have no "hard deadlines" (may they be artificial or given by nature),
and we currently have one decision rule which people can read about in our
wiki.

My personal vision of the future will be not one, but many decision rules
with different scopes, where some scopes can interfere with other scopes
under certain circumstances.
So first: the "oil drilling" as well as "charting dangerous waters" can
have a different scope and a different decision rule.
And second: it would be imaginable to make the "consensus period length"
dependent on the "degree of consensus" and the "acceleration of consensus",
so that given very mission critical urgent information, which convinces
enough people fast enough, can speed up the process by a lot. ...but
personally i'm not making a proposal for that kind of mechanism, because i
think it's to early and too advanced and we should first continue to
experiment with the basics.
