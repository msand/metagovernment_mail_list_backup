> Maybe as an exercise we each should list the top 5 concepts we'd like
> to see covered on the new home page.  Here are mine:
>
> 1) Representative government is inherently corrupt because of the
> money required/allowed to campaign for those offices.
>
> 2) Representative government is inherently dangerous to the general
> population because authoritarians, who are instinctively prejudiced
> against and aggressive toward those they consider outsiders, gravitate
> towards filling the role of representative.
>
> 3) The general population (who I call the "Neurotypicals") is better
> suited to making the decisions about how they want to live directly
> than relying on representatives to do it for them.
>
> 4) The technology to run a direct democracy of more than a few dozen
> people simply did not exist even 20 years ago.  But it does now, and
> the day when a sufficient percentage of the population is comfortable
> enough with it to run a government is rapidly approaching or in some
> places has already arrived.
>
> 5) If we want our government to function better, it's up to *us* to
> design and implement a better system because for damn sure our
> existing governments are not going to do it for us, nor can we trust
> corporations to do it because we cannot provide any monetary incentive
> without introducing inherent conflicts of interest and corruption.
>
> Then, a Call to Action (e.g., "So come help us design/implement/test a
> new system of government that we can use to govern organizations of
> any size simply, safely, and effectively.")
