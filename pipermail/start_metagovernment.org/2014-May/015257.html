This might be a minor point but I think 140 character is only too long when
viewing on a phone or some small device, 140 can easily fit on most
screens.  I'd argue 45 is way too little. You want to introduce a proposal,
one that could be on a complicated subject, so you don't want to paraphrase
and have it potentially misleading. You want it to be a complete sentence,
one that peaks the interest of all associated users to that idea. Having a
simplified title invites a more random audience. This is my rational
anyway.

Are all these versions together, under one page? As in 'the 1-line subject'
is the 'tweet', click on that opens up 'the human-friendly synopsis', like
a point form break down of the proposal, with attached links, then below
that is the actual bill which the people will be reading to vote on? Sorry,
little unclear on how your format will be presented.


The discussion aspect of some more technical proposals might go over most
people's heads, but I don't think a bill for voting should ever be too hard
for the average person to understand. It should be able to be broken down
and presented in a way that the general public will be confident enough
with the material to make an informed vote. By the time the discussion
process is done the truth of the proposal should already have been
established and the best path forward presented. Acquiring the votes is
just to actualize the best path, but there should be no mistake as to what
that path is and where it goes.
