Hi Michael,
personally I think consensus is overrated.

*We need to distinguish between situations that need (or are helped by)
consensus.* Like when we are deciding what to do with a common resource. *And
when consensus is not needed, maybe not even helpful.* *This is especially
true when we are deciding what projects to bring on. *If there is enough
support over a project to move it on, the project will go on. Regardless
what other people do.

You don't need consensus for a kickstarter project to take off. You
definitely want to have as much consensus as possible when deciding how to
share your nation annual budget. Those are opposite situations.

In most of my research I am realising how the context of the question
really changes the terms of the question.

In VgtA we tried strongly to have consensus. And in fact VgtA works well
for "how" questions and not for "what shall we" questions. But really the
how questions comes after the what question and is usually done only to the
people participating in a project. So now there is a common limited
resource. But even there if you don't find consensus you fork. So consensus
is not a real necessity often.


Pietro
