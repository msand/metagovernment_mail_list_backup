So I know that a group decision was made and I was asked to move this to
another thread so I am. Let me give an example of what just happened here
in a different context. If you want to keep the decision as is that's okay,
but don't say I didn't warn you.

Lets imagine you are drilling for oil. You work on a plan as a company and
decide to move forward. Right before you start mining someone visits and
says, "Hey you know, I have actually drilled there before and that angle
you chose will actually cause a bunch of damage to your equipment and cause
unforeseen losses. You might want to revisit." The group responds, "well,
since we already decided we have to honor that decision so we are going to
drill anyway".

Really? I would ask anyone who has experience in this field to challenge me
on this. I just didn't want to sit back and say nothing.  And really, you
want to sit behind a group decision that went on for a week in light of new
information that should effect your decision? I would hope the group is
smarter than that. Again, if anyone has experience in this field I would
gladly hear from him.

And again, if you want to stick with it by all means do. If the group
want's to I will support that decision. I just hope we won't hide behind
the "We have already decided, because we have already decided, and that is
the reason why we will do it" when there is new information to consider.

Cheers,
James
