Fortunately a lot of the groundwork is already being laid by other
people, intentionally or not.  I figure it's more like surfing than
software engineering: We can't predict when the straw that will break
the camel's back will come or even what form it will take, we just
have to have a plan ready so that when that last straw comes it will
be our system that gets implemented instead of one by another
Lenin/Hitler/Mao/etc.  I've read their works and their biographies and
am sure they've got nothing on us (though Mao in particular was almost
supernaturally lucky not to have been killed at a dozen different
points before he had amassed enough power to take out all of his
enemies).  I'm also pretty sure at this point that a willingness to
kill large numbers of people is not even a requirement for the
revolution itself, but merely an unfortunate side effect of the
personalities of the people who typically end up leading them.

You joke about The Plan, but I note that *all* of the problems that
brought low Zuckerberg's Newark education reform project ($100 million
apparently down the tubes) are specifically addressed and robustly
solved in The Plan (bad schools, bad parenting, rent-seeking unions,
authoritarian behavior, etc.). If you didn't see this, the New Yorker
article is a good (albeit very long) read:
http://www.newyorker.com/reporting/2014/05/19/140519fa_fact_russakoff?currentPage=all

I also note that the Facebook motto of "Done is better than perfect"
is prominently mentioned in that article, a motto I happen to agree
with although Newark I think shows that "done" sometimes doesn't
happen unless the plan is a lot closer to "perfect".  This I guess is
common ground between Ed and I.

Yeah, I've got a database designed and built and stuff up on the
screen, so that's something.  I have also decided to do the prototype
as an app (cross platform, of course) rather than as a web site.  We
can port it later, but I've discovered by studying the stuff being
output by Netflix/Amazon/Slashdot etc. that it takes a order of
magnitude (or sometimes two) more code to do something in a web page
than doing it in an app built in a high-level language.  This will
free up time for that language thing: I've been lurking on the
constructed languages mailing lists for a couple months now, and
although it's looking like more "cat herding", there is a hunger there
for something other than the usual fantasy-world languages most of
them seem to be working on...
