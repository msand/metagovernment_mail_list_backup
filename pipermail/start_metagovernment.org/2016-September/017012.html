Ha alright, clearly 'expert' was the wrong word to use. An advocate would
be a better word I guess. I mentioned earlier these 'experts' are
self-appointed, they are anyone who claims to have knowledge on the
subject. It's not a position of power, anyone can sign up for whichever
committee they have the most interest in.

While I agree with the idea that the vast majority of proposals will
> not come from The People directly (to keep the numbers down and the
> quality up, which is necessary to maximize efficiency), I'm not at all
> sure what percentage of proposals should pass in order to consider a
> system (or a proposer) a success. Considering the stuff that comes out
> of the "experts" in the US congress I'd be willing to say that if
> that's the quality of proposals we get that the pass ratio should be
> pretty close to 0%. But after a few rounds of that kind of humiliating
> repudiation I have faith that the proposal generators will actually
> bother to do a little real social engineering backed by actual
> measurement of what The People want and will accept. In your proposal,
> these "experts" (who I maintain will be SDAP because those are just
> the people that want to make the rules) will never even have any check
> on their behavior.


I agree, numbers down, quality up in proposals is key. The 'experts' can be
limited by the system only allowing a certain number of proposals per user
per year, 3 for example. Also having a comprehensive proposal creation
criteria will make it no small feat to properly get a proposal filled out
and on to the discussion phase. This way only if you have some confidence
it will get passed would you bother submitting.

Sorry for not communicating properly. This group of 100 SDAP's would all
need to be in the same 'weapon system committee' or whatever to pass that,
that's the only committee they can be a part of. In this case then, 200
outside users, not associated with the 'weapon system committee' would be
required to veto the proposal.

(big snip)

Point taken, that the committees would not be balanced, but that voice of
opposition would be there still. And would be a rallying cry when the
proposal passed the committee stage and was out in the general public
space. The users in the committee hold no power in voting once the proposal
is moved to the general public.
