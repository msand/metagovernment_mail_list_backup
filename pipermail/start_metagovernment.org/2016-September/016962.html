Maybe we should try something simpler: After being on the Aspen
Institute's mailing list for awhile and visiting their center a few
days ago, I'm now fairly confident in identifying them as part of the
problem rather than part of the solution. It is a very impressive
project (and facility), but if you look at who attends the Ideas
Festival (and who can afford the $5K+ entrance fees), it's a virtual
who's who of the oligarchs (e.g., the Koch's even have their own
building there!):
https://www.aspeninstitute.org/

In this case the "leverage point" I think is the definition of
"Leadership": What does that word really mean, and why is it so hard
to pin down? Seems to me "leader" and "leadership" are used in two
completely incompatible ways:
1) The Wikipedia definition, which applies to someone who facilitates
achieving common goals (e.g.,
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Leadership)
2) The way it mostly works in the "real world", which applies to
someone who is able to articulate their *own* goals and then
manipulate people into being subservient "followers" to help achieve
them.

I think we need a new word for the second definition. Maybe something
like "exploitership" or maybe "fabulead": getting people to do what
you want by making shit up that they find convincing...

What do you say?
