You may be right. Actually, one could just try it. A petition can easily be used as a voting tool. One creates a pair of (cross-referenced) petitions, with the same proposal, one for it, the other against it. So votes for and against can be counted. At change.org you can subscribe to petitioners (follow them), so one would keep receiving updates from them. This is a form of delegation. Following/un-following is like picking/dropping a delegate. It is not a perfect tool, but it is available for free. Would it be an interesting experiment?
