There are a lot of aspects of LD that are amazing and surprising.
This one for example works out because of the long debating times.
"Voter" is the wrong term. It sounds like there is no debate.

It is enough if one participant is paying attention and noticing
a bogus explanation for a bogus political choice to draw the
attention of many of the other participants. The result can be
a reorganization of delegations or simply that the proxy who
expressed that fallacious argumentation understands their mistake
and corrects it.

So I would guess that LQFB works things out with a healthy form
of inner attention economy. One thing that actually can disturb
the process is if proposal promoters do massive amounts of out-
of-band publicity, like advertising on Twitter - but then again
even if the influx of people is biased at first, the LQFB method
still leads them to confront also the criticalities of a proposal
and the alternative options. That's hugely better than say a
campaign on change.org.

Again I am taking guesses why it works. I just experienced that
it works. It would be important to have more scientifically proven
understanding of why it works. You can only achieve that if you
