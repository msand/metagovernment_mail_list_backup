I guess we need to define "useful", though: Is Pokemon Go "useful"?
FaceBook? People use things that are interesting, entertaining, etc.
Basically anything that makes them feel good. This is something we can
supply even before it does anything "useful".

> I do not expect this to be the case for a decision making system.

I don't actually know the population statistics on this, but if my FB
"friends" are any indication, people seem to derive great pleasure
from giving other people their opinion on things. This is something
that even beta-test government system can do better than any of those
forums because it at least offers the *potential* that these
expression of opinion will really matter at some point.

Again, to refute this you need to explain away not only FB, but also
organizations like MoveOn and Avaaz, although I suppose you might
appeal to people's delusion that slacktivism actually matters. But
then, we can do that too by simply sending the results of each vote to
the appropriate government official (which they will usually just
ignore, just as they do the petitions they get from everywhere else).
Bam! Instant "utility".
