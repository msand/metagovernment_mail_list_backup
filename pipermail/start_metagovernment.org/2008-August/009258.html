> I think our efforts have many similarities.  One notable difference  
> is that DemocracyLab is implementing a data structure that  
> presupposes that political thinking can be broken into distinct  
> Concepts: Values that people hold; Positions taken when applying  
> Values to Issues; and Policies advocating the implementation of  
> Positions.  These Concepts can be supported or opposed by Evidence  
> and Opinions.
>
As you may have noticed from Pietro's post earlier today, we are still  
working through some of this: we are exploring using pure folksonomy  
(i.e., tagging) for data organization, but already we are at least  
talking about distinct kinds of tags for different purposes. Your  
divisions do seem pretty natural.
