> in the wiki, or the other way around?
>

Good question.
We never defined a line.
Essentially people answer where they find a question.

Personally I tend to favour the wiki. This because I see it as more
stable and easier to let us recover the info after.
I see the wiki as a growing ice crystal, and the discussion board as
the still floating molecules of water. So I use the discussion is
someone asks something here, or if it is for a topic that is so new
that I don't know where to put it in the wiki.

When you write on the wiki, there is a strong risk that people will
not notice what is being said. People tend to only follow the pages on
the wiki they are interested in. So again, for new topics the
discussion group is good.
Once a topic has a certain amount of discussion in its history it
makes sense to record the summary of it in the wiki, and then any
subsequent discussion will often appear in the wiki.
