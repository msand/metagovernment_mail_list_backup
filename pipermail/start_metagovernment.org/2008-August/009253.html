Yes, it is interesting. After all in many political arena in italy

>
> I agree, that an egoistic incentive, is necessary but I can't see how
> negative feedback would be implemented here, apart from introducing
> scoring mechanism, separate from tags. You could argue of course, that
> somebody might tag something as "stupid", but then again, you would
> hardly want to find something like that again, so why would you give
> it any tag at all?

So tags will help you ascetein the qualities of a resolution, what it
refers to, if it is funny, interesting, brave, or what.
If it refers to what part of the world or to what community, but it
will NOT tell you how much the tagger agrees with the resolution
itself.
For that we need a separate mechanism.
What tags also tells us is how many people are interested in that
resolution (number of people who have tagged / number of people who
have opened the page).
This is all tags tells us.

It is hard to have negative feedback, but then you can have a person
writing "this is stupid", refering tot he first, and then people
tagging this second message as "true". Also I remember when a man was
asking his wife to sign a bdsm contract before entering in a marriage,
and she chose not to marry him. The tags used contained quite clear
negative feedback toward his behaviour. So when people want to give
negative feedback they DO use tags. Also if people know that their tag
will be read also from this pov they will probably use them more.

>
> Judging by your blog entry, you agree, that tags should have a weight,
> just that they should follow a power law and the interface should be
> simple, ie. the ordering determines their weight.
>
> First I'd like to suggest an interface for displaying the weight of a
> tag. This could be done by underlying the tag with a bar of a certain
> width or color with it's spectrum ranging from green to red. When
> adding a tag, you can click on an existing one, as with the delicious
> interface. The green component is then increased (probably with a
> flash that eases off, to draw the attention of the user, that she is
> distributing weights) and in accordance with the power law, the
> components of the other tags she has selected are reduced.
> The interface is reduced to one button for each existing tag and one
> click per tag, and I can hardly imagine it being much simpler than
> that.


yes, I think we are thinking in similar terms. Every time you add a
tag the weights of all the others is adjusted.

Under the list of tags we can see the weight as a series of bars, and
the user can modify them.
Of course if you modify one all the others go up or down to make sure
the summ is still 1. But what is important is that the default is a
power law, and it is a nomind to get the default.

But I like the idea of the bars. SO people know what they are doing.
