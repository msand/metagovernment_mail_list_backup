The Free Government project in the United States appears to be  
converging with us in many of their philosophies and approaches. They  
are still focused on the United States, but they seem to be getting a  
little momentum:
http://blog.freegovernment.org/

And their software, while quite different from what we have talked  
about, is already at what they consider to be release status:
http://www.freegovernment.org/bills

Their source is of course open and GPL:
http://github.com/foysavas/freegovernment-core/tree/master

Could some of the programmers in this group have a look at it and  
evaluate its worth as a codebase?

It seems like we should be talking to them about some sort of  
cooperation, but it would make sense to first evaluate their code. I  
find the end-user experience to be somewhat uninviting and confusing,  
but not bad for basically a 1.0.

Recommendations? Fork, ignore, invite, merge, discuss, watch...?
