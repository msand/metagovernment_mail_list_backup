Thanks Michael, excellent suggestions.  Your last question is the most
important, about users being able to "discover its overall purpose". We're
adding some additional features soon that should make the output screen,
and hopefully the purpose, a little more intuitive, but a bit of
explanation by video or diagram would be useful as well.

We're also strongly considering framing a new issue each month, and
distributing a monthly description of results recorded and insights
gleaned, making DemocracyLab both an engagement tool and a monthly
publication.  This could build audience, present information in a format
people are used to, and create reasons for ongoing engagement as new issues
are framed.

I'll let everyone know how it goes-

Mark

>  * If the list cannot honour manual ordering, then do not let it
>    receive dropped items.  This is misleading, and gave me false
>    confidence that I was on the right track.
>
>  * If the correct method is to drag the values from left to right,
>    then put an arrow from left to right.  No need for words.
>
>  * Label the drop box something like "Values I feel positively about"
>    rather than "You feel positive about".  Repeating the word
>    "values" makes the purpose clearer.
>
> Also maybe:
>
>  * Remove the rounded border from the large heads up display.  Or
>    something else to distinguish it from the controls below.
>
>  * Remove the instructions "See a description by hovering over one of
>    the values", because that is unecessary.
>
>    In general, there is instructional clutter in the valuable space
>    immediately preceding the controls.  Better to give the controls
>    priority of place.  Lengthy instructions are a warning sign that
>    the UI is difficult to use (which really it is not) or difficult
>    to understand.

> > For those of you unfamiliar with DemocracyLab, our unique presumption is
> > that we can facilitate a dialogue that creates opportunities for creative
> > problem solving by framing issues through three lenses:
> > 1) Why? - the values, principles and beliefs that guide our thinking
> > 2) What? - the objectives we seek to accomplish
> > 3) How? - the actions we take and the policies we implement
> >
> > We're currently running a demo of our software relating to Oregon's Tax
> > System.  I'd encourage you to take five minutes to try it out - don't
> worry
> > about not being Oregon residents.  You can find it linked in the upper
> > right on this page: http://democracylab.org/participate/web-developers/,
> > along with a link to download the codebase if you choose to.
>  Forewarning -
> > we're using facebook as our login system right now.  I know, I know -
> > you'll find adding additional login options as items #18 and #19 on our
> > issue-tracking board: https://github.com/bnfb/DemocracyLab/issues.  Feel
> > free to dive in!
> >
> > Our output screen starts to map out participants' thinking, using dynamic
> > histograms to show the connections between the values, objectives, and
> > policy preferences users have expressed.  Our belief is that there's
> > insight in the data, and that we're just starting to be able to express
> it
> > visually.  I'd welcome any and all feedback on what we're doing.
> >
> > One challenge we're hoping to address in the future is shared by others
> > working on active projects - managing the initiation, evolution and
> > evaluation of proposals.  How do you balance the competing needs of
> > different stages in its lifecycle?  One idea I've had is having proposals
> > gather sponsors, with redrafting tools evolving as the scale of the
> sponsor
> > base does.  For example:
> > 1 sponsor - the author controls everything
> > up to 10 sponsors - consensus drafting tools, 90% "yes" votes needed to
> > advance
> > 10-100 sponsors - idea is introduced to voting system, amendment drafting
> > system balances conformity to original intent with need for continued
> > improvement
> > 100-1000 sponsors - document direction becomes narrowly bound, but body
> of
> > evidence documenting rationale and likely impacts grows
> > 1000+ sponsors - we should be so lucky!  A bridge to cross then...
