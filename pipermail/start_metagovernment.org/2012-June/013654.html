> I have to admit I have some doubts over the idea of the delegation
> tree. Especially if you intend to map all the variations. One of the
> doubts is that the delegation tree is not necessarily a tree at all.
> Suppose you have a proposal A. Then two mutations: A1, A2. So now you
> have 4 options: A, AA1, AA2, AA1A2.

What do you mean with "options"? In my view, as a voter you have only 
three: A, A1 and A2. And ofc everybody has the option to create 
countless new mutations, but until then: only three. A is at the root 
and A1 and A2 are proposed improvements to A (branches coming out of the 
root), which have gathered a number of voters behind them, so the voters 
of A are inclined to talk to them, i.e. look for consensus.
