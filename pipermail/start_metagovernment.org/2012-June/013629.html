Thanks for your questions, Ed.

The public advocate is an existing position in New York City, to whom we would give most of the responsibilities associated with implementing the citizens' forum. Currently the position is essentially an ombudsman for citizens who have a gripe with city government, elected to a four year term, though there is more to it, described in the city charter, available from the site.

We are leaving the selection of software open in the hopes of stimulating advance in the field and allowing for innovation down the line.  Initially it would have to meet all of the requirements and functionalities provided for by the Citizens' Forum Amendment, along with any specified by the public advocate or state law.  But even if everything goes our way, it wouldn't be selected until 2015, so it seems a bit premature to solicit software recommendations.

What we really could use right now before the amendment's text is locked for petitioning, is recommendations for any aspects of the software, hardware, or implementation of the citizens' forum that anyone thinks we should require to maximise its usefulness or minimise its corruptibility / vulnerability to co-option. 

Best,
Olaf
