> Hi Paul and Olaf,
>
> I know Liquid Feedback, it is used by the German Pirate Party. And I 
> think it is one of the better e-dem-tools out there. You can vote, you 
> can delegate your vote, you can propose improvements to a position and 
> you can bring in your own position and collect votes for it. The only 
> handicap IMO is: by its concept the whole system gives more power to 
> the early drafters/voters and therefore tends to freeze at some point 
> and is therefore not suitable for large numbers of participants. This 
> is because a position can only be changed/improved by its author. If 
> he rejects such a request, people would have to leave him and start 
> all over again ... and then again and again ...
>
> The only solution I can see is Communicative Delegation:
> http://zelea.com/w/User:ThomasvonderElbe_GmxDe/Communicative_Delegation
>
> Best,
> Thomas

>> Olaf, in reviewing some of the lowercased proposal content, I think I 
>> have one primary suggestion.
>> As I understand it, you allow for citizens to propose ballot 
>> measures, and for them to go to election if they get enough support, 
>> right? And you allow for collaborative drafting and online deliberation.
>> But I think that combination still leaves out one crucial aspect. 
>> What we see in early attempts to bring in citizen participation is 
>> that many people propose many similar initiatives, and there is very 
>> little sorting mechanism. Further, we see people being asked to vote 
>> yes-or-no, or often yes-or-abstain, on these proposals.
>> Not only is it unrealistic to ask people to vote on the thousands of 
>> proposals that get introduced, it is also very restricting. What 
>> several of our projects are doing is working to build systems that 
>> bring people together in the drafting process, not just at the end 
>> voting process. In fact, if we do it right, the voting process is 
>> more like an affirmation of all the work that went into the drafting 
>> process, and ends up being a relatively minor aspect of the whole thing.
>> Enabling collaborative drafting is a good first start, but there 
>> needs to be a feedback loop right in that drafting process that helps 
>> not only winnow down the volume of proposals, but also which helps 
>> bring people together and build consensus.
>> I have not yet worked out how this aspect would fit into your 
>> existing documents, but first let us know if you've already 
>> accommodated this position, if you'd like help doing so, or if you 
>> disagree.
