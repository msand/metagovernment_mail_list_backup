Olaf, in reviewing some of the lowercased proposal content, I think I have one primary suggestion.

As I understand it, you allow for citizens to propose ballot measures, and for them to go to election if they get enough support, right? And you allow for collaborative drafting and online deliberation.

But I think that combination still leaves out one crucial aspect. What we see in early attempts to bring in citizen participation is that many people propose many similar initiatives, and there is very little sorting mechanism. Further, we see people being asked to vote yes-or-no, or often yes-or-abstain, on these proposals. 

Not only is it unrealistic to ask people to vote on the thousands of proposals that get introduced, it is also very restricting. What several of our projects are doing is working to build systems that bring people together in the drafting process, not just at the end voting process. In fact, if we do it right, the voting process is more like an affirmation of all the work that went into the drafting process, and ends up being a relatively minor aspect of the whole thing.

Enabling collaborative drafting is a good first start, but there needs to be a feedback loop right in that drafting process that helps not only winnow down the volume of proposals, but also which helps bring people together and build consensus.

I have not yet worked out how this aspect would fit into your existing documents, but first let us know if you've already accommodated this position, if you'd like help doing so, or if you disagree.
