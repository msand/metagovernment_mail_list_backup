A serious issue that I have with LiquidFeedback and other proxy voting
systems is the significant security flaws should they be deployed in the
United States. There is no way to prevent sockpuppets from overtaking the
system with a naked proxy voting network, and even clique detection or
other graph based methods are not always effective.

On the method of collaboration there needs to be a differentiation between
local and global synthesis. At the local group level, for instance my
neighborhood, it would be rather simple to amend a document and gather
consensus around that amendment. At the global level this becomes
impossible. For that reason the communicative deliberation process outlined
here, and its counterpart of pyramidal democracy, is the best way to
synthesize ideas.

What that looks like in reality I am unsure.

However I am open to including these kind of models inhttp://openassembly.org.
We are working on a new design for the occupy national gathering that
includes a small-world democratic decision model. I will definitely post my
experiences here.

Frank Grove

> bring in your own position and collect votes for it. The only handicap IMO

> request, people would have to leave him and start all over again ... and
> then again and again ...

> Olaf, in reviewing some of the lowercased proposal content, I think I have
> one primary suggestion.
>
> As I understand it, you allow for citizens to propose ballot measures, and
> for them to go to election if they get enough support, right? And you allow
> for collaborative drafting and online deliberation.
>
> But I think that combination still leaves out one crucial aspect. What we
> see in early attempts to bring in citizen participation is that many people
> propose many similar initiatives, and there is very little sorting
> mechanism. Further, we see people being asked to vote yes-or-no, or often
> yes-or-abstain, on these proposals.
>
> Not only is it unrealistic to ask people to vote on the thousands of
> proposals that get introduced, it is also very restricting. What several of
> our projects are doing is working to build systems that bring people
> together in the drafting process, not just at the end voting process. In
> fact, if we do it right, the voting process is more like an affirmation of
> all the work that went into the drafting process, and ends up being a
> relatively minor aspect of the whole thing.
>
> Enabling collaborative drafting is a good first start, but there needs to
> be a feedback loop right in that drafting process that helps not only
> winnow down the volume of proposals, but also which helps bring people
> together and build consensus.
>
> I have not yet worked out how this aspect would fit into your existing
> documents, but first let us know if you've already accommodated this
> position, if you'd like help doing so, or if you disagree.
>
