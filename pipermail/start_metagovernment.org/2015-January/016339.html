On 01/26/2015 02:31 PM, Scott Raney wrote:
>
> On Jan 26, 2015 6:52 PM, "Michal Štěpánek" wrote:
>
> >> but I think I'm still not clear on what anarcho-syndicalism is and 
> how it differs from corporate capitalism.  Seems to me the only 
> distinction of any consequence is that under anarcho-syndicalism you 
> are only allowed to invest in the company you work for/in, whereas 
> under corporate capitalism you are able to invest in any company.  
> Have I missed something?
> >
> > This is just secondary. The ownership of factors of production is 
> here to ensure the ownership of profit. It is mental exercise between 
> neoliberal: ~profit belongs to capital, and Marx: ~profit belongs to 
> workers IMHO
>
> I think I understand the pure communist case well enough: In that case 
> everyone collectively makes the decisions as to what to produce and 
> the distribution of any profit becomes straightforward because the 
> same organization (the State) handles that too.
>
> But anarcho-syndicalism is a different kind of animal: In that case 
> there *is* no state organization responsible for all the investment 
> choices and distribution of profit.  Instead individuals make these 
> choices.  My question is, what happens when an individual wants to 
> take the profit from the venture they're working in and use it as 
> capital to fund a different venture?  Are laws passed to prevent this, 
> or is it just up to some moral code (i.e., religion, like the Islamic 
> prohibition against loaning money with interest due) to prevent it?  
> How does one reconcile this restriction with the "freedom" the anarcho 
> part of the philosophy is supposed to provide?
