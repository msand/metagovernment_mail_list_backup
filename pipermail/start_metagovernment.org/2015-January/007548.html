I think only the last two articles in that book are worth reading unless
you are clueless about how screwed up our political systems are, but I
think I'm still not clear on what anarcho-syndicalism is and how it differs
from corporate capitalism.  Seems to me the only distinction of any
consequence is that under anarcho-syndicalism you are only allowed to
invest in the company you work for/in, whereas under corporate capitalism
you are able to invest in any company.  Have I missed something?

If not, my claim would be that the difference is primarily religious, and
is an analog to food prohibitions (e.g., against eating pork in Judaism and
Islam).  Does anyone besides Chomsky still advocate this position?  If not
here, is there a place anyone knows where they hang out?
