> Thanks for the reference, but unfortunately I didn't find any of the 
> stuff on that site very enlightening.   There's lots of handwaving 
> about how "workers uniting" will solve all of our problems, but almost 
> nothing about how any of the hard problems (particularly the kinds of 
> income redistribution that are necessary to provide for schools, the 
> disabled, retirement pensions, etc.) will be solved.
>
IMHO anarchist believe that social redistribution by "elites" e.g. 
government happens mostly under the threat of revolution, that social 
redistribution is natural to society of people and that "elites" are 
just preventing it.
>
> And it all has a kind of religious feel to it, like you're supposed to 
> take all these proposals on faith without examining any of the details 
> or fundamental assumptions very carefully.
>
I is about faith, but I thing it is better to believe that people are 
good and be a little bit wrong, than to believe that we are all rotten 
and force people to act according to it.
>
> There were a few insightful comments about communism in there, 
> particularly the need to go *through* an anarchist phase before true 
> communism can be achieved.  But even this was oddly reminiscent, 
> albeit completely contradictory, to what Trotsky had to say about the 
> need to go through a bourgeois phase to get from peasant farmers to 
> communism.  Which is it?
>
IMHO it is meant
1st  - peasant -> bourgeois  -> anarchist   -> communist
2nd - peasant -> bourgeois  -> ?                -> communist
but it could be absolutely wrong

> So I'm still stuck with the basic question: How does any form of 
> anarchism really deal with any sort of wealth inequality other than 
> handwaving about how it won't ever develop if the participants are 
> truly motivated to prioritize the enterprise over their individual 
> preferences?
>
If we look back at Spanish revolution, there was initial forced 
redistribution in Robin Hood style. I believe they were ready to repeat 
it, if they would see that there is some unjustified inequity again.
