Speaking from Vilfredo,
I have resisted inserting a discussion inside Vilfredo because I do
understand how important it would be to have it added in a really
meaningful way.

As a mathematician I have a sort of red light in my head that goes on
when my intuition tells me that there is an interesting solution to a
problem, and that I don't know it. The light has been on ever since
from when we built vilfredo. I know the discussion will eventually be
introduced with a complete integration. With people not voting for
something being allowed to explain why, and give feedback. With people
being invited to make new versions in particular instances. You can
see a pre pre proto version of this in the algorithm that calculates
the key people, and in the mail I wrote few days ago about the "lock"
people.

I don't want to code something wrong, because once it is coded it will
take longer to pull it off.

But I strongly support the idea that the discussion and the voting
should be integrated. Just in the right way. Which is out there... .
And is waiting to be brought to light.

Pietro




On 22 September 2010 14:03, Ed Pastore <epastore at metagovernment.org> wrote:
> On Sep 21, 2010, at 1:04 PM, Paul Sabin wrote:
>
>> I'd just like to pick up on a point from this discussion.
>>
>> Richard asked:
>> Could it [anonymous participation] apply to voting, at all?
>>
>> And Ed replied:
>> It could in a mixed discussion and voting system. Though prominent systems
>> in this project seem to be leaning away from that model.
>>
>> What do you mean by a mixed discussion and voting system, Ed? Surely the
>> voting systems in this project will also allow for discussion? Or will it be
>> the case that an open governance system will have to mix and match a voting
>> tool, a proposal writing/editing tool and a discussion forum in order to
>> have the complete package?
>>
>> I think it's paramount that these elements are as closedly integrated as
>> possible so that users engage in the whole process rather than just voting.
>
> I was pointing to a couple of projects which have been most active on the
> list recently.
>
> Votorola explicitly intended to outsource discussion, based on the idea that
> people form their own public discourse communities already, and trying to
> force a new one on them may be counter-productive. So their engine is
> designed to interoperate with external inputs as much as they can
> accommodate. (However, I think I recently saw them talking about reversing
> that position and in-sourcing discussion capabilities.)
>
> Vilfredo also is set up as a pure decision engine. People have hacked in
> discussion in the form of comment-proposals, but these can break the flow of
> the pareto front. It works much better (as it is currently built) if people
> discuss outside the context of the voting itself, such as I did with my post
> about Free Democracy.
>
> I too would like to eventually see close affiliation between discussion and
> voting, since I think it can greatly help push people toward synthesis.
