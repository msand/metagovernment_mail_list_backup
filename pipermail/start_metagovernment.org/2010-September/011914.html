On Sep 28, 2010, at 1:12 PM, Paul Sabin <psabin at gmail.com> wrote:

> I've been thinking about what I and others who are not directly  
> involved with development of software can do to help advance the  
> Metagovernment project. One idea I have returned to is a Q&A section  
> for the website which anyone could edit. I see this as serving  
> several important purposes:
>
> 1) To explain what is quite a complicated idea in bite-sized chunks,  
> e.g. "What does the name Metagovernment mean?", "What is meant by  
> radical transparency?" etc. The vast majority of this information is  
> already on the site, but this way of organising it may help to a)  
> guide first-time visitors through the concept in steps, and b) help  
> people to find specific information they are looking for. It is no  
> accident that corporations love to use Q&A sections to explain  
> tricky issues. I once translated one aimed specifically at a large  
> number of people who were about to be made redundant.
>
> 2) To anticipate and counter some of the preconceptions people have  
> about what Metagovernment is and how it would (not) work, e.g.  
> "Won't people be influenced to vote a certain way by the media?",  
> "How can one be sure that the system is safe from hackers?", "Who  
> will have the power to make sure that decisions are implemented in  
> the real world?", "Won't there be a bias towards the views of people  
> who spend all their time on the internet?"
>
> These two points both go back to the discussion we had about the  
> front page of the site and people's immediate reactions to the idea.  
> The next two are about how it might help us - people who are already  
> participating in the project:
> 3) To present questions which have more than one competing answer.  
> One problem with the mailing list is that a certain issue will be  
> discussed, then fall by the wayside as we move on to something else.  
> A Q&A could provide us with a prominent place for issues under  
> discussion. e.g. "Do anonymous interactions have a place within the  
> metagovernment?" I actually found this question on the page entitled  
> "Anonymous interactions within the metagovernment", but I don't  
> think I would have come across this page at all without searching  
> for the term "anonymity". If such questions had greater visibility  
> on the site then multiple positions on the issue could be displayed  
> and updated to reflect the current state of the debate, and we may  
> spark some new ideas in the process.
> 4) To imagine parts of the project which have not been implemented,  
> e.g. "How will discussion take place?" Pietro recently said about  
> how a discussion forum might be implemented in Vilfredo: "I don't  
> want to code something wrong, because once it is coded it will take  
> longer to pull it off." So let's have a place where we - both  
> project developers and those on the sidelines - can gather and  
> refine our ideas about how things should look when they are coded.  
> This would be a resource which different projects could draw upon,  
> so that hopefullly various concepts will be tried out and we can see  
> what works well.
>
> So my questions at this point are:
>
> - Do you think a Q&A section would serve these purposes?
>
> - Would it be easy enough to integrate into the site from a  
> technical standpoint?
>
> - Or is there a better way of addressing the issues I have described  
> above?
>
> Paul
