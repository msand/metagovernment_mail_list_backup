>>> wish to
>>> vote for it, but realize that a future employer may see them  
>>> voting for it
>>> and make assumptions about them as a result. So they vote against  
>>> it even
>>> though they want it to pass ...
>>
>> I agree with you, Ed. I made my peace with public voting though,  
>> because we
>> can allow each voter to have his own unique position. So the voter  
>> in your
>> example must not choose between yes or no, instead he can have the  
>> position
>> "I want further scientific research into the potential benefits of
>> marijuana." This is neither yes nor no, more something in between.  
>> This way
>> in such delicate questions a whole society can move slowly and  
>> gently over
>> time towards its consensus. Nobody needs to be radical in  
>> opposition to the
>> rest of society but step by step the whole society can reach a  
>> position,
>> which seemed radical before.
>
> The problem of inertia with our current models of governance tend to
> condense smaller issues into large, controversial yes/no votes.
> Online voting systems need not follow this pattern.  By eliminating
> the ability for a minority group to dictate the singular form of the
> vote and another minority group to effect a choke-hold on the entire
> process, I believe we will see a greater number of "tweaks", and a
> smaller number of "reforms".

Thomas and Richard, you are both quite right. It was remiss of me to  
base my argument a yes/no style system, when all along I have been a  
strong proponent of an adaptive, synthesis-oriented system. I stand  
corrected. Though I will continue to think this over and may come back  
with a new argument later. And I still see a benefit in a system which  
allows anonymous participation, if at a lower level of priority.

And this provides an adequate if not complete answer to my objection  
in terms of oppressive regimes (borrowing from Michael's logic).  
People in those regimes may not be able to vote their conscience  
safely, but they can push a little bit toward it. That beats the heck  
out of the status quo.
