Sure. Just avoid to follow the strategy of only voting for your
proposals a priori. Really read all the proposals and vote for the
viable alternative. A good rule of thumb is that your influence on the
discussion is the highest if you vote for about half of the
alternatives. So if you find yourself voting for everything or for
none you are not having any influence on the discussion. If you vote
for only one or all but one you are essentially holding the rest of
the group at a gun point ("ONLY THIS!", or "ALL BUT THIS!"), but if
you vote for about half you spread your influence through the whole
discussion.

On the other hand I do sometimes find myself voting for many/all
proposals. I consider this meaning that the discussion has reached a
point where I basically don't care. Everything that has been proposed
is ok for me.
