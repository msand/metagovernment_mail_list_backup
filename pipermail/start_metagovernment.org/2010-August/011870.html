As another Michael, I'll just jump in.  I have tended to lurk, largely
because you guys have been engaged with each other on this topic for a
while, and I feel somewhat a latecomer, and it's been hard to carve out
sufficient spare cycles to be a meaningful participant.

Anyway -- I wanted to throw out a term that I am using in my work:  Open
Stewardship.  It is as heavily informed by Open Space processes as it is by
other segments of the Open Meme (like open source).

Invitation, and ongoing Welcoming to the space and task of Governance (here
I choose Governance rather than Government - it's a wider concept and heads
towards the totality of the project of living together), which means also
ongoing orientation of new comers.  Consider that aspect an invocation of
Ancient Hospitality for any and all projects and groups and governing
functions - there is room for people to step up as far as they want or are
able.  There's more to my particular model of Open Stewardship than this,
but I just wanted to put the term out there, as well as the importance of
ongoing invitation/welcoming/orientation.

I'll be sharing more on the Open Stewardship concept here and on related
lists, as I am able.

Regards,

MM
