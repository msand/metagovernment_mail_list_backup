I only meant that you should float the most crucial/difficult design
problem in a *discussion* thread.  I didn't see anything that looked
deeply problematic in your list, tho, no showstoppers *and* crucial
(but I really don't know your domain).

Re: http://u.zelea.com/w/P2P/Poll_Decision_Conditions

We already spoke about those, and they seem to be the biggest concern
and a poor mesh with the current design.  Current design: free speech
= no meta-rules on voting; and pollservers have now evolved into
stupid databases of raw votes for external processing by other
servers.  That, plus the complexity and specialized/bespokedness of
these rules points to a layered solution implemented in code external
to the pollservers.

OTOH, all vote eligibility is scripted.  So it's anything goes if you
can somehow impl. your rules by rejecting votes at the count stage.
Even for our primary use case (eligibility based on residency, and
authenticated by trust network) none of it is hardcoded into the
Votorola pollserver.  It's all scripted by the admin, mostly here I
think: http://zelea.com/system/host/u/home/v/votorola/poll.js
(Or that could be just another example of code external to
pollservers.  All of the functionality in pollwiki/streetwiki belongs
to the category, too.)

But it's crucial to start w/ talk of problems not solutions, and
dialogue it in a thread.
