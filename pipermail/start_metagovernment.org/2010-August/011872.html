> I don't remember exactly where the thread is at the moment, but recently

> "real democracy."

But there are some problems with the term real. Imagine that you are
reading, in the future, an article about the history of democracy. You will
find names like direct democracy, representative democracy... always an
"adjective" + democracy. Real (or false) won't denote the kind of democracy
we are talking about, since any democracy well implemented (in theory) must
be a real democracy. Also, it will be possible to speak about false real
democracy and real real democracy.

I like how "real democracy" sounds (in fact, that's almost the meaning of
the name of Eudemocracia NGO; "εὖ" means "good" in ancient greek)... I would
pick it to denominate a project, for example, but not a form of government.

The nice things about this:
>
> 1. It contains the word democracy, which seems important and which is much
> more resonant than "governance."
>

I totally agree.


> 2. It implies that everything else branding itself as some kind of
> "democracy" is a lesser imitation... which is only too true.
