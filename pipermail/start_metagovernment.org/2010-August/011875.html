Branding/rebranding would imply that we have a new wine to sell, or an
old wine in a new bottle.  But I believe we have an *old* wine in an
*old* bottle and we just invented (or reinvented) the corkscrew.

I want to share a post of Alex Rollin's, because I think it's
brilliant and apropos: *

> others in a process means we, I, provide others with a guide to the

Alex probably knows what I'm going to propose (I only hope he can
forgive the metaphor).  The thing to do is to carry the bottle into
the meeting and uncork it.  Just get right to it, without ceremony.
It'll be awkward at first (lots to learn) and you'll have to make
shift (paper cups, straws, or whatever).  But it *should* work.

Enough of the metaphor (apologies).  Democracy is not wine in a
bottle, it's something entirely different (something better, we hope).
But what Alex seems to be suggesting (here and in his later posts) is
that if you haven't actually tried it, then you cannot speak of what
it *really* is.  You simply don't know.  You are trapped in the past.

I think there's only one way to escape.  Rebranding won't help.  What
you must do is:

  1. Carry the tools of democracy (the real tools) to wherever they
     *could* work.

  2. *Make* them work.  Learn first hand what the future is like.

  3. Carry what you learned back to the tool builders.  Work together
     with them.

Or am I wrong?  Is there another way?
