Giovani Spagnolo wrote:

> Google open up its API for developers and make AdSense optional for
> those who do not wish to see ads in their decision-making processes ?

So there's no more ads, unless the user explicitly requests them.
(Assume for now the most eager-to-please response.)  Ads are now a
user option, and they are turned off by default.  Most users leave
them off.  So now they have nothing to complain about, as far as ads
go.

Also, Google opens a public API (JoeVotes) to all of its competitors,
present and future.  They now have access to the same pool of voter
data, as does Google.  That data becomes a public commododity.
(Again, assume that JoeVotes follows all of Murphy's principles,
including public ballots.)  Users may now switch to any of the various
JoeVotes-compliant tools, if they wish.  Most of them do so wish,
because that's what they decided earlier.

This raises two new questions:

 iii) How is Google to recoup its capital, and turn a profit?

 iv) How is this different from a de-facto JoeVotes ecosystem, in the
     first place?
