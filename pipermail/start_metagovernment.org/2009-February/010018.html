I think we found a workable compromise where we shall only write about
the metagovernment contribution in the wiki.
Also by next monday I shall pack all that we have produced, bring it
to the meetings, and then we shall not discuss it publicly until the
evaluation is over. I think we can live with that.

Just to clarify, I do not agree with the Giovani's analysis of the
situation that the only problem was an EU directive that the material
must not be published before. I think this is more a guidelines that
means that you shouldn't trying to just replicate results that are
already known.

If this is the case (which is not), I think we should be much more
public in what we are doing.

On the practical case:
Yes, it is not easy (or possible) to add some tags just to a certain
page in a wiki. But it is possible to make some pages accessible only
to some people In particular we could make the pages accessible only
to logged in users, which essentially would make it unreachable for
the google spiders.

I think the page with the information is this:
http://www.mediawiki.org/wiki/Extension:WhiteList

But if Ed does not have the time to do it, I think we are out of luck.
Beside the working compromise does not seem to require it.

I hope this is ok with everybody,
Pietro
