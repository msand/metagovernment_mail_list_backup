Here's a fun puzzle: Fast forward 2 years...

GoogleVotes has won a de-facto monopoly of public decision making,
beating out BillVotes and YahooVotes by a landslide.  To recoup its
capital and turn a profit, Google begins placing ads in the voter
interface.  Two things then happen:

  i) The users respond by voting a decision, "No more ads, please."

 ii) Later still, they use the system to reach a consensus, "We don't
     like GoogleVotes anymore, we want JoeVotes."

JoeVotes is an open ecosystem, a rag-tag mix of tools with public
APIs.  It's pretty good, but it can't compete against a natural
monopoly, because it lacks a sufficient number of voters (quorum) to
be viable.

How could Google respond? *
