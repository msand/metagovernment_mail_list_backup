Mark Frischmuth wrote:
  
> I'm pleased to report that DemocracyLab has deployed its first working beta
> (http://democracylab2.appspot.com/).  The specific purpose of the software
> is for use with Oregon 150 <http://www.oregon150.org/projects/youth-legacy/>
> 's Project 2059, designed to enable high school students to create a
> collaborative vision for Oregon's future.
>
> The software has the essential functionality we're targeting - allowing
> users to rate values, positions, and policies, and to create connections
> between relevant concepts.  It's missing a great deal of functionality that
> will be necessary to deal with an abundance of information, once that
> problem occurs.  Our development team emphasized simplicity and usability
> when creating the software, believing that once we attract users we'll be
> better able to determine next steps for development.

I was playing with it.  It's the first app I've seen that uses
federated Google Accounts.  It's a nice stepping stone, till OpenID
catches on.

My impressions as a user - just the things that hit me first:

   i) Confusion over the difference between a Position and a Policy.
      Both *sound* rather concrete, but I suspect that a Position is a
      somewhat abstract or general goal, wheras a Policy is supposed
      to be a particular action.

  ii) Impatience with the chore of rating abstract Issues, Values and
      Positions.  Why does it want to know (the user wonders)?  If it
      needs that info, then can't it be *induced* from the votes I
      place on particular, concrete questions (Policies, I guess).

      Anyway, it's only the concrete actions that I (user) care about.

And these are just quibbles:

 iii) Clicking on Postion("Tax Carbon"), I can't see the vote tally
      until after I vote.  It's annoying that it hides this
      information from me.

  iv) I can't see how many people have voted, only the proportions.
      So there's no way to judge the extent/strength of consensus or
      dissensus.  (Should be easy to fix.)
