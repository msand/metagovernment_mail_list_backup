Manuel Barkhau wrote:
> Again this can be mitigated through clustering of users. Spammers
> cluster together with very little edges connected to the rest of the
> legitimate graph. This seems to work good enough??? as demonstrated by
> googles page rank algorithm. In a social network it has been
> demonstrated with advogato.

Or consider Votorola's own trust network:

  http://zelea.com/project/votorola/a/design.xht#trust-network
  (sorry, it's rough - ask if you need info)

It's very strict.  Trust edges extend from the root (admin), via
elected registrars, out into the wider populatation.
Self-authenticating clusters are unreachable, and therefore untrusted.

A cluster that managed to get trust edges couldn't last long.  It
would attract doubt signals:

  http://zelea.com/project/votorola/a/design.xht#doubt-signaling

Its edges would then be pulled by the voters - or, failing that, by
the registrars.
