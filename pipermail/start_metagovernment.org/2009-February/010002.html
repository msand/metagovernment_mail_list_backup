Mark Murphy, excerpting his earlier email, wrote:
> This is a system of issues ... an positions taken by citizens on
> those issues.
> 
> John Q. Citizen can raise an issue by posting a Web page marked up in a
> POSH format, providing the title and description of the issue, the
> community to which it applies...

Here's the definition of an "issue" - what I call a "poll", and others
call a "proposal" or "resolution".  This is what you'd get, for
example, after processing Mark's POSH (or RDFa), to extract the
embedded data:

  http://zelea.com/project/votorola/a/poll/leader.rdf

Here's the data structure.  I only define the parts that I happen to
need, at the moment (the "leader config"):

  http://zelea.com/project/votorola/a/poll/Leader.xsd

And here's the semantics of it:
 
  http://zelea.com/project/votorola/a/poll/Poll.rdf

Those are potential inter-project standards, cutting across
specialized facilities.  So, for example, discussion facilities may
use them to encode the topics of discussion, and to correlate them
with voting results; and voting facilities may use them to pool the
results; and so on.

(You know, it's gotta be open, in the end.  There's no other way.
 Whatever time we put into the internals of our own projects - coding,
 documenting, promoting - is a high-risk investment.  Most of the
 projects are going to fail, and disappear without a trace.

 On the other hand, whatever we contribute to the open ecosystem, it
 can't ever be lost.  Because that's the future.)
