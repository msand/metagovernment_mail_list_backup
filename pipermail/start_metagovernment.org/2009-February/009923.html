Within reason. The "basic rule" being disputed is the concept of a
human champion vs an idea as the champion.

That "basic rule" may well be universal. I cannot think of an example
where it does not apply. The nearest is the example of Open Source
software, but even then we still have the heroic Stallmans and
Torvalds', fighting against the evil Gates'.

But if we are successful then we are writing our own history. There is
no need to be meek or falsely modest about it. The goal is that
significant.


> We are no different from any other group.  We have an idea.

philosophical debate about whether one should abandon ones ideals in
order to reach them - so I doubt we'll get anywhere discussing that.

But what you haven't answered, is why we "sure as hell" need a leader
if we'll have a working end-user version of Metascore within a few
months?
