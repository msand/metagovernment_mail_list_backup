> Dear all,
> Few days ago we had a chat meeting with the metagovernment group
> (members? gang?).
> I presented my affiliation to this project and offered to be the
> interface with them, since I was de facto bringing on ideas that were
> being developed with them. There was some interest and we agreed that
> I would have started writing up some ideas on the metagovernment wiki.
> This page should sum up the metagovernent position, and (more
> relevantly to this discussion) present ideas that can be used in our
> proposal. The page is work in progress, still since some of you are
> writing up some ideas, I invite you all to read them here:
> http://metagovernment.org/wiki/FP7
>
> The inter-community distributed idea was something that was presented
> in this very list. A similar idea was also presented in
> metagovernment, as having various versions of the whole community
> being present. I am personally not an expert on the topic and I only
> list it there to differentiate respect to the -Intercommunity-
> distribution system. That is the software technology that would define
> the comunications between different instances of the software, used by
> different communities.
> I think the two problems (intercommunity comunication and
> intracommunity comunication) are ultimately linked. At the most
> granular level we are dealing with a set of objects, each object is an
>
> "action" that a user does in a community
>
> And the possible actions will be like: (1) presenting a proposal, ?(2)
> voting for a proposal, (3) tagging a proposal, (4) commenting a
> proposal.
>
> We then have to handle how does the information (proposals, votes,
> tags and comments) spread inside a community, and between communities.
>
> Please also note the work on Tags, and the "Developing the theory of a
> sophisticated scoring system" which was suggested by one of our
> members Matias Battocchia.
>
> Please let me know what you think of all this, and how can we move forward.
> I shall stay in Rome on the first week of March.
>

> Pietro
