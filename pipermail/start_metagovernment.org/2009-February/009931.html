I proposed to Ed the same thing as Robert once. His position about this
issue has always been the same and we can not neglect the existence of
progress, because from that time to today work has been done. So a leader is
not strictly necessary (in this case); anybody who really wants to
contribute can realize a job according to his/her abilities and time, one
he/she believes valuable. Although, it is true that coordination between
workers and task-planning produce something bigger, it is synergetic!
Maybe we should try to listen to Robert and have a leader, a person whose
function is to make coordination possible —an organizer, I like to say—. And
maybe we should think as Ed too, and let the organization to emerge from our
common vision and individual acts. So...

We may do this: use the
Schulze<http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Schulze_method>voting method to
select "orders to follow" from an "orders pool" (some kind
of brainstormed list). The group will lead itself. Was this a SYNTHESIS?
