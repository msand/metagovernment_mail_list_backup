On Feb 7, 2009, at 6:26 AM, Manuel Barkhau wrote:

> I do have a schema for secret ballot. You can verify your vote and
> everybody can verify the outcome.

We can also potentially use anonymous users as a form of "secret  
ballot."

I finally came up with a response to Michael's earlier criticism of  
anonymous users in a user-scoring system. The criticism was that  
anyone could set up a host of anon users and have them all rank each  
other positively, thus horribly skewing the system.

As an answer... we could simply allow anon users to propose  
legislation and to make comments, but not to apply scores. Sure, this  
then doesn't qualify as a "ballot" per se, but it does allow people  
who may be frightened of reprimand to nonetheless make their voices  
heard. I believe this qualifies to Murphy's Nyms standard.
