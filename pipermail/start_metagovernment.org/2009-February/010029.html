Welcome Gerold,

> I am from Austria and started a page presenting my ideas at 
> http://www.opentution.org which has the same goal as metagovernment. The idea 
> for the platform came after the european constitution got rejected by the 
> french and dutch people four years ago which started a lot of discussion about 
> it. The main complaint at that time was that the constituion was developed 
> with the people excluded. With that I realised that there is actually no 
> possibility to take part in a development process for laws and thought that 
> maybe the use of open source technics in this area could solve that problem.

You allow competing drafts - variants of a single proposal.  So there
will initially be dissensus.  Are there any mechanisms to afford
(enable and promote) a consensus, on a single draft?

How do you guard against the "bias", as Tim here decribes it, "towards
those relatively few entries [variants] that manage to gain an early
lead in net votes"?

  http://groups.dowire.org/groups/consult/messages/topic/2IbPilDgy4CLnyMHbSjPLB

In other words, how can a newly introduced idea - a better variant -
climb to the top of the heap?

> My goal now is to implement such a system for austrian politics in the way 
> that people first make proposals independently of the government and then if 
> the proposals are good enough and accepted by a broad band of people from the 
> platform the document will be sent to the government. My hope is that 
> governments accept the proposals because of the good quality this kind of 
> system will produce. Reason comes from the good quality of Wikipedia.

Votes are maybe the best measure of quality.  If a proposal has a
stable consensus - stable, mind - then politicians are going to pay
attention.
