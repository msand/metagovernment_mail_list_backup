Here's a free collection of essays, based on a conference in 2008.
It's interesting, worth a scan:
http://rebooting.personaldemocracy.com/

  How would completely reorganizing our system of representation work?
  Is it possible to redesign our government with open doors and
  see-through walls? How can we leverage the exponential power of
  many-to-many deliberation for the common good?"

I've been thinking about one of the essays - Mark Murphy's 'The
"Killer App" of Public Participation'.  He defines 9 principles for
such an application:

  1. Openess - user access, no front filtering
  2. Public - no secret ballot
  3. Standing - local residency authenticated
  4. Nyms - secret ballot as an option, 2 notwithstanding
  5. Framing - anybody can raise an issue for voting
  6. Structure - interface to government, assuring action
  7. Unencumbered - as in open source
  8. Unimpeded - by government
  9. Self-policing - by voters

Votorola scores 8.  We're remiss on item 4, because we haven't yet
documented the design for a secret ballot option.  What's the "Murphy
Score" of the other projects on the list - DemocracyLab, MetaScore,
and so forth?
