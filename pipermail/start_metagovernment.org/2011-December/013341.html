On Dec 4, 2011, at 3:54 AM, Michael Allan wrote:

> If the "rust filter" is personal (as I think you suggested) and not
> central, then I agree with the solution.  Here again, it's a socially
> mediated solution (free interpretation) as opposed to a systematic
> one.  The raw, unfiltered results are still visible to everyone else.

Would a partially-systemic solution necessarily be bad? What about employing a "ping" of some kind on polls which are long-lived and/or on ones in which the front-runners are shifting positions. So under certain circumstances, such as a leading delegate radically changing proposals, everyone voting in the poll is asked to affirm their current position or have it deprecated? The ping could be on-demand only, sort of like when a status quo political candidate candidate asks for a recount.
