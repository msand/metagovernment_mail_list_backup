Hi Alex,

I did not actually know range voting before you presented it.

Shulze, is a form of Condorcet.
Condorcet and Borda tend to require people to list all proposals in order.

Part of the problem with them is that it is complex for people to vote.
Range voting looks easier to use than either of the two.
And much easier to program it too (no need to use JS).
And it takes more info than a simple approval voting.

On the other side I never heard of it.
After a long search I could find a copy of the original studies in the
internet archive.

I added it to my notes in the wiki:
http://www.metagovernment.org/wiki/User:Pietro_Speroni_di_Fenizio

We should make a wiki page about range voting and link it to the
(never concluded) page on voting theory. Also adding the pdf of the
file.

On the page you sent us there is some mathematical claims that shows
Range voting as way better than anything before. I need to look into
those slowly and ask to some colleagues that are more inside the
voting theory field, but it looks very promising.I will consider
adding it to Vilfredo as well.

Many thanks,
Pietro

>> Yes. And if you explicitly were for the previous candidate of your
>> switching
>> endcandidate, then you should have voted for her in the first place,
>> because
>> you always take the closest position (can be due to social connections
>> also).
>> If the switch happens because of some corruption, then there will be
>> enough
>> time to realize the change. A single voter can already wake up the other

>> by

>> but in the content and subject of the position and plan where the
