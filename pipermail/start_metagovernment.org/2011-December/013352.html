I'd love to cooperate.  Although it might be difficult to get that
started, I feel certain that a technical monopoly is *impossible* in
this field.  So *some* kind of cooperation is in the future.

Or if we can't make it happen alone, then maybe Occupy can help us.
Stephen and I were discussing how to kick-start alpha trials (another
need Occupy can meet), and we had the idea of housing both initiatives
under a single workgroup (? or something) in Occupy itself.  See:
http://www.nycga.net/groups/structure/forum/topic/what-about-the-people-who-dont-have-time-to-go-to-general-assemblies-enough/?topic_page=5&num=15#post-12578

I bet just the *idea* of avoiding a Facebook/Twitter-like monopoly of
e-democracy will be enough to attract a few motivated activists (lots
of them in Occupy, naturally).  With their help, we could use the
tools (even as they are) to get *all* technical projects to cooperate,
just enough to ensure the survival of *each*.

What do you think?  It would be great if you guys could help, because
we'd definitely hit the ground running.
