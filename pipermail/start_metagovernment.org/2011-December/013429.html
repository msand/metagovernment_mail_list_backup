> On this paper there was also this passage:
>
> "The issues discussed in this paper are totally independent of the
> puzzling question of how to [...].
> Neither will Range (nor Condorcet-LR) voting solve such societal
> problems as unwise voters and inaccurate vote tallying*.
>
> *For example, R.M. Nixon was re-elected US president in 1972 by a
> "landslide": he got 61% of the vote, the second highest percentage
> ever recorded. Essentially every voting system would have declared him
> the winner. However since Nixon then became the only president to
> resign, with most of the key members of his cabinet jailed on
> corruption charges, most people would agree this was not the best
> possible decision."
>
> Which I see it as another reason why any form of representative voting
> is dangerous. Although I might understand how if the voters were able
> to take away their vote the damage would be diminished (but not
> avoided).
