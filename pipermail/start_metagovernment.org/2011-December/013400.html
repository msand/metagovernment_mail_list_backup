Ah no...
..i think all voters have to decide for themselves if they think a change
was minor or huge and they can act on that insight as they please.
(voters are probably notified when a change occurs)

But in case a voter doesnt act at all, vote rusting will melt away his vote
on a single proposal.
If he then decides to reinforce a vote, it will reset the rusting for that
vote, but not his other votes. He has to reinforce every vote on its own.

I dont know about the speed of rusting, but as long as the proposal that
was originally voted on does not change, it should be very very slow.

So, ...all I am saying is, that if a proposal changes (through delegation
or through editing of the author) AND the voter doesnt act at all, vote
rusting should be accelerated compared to vote rusting on a not-changed
proposal.
=> So in case a voter is completely ignorant of ongoing actions which
change the proposal he voted for by "a lot of lines", vote rusting is the
backup mechanism.

Dont you think in case of complete long time inactivity of a voter who
voted for proposal A and B, vote for A should rust faster than vote for B,
in case of proposal A was changed by a lot of lines through editing and
further delegation, while proposal B still remains in its original version?
