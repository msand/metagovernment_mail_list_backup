> On Sunday, December 11, 2011 11:42:24 PM alex at twister11.de wrote:
> > As far as i know, if you shift your vote from ur proposal to the proposal
> > of someone else, u take all ur voter with you.
> >
> > Thats why "Vote Rusting" or I think even better "Change-dependent
> > accelerated vote rusting" would make sense.
> > Your voters might be informed that you shifted your vote and so they
> > hopefully reinforce their vote or else the accelerated vote rusting,
> > depending on the amount of change between ur proposal and the proposal
> that
> > you shifted to, will sooner or later melt your support away.
> >
> > thats also an additional incentive for you to negotiate with the person
> you
> > want to shift to, because you dont only should take your oppinion into
> > account, but also the oppinion of the supporters of your proposal.
>
> Yes. And if you explicitly were for the previous candidate of your
> switching
> endcandidate, then you should have voted for her in the first place,
> because
> you always take the closest position (can be due to social connections
> also).
> If the switch happens because of some corruption, then there will be enough
> time to realize the change. A single voter can already wake up the other
> "rust" ones, so a hard shift is unlikely. But corruption has to be found by
> the voters themselves, because it does not lie in the formal voting
> process,
> but in the content and subject of the position and plan where the
> corruption
> of this very plan can happen. This cannot ultimately be fixed by voting
> mechanisms, they only allow to form a collective will to deal with these
> issues and form a social structure including voting on issues to avoid
> them.
>
> We can also mail all voters before the final deadline of the vote to check
> their position and if they don't confirm then ignore them (not mechanical
> solution compared to the rust algorithm, which assumes inactive users are
> uninterested users in the end and then bug the inactive/occupied user with
> confirmation requests).
>
> Instead of making this issue a core one and impose a specific technological
> concept on it, I would rather focus on what keeps the active users
> involved,
> because this is the real drive behind fair participation and social
> mechanism
> to clean up the rust voters. If somebody is not interested, you cannot
> formally force him/her to be that or to leave, you can only define a
> mechanism
> to sort him/her out in the plan itself. This is already a bad scenario and
> shouldn't be standard on a successful e-democracy platform imho. As I
> said, a
> single voter recognizing the corruption is enough.
