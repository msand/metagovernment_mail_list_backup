Quoting your blog: 
"To facilitate coordination and organisation so that these systems may become 
robust, usable and interoperable in order to form the basis for grass-roots 
decision making and organisation. It appears that the technology and the 
momentum are building, and the time has come to act and be the change we want 
to see, by building the tools and putting the practices to the test."

We have tried to address this problem for the voting infrastructure with (1) 
and in general with (2). If you look besides communication media like diaspora 
or friendica, you will find that voting mechanisms in itself fit in between 
these. No reason to duplicate, but also no reason to directly hardwire some 
voting system in each communication medium. Instead we propose to address the 
issue of (delegated) voting in a modular open network approach. This allows 
different decision systems to coexist even in the same communication or 
drafting media, fitting to the grass roots nature of social movements. To make 
decision mechanisms interesting, a common network is necessary, otherwise the 
small user base of a specific tool quickly limits the audience.
