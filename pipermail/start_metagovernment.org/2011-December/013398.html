> No, I don't think it is good to reward voters, who vote in many polls.

No, i think each vote rustes individually. So if a voter votes for hundreds
of proposals, he has to refresh each one seperately.
The acceleration of rusting also depends on the amount of change.

> Yes, exactly! So how, if we would make this a social desicion right from
the start ... and not an automatic one?

I dont get you. Whether a change is huge or not or whether a poll is
corrupted or not should be a social decision and people will realize the
corruption and change the tree structure to exclude the corrupted node at
the top of the tree. The rusting mechanism is just a fallback mechanism.

Chosen proposals should be reinforced on a regular basis aand rusts away
otherwise. The rusting should be very very slow, but should accelerate as
soon as the proposal is changed.
This means, that when the path from me to the root node of the tree i voted
for (<= maybe read that twice ;)) changes, a voter should confirm this
change.
If he doesnt, the rusting accelerates by the degree of difference between
the proposal he initially voted for compared to the proposal that is active
after a change took place. (e.g. by the delegate editing his proposal OR by
the delegate delegating his vote to someone else)
The acceleration can be undone as soon as changes are confirmed by the
voter.

What do you think? Is it clear what i mean?
