>> Let me answer your suggestion:

>> >> The 5 people did not have to give him the vote again.
>> >
>> > At them moment it is like you say, Pietro. But it would be a relativly
>> easy
>> > feature to include: "My vote gets automatically withdrawn, when you
>> change
>> > your postion a bit/ a lot/ completely ..." So this is no question of
>> > concept.
>>
>> I am afraid it would not be enough. Let me explain:
>>
>> The problem in a democracy is two fold. On the one side you want that
>> your vote is counted, that you can change it, that you can collaborate
>> in the drafting and so on.
>> On the other side you want that also the vote of other people is
>> counted and represents correctly what they feel/believe/think.
>>
>> If my vote is correctly counted, but the other people vote is not
>> because they are too lazy to keep track of it, now the problem raises
>> is what happens to all this power left untaped.
>>
>> If that power remains attached to someone (because they simply could
>> not be bothered to update it), the result is that I will live in a
>> democracy dominated by the people that have convinced the lazy buggers
>> to vote for them, and then forget about it. This is not a democracy I
>> want to live in, and I bet is not a democracy where YOU would want to
>> live in either.
>>
>> So what should happen with the heavy power of the lazy voters?
>> (It's heavy because it takes a lot to move it.  But it's also heavy
>> because once it has moved it is hard to change it again.)
>> My idea is that the right of vote should be proportional to how
>> willing is a person to be bothered to let his voice be heard.
>> If you a better suggestion that satisfies my need not to be governed
>> by the people who are too lazy to update their vote, I would be happy
>> to hear it. But not a rusting system, that only limits the damage, but
>> does not eliminate it.
>>
>> As such I am not just having a requirement about my voice, but also
>> about how others should be allowed to organise between them. In this I
>> am stepping beyond my direct circle. I am saying: not only I want my
>> voice to be heard, but I do not want that Ed (for example) is allowed
>> (legally or technically) to give to Thomas the right to vote for him
>> more than one time. If Ed wants someone else to vote for him he should
>> repeat his desire each time.
>>
>> I know this sounds unfair, what right do I have to discuss the way Ed
>> and Thomas organise between them?
>>
>> But, you see, I do have to live in the society generated also by
>> Thomas and Ed interaction. So their relation is effectively touching
>> me. And if the result of their relation is that I will be governed by
>> people only because some other people are too lazy to follow what is
>> going on, I oppose such organization.
>>
>> And this is why I also cannot support a system that counts voting
>> independently on how they are gathered. As my understanding is that
>> Outcast does.
>>
>> But I am willing to change my position once someone convinces me of a
>> better option.
>>
>>
>> >
>> >
>> >>> So the only way an actor can become a "politician" is if they draft a
>> >>> proposal on every issue, and if each of their supporters decides to
>> vote for
>> >>> them each time.
>> >
>> >
>> > I agree with Ed here.
>>
>> Let's say that I am an actor with a vast support. I support a certain
>> very popular position (rape is illegal even between married couples in
>> muslim countries). I in this way gather million of people supporting
>> me supporting that position. As the position slightly changes (is sex
>> by surprise rape?), people keep on supporting me, not to have to work
>> in following that position. Eventually I gather a lot of people who
>> are willing to support me on any issue ("I like this guy, he sings
>> such a KOOL songs, and I totally DIG him when he was ..., and my
>> supporting him I can also support the really cool social fight he is
>> participating in"). Then I (actor) receive an offer, someone offers me
>> a million dollar if I give my support for their proposal (and
>> subsequent drafts). I secretly accept, I support them, and with me
>> come most of my supporters.
>>
>> Yes, this would happen;
>> yes, often this would lead to an uproar from the public;
>> no, the uproar would not happen all the time;
>> (Is the time when it does not happen that worries me)
>>
>> So a proxy voting is essentially a system where people by default will
>> give their vote away, and then have to work each time to make sure it
>> was used wisely. Instead we need a system where by default the vote is
>> nil. And people need to work (by voting) to make themselves be heard.
