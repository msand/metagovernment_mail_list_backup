I have found that instead of reorganising all the content, often it is
enough to have an
"if you are new" page.
The page describes briefly the prject (like a summary or an abstract)
but then sends to an ordered list of pages
where all the details are presented.

By the time a person has read all the pages linked to this first page
(i.e. a serch on depth 1, for the IT geeks),
they will have read all the (important?) pages of the wiki and of the
project. In fact in this way we are not bound to only write links to
the wiki, but also to other pages around the web.

For example, my presentation in videolectures was not specifically on
metagovernment, but if we had a presentation available somwhere in the
net that might be a really good to start.

Cheers,
Pietro
