> Hello,
> want to bring everybody's attention to what has been going on in the last
> year from a political point of view.
>
> Let's look at some elements:
> A) Obama is using the net to comunicate weekly what he is doing.
>
> This is not only important as a one way informatin, but as a two way
> information.
> People are allowed to comment, and more important than this are allowed to
> vote on other people's comment. This by itself is revolutionary.
>
> B) In Italy the minister goes no youtube and asks for feedback. Similar
> situation.
> C) another minister does the same on facebook.
>
> And today something which is subtly the next step has also happened:
> a politican (Antonio DI Pietro), (a strong opposer to Berlusconi) with a
> Blog, facebook account, and so on, has joined a facebook group
> called:"fatti
> processare buffone".
> (Litterally, "let yourself be processed, clown", a group inspired on what a
> man shouted to Berlusconi years ago).
>
> Details here, although the english version is not yet avaiable:
> http://www.antoniodipietro.com/2008/12/lillusionista.html
>
> This is very another small revolution. Here you have a key player of the
> modern italian political sceene, who not only writes in his blog (1 way),
> reads comments (2 way), read comments that have votes (1 to many), but...
> follows and joins "somone" unknown in his initiative. Who started the
> group?
> I don't know, it does not matter.
>
> But the group, and the initiative has now been followed by many people,
> among them a major italian politician.
>
> In a sense part of what we were envisioning for metagovernment, the fact
> that ideas became more important than single politicians, is starting to
> come true.
>
> What do you expect the next steps will be in this direction?
>
