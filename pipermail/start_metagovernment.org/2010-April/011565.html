Dear Metagov Colleagues, DOC's open gov Goal
<http://xml.gov/stratml/carmel/DOCOpenwStyle.xml#_795c42d4-519f-11df-9dc8-39
4c7a64ea2a>  IV is to "Adopt leading practices in proactively soliciting
participation by the public [and] increase employee participation in the
formulation of significant policies and strategic plans."

 

Their Objective
<http://xml.gov/stratml/carmel/DOCOpenwStyle.xml#_795c44f0-519f-11df-9dc8-39
4c7a64ea2a>  IV.1 includes  "post[ing] additional strategic questions on
[their] Open Government website for public input."  (It seems to me enough
questions already exist and that what is needed are more strategic answers,
i.e., documented goals and objectives.)

 

Their Objective
<http://xml.gov/stratml/carmel/DOCOpenwStyle.xml#_795c4d24-519f-11df-9dc8-39
4c7a64ea2a>  IV.5 is to "Evaluate the wide array of ideation tools and
launch a solution by January 2011."  (Ideas are a dime a dozen.  Good
implementations are rare and, thus, valuable.  Good implementations require
good plans.) 

 

And their Objective
<http://xml.gov/stratml/carmel/DOCOpenwStyle.xml#_795c4f22-519f-11df-9dc8-39
4c7a64ea2a>  IV.6 is to "Ensure that open source offerings are fully
considered during procurement processes."

 

I plan to suggest that they begin maintaining their own open gov plan in
StratML format.  Beyond that, these objectives sound like opportunities for
Metagov.  It will be interesting to see whether we can make anything of
those opportunities.

 

Owen
