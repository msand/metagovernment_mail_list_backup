Task 1a (centralizing in a multi-area wiki) is nearly done:
http://u.zelea.com/w/User:Mike-ZeleaCom/G/p/vop
Here's what I propose, to finish it off:

   i) Blank the main page: http://u.zelea.com/w/Site:Main_page
      We have a multi-area wiki - many states, cities, organizations -
      but each is supposed to be moving to a separate wiki in future.
      There'll be no main page, then.

      The current navigational content of the main page could be
      copied to a particular area, such as http://u.zelea.com/w/G
      Afterwards, we might use the main page to explain what we're
      doing in this wiki, or just leave it blank.

  ii) All subpages under a particular area get the same top-left
      image, as though they were already hosted in a separate wiki.
      Clicking on the image will take you to the area page (not the
      main page).  Ditto for the main-page link in the sidebar.

      Votorola (for its part) will display the area image at the
      top-left of voting pages (with the same link functionality).

      Each area will be sovereign in its subpages (do as you will).

 iii) The logotipo extension will be removed.  It's inefficient and
      the external images are target for abuse.  Any page-specific
      images that are needed may be uploaded in the usual way and
      inserted as page content.

Are these changes OK?  Anything else needed for multi-area support?
