Dear Metagov Colleagues, perhaps you already know about MS's TownHall
<http://code.msdn.microsoft.com/TownHall>  but this is the first I've heard
of it.  While they
<http://microsoftontheissues.com/cs/blogs/mscorp/archive/2010/04/19/microsof
t-townhall-building-public-online-communities.aspx>  suggest social
networking tools are maturing and what is needed is an even greater level of
engagement, in my view the tools remain highly immature and most of us
already have more (relatively unproductive) "engagements" than we can
handle.

 

In that sense it seems to me tools like TownHall are tantamount to "paving
the cow path" with respect to old-fashioned discussion and debate, which may
be "fun" (now we can even do it on our all-purposeful cell phones . isn't
that wonderful!) but tends to lead to greater polarization, if not gridlock,
when what is truly needed are new and better (super highway) tools to help
people *work together* *more productively* to *allocate (their own)
resources* and *exert (their own) efforts* to accomplish *common objectives*
(and to measure and report to stakeholders the degrees to which they are
able to do so).

 

If the MS folks are seriously open to greater engagement with folks like me
(which I doubt), I will suggest they incorporate the Strategy Markup
Language (StratML) standard (ANSI/AIIM 21:2009) into a more mature and
potentially productive version of TownHall.  Unless and until it supports
something very much like the open StratML standard, TownHall looks to me
like yet another "pretty face <http://www.microsofttownhall.com/> " in the
crowd . or dare I say yet another attempt to put lipstick on a (proprietary)
pig.
