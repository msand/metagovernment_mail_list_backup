Dear Pledgebank Team, I subscribed to the Metagov listserv to explore
prospects for facilitating feedback on goals and objectives rendered in
Strategy Markup Language (StratML) format.  Via the Metagov listserv, I
learned of your service.  While any feedback may be better than no feedback,
meaningful offers of assistance may be the best kind of feedback.

 

I encourage you to consider enabling usage of Pledgebank to enable
individuals to offer their time, expertise, money, and other resources
toward the realization of goals and objectives rendered in StratML format.

 

The purposes of the StratML standard (ANSI/AIIM 21:2009) are outlined at
http://xml.gov/stratml/index.htm#DefinitionPurposes  (We hope it will
eventually become an ISO standard.)  Thus far, nearly 600 strategic plans
<http://xml.gov/stratml/drybridge/index.htm>  have been rendered in StratML
format, including the plans of more than 260 nonprofit, public
<http://xml.gov/stratml/drybridge/index.htm#Nonprofits>  service
organizations.  The prototypical StratML-enabled services that have been
developed so far are listed at http://xml.gov/stratml/index.htm#Services
However, none of them yet leverages the StratML <Identifier
<http://xml.gov/stratml/draft/StratMLGlossary.xml#Identifier> > element to
enable feedback on individual goal and objective statements.  (Joe Carmel's
XForms form automatically creates GUID IDs for each goal and objective
statement contained in plans authored or edited using the form
<http://xmldatasets.net/XF2/stratmlxform3.xml> .)

 

If, by any chance, this is an opportunity Pledgebank may wish to pursue, the
open <http://xml.gov/stratml/drybridge/index.htm#OpenGovDirective>
government plans that U.S. federal agencies have been directed to compile
and maintain might provide an excellent focus for such feedback services.
See, for example, the "Participation" goals/objectives in each of the plans
currently in the collection:  USDA
<http://xml.gov/stratml/carmel/USDAOpenwStyle.xml#_98d8978c-438c-11df-937e-f
67d7a64ea2a> , DOC
<http://xml.gov/stratml/carmel/DOCOpenwStyle.xml#_dd096db8-51a7-11df-97ed-6f
527a64ea2a> , DHS
<http://xml.gov/stratml/carmel/DHSOpenwStyle.xml#_133e7786-4c24-11df-aa6e-a0
287a64ea2a> , HHS
<http://xml.gov/stratml/carmel/HHSOpenwStyle.xml#_62f3348c-4be9-11df-b83a-83
487a64ea2a> , DOI
<http://xml.gov/stratml/carmel/DOIOpenwStyle.xml#_3c2dac6c-50e7-11df-9b47-32
717a64ea2a>  & VA
<http://xml.gov/stratml/carmel/VAOpenwStyle.xml#_3d454106-47e5-11df-a5f9-b14
c7a64ea2a> .   (As soon as time allows, I plan to convert all of the
agencies' open gov plans to open StratML format.  Hopefully, consistent with
the spirit of objective
<http://xml.gov/stratml/carmel/OGDwStyle.xml#_7d88050e-142e-11df-a454-3f207a
64ea2a>  1.b contained in the Open Gov Directive, agencies will eventually
maintain their own plans in StratML format.)

 

Needless to say, if you do decide to take up this challenge, I will be more
than happy to assist in any way of which I am capable.

On Wed, Apr 21, 2010 at 9:34 AM, Ed Pastore <epastore at metagovernment.org>
wrote:

 

I would be likely to be interested in joining your panel, if you would be
interested in a sort of devil's advocate (from the far side of the
spectrum). That is, while I think the general idea of the Open Government
Directive is laudable, I also think it is deceptively short-sighted. It is
wonderful that the public is being invited into more of the machinations of
the bureaucracy of the Federal government, but there is very little being
done to promote the core of democracy: inviting people into the
decision-making processes of the legislature and executive (or even into the
final decision-making of the bureaucracy). I also am critical of the
top-down Federal approach, when the real downfall of closed government
happens at the local levels, where corruption and secrecy often run rampant.

 

So if you would like that perspective on your panel, I'd be happy to
consider joining you. If you think it would dilute focus... I understand
that as well. :)
