I hope we're not duplicating other efforts.  I don't think we are, so
it *is* kind of exciting.  One possible approach for anyone who is
able to contribute:

 1. Imagine your ideal e-democracy.  The infrastructure is wide open,
    so just about anything goes here (or at least that's the
    meta-ideal).

 2. Climb into the common infrastructure (rough as it is) and start
    doing it.  You'll soon be blocked by something, or lack thereof.

 3. Discuss the block so we all understand what the problem is.

 4. Work with us on a solution.

    (back to 2)

This is alpha development.  I think we have e-democracy (as a whole)
at the alpha stage.  If so, a core of motivated users should be able
to drive the development forward at a reasonable pace (weekly or
monthly cycles), fast enough to keep us all motivated.
