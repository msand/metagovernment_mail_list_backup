"In programming, as in many fields, the hard part isn't solving problems,
but deciding what problems to solve." (http://www.paulgraham.com/gh.html)

A grassroot-oriented version of Metascore could be of great help: I remember
some math's classes where in order to reach the most general solution to a
problem, an specific one must be found first. While you are at your office,
club or other small organization try to respond yourself: How Metascore
should be *here*? The abstractness with which we deal daily when thinking
about the software could disappear in great part; and deciding what problems
to solve, easier.

In http://www.metagovernment.org/wiki/Metascore/PrototypeB you can write
your answer to that question. We don't have to code this prototype, just
design it. It's like a useful exercise.
