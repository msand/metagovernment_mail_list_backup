> the
> first question that pops into my mind is how you hand out initial
> whuffie and prevent inflation.

I think this is a question for Pietro (ie, a mathematician). :)

But if scores are something that are spent as much as they are  
accumulated (an idea used to some extent in Whitehouse2), then it may  
be a self-solving problem. If it costs you fractional points to give  
points to someone else AND it also costs you points to vote on  
propositions, then there should be enough scarcity of points that it  
won't devalue over time.

As for what initial score to hand out... I think it would be more  
likely to start at zero and to gradually accumulate based on  
participation.
