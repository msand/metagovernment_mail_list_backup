Manuel, thanks you for encouraging your Metagovernment colleagues to consider the potential benefits of the emerging StratML standard.

StratML is not just for "government transparency"; we aim to establish it as an international voluntary consensus standard for potential use by *all organizations* worldwide.

Consider these relationships to the description of Metagovernment:

"This form of democracy, called open source governance, does not entail traditional voting or majority rule. Instead, people may help govern any community as much or as little as they wish by creating, discussing, and supporting proposals."

StratML will enable anyone to propose goals and objectives in which they may wish to engage others, and they can post StratML documents anywhere on the Web.  Hopefully, StratML will also facilitate the establishment of metrics by which progress can be measured in the pursuit of such goals and objectives. 

"User input is weighed by other users through a scoring system and brought to the attention of other participants interested in that input."

This is the very kind of feedback we hope to enable through StratML, so that folks aren't forced to wade through voluminous PDF files to find goals and objectives of interest to them.  However, particularly with respect to voluntary and charitable organizations, the most important feedback is the amount of resources that supporters are willing and able to contribute toward realization of the objectives of interest to them.

Thanks again.
