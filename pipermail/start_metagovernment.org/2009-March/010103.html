On Mar 24, 2009, at 4:21 PM, Pietro Speroni di Fenizio wrote:

> I find it interesting that when I got involved with greenpeace, many
> years ago, I eventually left for the very hierarchical nature of the
> organization, the way in which information was not spread, the way few
> people would decide who would act and when, and the way money was
> handled.
>
> Was it more open I would have kept on working with them.

In most nonprofits, there is a fear among the founders (or later on,  
by a "cabal" of insiders) that if they open up too much, they will  
lose the project vision. With Metagovernment, I have found that my  
original vision has indeed been changed repeatedly by the open  
group... each time for the better. The key, as I see it, was in  
developing a good set of Basic Principles and letting the open group  
expound from there.
