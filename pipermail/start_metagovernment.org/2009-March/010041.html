There was a tweed at the beginning of the Camp saying: 

"1 gov can't solve probs for 300million ppl, but 300mln people can solve 
problems for 1 gov't"

I think this brings the current situation pretty much to the point.

Am Sonntag 01 März 2009 schrieb Ed Pastore:
> I'm pretty tired, but want to share some initial reflections from
> Transparency Camp 09.
>
> People loved http://www.whitehouse2.org  Why? Because they could see
> it! Yes, it is nicely done, and Jim Gilliam is a good advocate for it;
> but the thing that really swayed people was its accessibility... and
> its existence.
>
> There were numerous times when I saw the ideas of Metagovernment
> blooming in people's heads. But they couldn't really grasp it because
> they couldn't see it (and because still too many people are deeply
> conditioned to think an authority figure is necessary). I am now fully
> on board with Manuel that we need software before we can promote. My
> promotions sounded like fringe-babbling without something to point to.
> No amount of publicity will be able to compare to our successful
> governance of ourselves via Metascore. >95% of people are instantly
> dismissive of governance without singular authority, so the burden is
> entirely on us to prove it before we have a chance of reaching them.
>
> People (including me) were also impressed with yourowndemocracy.org. I
> think the server is down right now, but you can also see
> http://www.gongszeto.com/ for Gong Szeto, the author. He also was quite
> eloquent and clearly a capable programmer.
>
> I invited both Jim and Gong to join the list server, and I think you
> may see them around soon. I also finally got to meet Mark Frischmuth,
> who is every bit as nice as his online persona would lead you to
> believe. And I corresponded with Craig Newmark (founder of
> Craigslist), who said he would forward info on us onward to the folks
> at PersonalDemocracyForum.
>
> That's most of what I have to report. Mostly, people were very buried
> in the status quo, and very focused on the American federal
> government, so my influence there was somewhat limited. I'm looking
> forward to the eDemocracy camp in April which will be more up our
> line. If we can have something to actually show people there, it would
> be tremendous... but even if not, I think I will have a slightly more
> receptive audience.
>
> I met with folks from several other somewhat related projects, and I
> am again convinced that our approach is on the right track. Our growth
> model from small communities, our international community, our
> flexible-organic structure, and our emphasis on synthesis all make for
> a strong offering... if only we can get it rolling. I would again urge
> everyone here to either consider contributing to the code/interface or
> to pick one way to reach out to other programmers and try to find
> someone else who will help with the code or interface of Metascore.
>
