As part of the FP7 proposal we looked at some of the state of the art
websites.

We were explained [OMISSIS] that the UK has its egovernment website.
Eventually we could find it:
It is produced by those guys:
http://www.mysociety.org/

The description is here:
http://www.mysociety.org/projects/no10-petitions-website/

It is available for reuse here:
http://www.mysociety.org/services/petitions/

And the website is here:
http://petitions.number10.gov.uk/

It is a petition website, simple and honest, where people can make
petitions and vote on others petitions. The petition has the same
political power than normal petitions (an epsilon more than null, but
they do generate an official answer from the government). I think we
should all study this website as it seem the first egov website
actually running. Most of petitions which had even a few hundreds of
supporters generated an answer. And the answer is long and detailed.

Look for example on the petition to legalise cannabis:
http://petitions.number10.gov.uk/legalisecannabis/
and the answer:
http://www.number10.gov.uk/Page12399

Most of petitions have very few people who sign. Is it a power law?

I know we can do better than that, but in its limited scope it is
still a very interesting project.

Cheers,
Pietro
