>
> What happens if I buy pornography today, and then 5 years from now
> pornography is outlawed?
>

If it is legal to buy pornography now and if it pleases you to buy, please
do so ... When it becomes illegal, you cannot be questioned for buying it
earlier, but you have moral obligation to return / destroy / account for
it!  The biometric smart card would help to curb such illegal action as
every transaction would get accounted genuinely. Arms trade, drugs trade,
money laundering etc. would vanish.

>
> What happens if I bought some cancer drugs for my mother, and then apply
> for life insurance and am declined because they think I may have cancer?
>

If your mother had cancer, it would show up in her biometric medical
records. If you bought medicines for her, it has to be so declared. On a
medical check-up, the insurance company can ascertain that you do not have
cancer and that your mother has cancer. Hence there would be no problem with
your insurance cover!

>
>
> These are just the first three things that come to my mind.  There are an
> infinite number of ways that this system could be abused.
>

Biometric smart card would leave an irrefutable / indelible proof of actions
and hence an audit trail for any one to follow any time ....

>
> Again, privacy has a long and proven history of protecting people from
> oppression.  You must find another way to eliminate corruption that protects
> people's privacy.
>

Random ID would protect privacy of those who function within the law. Law
breakers can be identified by community / society and hence would fear wrong
action. Right action need not be feared. When challenged by any one the
action has to be defended as right. If it cannot be defended, then it is
wrong and such action has to be reversed or punishment faced.

>
> To convince you to take a different path, have you looked for any
> correlation between a country's privacy index and their corruption index?
> For example, Canada is 9th of 180 on Transparency International's Corruption
> Perceptions Index.  They are 2nd on Privacy International's yearly privacy
> ranking of countries.  So clearly it is possible to have low corruption and
> high privacy.
>

That possibly speaks of the high level of ethics of the individuals, family,
community, organizations, states and nation. I am not following Canadian
ethics to comment on it. But my hunch is, the ethics followed in canada may
not be uniform throughout the country. There must be pockets of relatively
unethical action, followed in public / private life. Probably their wants
may be limited within their means and probably greed has not over taken
them!

>
>
> All this to say that, yes, eliminating all personal privacy may reduce
> corruption, but it would also eliminate many other rights and freedoms that
> humans enjoy.
>

What one does with oneself or family is privacy. But when it spills into the
public domain, then it should be transparent.

If we were being bothered by some rabbits eating our garden, we could spray
> poison from an airplane on all the country surrounding our garden, killing
> the rabbits, and many other animals and people as well, destroying the
> ecosystem entirely.  Or, we could set some small traps that only rabbits
> could trip in our own garden.  Which seems a better solution?
>


I can only go by my pet whom we lost a few months ago. I felt, he had no
worries in life. Every thing was transparent for him and he lived by the
moment and not apparently worried about the past / present / future but to
enjoy the present to the maximum.

To give another analogy, one man's food is another man's poisin. A
businessman may be saying he is doing ethical business with the customer;
but he may be using the law to his advantage when he uses his contact in the
government to reduce the import duty on his raw materials and he is able to
make a huge profit.

A multi purpose biometric smart card linked public transaction would show up
the cost of import and cost of manfacture vis-a-vis sale price and profit.
If the profit margins are high, he could well be questioned, if made public
and others can come in and reduce the price, etc.

He may even treat his contact in government to a fully paid vacation in
Hawaii!!  In the first step, both of them would not be indulging in the
wrong action, as they can get exposed that the trip was ill gotten and paid
by some one else as the biometric smart card of both would reveal the actual
situation!!
