> Thanks for checking it out. Yeah, basically it can currently do two things:
> 1) Polls/votes/elections (difference between those being mainly
> whether the results are decisive or just informative).
> 2) Find which other users have voted similarly (to help decide who to
> vote for in elections).
> 
> There are some extra features in these, for example:
> -You can add argument to your vote to explain why you voted the way
> you did and these are displayed in the summary page (1), which might
> convince others to vote the same way. It also allows you to see how the
> opponents argue for their stance, so you know which points you should
> argue if you wish to make them change their minds.
> -While most of the votes are simply yes or no since the mathematics
> gets pretty hairy as soon as there are more than two options to vote
> for, in some things like elections it is inevitable so recently I
> added the possibility to add multiple-choice questions (2). Since
> there are several methods to handle multiple-choice voting, there is
> an option to choose which method to use. Currently supports Instant
> Runoff Voting, Range voting and Schultze method. Be warned though that
> this is the most recent feature and probably pretty buggy. Also mostly
> undocumented for now.
> 
> But still, those are the two things it does, nothing more.
> 
> > Do you plan to integrate means to reach consensus/overcome
> > conflicts or do you rely upon established institutions to track them?
> 
> Probaby not at least in the near future. Apart from adding arguments
> to votes there is a simple discussion forum, but nothing more
> sophisticated than that so need to use other tools for these.
> 
> > Public tracking and documentation of assent is in our interest in general,
> > especially since technically separated communities cannot reach consensus at
> > all. (2) Do you have any ideas upon cooperation with other tools?
> 
> Haven't really thought about that until now, but that vote mirroring
> is an interesting concept. I probably could at the minimum provide
> some kind of API to read votes/questions by other voting systems,
> that should not be too difficult to do.
