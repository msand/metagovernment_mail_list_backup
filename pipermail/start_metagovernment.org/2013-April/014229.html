>     The ongoing discussion, "Helping the Pirate Party Disappear" is
>     predicated on the assumption that being governed by assemblies of
>     elected representatives is a good idea. (The discussants just
>     don't want the representatives to be elected through political
>     parties.)
>
>     Pietro has advanced in his article what I take to be a devastating
>     critique of representative democracy. The political parties
>     discussion is moot if representative democracy is a bad idea. In
>     that case, we should be working to render representative democracy
>     obsolete, not just working to render political parties obsolete.
>
>     I take this discussion about political parties to be an example of
>     a problem facing the Metagovernment community: we listen to one
>     another only selectively, and engage one another only selectively,
>     each sub-community preferring instead to spend efforts "preaching
>     to its own choir". One consequence of this is that the
>     Metagovernment community as a whole continues to not be a
>     collaborating community, but merely a conversation community (like
>     a cocktail party).
>
>     Rational discourse carried to conclusion is hard work. But it can
>     produce solid results, from the perspective of enabling productive
>     collaboration. If we wish to make significant progress within our
>     lifetimes, we need to work together.
