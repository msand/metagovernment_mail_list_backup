On Mon, Oct 27, 2008 at 12:46 AM, Mark Frischmuth <mark at democracylab.org> wrote:
> Identity authentication will be a significant challenge, though one we're
> postponing for now.  The gold standard would be cross referencing website
> registrations with voter registrations, but that's a long way off.  In the
> shorter term we are likely to implement a credibility system that attaches
> greater weight to users who provide more demographic data, and demonstrate
> through their participation patterns that they are human.

I always had in mind that a vouching system (e.g. "I know this person
to be real") would make it very difficult to create, or at least
perpetuate, fraud - as entire networks of people would be bridged to
the majority via a small number of conspirators in a statistically
anomalous fashion.

That said, I haven't done any real research upon it yet.. so it
propably has some holes - but if it could be made to work I think I
would find it personally preferable to something linked to votor
registrations. Especially as the latter can be easily (and has been
for at least the last three US elections) manipulated for political
gain.
