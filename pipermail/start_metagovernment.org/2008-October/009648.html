You should have a look at
http://video.google.com/videoplay?docid=-5092930485716426869&ei=CWCPSNfTHISm2AK4w4HyDw

The first attack I can think of is an entire group collaborating in
vouching for each other. The best solution I've heard of for this
problem, is constructing a graph and finding a path (taking vouches to
construct edges) from you to the other person, degrading the flow of
every edge as you go deeper into the graph. You can then calculate a
trust value with the flow from you to the person in question.
If nobody trusts any of the attackers, the flow to one of them should be 0.
