On Sat, Oct 11, 2008 at 2:06 AM, Michael Allan <mike at zelea.com> wrote:
> OK, but your ideals are *your* ideals.  I wanted to convince you that
> your responsibility extends beyond the expression and evolution of
> your own ideology.  It extends to enabling other people to express and
> evolve *their* ideals.  You cannot fulfill that responsibility except
> by being ideolically and politically neutral with regard to ends and
> means.

I have to say, I'm so happy to see this issue raised. I have been in
agreement with Michael for some time, and have attempted to express those
opinions on questions of whether to support or not support unweighted,
direct voting (one person, one vote) above or beyond a consensus score, and
on questions of whether to support or not support political parties and
other groups/organizations in the interface. Probably the best summary of
the debate would be the snippet of a conversation from May, 2008 I have
included below.

I would say, this is a question we should address as a group regarding our
approach to developing software that may be used to mediate
politically-charged discussions, if not implement legislation: Do we want to
encourage citizens to engage in a constitutional convention, developing the
structure of their own government?? Or, are we attempting to promote a view
(ie, no voting) that people can choose to use or not use at their will??

My guess is that the 'meta-' in metagovernment implies that the government
governs itself -- ie, that it can evolve. And I would argue strongly for
developing software that anticipates this evolution through extension and
modification (perhaps with a modular design). Particularly for projects such
as metascore where the application may not be solely confined to governance.
