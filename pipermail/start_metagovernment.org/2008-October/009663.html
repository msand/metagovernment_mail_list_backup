>> reputation based upon quality of ideas (rather than other things

>> etc).  This presents a great opportunity IMHO...to introduce

>
> I think an important point is that the filtering algorithm need not be
> static/global/corruptible, but may allow for different perspectives
> potentially unique and dynamic to each user. For example, a home page
> may display multiple "Top 5 proposals which..." boxes fulfilling the
> following:
>
> * Are from people who vote in similar ways as you do (issues you might
> want to support)
> * Are from people who vote in entirely opposite ways as you do (issues
> you might want to fight)
> * Are from people who vote in the most bipartisan fashion - highest
> similarity with global voting patterns
> * Are showing rapid spread through the voter network (hot issues)
> * Have parameters limited to the last X hours/days/months
> * Are being voted upon by your favourite
> celebrities/scientists/friends/religious leaders
> * Etc
> * Any combination of above.
>
> Allowing the user to ultimately choose which perspectives they are
> interested in, I hope, does away with the majority of Darwins
> concerns. If a user wishes to participate in a limited interest-group
> fashion then the software should not deny this usage, but neither
> should it limit a user who wants to use it in a multitude of other
> interesting ways.

Note that many of these functions may be possible through Manuel's  
existing concept of a robust tagging system (possibly combined with  
add-ons to provide these "custom" functions).

Also note that conclusions reached in long conversations on this list  
should be summarized on the wiki and/or the dev list, otherwise good  
ideas developed here might not make it into the code. :)
