I'm keen on stories, so I prefer Derrek's overview:

  https://clearspace.democracylab.org/thread/1002

With respect to norms (laws, plans, policies), your use case is
similar to Votorola's.  Users collectively assent to (and I guess
draft) a policy position.  It is then acted upon by Congress (etc.).

If you add nomination and assent for Congressmen, then you have a more
complete story.  This one has "sizzle", just like yours:

  Scenario 1. (Park improvement plan)
  http://zelea.com/project/votorola/d/theory.xht#scenario-1

But here's the missing "steak":

  Scenario detail 1.2.
  http://zelea.com/project/votorola/d/theory.xht#scenario-1.2

When it comes to action, normative assent (policies or plans) and
electoral assent (Congressmen or Mayors) will probably be mutually
reinforcing.

> You may also be interested in viewing the "Data Model" discussion available
> here:
> https://clearspace.democracylab.org/community/website
> 
> I'll look forward to any feedback you're able to provide.

You have no design docs yet.  I think you are still prototyping a
mechanism that links assent (votes) to content creation?  The design
problem is to aggregate the content into a consensus text.  (I would
compare your mechanism with Votorola's cascade voting + recombinant
text.)

You have not yet implemented (still too early) such facilities as
system distribution and administration across multiple regions and
juridictional levels of government, divisional boundaries of electoral
districts, voter registration and authentication by district, and
verification of counts?  Votorola might be of help with some of these.

If I'm misreading anything (or just plain wrong), please correct me,
