On Sun, Oct 19, 2008 at 1:41 PM, Ed Pastore <epastore at metagovernment.org> wrote:
> Just an informational post about my presentation...
> http://www.webexpo.cz/prednaska/web-20-for-governance/
>
> I spoke yesterday to a group of about 100 people. There are about 500
> attendees at the conference, but considering that only one other speech was
> in English (and unlike mine, that was interpreted into Czech), I'd say
> attendance was quite good.
>
> Language was somewhat of a barrier, but they seemed to get the drift.
> Questions after the speech were the same ones we've encountered on the list
> and wiki... e.g., what to do in an emergency, how can we solve for corrupt
> system admins, what about cabals trying to game the system. In other words,
> they were listening and thinking about it. Sounds like a success to me.
>
> After my speech and later that evening, I talked to a few attendees in
> depth. They seemed to think the project was a great idea. Some were more
> inclined to favor the sort of "mixdemocracy" projects listed on our related
> projects, and I encouraged them to go forward with that. It's a logical
> intermediate step between representative democracy and true open source
> governance. Others seemed enthusiastic, and I think a few may have joined
> the list already (if so, please feel free to introduce yourselves).
>
> Video of my speech may be available later; I'm not quite sure. In any event,
> it mostly followed the Presentation page on the wiki. I'll post an update to
> that when I get back to the 'States. I had some technical difficulties (my
> speech notes weren't showing up on my computer, so I forgot some stuff and
> overly repeated other stuff), but overall I think it went well.
>
> I should be active on the list again by the end of this week.
