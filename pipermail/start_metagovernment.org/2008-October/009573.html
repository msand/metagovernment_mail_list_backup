Developing the emerging "Fifth Estate" of participatory news, media, and
civic change

Bridging two established programs at MIT—one known for inventing alternate
technical futures, the other for identifying the cultural and social
potential of media change—the Center for Future Civic Media is a joint
effort between the MIT Media Lab and the MIT Comparative Media Studies
Program. It is made possible by a four-year grant from the Knight
Foundation.

The Center for Future Civic Media is working to create technical and social
systems for sharing, prioritizing, organizing, and acting on information.
These include developing new technologies that support and foster civic
media and political action; serving as an international resource for the
study and analysis of civic media; and coordinating community-based test
beds both in the United States and internationally.
