I think one thing that's needed is understanding that some problems can
work themselves out. People don't bother voting because they see it as
pointless, and current legislation is written in legalese and are all made
by pro-establishment parties. Once we're asking them to tune into what
their own communities are doing, to the questions Their communities find
important, 'voter turn out' becomes a different ball game. When we localize
we also contextualize.
