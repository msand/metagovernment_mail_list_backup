One thing that I gleaned from this discussion that I perhaps glossed over
before, is the notion of buying votes. With low voter turnout, even in the
case of the proxy situation, it might sometimes only require a few paid
individuals to swing a vote. Aside from imposing fines for voter bribing
and maybe even rewards for exposing bribing attempts, what else is needed?

I'm thinking in the case of Scott's proxy system that if, after the initial
voting stage is over when users have the option to switch their vote. If
enough of the matched proxies switch their vote, say over 30%, then the
rest also switch automatically. Since that would more than likely indicate
that the match is not accurate for the sphere they're influencing. Then
have an option for the users who got switched automatically to go back if
they want. Maybe that's unnecessary, but a thought anyway.

Cheers
