> Community also stakeholders. Everyone is community of some government.

> Stakeholder is used for the cases when a decision is enough relevant just to

> some people, e.g. aviation safety.



I disagree with the characterization and example: Everyone has "stake"

in aviation safety, as they never know when they're going to have to

fly and certainly don't want planes falling on their houses. But the

same is true of *every* public policy decision: I'm not a woman, but I

have a "stake" in deciding what a moral abortion policy is. I don't

have any school-age children, but have a "stake" in how our education

system works and on tax allocation to pay for them. All using the term

"stakeholder" does is allow some people to claim extra privilege.

Unfortunately, as it turns out, it's usually exactly the *wrong*

people to be deferring those decisions to because they are exactly the

people who will abuse those privileges. Allowing "stakeholders" to

define policy is merely an expediency because government doesn't want

to have to deal with them complaining about how they're being treated.

But as long as we can control SDAP influence and thereby ensure that

neurotypicals are making the decisions, they will by definition be

"fair" because it will reflect human needs and human nature and won't

be just the result of some group or personality type gaming the

system.
