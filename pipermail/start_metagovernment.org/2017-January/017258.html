I think with the right set up DD can help change creep in as long as it's
proving effective elsewhere. Earlier today I heard from someone who thought
we expected everyone to just hop on board from day one, like that was
necessary or even possible. It starts with a really solid design that
succeeds in creating communities that thrive and expand.

Plus even if the majority ruled they really would still be at least
thinking about sustainability/not killing themselves. Even a tyranny of the
majority beats self-extinction. But also that when we try appealing to
people in power, we often forget that those people's hands are seriously
tied. And that our interests involve big players making less money
otherwise it wouldn't be a problem. So at least when we have to appeal to
'majorities' things like compassion, respect for dignity, and self respect
can actually come into play. The bottom line isn't money, and any proposed
forms of oppression will be visible to everyone as well as increasingly
subject to their criticisms.

Plus before full scale implementation DD can be used to create global
accords, so if some community of shitheads tries making slavery or
something else that violates the global accords, we can sanction, boycott
and all kinds of other things to make that option too expensive for them.
Especially if instead of fighting to get them ruled by our choice of rulers
we're simply saying basic, highly defensible things like 'no slavery'. If
people try making global pacts that are too nitpicky it'll be easy for
others to argue we should respect localities sovereignty and that global
pacts are for basic human rights.

I think we need to start making better infographics. We're reaching the
Galileo stage of this movement where we know the answers to our critics
better than they do. If we all start making solid explanatory media then we
can start hitting the culture wars hard. Like a movement truly for and by
the people should be!
