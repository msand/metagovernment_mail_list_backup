Ed's reference to "ground swell" prompts me to:



1)      Forward a link to the StratML document in which the essence of the
book by that name is distilled -- http://xml.gov/stratml/Groundswell.xml --
and

2)      Suggest that we flesh out Metagovernment's strategic plan --
http://xml.gov/stratml/MGP.xml -- into a *performance plan* incorporating
proposed inputs, processes, and outputs (and associated stakeholders,
particularly of the performer type) along the lines that Ed is suggesting.



While AIIM's StratML Committee is just beginning to specify Part 2 of the
StratML standard, which will contain the elements of performance plans and
reports, we anticipate having a first draft of the schema before the
committee's next telecon on Tuesday, April 14.
http://xml.gov/stratml/index.htm#WorkResults



It would be neat if Metagovernment.org could use its own Metascore software
to enable stakeholder feedback on the elements of its own strategic and
performance plans.  The sooner we ourselves begin to do what we are
suggesting, the sooner we can expect to seriously think that others may wish
to start doing likewise.



Needless to say, if there is will among the group to do so, I will be happy
to render Metagovernment's performance plan in conformance with the
soon-to-be available first-draft of the schema for Part 2 of the StratML
standard, as a proof-of-concept pilot test of the draft schema as well as in
support of the vision of Metagovernment.
