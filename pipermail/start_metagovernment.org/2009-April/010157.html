> Hi Ed,
> I would say that for now Giovani's idea to build the separate
> components first, and then put them together is probably more
> appropriate. Somehow each brick is going through its own personal
> evolution, to do its work perfectly. Then we might find that different
> situation (i.e. size of the group, percentage of people participating,
> etc...) might call for different instruments.
>
> The idea of a single instrument that work on every scale might not be
> possible. And this limit might not depend on computer power but on
> human power. Imagine a solution that requires every participant to
> read every solution of the other. It might works wonders for small
> groups, but be unusable for groups of more than 100 people. But this
> would not mean that we cannot split a complex problems into a series
> of simpler and solvable ones. Only that now we are using wo
> instruments, the one that you use to solve a single problem, and the
> algorithm that splits up the complex one into smaller components. You
> see what I mean when I speak of different components?
>
> So I would say we will have more than one prototype, and we might even
> have the same idea being implemented with slight variation here and
> there, with the effect of seeing how robust the results are. It's all
> food, at the end ;)
