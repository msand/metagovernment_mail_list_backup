I was able to attend the below-referenced session, and found that five  
of the six platforms there were not so much debate visualization tools  
as websites which try to organize debates on specific issues. In each  
case, their goal is to inform peoples' decisions about issues, but not  
to help drive toward any sort of consensus.

http://debategraph.org/ however is indeed a sophisticated  
visualization tool. As is I planned on talking with David Price, who  
was presenting it, but was not able to coordinate schedules (my  
presentation was right after his, and I had to leave early to  
prepare). However, Pietro and/or Manuel, I think you may want to talk  
to him about leveraging his tool toward Metascore. (Or Pietro, do you  
already have a visualizer involved in the FP7 proposal?)

I have been conversing with him by e-mail, but I am not the right  
person to carry the conversation forward, so... if one of you would  
like, I'll e-introduce you.

Also http://deepdebate.org/  (which was not represented at the above  
panel) is an interesting a deliberately less complex tool which may  
also be of interest.

P.S. I am off on a business trip for the next week so may be sporadic  
in communication. But please do go forward with discussions, of course.
