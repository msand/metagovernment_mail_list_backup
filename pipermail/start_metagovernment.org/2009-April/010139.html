Ed, presentation (styling) of the data is not among the purposes of the
StratML standard itself: http://xml.gov/stratml/index.htm#DefinitionPurposes


 

However, Ken Holman of Crane Softwrights has provided a basic HTML style
sheet that can easily be applied to StratML documents, as I have now done at
http://xml.gov/stratml/index.htm#Other or, more specifically,
http://xml.gov/stratml/crane/MGP.xml  

 

You can check out how Xopus has styled former President Bush's Management
Agenda at http://xml.gov/stratml/index.htm#FedAgencies   Note that one of
those renditions is editable using no additional software other than a
browser.

 

Mark Logic also applies styling in its StratML search service prototype:
http://xml.gov/stratml/index.htm#SearchServices  If you'd like to check it
out, the UID and password are respectively:  stratml_demo & marklogic   

 

I understand that Mind Touch is also considering implementing StratML in
Deki Wiki.  If they do so, it might be interesting to use it to
collaboratively flesh out Metagovernment.org's mission, values, and goal
into a performance plan against which progress can be measured and reported
on the Web.

 

The StratML Committee is just beginning to consider Part 2 of the StratML
standard, which will contain the elements of performance plans and reports.
http://xml.gov/stratml/index.htm#WorkResults  However, if there is interest
among subscribers to the Metagovernment Startup Committee in drafting a
performance plan, I will be more than happy to render it in StratML format -
if a collaborative means is not yet available by which all of the members of
the Startup Committee can participate directly in doing so.
