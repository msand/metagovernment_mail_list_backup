I am a little frazzled today and don't have much time, but I can at  
least share a few links I took away from eDemocracyCamp (most of which  
I tweeted earlier).

This group has talked a lot about how to visualize debates, for example:
http://www.metagovernment.org/wiki/Talk:PrototypeA#Comments_structure
http://www.metagovernment.org/wiki/FP7#Tags
and I saw brief demos of a couple of nice systems:
http://debategraph.org/
http://deepdebate.org/debates/221
I hope that tomorrow I will be able to get to this session at the POLC:
http://www.opencongress.org/wiki/The_Future_of_Public_Debate_Online
where I will see several more. Look at the great links on the above  
page, under "Speakers."

On collaboration systems, http://www.pligg.com/ seems interesting.  
Sort of a CMS with an internal Digg-like system. It is open source,  
uses a 5-star scoring system, and... well, have a look. Somewhat  
similarly, http://www.mixedink.com/ is a mass-collaboration writing  
system.

I also found http://opendialogcoalition.com/web/ interesting and think  
that they need to talk to both Owen and Pietro about efforts for data  
normalization and open architectures for sharing between communities.

I think there was more, but I am under-slept and need to rest up for  
POLC tomorrow. I'll try to write about that, but am leaving the next  
day on a business trip so may be on limited involvement for another  
week.
