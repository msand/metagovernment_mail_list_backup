My best guess is Metagovernment will follow a bottom-up direction, but will
face more and more difficuties as it tries to deal with more complex
communities. For instance, I think it can govern a residence condo quite
well, managing even the operative staff. When you go up to small
communities, like neighborhoods/small countys, probably Metagovernment can
take care of it on its own too.

However, when you go up to cities and more complex social structures I think
an administrative staff will be absolutely needed. As a matter of fact, I
once thought of different government setting of direct democracy, mostly
based on Athenian Democracy. That government would have executive and
judiciary powers, but the legislative would be extinct. In its place there
would be a Metagovernment like structure with powers such as:
-sanction or veto, totally or partially, Legislative issues;
-sanction or veto, totally or partially, executive and budget plans proposed
by the Excutive Power;
-sanction or veto, totally or partially, some executive decrees (such as
military issues and wages of public workers);
-elect and fire the highest authorities of other Powers (such as president,
prime-minister, ministers, or other entities the new government might use)
-judge political crimes done by the highest authorities of other Powers;
and,
-vote on any other matter relative to the State, exceptionally proposed
through population initiative, with a higher authority than any other Power.
Therefore this "Peoples Council" would be the sovereign ruler, changing
administrative staff decrees whenever the population sees fit, albeit
leaving some degree of freedom for the other Powers.

So, in my opinion, an administrative staff is needed and Metagovernment can
only replace the entire government structure in a quite distant future,
one where people actually have a lot of spare time to do it. Then again,
there are always the ideals of Bertrand Russel and Domenico de Masi on
Creative Idleness...

> (Probably this mail won't add new ideas, just make them explicit.)

> It is not clearly how Metagovernment is going to *replace* the entire
> government mechanism of a community (but that is what makes this project
> different and special). I think that the opinion between project's members
> is divided in those who believe this could be done and those that are pretty
> sure this is impossible.
>
> Metascore is intended to act as a collaborative legislative power. Nobody
> has said a word about judicial power, anyway I'm sure this will be resolved
> in the future. And finally... the executive power. In an organization we
> often see two kinds of actions: operative and administrative. For example
> —in a school—, a teacher does the first and a director the second. The
> teacher has no powers and the director is an executive.
>
> The question is: who will do the administrative work?
> a) The members through "Meta administration". They are remunerated as in an
> open company.
> b) The organization hires an administrative staff. They are somewhat
> powerless, they follow resolutions given from Metascore, but they organize
> and give orders to the operative staff.
