> Dear Ed,
> dear list,
>
> yes, in vague terms I can. In more precise terms not yet.
>
> The FP7 proposal was sent, and it was good. I am very pleased by the
> resulting document.
> We are now a series of 6 partners, of which 5 scientific. Coimbra is
> mainly involved in developing the new knowledge, given the past
> knowledge. Essentially what we all have been interested. In doing this
> we will have enough money to employ at least 2 PhD students, and maybe
> more.
>
> Part of our work will be done in investigating and employing the full
> power of the tags.
> Another part in describing how given n people who are proposing
> solutions on a topic we can integrate them in a coherent whole. We
> proposed two different solutions, on the spot, but we also said we
> will investigate for any other solution that might work.
> (A personal thanks to Mathias who sent me a book reference on voting.
> That book is great, I think you should share it with the whole group.
> Not only I referenced it in the proposal, but I learned a great deal
> from it. Thanks again.)
>
> I see one of the PhD student will mainly work on the tags, and the
> other on synthesizing group knowledge.
>
> I expect that in Coimbra, we will all work generally as a team, with
> everybody feeding each other with ideas. One other partner is
> specifically employed to program all this stuff at a professional
> level. Which mean that although we are all going to program, we will
> also have a lot of help from outside. So we can focus on the research
> behind it. The prior work, the possible solutions. In short make a dam
> good work!
>
> I just received some preliminary data, and we are competing with other
> 50 projects for about 10 positions. So one project on 5 will get
> funded. I think we have reasonable chances, as I see our project as
> really good. But then I have been told that NO ONE gets more than 30%
> (or less that 10%) of probability in this game. It is a lot a matter
> of luck, and often is the wrong project that gets funded.
>
> Note that among the elements of the project we are also suggesting to
> fund a conference each year in Europe on the topic. This will not only
> promote the project but bring on the awareness on the topic. I think
> if our project gets funded, this might be one of the greatest reasons
> of interests for our list.
>
> Please forgive me if I cannot enter more in the detail. There is a lot
> more, and it is all really cool. Of course all the members of this
> list can apply for a PhD position, although I will need to take the
> best that appears to me, without making any favoritism toward anybody.
>
> Manuel, FYI, another partner is another German University, so you
> might want to apply there if you don't fancy applying for the the
> warmth, and sun of Portugal, or someone with a stronger CV appears.
> But the synthesizing group knowledge stuff, and the tags, are only
> happening here.
>
> Also all the code that is being produced will be released, although I
> don't think this will be done as it is produced, but as it is common
> in academic it will be released at the end (or halfway).
>
> Now we have a big jigsaw of competing interests that need to be put
> together to go forward. If I sent you any info on this stuff, please
> wait to use it until it has been released (more about it later). We
> want to get something out as soon as possible, and we want to get a
> paper out as soon as possible too. This to permit to everybody to join
> in in the global think tank. And this can only be done when the first
> paper is out to avoid late discussions (if you have worked in academia
> you know what I am referring to).
>
> But the paper in turn need to wait until the EU has evaluated our  
> project.
> Also the paper will need some simple test cases.
>
> SO what I think will happen is that
> a) we shall write a small prototype of a few ideas, and go forward
> with that. I am so excited about this stuff that I cannot sit still
> unless I know some work is progressing.
> b)  the prototype will enter an extensive alpha stage that will enter
> when the paper is out, and the EU has evaluated the project (with ANY
> result).
>
> While the project is in alpha stage some people of this list will be
> allowed to play with it. But not the general public, or people we
> don't know. This because the EU project evaluation is tied to this
> work, and we don't want to screw that up, right?
>
> I should also mention that you should all greatly thank Giovani for
> the work done. Not only he started this whole FP7 project, he also
> worked for it until the last day, making often the nights ( I would
> receive emails in the morning), and he really worked hard in
> accommodating and smoothing out some hardness we had inside the group
> due to real big cultural differences. Without him the proposal would
> not only have started, but also once started not have reached this
> level. Even if the proposal will not be approved, the ideas we
> developed and clarified in the proposal will keep on going forward
> (although at a slower rate). And this by itself is a great success.
>
> Manuel, Mathias, and Ed, I know I sent you some info about a few of
> the ideas we were working on. Please keep those info for yourself, and
> wait before applying them (even in your personal project). It is
> fairly important that it all appears (as it actually was) that those
> ideas were generated from the FP7 team. If someone finds some of those
> ideas released out in the open, he might think that the ideas were not
> original, and then the whole project is at risk. Plus it would be
> quite unfair toward those who came out with them.
>
> I am right now in England, in the same town of one of the EU partners,
> and we have already started some preliminary work, and test cases on
> the prototype. Stay tuned.
>
> Best Regards,
