Thomas von der Elbe schrieb:
> Wouldn't this sound strange to the public: "Here is the contract,
> which says how the control is going to be placed in your hands, but
> we might still change it, even if you have 999 votes already!" ?

That's true, it looks fishy.  And the veto also looks fishy, at first
glance.  I guess we have to explain to folks that if they succeed in
building a 999-vote consensus, then it's perfectly safe.  Their
consensus cannot be modified (or vetoed) by the caretakers, only the
use of the *domain name*.  People can easily find another domain name,
and carry on with that.

Maybe the caretakers all become corrupted by power, and "steal" the
name away from the public?  To prevent that, we probably need 3 or 4
caretakers.  Then the likelihood of a conspiracy is close to zero.
Who wants to be hated by the general public?

Leaving that aside (barring the possibility of a conspiracy), then
it's always better to have an amended contract that *keeps* the domain
name alive, rather than a vetoed contract that kills it.  So the main
problem becomes preventing the caretakers from being forced into a
veto situation that nobody wants, simply because they can't modify the
contract.

Does that make sense?  Or am I missing something?  (It's getting late
here.)  In the meantime, here's another change to limit the likelihood
of a unwanted veto.  This one provides a "cooling off" period.  It
also specifies how the costs of veto are shared by all the caretakers:
http://u.zelea.com:8080/v/w/Diff?b=3424&a=3481
