I have moved away from the immediate vicinity of Washington, and would  
have difficulty attending this conference this year.

I did attend the first one, and found it to be very energetic and  
interesting, though very focused on the status quo. I saw a couple of  
times when I could tell people were thinking that the current  
institutions of governance were entirely outmoded, but they couldn't  
make the intellectual leap to actually taking steps toward changing  
it. By far, almost everything there was about making government *data*  
more open and accessible, with the question of actual governance being  
a non-issue. At most, people were interested in building more  
effective systems to let representatives receive input from their  
constituents. But everyone assumed the representatives had to stay for  
some reason.
