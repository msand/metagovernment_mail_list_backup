the draft rules was making my head hurt. Every time I had a little  
time to sit down and think about the project, I would turn to that  
draft, my head would spin, and I would give up. So I'm guessing that  
that implies that it is overly complex.

I'm willing to go with your proposal, Thomas, as currently drafted:
http://metagovernment.org/wiki/User:ThomasvonderElbe/draft_rules

So to support that, do I vote for you, or change my own proposal to  
match yours? In either case, can you humor me and tell me step-by-step  
how I would do it?

Thanks.

>>>>> @ Ed:
>>>>>> I don't want to get overly complex, but perhaps it would be  
>>>>>> reasonable
>>>>>> to use some more complex math than simple counting or  
>>>>>> percentages.
>>>>>>
>>>>>> For example, how defining consensus (C) as:
>>>>>> ( X * (P+2) ) - 1, rounded up.
>>>>>>
>>>>>> where P = the number of people voting,
>>>>>> and X = a variable set by the software, defaulting to 0.8.
>>>>>>
>>>>>> Therefore, for X = 0.8...
>>>>>> For 1 person, C is equal to 2. (i.e., impossible)
>>>>>> For 2 people, C is equal to 3. (i.e., impossible)
>>>>>> For 3 through 7 people, C is equal to P. (i.e. 100%).
>>>>>> For 8 people, C is equal to 7.
>>>>>> For 20 people, C is equal to 17
>>>>>> For 100 people, C is equal to 81.
>>>>>> For very large numbers of people, C is basically equal to 80%  
>>>>>> of P.
>>>>>>
>>>>>> In other words, the standard is, roughly:
>>>>>> Impossible for one or two people. Complete consensus for small  
>>>>>> numbers
>>>>>> of people. Near-consensus for intermediate groups. And 80% (or  
>>>>>> X) for
>>>>>> large groups.
