David Bovill wrote:
> 1) The physical (theatrical) interface, within which groups meet and
> how these connected spaces can be made to embrace a full range of
> participants and expressed human values.
>
> 2) The legal aspects of the technical language that these spaces are
> programmed in. How to both democratise the underlying technology and
> make the decisions legally binding.

The word "legal" confuses me here.  By democratizing the technology, I
assume you mean the administration of the technical infrastructure and
its associated institutions.  Not the technology per se, which is the
knowledge of those.  (This is just quibble over words.)

Making the decisions legally binding might be a separate topic.  But
here the word "legal" appears to beg the question of exactly how they
would be made binding: by law.  (Again, just a quibble.)

> 3) Longitudinal studies. To establish a research paradigm based on
> real world effects of democratic methodologies on long term group
> behaviour.

To establish such a paradigm would require a successful project of
empirical research which, given the nature of the study you propose,
could only be conducted over the long term.  4 years is probably
insufficient time.  (Item 3 seems impractical.)
