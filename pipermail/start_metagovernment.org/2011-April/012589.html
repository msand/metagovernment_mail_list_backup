On Apr 5, 2011, at 9:44 AM, Michael Allan wrote:

> I don't think it would be overly ambitious, though it might pan out as
> more of an exchange than a simple subsumption.  They have a
> rudimentary voting facility that doesn't easily dovetail with other
> media, such as drafting or discussion - not to mention other voting
> facilities.  So they won't likely be strong on that front.  We could
> maybe show them a thing or two.

The problem here seems to be in sorting out the irrelevant posts. I  
can see people posing important questions on Facebook, but I can  
imagine that 95% of what appears in the Questions will be on the order  
of "who is going to advance in American Idol" and "What should I get  
Susan for her birthday."

Can you think of a way to tie into only the governance-related  
questions without any input from Facebook? All I can think of off hand  
is a simple hack: telling people that if they add something into their  
question, then it will get pulled into the Crossforum. In other words,  
something on the order of a hashtag, where the connections are made by  
the users, regardless of the underlying structure.

> On the other hand, they might still manage to attract lots of users.
> Not all of them would remain 100% satisfied, of course, and some might
> even stir up trouble for the company.  We could maybe take some of the
> pressure off by offering connections to alternative toolsets.

There is a lot of potential there. People may start out on Facebook  
because that's what they know, but when they find that they have  
hundreds of answers and a simplistic voting system, they may start  
looking outward for something more robust. The Crossforum should allow  
them to easily migrate the discussion to a more robust platform  
without necessarily losing all of the initial input (nor the social  
publicity, which is not to be undervalued).
