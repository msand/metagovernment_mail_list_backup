Thomas von der Elbe wrote:
> @ Mike and Ed: yes, consenus-rule has worked so far for us, but
> should we really write it down as such? I am afraid of the case,
> where a single person can block our whole project forever. I mean,
> we are a special group in the sense, that we are open to everybody
> on the planet! I think consensus-rule works better for closed groups
> where everybody knows each other. Do we want to be that vulnerable?

I don't think we'll be all that vulnerable.  First, in order to take
advantage of any perceived vulnerability, an attacker would have to
participate.  So we'd win in the first instance, because participation
is our #1 aim.

In the second instance, any attack would give us a legitimate reason
to propose changes to the threshold rule in our defense.
