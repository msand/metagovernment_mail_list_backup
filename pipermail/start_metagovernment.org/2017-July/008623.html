Interesting how they do English and Spanish not with subtitles but
with English on the left channel and Spanish on the right.

I'll admit to just skimming it (it's over 2 hours long!) but what I
saw is the usual detailed descriptions of the problems with a lot of
handwaving about solutions. This one, like most of the "commons" based
proposals seemed to me particularly weak on the transition plan. They
just seem to fundamentally lack any awareness that it's
*decisionmaking* that's the problem, not anything to do with the
economy: Even if there *were* something fundamentally wrong with plain
old capitalism (which they are far from proving, or even providing
arguments that couldn't easily be ripped to shreds by anyone who
understands both systems) you *still* have to solve the decisionmaking
problem first in order to implement any replacement. They all seem to
live in this fantasyland where oligarchs just voluntarily give up
their power as a response to a small minority of activists asking them
to do so, armed only with "better ideas". Has any revolution happened
that way?
