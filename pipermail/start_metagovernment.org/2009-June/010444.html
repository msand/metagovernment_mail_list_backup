I'm not extremely concerned about how to approach other groups, as we  
have already attracted several. People from Votorola, Vilfredo,  
Telemetrics, and Metascore have voiced support for the proposal. And I  
believe those from NationBuilder and DemocracyLab would also be  
interested, judging by their past participation in this list. And then  
we also historically have had interest from several other groups as  
listed on the Related Projects page. Once we have a large handful of  
such groups, their mere presence should help attract others. (Though  
of course we should actively reach out to others.)

As for a plan, I think we have a good start at http://metagovernment.org/wiki/Startup/Organization/Proposal_2 
  and would invite you and others to expand/refine it as you can.  
There are many sub-projects which will need to be tackled as we start  
to expand on that plan.

Concerning identifying stakeholders, it seems a bit strange in our  
case. We can rightly claim that eventually our beneficiary- 
stakeholders will be every single human being on the planet (and  
perhaps other intelligences as well).

But concerning participation, I think there are basically two kinds of  
stakeholders:
- those actively, formally involved in the creation of governance  
software
- others who wish to be involved in the overall process, promotion,  
etc. but are not currently involved in a specific project.
We need the resulting organization to be able to allow effective  
communication among members of the first group without having it  
swamped by members of the second. Yet, being an inherently inclusive  
group, we need to do that in such a way that members of the second  
group can contribute to the conversations of the first.
