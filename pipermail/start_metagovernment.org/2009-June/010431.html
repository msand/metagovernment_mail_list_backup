You can play around with OpenSocial best on ning.com.  If you start a social
network on there, there's a way for you to add difference OpenSocial apps to
it.

OpenSocial hasn't been particularly popular, and it's kinda clunky how it
works.   It was released by Google and MySpace primarily as an answer to
Facebook's platform.   Now, I think the model is moving more towards how
Facebook Connect works, where the social graph can be used within apps
outside of the Facebook wall.
