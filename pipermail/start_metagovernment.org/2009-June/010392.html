Ed Pastore wrote:
> A simple extension of this [neighbourhood trust network] would be to
> require people to submit a 10-second video with each post they make,
> where they just state their name and the proposal they are referring
> to.
>
> I know not everybody has vid capabilities right now, but that is certainly 
> changing. Til then people could post a pic of themselves holding a piece of 
> paper with a ref to the proposal on it.
>
> I know this sounds a little laborious (though not if the sw is 
> well-designed), but it does readily prove consistency of identity in 
> relation to the proposal at hand. And it is ammenable to integration with 
> facial recognition and voice-printing, should a particular community want 
> to use those.

I think it sounds OK.  Offhand I see no technical problems.  I'm
pretty sure we'll need multiple authentication methods (and variants
of each) for different purposes, and I bet we'll often want to combine
them too - similar to showing 3 pieces of ID - for particular
authentication tasks.

I guess none of that can happen, though, without an open architecture.
I agree with Thomas (and Owen too, I think) that the openness ought to
extend across projects, organizations, etc.  So all of this seems to
be pointing to a networked architecture.

It's almost like the technical questions are kind of boring, because
they are relatively easy to answer.  They don't seem to be the bone of
contention, anyway.  I feel it's more difficult to answer Thomas.
