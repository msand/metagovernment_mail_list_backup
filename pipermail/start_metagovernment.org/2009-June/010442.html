I think this is super-exciting, especially because you have identified a target population with the need for a system like this, and (potentially) the resources/will to make it happen. My applause to you both!! And, best wishes.

In observing the other projects with and without representation on the list, it seems that *local* implementations may be more successful in conveying our broad concepts their utility, since the exercise in global or national spheres often feels theoretical. At this point, there is little perceptible impact.

I have often considered a similar approach here in the US, only through the community parent-teacher organizations that participate in governing the local school systems. These groups do not often mature to be politicians, but the debates are heated and the stakes high. But, this population does not have IT resources on hand, nor any interest in development so the software has to come first.

All this is to say, how very excited I am to hear your plans. I hope they serve as a model for similar initiatives.

Please post to the list with ideas/updates as I for one am interested to know how it goes.

Jacki
