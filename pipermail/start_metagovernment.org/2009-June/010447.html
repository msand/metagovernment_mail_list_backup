As Jacki said, open source governance ideas go global, but  
implementation is better suited locally. I've reading the law  
(unpleasant task) about non-profit organizations in Argentina, I have  
both: good and bad news. Conforming the group of the first kind...  
mounting one costs around 90 dollars (plus some time, at least seven  
founders, and a lawyer—I'm trying to get one for free), and it can be  
legally related to similar foreign non-profit's (yes, we can have an  
international web of them). The bad news is the archaic conception of  
'civil organization' here, it seems made for neighborhood sportive &  
social clubs (you need to relate the organization to a physical  
place, for example). But well, nothing that bad.

I speculate with forming one as soon as possible; so, it can be  
presented at Wikimania (along the new truly meta-project air of  
Metagovernment). As we've said, a non-profit will surround the  
movement with a lot of seriousness.

Also I met a guy nicknamed Maiqui —via another friend— who was

reasons. (I remember finding Metagovernment googling, (even) when it  
was a project on Sourceforge.) One of them maybe a very soft use of  
the Internet; the ads are going to tackle this down.
