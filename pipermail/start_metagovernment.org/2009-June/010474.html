> This is a wonderful usability model, but I'm not entirely clear what  
> the usability model is for some of the other projects on  
> Metagovernment. Are each of us aiming at different aspects of the  
> decision making process, such as primary elections, governing  
> policies, et al? And if so, should we seek for some basic level of  
> interoperability to allow the user to move from the brainstorming  
> phases to the election phases, all the way down to the point at

That's the idea behind the re-organization proposal:
http://metagovernment.org/wiki/Startup/Organization/Proposal_2
which I would like to remind everyone to give input into by June 30:
http://metagovernment.org/wiki/Startup/Organization#Proposal_2

There is also the corollary Standardization process, which is in its  
infancy:
http://metagovernment.org/wiki/Standardization

> Essentially, there are too many paths for a user to take towards an

> I can contribute something of value from my own bag o' skills.

I think it will be wonderful for the real-world marketplace to play  
with all of these various formations and see what develops. But if  
you're looking for a place to contribute, the Metascore project is  
dying for your help and is the only of these many projects that is  
very actively seeking just the sort of help you could provide. Its  
user experience is its greatest weakness at this time.
