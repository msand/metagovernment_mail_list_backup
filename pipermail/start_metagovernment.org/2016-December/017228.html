We propose and vote on taxes.  Here in Oregon:

We proposed a tax on gross revenue of certain larger corporations via
initiative .  We voted.  We failed.  Opposition made up the label "sales
tax".  Supporters wanted to better fund education.

The money against the tax came from corporations who have many low margin
inventory turns per annum.  The support money came from teacher unions and
progressives and such.

We have a billion dollar deficient, lowest corporate tax rate in the nation
and the second lowest graduation rate in the nation and high per student
expenditure.

Our state constitution make education the top priority.

I suspect this is the best summary that ever was published on the
initiative.

I voted for it but I  expect it would not have improved education much.

Further we have a multitude of  taxing districts.  The boards thereof may
propose levies and bond measures to the voters.  The voters can propose and
vote on new districts.
