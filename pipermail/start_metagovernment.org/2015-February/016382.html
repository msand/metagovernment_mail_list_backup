have fun guys wasting time with debating exact wording.
the problem is complex and i'm sure it can be put into a math formula - the
details of that formula can be debated until the last day of the universe.
...while every day life of billions of people is going on as usual.

If you figure out a definition you agree on, you will then shake hands and
have a nice warm feeling of hapiness... then the next guy comes along who
has slightly different definitions or perspective and he will again go with
you through all the history of discussion and if you are lucky it will just
take you a few months of talking in circles to get yet a third ally or
maybe you will even fight because for some reason its hard to agree of
whether "global" or "worldwide"  ...or "resource" or "money" or "debt" or
"centralbanking" or "capitalism" is the main word that should be put the
focus on.

And in addition, once agreen on that, then the strategic discussion starts,
whats smarter to talk about to the general public? should it be more
careful and use new words like "plutocracy" and add a little "twist" to it
so that people need to look up the word and think, or is it easier to
mobilize with using "capitalism" or dont we live in the world of the
internet? maybe something more 2.0ish?


RI would suggest, stop WASTING time with talking weeks and months over the
difference between certain words and phrases, but instead start creating a
new practice of collaboration - in particular: programming to improve this
world :-)

Vilfredo is a project that seems to be a success compared to the average
project somehow associated with metagovernment and they work their asses of
and figure out the details while they learn. This approach uses the same
forces used by "traditional entrepreneurship" to build the next sucessful
global corporation.

I think open source can do something similar and its these day more true
than ever...

> I also believe that we live in global plutocracy. Resources are power
> weather it's money, people, product, service, energy, or intelligence. It's
> a game of probability and manipulation of human psychology. Give a dog a
> bone, give a politician that which fulfills desire. The more you have the
> more you can do, opening new doors of opportunity. If ten doors lead to
> nowhere you still have hundreds more to try.
>
> Humans are good at milking resources, continually expanding on knowledge
> and technology to more efficiently extract what is considered wealth. First
> came the gathering of basic needs, studying animals behavior and hunting
> them. Next came farming. Alongside were the exraction of resources for
> tools. This continued to grow into more complex forms of extraction. Now we
> have people extracting wealth from extremely complex systems, like the
> stock market, legal loopholes, and the manipulation of law itself.
> Corporations use their resources to study the complex systems of economics,
> politics, advertisement, etc, in order to find ways in gaining more
> resources. The most successful corporations are like clockwork machines,
> that find exploits in systems or efficient ways to extract from the systems.
>
> Why reinvent the wheel when we see examples in our environment of what
> actually works to influence continuous political change? The best example
> that has been given to us throughout the centuries are those who have lots
> of resources. In modern times these are the corporations. Isn't the one who
> controls the most resources the actual politician or true leader? Kings,
> elites, and corporations, they make the true decisions.
>
> This is exactly what I want to do. I want to create a business that pays
> people fairly to do what they enjoy, finding market opportunities that
> better society, and make a lot of profits to reinvest back into society.
> Change things where change is already taking place, corporate influence on
> politics, corporate influence on information. Build a machine that has
> foundational policies that rest upon what's good for society rather than
> what's solely good for profit. Build that machine with the mechanisms of
> business, solid technologies, open source innovation, and competition.
> Build a machine in which resources do not concentrate wealth to the degree
> of modern capitalism. This successful machine is a business with the
> purpose of making profit for its participants and the surrounding
> community, the true investors.
>
> Lets turn the tide by understanding the mechanisms of the modern world and
> work with those mechanisms rather than against them. Let science pave the
> way.
