This seems to me a good start and could be a very useful tool for
convincing people that plutocrats are not the only thing our new
systems need to focus on.

>                    Authoritarians         Plutocrats
> location       national                  global

This sounds more like "orientation" to me: Authoritarians are
nationalist (or more accurately tribalist), but plutocrats are more
oriented toward economic boundaries than geopolitical boundaries.

> personality  prejudiced              selfish

I'd probably use "aggressive" over "prejudiced".  Both groups are prejudiced.

> job security voted                      inherited

Good, although maybe "source of power" would be a better name for this category.

> education    poor                        best

Not exactly: There are a lot of very well educated authoritarians
(Bush/Cheney, Hitler, and Mao were all quite well educated).  If there
is an educational distinction, it's that plutocrats use educational
institution as a filter (their associates all also were educated at
famous private schools) whereas authoritarians will pick their
associates from the public at large.

> safety          public person         hidden

Again, not exactly: Authoritarian *leaders* have to be very public,
but the followers do not have to be.  Maybe call this "exercise of
power", and for authoritarians it would be "through an authoritarian
leader" whereas plutocrats exercise power through *any* leadership.

> deadliness   World war II           World war I, Vietnam,...

I'd break this into "scale of conflicts", with authoritarians being
oriented toward large-scale and bet-the-farm conflicts whereas
plutocrats are more into small scale "strategic advantage" types of
conflicts.  Plutocrats are also more "regime change" oriented (the
installation and support of corrupt regimes in central and south
america and the Iraq war being better examples than any of the more
typical wars).

A few more:
power goals              political/social       economic
individual freedom     suppressed           permitted
favorite prejudice      racial/cultural         economic class

It's a hard exercise, because there are so many individuals (again
